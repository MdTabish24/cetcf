Detailed Syllabus
Module-by-Module Breakdown

Each module is designed to be practical-first. Theory gives the foundation; practical sessions build the real skill.

01
Skin Science & Client Consultation
Week 1 – 2  |  30 Hours
  • Skin anatomy — layers, appendages, functions
  • Skin types — oily, dry, combination, sensitive, normal
  • Fitzpatrick skin tone classification & undertones
  • Common skin conditions — acne, pigmentation, rosacea
  • Client intake form & skin analysis procedure
  • Patch test — purpose, method, interpretation
  • Contraindications & when to refer a client
  • Professional ethics & client confidentiality
  • Hygiene, sanitation & cross-contamination prevention
  • Indian skin tones — choosing suitable shades
02
Makeup Tools, Products & Colour Theory
Week 3 – 4  |  30 Hours
  • Makeup brush types — function, density, material
  • Sponges, blenders & applicators — usage & care
  • Product knowledge — primer, foundation, concealer, powder
  • Eye products — shadow, liner, mascara, lash glue
  • Lip products — liner, lipstick, gloss, plumper
  • Blush, bronzer, highlighter — differences & placement
  • Colour wheel — primary, secondary, complementary
  • Colour correction — peach, green, lavender, yellow
  • Product shelf life, storage & hygienic usage
  • Reading INCI labels & identifying key ingredients
03
Foundation Makeup Techniques
Week 5 – 6  |  30 Hours
  • Skincare prep before makeup — cleanse, tone, moisturise
  • Primer application — skin type-wise selection
  • Foundation — types (liquid, powder, stick, mineral)
  • Foundation application — brush, sponge, fingers
  • Shade matching — undertone, depth, finish
  • Concealer — under-eye, blemish, spot correction
  • Baking & setting powder technique
  • Contouring & highlighting — face shape guide
  • Blush application — placement for different face shapes
  • Setting spray — types and when to use
04
Eye, Lip & Advanced Makeup Artistry
Week 7 – 9  |  45 Hours
  • Eye shape analysis — almond, hooded, monolid, round, deep-set
  • Eyeshadow — transition, lid, crease, liner blend technique
  • Classic eye looks — natural, cut crease, smokey eye
  • Eyeliner styles — tightline, wing, Arabic kohl, graphic
  • Mascara techniques — volume, curl, length
  • Strip lash & individual lash application
  • Eyebrow shaping — mapping, pencil, powder, gel
  • Lip liner — shaping, overlining, ombre technique
  • Day makeup vs party/evening makeup differences
  • Layering & long-lasting makeup build-up
    • 05
    Bridal & Special Occasion Makeup
    Week 10 – 11  |  30 Hours
    • Bridal consultation — skin prep schedule (pre-bridal)
    • Waterproof product selection for bridal looks
    • North Indian bridal makeup — heavy coverage, glam
    • South Indian bridal look — natural, glow-forward
    • Muslim bridal makeup — modest, elegant style
    • Modern minimalist bridal — dewy skin focus
    • Hair accessories & jewellery placement basics
    • Airbrush makeup — concept, products, technique overview
    • Photography-ready makeup — lighting considerations
    • Touch-up kit preparation for bridal day
    06
    Salon Operations & Professional Skills
    Week 12  |  15 Hours
    • Salon setup — workstation layout, lighting, tools
    • Appointment management & client booking
    • Service pricing — costing makeup packages
    • Retail product selling — recommending homecare
    • Building a portfolio — photography tips for work
    • Social media for beauty professionals
    • Client feedback & handling complaints
    • Professional grooming & personal presentation
    • Tools sterilisation — UV box, alcohol, disposables
    • CETCF exam preparation — theory, practical & viva
    Assessment & Marking
    Exam Structure

    The exam is fully automated. All three components are completed online via the CETCF portal.

    📝
    Theory — MCQ
    50 Marks
    25 multiple-choice questions × 2 marks each. 45-minute timer. Auto-scored instantly on submission. Topics from all 6 modules.
    📸
    Practical — Portfolio Upload
    50 Marks
    Upload 4 required evidence items — before/after photos, contouring close-up, eye detail, and tools setup. Auto-scored on completion.
    Component Marks Format Mode Pass Requirement
    Theory (MCQ) 50 Marks 25 Questions × 2 Marks Online Portal — Auto Timer (45 min) Minimum 25/50 (50%)
    Practical Assessment 50 Marks 4 Evidence Uploads — Photo/Video Upload via CETCF Portal Minimum 25/50 (50%)
    Total 100 Marks — All Online ✓ 50 / 100 to Pass
    Practical Submission
    What to Upload for Practical Assessment

    Upload the following 4 evidence items through the CETCF candidate portal. Each item carries defined marks. Total: 50 Marks.

    1
    Before & After Makeup Photos — 2 photos required
    Client's clean face (before) and completed makeup look (after). Both well-lit, face clearly visible. Upload both together.
    20 Marks
    2
    Contouring & Highlighting Close-up — 1 photo required
    Face close-up clearly showing contour placement, highlight, and blush. Good lighting essential.
    15 Marks
    3
    Eye Makeup Detail Shot — 1 photo required
    Close-up of eyes showing eyeshadow blend, eyeliner application, and lashes. Eye shape and technique clearly visible.
    10 Marks
    4
    Clean Workstation & Tools Photo — 1 photo required
    Your organised brushes, products, and clean workspace. Demonstrates hygiene awareness and professional setup.
    5 Marks
