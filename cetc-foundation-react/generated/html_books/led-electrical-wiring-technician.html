LED & Electrical Wiring Technician

📚 Sector: Electrical & Electronics Trades 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the essentials of modern LED technology and electrical wiring systems used in homes, shops, and offices across India. This hands-on course trains you to install, maintain, and troubleshoot LED lighting and electrical circuits safely and professionally. Gain industry-recognized certification and start earning within weeks of completing the program.

This foundation-level course covers everything needed to become a skilled LED and electrical wiring technician in the Indian market. You will learn electrical safety principles, wiring installation techniques, LED lighting systems, circuit troubleshooting, and maintenance practices. The program combines classroom learning with practical hands-on training using real tools and materials used in Indian residential and commercial settings.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (Class 8th students can apply)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required — course designed for absolute beginners
  • Already working as electrician? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required — Aadhar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport
  • Open for all genders and categories — no discrimination
  • Physical fitness required — ability to climb ladders and work at heights
  • Color blindness test may be required for electrical work

Modules

Module 1: Electrical Safety & Basic Concepts

Week 1 | 15 Hours

  • Electricity basics: Voltage, Current, Resistance, and Power — understanding Ohm's Law and how electricity flows
  • AC vs DC current — differences and applications in Indian homes and industries
  • Electrical hazards and safety precautions — preventing shocks, burns, and fires in daily work
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) — correct use of gloves, goggles, helmets, and footwear
  • Indian electrical safety standards and IS codes — compliance with Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)
  • Fire safety and emergency procedures — handling electrical fires and first aid basics
  • Grounding and earthing systems — protecting equipment and people from electrical faults
  • Common electrical accidents in India — case studies and prevention methods

Module 2: Electrical Tools, Equipment & Materials

Week 1-2 | 20 Hours

  • Essential hand tools — screwdrivers, pliers, wire strippers, crimpers, and proper usage techniques
  • Measuring instruments — multimeters, voltage testers, continuity testers, and how to read them
  • Power tools — drill machines, angle grinders, and safety while using them
  • Wiring materials — copper wires, aluminum wires, cables, and Indian wire gauge standards
  • Conduits and accessories — PVC pipes, metal conduits, junction boxes, and fittings used in India
  • Switches, plugs, and sockets — types available in Indian market and their specifications
  • Circuit breakers and MCBs — understanding single-pole and three-pole breakers
  • Electrical panels and distribution boards — layout and component identification
  • LED bulbs and fixtures — different types available in Indian market (A19, GU10, T5, Batten lights)
  • Cables and connectors — selection based on voltage and current requirements
  • Testing equipment — insulation testers, earth testers, and clamp meters

Module 3: Wiring Installation & Circuit Design

Week 2-3 | 25 Hours

  • Wiring systems in India — radial circuits, ring circuits, and single-phase systems used in homes
  • Load calculation — determining wire size and circuit capacity for Indian residential buildings
  • Wire sizing and selection — choosing correct gauge based on current and distance
  • Installation techniques — concealed wiring, surface wiring, and conduit routing in walls
  • Junction boxes and connection methods — proper joining and insulation of wires
  • Switch and socket installation — height standards, placement, and wiring connections
  • Series and parallel circuits — understanding circuit configurations and applications
  • Three-phase wiring basics — used in shops, factories, and commercial buildings
  • Distribution board setup — MCB arrangement, earthing connection, and labeling
  • Cable tray and conduit installation — routing cables safely through buildings
  • Earthing electrode installation — copper rod placement and testing
  • Wiring for different appliances — fans, geysers, air conditioners, and pumps

Module 4: LED Lighting Technology & Installation

Week 4-5 | 25 Hours

  • LED technology fundamentals — how LEDs work compared to incandescent and CFL bulbs
  • LED specifications — lumens, color temperature, CRI, and wattage explained
  • Types of LED lights — bulbs, tubes, panels, spotlights, and decorative lights available in India
  • LED drivers and power supplies — selecting correct drivers for LED installations
  • Thermal management — heat dissipation in LED fixtures and importance of proper installation
  • Color temperature selection — warm white (2700K), cool white (4000K), and daylight (6500K) for different spaces
  • LED wiring and connections — series vs parallel arrangements and voltage drops
  • Dimming LED lights — compatible dimmers and circuit considerations
  • Smart LED systems — WiFi-enabled bulbs, IoT integration, and voice control basics
  • LED downlights and recessed lighting — installation in ceilings and walls
  • LED strips and linear lighting — flexible strips for decorative and functional use
  • Troubleshooting LED failures — flickering, dimming, and non-functioning lights

Module 5: Maintenance, Troubleshooting & Repair

Week 5-6 | 20 Hours

  • Regular maintenance schedule — daily, weekly, and monthly checks for electrical systems
  • Visual inspection methods — identifying damaged wires, loose connections, and corrosion
  • Testing and diagnosis — using multimeters to check voltage, continuity, and resistance
  • Circuit troubleshooting — locating open circuits, short circuits, and ground faults
  • LED light failures — identifying driver issues, LED chip failures, and connection problems
  • Switch and socket repairs — replacing damaged switches and fixing poor connections
  • Wire damage assessment — determining if wires can be repaired or need replacement
  • Overload and short circuit protection — understanding MCB tripping and resetting procedures
  • Earthing system testing — checking earth resistance and ensuring proper grounding
  • Common electrical problems in Indian homes — flickering lights, burning smells, and tripping breakers
  • Safety during repair work — isolating circuits and preventing electrical hazards
  • Documentation and record keeping — maintaining maintenance logs and reports

Module 6: Professional Practice & Indian Regulations

Week 7-8 | 15 Hours

  • Indian Electrical Code (IEC) standards — compliance requirements for all electrical installations
  • National Building Code (NBC) — safety norms for residential and commercial buildings
  • Electricity Act 2003 — regulations and responsibilities of electrical workers
  • State Electricity Board (SEB) guidelines — local regulations and approval processes
  • Installation certificates and documentation — maintaining records for warranty and compliance
  • Cost estimation — calculating material and labor costs for wiring and LED projects
  • Customer communication — explaining work, timelines, and safety to non-technical clients
  • Business setup for electricians — licensing, GST registration, and becoming self-employed
  • Quality standards and workmanship — delivering professional-grade installations
  • Electrical load management — helping customers optimize energy consumption
  • Career advancement — upskilling and specialization opportunities in electrical trades
  • Entrepreneurship opportunities — starting electrical contracting business in India

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice & Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on assessment of wiring installation and LED lighting setup

Practical Tasks:

  • Single-Phase Wiring Installation (20 Marks): Candidate must install a complete single-phase wiring circuit including switch, socket, and light fixture with proper earthing and safety compliance
  • LED Light Installation & Troubleshooting (15 Marks): Candidate must install LED lights (bulbs, tubes, or strips), test for proper functioning, and diagnose and fix a faulty LED circuit
  • Circuit Testing & Diagnostics (10 Marks): Candidate must use multimeter to test voltage, continuity, and resistance in given circuits, identify faults, and document findings
  • Safety & Maintenance Demonstration (5 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate proper use of PPE, safe work practices, and perform maintenance checks on electrical installations

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand electrical safety principles and follow Indian electrical codes and standards
  • Identify and safely use electrical tools, equipment, and materials common in India
  • Plan and execute electrical wiring installations for residential and commercial buildings
  • Install, test, and troubleshoot LED lighting systems and circuits
  • Diagnose electrical faults using testing equipment and perform repairs professionally
  • Maintain electrical systems and provide quality service to customers while following regulations
