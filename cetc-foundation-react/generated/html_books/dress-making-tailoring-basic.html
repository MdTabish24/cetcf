Dress Making & Tailoring (Basic)

📚 Sector: Fashion, Tailoring & Textiles 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks / 180 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the art of dress making and tailoring from scratch with this practical 12-week foundation course. Learn to measure, cut, sew, and finish garments professionally using traditional and modern techniques suited to the Indian market. Start your career as a skilled tailor or begin your own tailoring business.

This course covers all essential skills needed to become a professional dress maker and tailor. You will learn fabric selection, pattern making, cutting techniques, hand and machine sewing, fitting adjustments, and finishing methods. The course includes hands-on practice with real garments like salwar kameez, kurtis, shirts, and simple dresses, preparing you for immediate employment or self-employment in the tailoring industry.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior tailoring experience required
  • Already working as a tailor? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Applicable for all candidates irrespective of gender, caste, religion, or economic background
  • Physical fitness required for standing and detailed work for extended periods
  • Interest in fashion and garment construction preferred

Modules

Module 1: Introduction to Dress Making & Tailoring Industry

Week 1-2 | 30 Hours

  • Overview of Indian tailoring industry — history, market demand, and growth opportunities in metros, towns, and villages
  • Types of tailoring businesses — retail tailoring shops, boutiques, custom tailoring, ready-to-wear alterations, online tailoring services
  • Tailoring career pathways — employed tailor, self-employed tailor, boutique owner, online tailor, production supervisor
  • Safety and hygiene in tailoring workspace — proper ventilation, lighting, ergonomics, and health precautions
  • Professional ethics and customer communication — taking orders, managing expectations, delivery timelines, complaint handling
  • Introduction to tools and equipment — sewing machines, scissors, measuring tapes, pressing tools, and their care
  • Understanding garment construction basics — seams, hems, collars, cuffs, and sleeves in traditional Indian and Western wear
  • Fabric identification and quality assessment — cotton, silk, blended fabrics, synthetic materials common in India
  • Introduction to body measurements and sizing — standard Indian sizing charts for women, men, and children
  • Costing and pricing of tailoring services — material cost, labour cost, overhead, and profit margins in Indian market

Module 2: Body Measurements & Pattern Making Fundamentals

Week 3-4 | 30 Hours

  • Understanding body anatomy — key measurement points on human body for accurate fitting
  • Taking accurate measurements — chest, waist, hips, length, sleeve length, shoulder width for different garment types
  • Measurement tools and techniques — using measuring tapes, reading measurements correctly, common measurement mistakes
  • Creating measurement charts — recording measurements for individual customers, maintaining customer records
  • Introduction to pattern making — basic pattern grading and scaling for different sizes
  • Using commercial patterns — reading pattern instructions, identifying pattern pieces, understanding grain lines
  • Adapting patterns for Indian body types — adjustments for Indian women's curves, length variations, regional preferences
  • Marking and labeling patterns — identifying grain lines, fold lines, seam allowances, and notches
  • Pattern storage and organization — protecting patterns, cataloging for quick access, digital pattern management
  • Creating basic patterns for salwar, kameez, and simple kurtis — hand-drafted patterns for popular Indian garments
  • Understanding ease and fitting allowances — extra space for comfort in different garment types
  • Practice sessions — measuring 5 different people, creating measurement cards, drafting basic patterns

Module 3: Fabric Selection, Preparation & Cutting Techniques

Week 5-6 | 30 Hours

  • Fabric types and their properties — cotton, silk, polyester, blends, linen, and their suitability for different garments
  • Understanding fabric weight and drape — light, medium, heavy fabrics and their tailoring requirements
  • Fabric grain and nap — understanding warp and weft, identifying nap direction, grain-line importance
  • Checking fabric quality — inspecting for defects, color fastness, shrinkage potential, thread pull
  • Fabric shrinkage and pre-treatment — washing, steaming, and preshrinking before cutting
  • Laying out and cutting patterns — arranging patterns on fabric to minimize waste, understanding layout for nap fabrics
  • Cutting techniques and tools — using scissors, rotary cutters, cutting mats, maintaining blade sharpness
  • Marking fabric — using tailor's chalk, marking wheels, thread tracing for pattern details
  • Cutting accuracy and precision — straight lines, curves, corner precision, notch marking
  • Organizing cut pieces — labeling, storing pieces to prevent loss, protecting from dust and damage
  • Calculating fabric requirements — estimating yardage for different garments, accounting for pattern repeats and nap
  • Practice cutting — cutting pieces for salwar, kameez, kurtis, and simple shirts with accuracy

Module 4: Hand & Machine Sewing Techniques

Week 7-8 | 30 Hours

  • Threading and needle selection — choosing correct needle size, thread weight, and threading machines properly
  • Basic hand stitches — running stitch, backstitch, slip stitch, whip stitch, and their applications
  • Hand stitching for finishing — hemming, attaching buttons, blind stitching, ladder stitch for invisible seams
  • Introduction to sewing machines — understanding machine parts, tension adjustment, bobbin threading
  • Straight stitch machine sewing — maintaining straight lines, consistent seam allowance, speed control
  • Machine stitching practice — sewing straight seams, curved seams, corner turning, and seam accuracy
  • Seam finishing techniques — zigzag finish, pinking, binding, French seams for different fabric types
  • Topstitching and decorative stitching — using topstitch for strength and design, basic decorative effects
  • Buttonholes and button attachment — machine-made buttonholes, hand-finished buttonholes, secure button attachment
  • Understanding stitch length and tension — adjusting for different fabrics, troubleshooting common sewing problems
  • Chain piecing and batch sewing — efficient production techniques for multiple garments
  • Practical sewing projects — stitching sample seams, practicing buttonholes, sewing simple garment pieces

Module 5: Garment Construction & Assembly

Week 9-10 | 30 Hours

  • Understanding garment construction sequence — proper order of assembling garment parts for quality results
  • Constructing salwar — drafting, cutting, sewing side seams, inseams, attaching waistband, creating drawstring casing
  • Constructing kameez — assembling front and back pieces, attaching sleeves, neckline finishing, slit construction
  • Constructing kurtis — simple A-line and fitted styles, sleeve options, neckline treatments, button placement
  • Constructing basic shirts — collar attachment, button bands, cuff construction, side seams, hem finishing
  • Sleeve insertion techniques — setting sleeves for good fit, armhole depth, sleeve head height, cap ease
  • Neckline finishing — bias binding, facing, collar attachment, round neck, V-neck, and square neck finishes
  • Waistband construction — elastic waistbands, drawstring casings, faced waistbands, interfacing selection
  • Interfacing and stabilization — choosing correct interfacing weight, application methods, collar and cuff stiffening
  • Hem techniques — hand-stitched hems, machine hems, blind hems, rolled hems for different garment types
  • Pocket construction — patch pockets, set-in pockets, welt pockets, and their placement
  • Practice assembly — constructing complete salwar, kameez, kurtis, and simple shirts under guidance

Module 6: Fitting Adjustments, Finishing & Professional Skills

Week 11-12 | 30 Hours

  • Understanding fit and comfort — chest fit, waist fit, hip fit, length adjustments, and ease allowances
  • Common fitting problems and solutions — tight chest, loose waist, short length, uneven hems, sleeve issues
  • Taking fitting adjustments — marking adjustment lines, understanding grain shift, planning alterations
  • Making alterations — taking in and letting out seams, shortening and lengthening, repositioning seams
  • Pressing and finishing techniques — proper pressing tools, temperature settings for different fabrics, final pressing
  • Removing wrinkles and creases — steam pressing, dry pressing, protecting garment finish during pressing
  • Quality control checks — inspecting seams, checking thread colors, verifying measurements, final appearance review
  • Packaging and presentation — folding garments professionally, packaging for delivery, care instructions for customers
  • Documentation and record-keeping — maintaining customer records, garment specifications, delivery dates, payment records
  • Troubleshooting common defects — puckered seams, loose stitches, uneven hems, thread tension issues, and corrections
  • Building customer relationships — handling complaints, making adjustments without charge, building repeat business
  • Final capstone project — creating complete garment from measurement to delivery with quality finish

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice, Short Answer, and Descriptive) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Candidates must complete practical tailoring tasks demonstrating proficiency in measurements, cutting, sewing, and finishing

Practical Tasks:

  • Salwar-Kameez Set Construction (20 Marks): Candidate must take measurements, draft pattern, cut fabric, and complete construction of a full salwar-kameez set with proper finishing, seams, and hems
  • Kurti or Shirt Construction (15 Marks): Candidate must construct a complete kurti or shirt including collar attachment, sleeve insertion, button placement, and professional finishing
  • Fitting and Alteration Skills (10 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate ability to take accurate measurements, identify fitting issues, and make proper alterations to achieve good fit
  • Hand Stitching and Finishing (5 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate proficiency in hand stitches, buttonhole making, button attachment, and invisible hemming techniques

Learning Outcomes

  • Accurately take body measurements and create measurement charts for different garment types
  • Draft, grade, and adapt patterns for salwar, kameez, kurtis, and basic shirts suitable for Indian body types
  • Select appropriate fabrics based on garment type, season, and customer requirements
  • Cut fabric pieces accurately using proper layout and cutting techniques with minimal waste
  • Execute hand and machine stitching with precision, including seam finishing and decorative stitches
  • Assemble complete garments following proper construction sequence and quality standards
  • Identify and correct fitting problems through proper alteration techniques
  • Apply professional finishing touches including pressing, hemming, and quality control
  • Maintain customer records, manage tailoring orders, and provide excellent customer service
  • Start and manage a small tailoring business with proper costing, pricing, and record-keeping
