Cake Decoration & Pastry Art

📚 Sector: Food, Catering & Hospitality 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the art of cake decoration and pastry making through hands-on training designed for the Indian food industry. This 2-month foundation course equips you with professional skills in fondant work, piping techniques, pastry preparation, and creative cake design to launch your career in bakeries, hotels, and catering businesses across India.

This comprehensive program covers essential cake decoration and pastry art techniques including basic cake baking, frosting application, piping designs, fondant sculpting, pastry dough preparation, and creative plating. You'll learn industry-standard practices used in Indian bakeries, wedding catering, and hospitality establishments, with focus on cost-effective methods and popular Indian flavor preferences.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior professional experience required
  • Already working as a baker or cake decorator? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam to get certified faster
  • Valid government-issued ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Voter ID, or Driving License)
  • Medical fitness certificate (optional, recommended for food handling roles)
  • Open to all Indian citizens and foreign nationals with valid work permits
  • No age upper limit — career changers and retirees welcome

Modules

Module 1: Fundamentals of Cake Making & Baking Science

Week 1-2 | 20 Hours

  • Introduction to cake decoration industry in India — career opportunities, job market trends, and industry standards
  • Basic cake types and classifications — sponge cakes, butter cakes, chiffon cakes, eggless variants used in Indian bakeries
  • Essential ingredients and their functions — flour types, leavening agents, eggs, dairy products, and Indian substitutes
  • Baking chemistry and temperature control — oven management, altitude adjustments for different Indian regions, baking time calculations
  • Common baking faults and troubleshooting — sunken centers, dry cakes, uneven rising, and prevention methods
  • Cake cooling, leveling, and storage techniques — proper cooling racks, cutting straight layers, moisture retention methods
  • Safety and hygiene in bakery operations — food safety certification requirements, FSSAI guidelines, personal hygiene standards
  • Equipment familiarization — mixers, ovens, cooling racks, measuring tools, and workspace organization
  • Costing and portion control — calculating recipe costs, pricing strategies for Indian market, waste management

Module 2: Frosting, Icing & Crumb Coating Mastery

Week 2-3 | 18 Hours

  • Buttercream frosting variations — American, Swiss, Italian, and Indian desi ghee-based versions
  • Cream cheese frosting and whipped cream preparations — stability techniques, flavor infusions with cardamom, rose, mango
  • Ganache making and tempering — dark, milk, and white chocolate ganache, achieving glossy finishes
  • Fondant icing basics — making from scratch, using commercial fondant, coloring techniques with food-safe dyes
  • Crumb coating methodology — first layer application, drying time, achieving smooth surfaces for final coating
  • Frosting consistency and temperature management — working with Indian kitchen temperatures, preventing melting during application
  • Color mixing and blending — primary colors, pastels, metallics, achieving popular Indian festival colors
  • Flavored frostings — vanilla, chocolate, coffee, eggless options, incorporating Indian flavors like pistachio and saffron
  • Frosting storage and shelf life — refrigeration techniques, room temperature working times, batch preparation for events
  • Troubleshooting frosting issues — grainy buttercream, broken ganache, separation problems, and fixes

Module 3: Piping Techniques & Decorative Design

Week 3-5 | 25 Hours

  • Piping bag selection and filling — cloth vs disposable bags, filling techniques, avoiding air bubbles
  • Piping tip identification and usage — round tips, star tips, leaf tips, petal tips, basket-weave tips, and specialty tips
  • Basic piping strokes — dots, lines, shells, scrolls, and continuous patterns used in professional cake design
  • Flower piping techniques — roses, tulips, daisies, marigolds, and Indian flower designs popular in wedding cakes
  • Leaf and vine piping — realistic green work, fern designs, ivy patterns for elegant cake presentations
  • Writing and lettering on cakes — script writing, block letters, number piping, achieving consistent sizing
  • Border designs and finishes — simple borders, rope borders, beaded borders, and decorative edge work
  • Combination designs — integrating multiple piping techniques into cohesive cake designs
  • Practice exercises on cardboard and dummy cakes — repetition methods, speed building, consistency development
  • Design planning and sketching — drawing cake designs, calculating spacing, adapting designs for different cake sizes
  • Piping under pressure situations — wedding orders, event deadlines, maintaining quality under time constraints

Module 4: Fondant Work & Sculptural Cake Art

Week 5-6 | 18 Hours

  • Fondant preparation and conditioning — kneading techniques, temperature management, achieving smooth workable consistency
  • Fondant rolling and draping — using rolling pins, achieving even thickness, covering cakes smoothly without air bubbles
  • Fondant coloring and marbling — incorporating colors evenly, creating marble effects, ombré techniques
  • Fondant cutting and shaping — using cookie cutters, creating geometric shapes, precision cutting tools and techniques
  • Hand-molded fondant decorations — flowers, fruits, animals, figurines, and Indian cultural symbols
  • Fondant texture creation — embossing tools, lace patterns, imprinting designs, creating realistic surfaces
  • 3D fondant sculptures — tiered designs, structural support systems, weight distribution, dowel placement
  • Cake board preparation and assembly — covering boards, securing cakes, leveling and alignment techniques
  • Fondant troubleshooting — cracking, stickiness, color bleeding, and environmental humidity management
  • Fondant storage and preservation — proper wrapping, shelf life, preventing hardening, rehydration methods

Module 5: Pastry Doughs & Fillings for Cakes

Week 6-7 | 19 Hours

  • Shortcrust pastry preparation — mixing methods, gluten development control, achieving crumbly texture for tarts
  • Puff pastry making — lamination techniques, fold counts, resting periods, achieving crispy layers
  • Choux pastry and eclairs — piping consistency, baking techniques, hollow center achievement, filling methods
  • Sweet tart dough variations — enriched doughs, blind baking, preventing soggy bottoms, crimping edges
  • Cream fillings — pastry cream (crème pâtissière), diplomat cream, mousseline, and Indian condensed milk versions
  • Fruit and jam fillings — fresh fruit preparations, fruit curd making, jam cooking, preventing leakage in cakes
  • Chocolate fillings — chocolate mousse, chocolate ganache, chocolate truffle fillings, tempering chocolate
  • Nut-based fillings — almond paste, marzipan, pistachio fillings, cashew cream, and Indian dry fruit preparations
  • Custard and milk-based fillings — traditional custard, rabri-inspired fillings, khoya incorporation, flavor infusions
  • Filling storage and handling — shelf life management, preventing contamination, batch preparation for events
  • Balancing flavors and textures — combining complementary fillings, preventing flavor clashes, achieving moisture balance

Module 6: Professional Cake Design, Themes & Event Execution

Week 7-8 | 20 Hours

  • Wedding cake design principles — tiered structures, color coordination, theme development, Indian wedding aesthetics
  • Birthday cake designs for different age groups — children's themes, character cakes, milestone celebrations, trending designs
  • Festival and occasion cakes — Diwali designs, Holi colors, Christmas cakes, New Year themes, regional celebration cakes
  • Corporate and event cakes — logo incorporation, professional designs, bulk order management, consistency across batches
  • Custom design consultation — client communication, sketch development, managing expectations, pricing custom work
  • Theme-based decoration — floral themes, geometric patterns, vintage designs, modern minimalist styles, Indian traditional patterns
  • Cake assembly and stacking — dowel placement, structure stability, transporting tiered cakes, on-site assembly techniques
  • Scaling recipes for different cake sizes — mathematical calculations, ingredient adjustments, baking time modifications
  • Quality control and presentation — final inspection checklists, photography-ready presentation, delivery standards
  • Time management for events — production scheduling, batch baking, decoration timelines, delivery logistics in Indian context
  • Customer service and follow-up — handling complaints, managing revisions, building repeat business, online marketing basics
  • Seasonal specialties and trending designs — Instagram-worthy cakes, viral designs, regional preferences, sustainable practices

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice, Short Answer, Descriptive) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on cake decoration and pastry preparation demonstration under supervised conditions

Practical Tasks:

  • Basic Cake Baking & Frosting (15 Marks): Candidate must bake a sponge cake from scratch, level it properly, apply crumb coat and final frosting coating smoothly on a 6-inch round cake
  • Piped Decoration Design (15 Marks): Candidate must create a cake design using at least 5 different piping techniques including flowers, borders, and writing with consistent quality and proper spacing
  • Fondant Work & 3D Decoration (10 Marks): Candidate must cover a cake with fondant smoothly, create at least 3 hand-molded fondant decorations (flowers/figurines), and demonstrate proper color blending
  • Pastry Preparation & Filling (10 Marks): Candidate must prepare one pastry dough from scratch (shortcrust or choux), bake it properly, and prepare one cream filling with correct consistency and flavor

Learning Outcomes

  • Prepare various cake types with proper baking techniques and consistent quality following food safety standards
  • Create professional-quality frostings and icings with different flavors and textures suitable for Indian palate preferences
  • Execute advanced piping techniques to create decorative designs including flowers, borders, and lettering on cakes
  • Work with fondant to create smooth cake coverings and hand-molded 3D decorative elements for themed cakes
  • Prepare pastry doughs and complementary fillings that enhance cake flavors and maintain structural integrity
  • Design and execute complete cakes for various occasions including weddings, birthdays, and festivals while managing time and client expectations effectively
