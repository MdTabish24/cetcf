Garment Quality Inspector

📚 Sector: Fashion, Tailoring & Textiles 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the art of garment quality inspection and become a critical quality control professional in India's thriving textile and fashion industry. This 8-week foundation course trains you to identify defects, ensure international standards compliance, and maintain product excellence across all garment manufacturing processes.

This comprehensive program covers fabric inspection, stitching quality assessment, finishing standards, defect identification, measurement accuracy, and quality documentation. You'll learn industry-standard inspection techniques used in factories across Tiruppur, Bangalore, Delhi, and Mumbai, with hands-on practice on real garment samples and inspection tools.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or 16 years as per local regulations)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required — foundation course for beginners
  • Already working in garment industry? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Applicable for all candidates — no gender, caste, or community restrictions
  • Physical fitness required — able to stand for inspection work and handle garments

Modules

Module 1: Fundamentals of Garment Quality & Industry Standards

Week 1-2 | 15 Hours

  • Introduction to Indian textile and garment industry — Overview of major garment manufacturing hubs (Tiruppur, Bangalore, Surat, Delhi NCR), export markets, and industry growth
  • Quality management concepts — Definition of quality, importance in fashion industry, cost of poor quality, and customer satisfaction metrics
  • International quality standards — ISO 9001, ISO 13485, GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard), and Indian Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) requirements
  • Role and responsibilities of quality inspector — Job scope, reporting structure, ethical standards, and workplace conduct in garment factories
  • Quality control vs Quality assurance — Difference between QC and QA, preventive vs corrective actions, and their roles in production
  • Garment manufacturing process overview — Raw material sourcing, cutting, sewing, pressing, packing, and where quality checks occur
  • Safety and hygiene standards in inspection — Personal protective equipment (PPE), workplace safety, and hygiene protocols in inspection departments
  • Introduction to inspection tools and equipment — Measuring tapes, weighing scales, magnifying glasses, color matching lights, and thread counters

Module 2: Fabric Inspection & Raw Material Quality

Week 2-3 | 18 Hours

  • Fabric structure and terminology — Warp, weft, yarn count, thread density, GSM (grams per square meter), and fabric weight measurement
  • Types of fabrics used in garments — Cotton, polyester, blends, knit fabrics, woven fabrics, and their quality characteristics
  • Fabric inspection methods — Four-point system, ten-point system, visual inspection under standard lighting, and defect marking procedures
  • Common fabric defects — Holes, tears, stains, color variations, slubs, neps, pilling, and how to identify and classify them
  • Fabric weight and density testing — GSM calculation, thread count measurement, and shrinkage testing procedures
  • Color fastness and testing — Color bleeding, color matching standards, batch consistency, and visual assessment techniques
  • Fiber content verification — Simple tests to identify fiber types, label accuracy, and documentation of raw material specifications
  • Receiving inspection procedures — Checking fabric rolls, documenting batch numbers, lot numbers, and quality certificates from suppliers
  • Fabric defect documentation — Creating inspection reports, defect mapping, and communication with production team about fabric issues

Module 3: Garment Cutting & Sewing Quality Assessment

Week 3-4 | 17 Hours

  • Pattern grading and marker making — Understanding size grading, marker efficiency, and how cutting errors begin at this stage
  • Cutting quality standards — Accurate cutting, straight edges, notch accuracy, and preventing shrinkage-related cutting issues
  • Stitching quality parameters — Stitch length (SPI - stitches per inch), stitch tension, seam strength, and consistency across garments
  • Common sewing defects — Skipped stitches, broken threads, puckering, uneven seams, loose stitches, and twisted seams
  • Seam types and their inspection — Flat seams, French seams, overlocked seams, topstitching, and quality standards for each type
  • Thread quality assessment — Thread color matching, thread strength, lint-free requirements, and thread tension settings
  • Buttonhole and button attachment quality — Correct spacing, reinforcement, button strength, and functional testing
  • Zipper and closure inspection — Slider movement, tooth alignment, puller functionality, and durability testing
  • Collar and cuff quality — Shape consistency, interfacing integrity, crispness, and attachment strength
  • Pocket quality assessment — Straight lines, secure attachment, reinforced corners, and functional testing
  • Seam strength testing — Understanding break strength requirements, tear testing methods, and documentation of results

Module 4: Pressing, Finishing & Final Inspection

Week 4-5 | 16 Hours

  • Pressing standards and techniques — Temperature settings for different fabrics, steam pressing, flat pressing, and crease permanence
  • Pressing defects — Shine marks, water spots, uneven pressing, and heat damage identification
  • Shrinkage and dimensional stability — Post-wash shrinkage standards, relaxation shrinkage, and measurement procedures
  • Garment finishing operations — Hemming quality, label attachment, care label placement, and final touches
  • Hem quality inspection — Stitch consistency, hem width accuracy, invisible hemming, and durability testing
  • Label and tag inspection — Label positioning, attachment strength, readability, and compliance with regulations
  • Care instructions and compliance — Correct labeling, language requirements, washing symbols, and legal compliance in India
  • Final visual inspection checklist — Systematic inspection from collar to hem, checking all seams, closures, and overall appearance
  • Garment measurement verification — Using size standards, measuring key points (chest, length, sleeve), and tolerance acceptance
  • Defect classification — Critical defects (non-sellable), major defects (acceptable with discount), and minor defects (cosmetic issues)
  • Packing quality — Proper folding, wrinkle prevention, packaging integrity, and labeling accuracy

Module 5: Measurement, Grading & Size Compliance

Week 5-6 | 18 Hours

  • Standard measurement points — Understanding where to measure (shoulder, chest, waist, hip, length, sleeve length) for different garment types
  • Measurement tools and accuracy — Using measuring tapes correctly, digital scales, measuring rulers, and maintaining tool calibration
  • Size charts and grading systems — Indian size standards (XS, S, M, L, XL), international sizing (US, EU, UK), and grade rules
  • Tolerance standards — Acceptable variation ranges for each measurement, understanding ±0.5 inch tolerance, and grade-to-grade consistency
  • Measurement documentation — Recording measurements accurately, creating size reports, and maintaining quality records
  • Fit assessment — Visual fit evaluation, comfort assessment, and functional movement testing
  • Size-wise defect tracking — Identifying if certain sizes have recurring issues, trend analysis, and root cause investigation
  • Shrinkage and relaxation measurement — Pre-wash and post-wash measurements, shrinkage percentage calculation, and standards compliance
  • Garment type-specific measurements — T-shirts, shirts, trousers, sarees, dresses, and how measurement points vary
  • Size consistency across batches — Ensuring size consistency in bulk production, batch-to-batch variation monitoring, and corrective actions
  • Digital measurement systems — Introduction to 3D body scanning, automated measurement systems, and future technology in garment inspection

Module 6: Quality Documentation, Reporting & Professional Development

Week 6-8 | 36 Hours

  • Quality inspection reports — Creating detailed inspection reports, defect documentation, and clear communication with production teams
  • Defect tracking and trend analysis — Recording defect patterns, identifying recurring issues, statistical process control (SPC), and improvement recommendations
  • AQL (Acceptable Quality Level) standards — Understanding AQL 1.5, AQL 2.5, sampling plans, and inspection lot sizes used in Indian factories
  • Sampling procedures — Random sampling, stratified sampling, lot-based sampling, and how to select garments for inspection
  • Statistical quality control basics — Control charts, process capability, Cp and Cpk values, and data interpretation for non-technical professionals
  • Communication with stakeholders — Reporting to supervisors, discussing issues with production teams, vendor communication, and client feedback
  • Corrective and preventive actions (CAPA) — Identifying root causes, implementing solutions, tracking effectiveness, and documentation
  • Quality cost analysis — Understanding cost of poor quality, cost of prevention, and presenting business case for quality improvements
  • Compliance and regulatory requirements — Indian textile regulations, export compliance (CE marking, safety standards), and documentation requirements
  • Digital quality management systems — Introduction to QMS software, inspection apps, data recording, and report generation
  • Continuous learning and skill development — Industry certifications (Six Sigma, ISO auditor training), professional development, and career growth
  • Workplace ethics and professional conduct — Maintaining integrity, confidentiality, conflict resolution, and professional relationships in quality department

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice & Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Live garment inspection and quality assessment tasks performed under examination conditions

Practical Tasks:

  • Fabric Inspection Task (15 Marks): Candidate must inspect a fabric sample (3 meters) using four-point or ten-point system, identify and mark all defects, calculate GSM, assess color fastness, and submit inspection report with defect mapping
  • Garment Quality Assessment (20 Marks): Candidate must inspect a complete garment (shirt/trouser/dress), check all stitching quality, seams, closures, hems, labels, and identify all defects (critical, major, minor). Submit detailed inspection checklist and defect report
  • Measurement & Sizing Verification (15 Marks): Candidate must measure a garment at 8+ key points using measuring tape, verify measurements against provided size chart, calculate tolerance variations, and confirm size accuracy. Submit measurement sheet with calculations

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and classify fabric defects using industry-standard inspection methods (four-point and ten-point systems)
  • Assess garment stitching quality, seam strength, and sewing defects with accuracy and consistency
  • Perform accurate garment measurements, verify size compliance, and document measurement variations
  • Apply international quality standards (ISO, GOTS, BIS) and AQL sampling procedures in inspection work
  • Create detailed quality inspection reports, maintain defect documentation, and communicate findings to production teams
  • Implement corrective and preventive actions (CAPA) to improve garment quality and reduce defect rates
