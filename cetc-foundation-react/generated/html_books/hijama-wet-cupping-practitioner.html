Hijama / Wet Cupping Practitioner

📚 Sector: Wellness & Alternative Therapy 🎓 Level: Intermediate ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks / 180 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the ancient Islamic healing art of Hijama (Wet Cupping) with modern anatomical knowledge and safety protocols. This intermediate course trains you to become a certified Hijama practitioner, combining traditional wellness techniques with professional healthcare standards applicable across India's growing alternative therapy market.

This 12-week comprehensive program covers the complete science and practice of Hijama therapy, including human anatomy, disease conditions, cupping techniques, sterilization protocols, client consultation, and business management. You'll gain hands-on training in performing safe and effective wet cupping treatments while understanding the physiological benefits, contraindications, and ethical practices required in the Indian wellness industry.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 18 years (preferably 21 years for better learning retention)
  • Minimum education: Class 10th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior medical qualification required
  • Good physical health and stamina required for standing and manual work
  • Valid ID proof required (Aadhar, PAN, or Passport)
  • Medical fitness certificate recommended before enrollment
  • Already working in wellness field? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Applicable to Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals
  • No criminal record required for practice registration

Modules

Module 1: Fundamentals of Hijama & Historical Context

Week 1-2 | 30 Hours

  • Introduction to Hijama therapy — definition, origin, and Islamic medical principles
  • Historical evolution of cupping in Unani, Ayurvedic, and modern wellness practices
  • Types of cupping methods — dry, wet, fire, and modern silicone cupping explained
  • Difference between Hijama (wet cupping) and other cupping techniques
  • Scientific basis of Hijama — how it works on body systems
  • Regulatory framework for alternative therapy practitioners in India
  • Professional ethics and code of conduct for Hijama practitioners
  • Introduction to client consultation and consent forms
  • Safety standards and hygiene protocols overview
  • Career opportunities in India's wellness industry

Module 2: Human Anatomy & Physiology for Hijama Practice

Week 3-4 | 30 Hours

  • Skeletal system — bones, joints, and anatomical landmarks relevant to cupping points
  • Muscular system — major muscle groups and their functions in pain and tension
  • Circulatory system — blood flow, lymphatic system, and how cupping affects circulation
  • Nervous system — reflex zones, meridians, and nerve pathways
  • Skin anatomy — layers of skin and healing response to cupping
  • Respiratory system — lungs, bronchi, and cupping benefits for respiratory conditions
  • Digestive system — organs and how cupping supports digestive health
  • Immune system — lymph nodes, spleen, and immune response to therapy
  • Endocrine system — hormones and cupping effects on glandular function
  • Common anatomical variations in Indian populations
  • Age-related anatomical changes and modifications needed for elderly clients
  • Gender-specific anatomical considerations in practice

Module 3: Hijama Points, Meridians & Treatment Protocols

Week 5-6 | 30 Hours

  • Traditional Hijama points according to Sunnah and Islamic teachings
  • Major cupping points — location, anatomy, and therapeutic applications
  • Meridian system — understanding energy pathways in Eastern medicine
  • Cupping point mapping for back, shoulders, neck, and head regions
  • Cupping points for lower body — legs, knees, and feet
  • Cupping points for internal organs — stomach, liver, kidney, spleen
  • Seasonal cupping protocols — spring, summer, autumn, winter adjustments
  • Gender-specific cupping point recommendations
  • Age-appropriate cupping protocols for children, adults, and elderly
  • Customizing treatment plans based on individual health conditions
  • Point selection for specific diseases — migraine, asthma, arthritis, hypertension
  • Combination protocols — using multiple points for comprehensive treatment

Module 4: Hijama Techniques, Equipment & Sterilization

Week 7-8 | 30 Hours

  • Equipment required for Hijama practice — cups, lancets, tools, and accessories
  • Types of cups — glass, bamboo, plastic, and silicone cups explained
  • Cupping methods — fire cupping, hand-pump cupping, and electric cupping
  • Wet cupping technique — step-by-step procedure and best practices
  • Scarification methods — safe and effective bleeding techniques
  • Creating vacuum — manual and mechanical methods
  • Timing and duration — how long to keep cups on skin
  • Intensity levels — light, medium, and strong cupping for different conditions
  • Sterilization protocols — autoclave, disinfection, and infection control
  • Single-use vs reusable equipment — advantages and disadvantages
  • Proper disposal of medical waste and bio-hazardous materials
  • Maintaining hygiene in treatment room and equipment storage

Module 5: Health Conditions, Contraindications & Client Assessment

Week 9-10 | 30 Hours

  • Musculoskeletal conditions — arthritis, back pain, neck pain, sports injuries
  • Respiratory conditions — asthma, bronchitis, cough, and sinusitis management
  • Neurological conditions — migraine, headache, sciatica, and nerve pain
  • Digestive disorders — constipation, acidity, IBS, and liver problems
  • Circulatory issues — high blood pressure, poor circulation, and varicose veins
  • Skin conditions — eczema, psoriasis, acne, and dermatitis
  • Immune system support — general wellness, fatigue, and immunity boosting
  • Absolute contraindications — conditions where Hijama is not safe
  • Relative contraindications — conditions requiring modified approach
  • Medications that affect cupping — blood thinners, steroids, and antibiotics
  • Pre-treatment assessment — health history, physical examination, and vital signs
  • Informed consent — explaining procedure, benefits, risks, and aftercare to clients

Module 6: Professional Practice, Business Management & Client Care

Week 11-12 | 30 Hours

  • Setting up a Hijama clinic — location selection, space requirements, and setup costs
  • Licensing and registration — government approvals needed in Indian states
  • Insurance and liability coverage — protecting your practice legally
  • Client consultation skills — effective communication and needs assessment
  • Creating treatment plans — personalized protocols and expected outcomes
  • Documentation and record-keeping — maintaining client health records
  • Pricing strategies — competitive rates across Indian metros and tier-2 cities
  • Marketing your Hijama practice — social media, word-of-mouth, and local promotion
  • Building client relationships — retention, follow-ups, and testimonials
  • Aftercare instructions — post-treatment care and lifestyle modifications
  • Managing adverse reactions — handling bruising, discomfort, and rare complications
  • Continuous learning — staying updated with latest research and techniques

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice and Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Practical demonstration of Hijama techniques, client assessment, and treatment execution

Practical Tasks:

  • Client Assessment & Consultation (15 Marks): Candidate must conduct a complete client health assessment, take detailed history, identify contraindications, and create a customized treatment plan with proper documentation
  • Hijama Technique Execution (20 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate proper cupping point selection, equipment preparation, sterilization, cup application, vacuum creation, and safe removal on a training model or volunteer
  • Wet Cupping Procedure (15 Marks): Candidate must perform complete wet cupping procedure including skin preparation, scarification, bleeding management, cup application, and proper aftercare instructions

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the scientific principles, history, and cultural significance of Hijama therapy in Islamic and Indian wellness traditions
  • Demonstrate comprehensive knowledge of human anatomy, physiology, and body systems relevant to cupping practice
  • Identify correct Hijama points, meridians, and customize treatment protocols for various health conditions
  • Perform safe and effective wet cupping techniques with proper sterilization and infection control protocols
  • Assess clients thoroughly, identify contraindications, and manage adverse reactions professionally
  • Establish and manage a successful Hijama practice with proper documentation, ethical standards, and business acumen
