Four Wheeler (Car) Service Technician

📚 Sector: Automobile & Motor Trades 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks / 180 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the essential skills of car servicing and maintenance with this industry-aligned Foundation course. Learn to diagnose common car problems, perform routine maintenance, and deliver quality service that keeps vehicles running smoothly. Perfect for aspiring technicians looking to start a rewarding career in India's booming automotive sector.

This 12-week intensive program trains you in all aspects of four-wheeler (car) servicing, from engine maintenance and oil changes to brake systems and electrical troubleshooting. You'll gain hands-on experience with real vehicles, learn to use diagnostic tools, and understand customer service principles. By completion, you'll be job-ready for service stations, dealerships, and independent garages across India.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (with parental consent for 14-17 years)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required — open to all interested candidates
  • Already working as mechanic? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid photo ID proof required (Aadhar, PAN, Driving License, Passport)
  • Medical fitness certificate recommended for workshop safety
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals

Modules

Module 1: Automobile Industry Fundamentals & Safety Practices

Week 1-2 | 30 Hours

  • Indian automotive industry overview — Market size, major manufacturers, employment opportunities in India
  • Career paths in car servicing — Dealership technician, independent garage work, mobile service units
  • Workshop safety standards — PPE requirements, fire safety, electrical hazards in service bays

Module 2: Engine Maintenance & Lubrication Systems

Week 3-4 | 30 Hours

  • Oil and lubrication fundamentals — Viscosity grades, synthetic vs mineral oil, Indian oil brands

  • Oil filter selection and installation — Understanding filter specifications, common Indian car models
  • Engine coolant systems — Antifreeze types, coolant flush procedures, water pump basics
  • Air filter maintenance — Engine air filters, cabin air filters, replacement intervals
  • Spark plugs and ignition system — Types, gap settings, replacement for petrol engines
  • Diesel engine specifics — Fuel filters, glow plugs, diesel injection basics
  • Engine oil leaks — Identifying sources, gasket replacement, sealant application
  • Preventive maintenance schedules — Following manufacturer guidelines, service intervals in Indian conditions
  • Engine performance checks — Compression testing basics, visual inspection for leaks
  • Documentation of oil change records — Service cards, oil used, next service date

Module 3: Brake Systems & Suspension Service

Week 5-6 | 30 Hours

  • Brake system overview — Hydraulic principles, master cylinder, brake lines, safety importance
  • Brake pad inspection and replacement — Disc brakes, drum brakes, wear indicators
  • Brake fluid management — Flushing old fluid, bleeding air from lines, DOT standards
  • Brake rotor servicing — Checking runout, resurfacing, replacement procedures
  • Brake shoe adjustment — Drum brake maintenance, self-adjusting mechanisms
  • ABS system basics — Anti-lock brake function, warning light diagnosis, sensor cleaning
  • Suspension components overview — Springs, shock absorbers, struts, bushings
  • Shock absorber and strut replacement — Removal, installation, alignment considerations
  • Suspension bushings and bearings — Wear signs, replacement procedures, noise diagnosis
  • Wheel alignment basics — Toe-in, camber, caster concepts, alignment importance
  • Steering system service — Power steering fluid, pump function, tie rod inspection
  • Brake and suspension safety checks — Testing procedures, customer communication on safety issues

Module 4: Electrical Systems & Battery Management

Week 7-8 | 30 Hours

  • Car electrical system overview — 12V DC systems, battery, alternator, starter motor
  • Battery types and specifications — Lead-acid, AGM, capacity ratings, terminal types
  • Battery testing and maintenance — Specific gravity, voltage testing, cleaning corroded terminals
  • Battery replacement procedures — Safe removal, installation, terminal connections, polarity importance
  • Alternator function and testing — Charging system basics, voltage regulation, output testing
  • Starter motor operation — Solenoid function, starter removal and inspection
  • Wiring and electrical connections — Understanding wiring diagrams, color codes, terminal types
  • Lighting systems service — Headlights, tail lights, indicator bulbs, LED vs halogen
  • Fuse and relay systems — Identifying fuses, relay function, common electrical faults
  • Windshield wiper and washer systems — Motor replacement, blade maintenance, fluid refilling
  • Dashboard warning lights — Meaning of common lights, diagnostic approaches
  • Basic electrical troubleshooting — Multimeter use, continuity testing, short circuit identification

Module 5: Transmission, Cooling & Fuel Systems

Week 9-10 | 30 Hours

  • Transmission types — Manual vs automatic, CVT basics, common Indian car transmissions
  • Manual transmission fluid service — Checking levels, changing gear oil, drain plugs
  • Automatic transmission basics — Torque converter, fluid circulation, service intervals
  • Transmission filter and fluid replacement — Proper procedures, fluid specifications
  • Cooling system components — Radiator, thermostat, water pump, hoses
  • Radiator cleaning and maintenance — External cleaning, internal flushing, leak detection
  • Thermostat function and replacement — Temperature regulation, replacement procedures
  • Coolant selection for Indian climate — Summer cooling, winter protection, mixing guidelines
  • Fuel tank and fuel pump basics — Tank inspection, pump removal, fuel filter replacement
  • Fuel injector cleaning — Carbon buildup, cleaning procedures, fuel system additives
  • Fuel pressure testing — Using fuel pressure gauge, diagnostic procedures
  • Emission control systems — Catalytic converter basics, particulate filter awareness, emission standards in India

Module 6: Diagnostic Tools, Troubleshooting & Professional Service Skills

Week 11-12 | 30 Hours

  • Diagnostic scanner basics — OBD-II scanners, reading fault codes, common error codes
  • Using multimeters and testing equipment — Voltage measurement, resistance testing, continuity checks
  • Pressure gauges and leak detection tools — Fuel pressure gauge, cooling system pressure tester
  • Vehicle lift safety — Proper jacking points, safety stands, load distribution
  • Tire maintenance and replacement — Rotation, balancing, pressure checks, tread depth measurement
  • General troubleshooting methodology — Systematic approach, customer complaint analysis, logical diagnosis
  • Common car problems and solutions — Starting issues, overheating, brake noise, electrical faults
  • Service quality standards — Cleanliness, attention to detail, completeness of service
  • Customer communication skills — Explaining problems simply, managing expectations, follow-up service
  • Service documentation and billing — Accurate job cards, parts tracking, invoice generation
  • Warranty and after-sales service — Warranty period explanation, customer care follow-up
  • Professional development and skill upgrade — Continuous learning, manufacturer training programs, specialization opportunities

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice & Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on assessment of car servicing tasks performed on actual vehicles under supervision

Practical Tasks:

  • Oil Change and Filter Replacement (10 Marks): Candidate must safely drain engine oil, replace oil filter, refill with correct oil grade, and check levels. Assessment includes safety practices, proper waste disposal, and documentation.
  • Brake Pad Inspection and Replacement (10 Marks): Candidate must inspect brake pads for wear, measure thickness, replace worn pads, and test brake function. Must demonstrate knowledge of disc brake systems and safety procedures.
  • Battery Testing and Terminal Cleaning (10 Marks): Candidate must test battery voltage, clean corroded terminals safely, check alternator output, and document battery condition. Must follow electrical safety protocols.
  • Diagnostic Scanning and Fault Code Reading (10 Marks): Candidate must connect OBD-II scanner to vehicle, read fault codes, interpret error messages, and suggest appropriate remedies. Must demonstrate proper scanner operation.
  • Service Documentation and Customer Communication (10 Marks): Candidate must complete accurate job cards, list all work performed, calculate labor and parts costs, and demonstrate professional communication with customer about service performed.

Learning Outcomes

  • Perform routine car maintenance tasks including oil changes, filter replacements, and fluid top-ups safely and efficiently
  • Diagnose common car problems using diagnostic tools and systematic troubleshooting methods
  • Service brake systems, suspension components, and steering systems according to manufacturer specifications
  • Maintain electrical systems including battery, alternator, and lighting with proper safety precautions
  • Complete service documentation accurately and communicate professionally with customers about vehicle maintenance
  • Apply workplace safety standards and environmental regulations in automotive service operations
