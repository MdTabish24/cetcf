Carpenter & Wood Work Technician

📚 Sector: Construction & Building Trades 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks / 180 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the essential skills of carpentry and wood work to build a rewarding career in India's booming construction sector. This foundation-level course teaches you how to work with wood, use professional tools, and create quality furniture and structures that meet industry standards. Perfect for beginners who want to become skilled carpenters or wood work technicians.

This 12-week course covers everything a beginner carpenter needs to know—from understanding different types of wood and tools, to measuring and cutting accurately, joining wood pieces, finishing surfaces, and creating furniture designs. You'll learn both traditional Indian carpentry methods and modern techniques used in commercial construction. Hands-on practice with real tools and materials ensures you're job-ready from day one.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (with parental consent if below 18)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass
  • No prior carpentry experience required
  • Already working as carpenter? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam for faster certification
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Applicable for all candidates including persons with disabilities (with reasonable accommodations provided)
  • No gender restrictions — women encouraged to apply

Modules

Module 1: Introduction to Carpentry & Wood Work Industry

Week 1-2 | 30 Hours

  • Overview of carpentry profession in India — career scope, types of carpentry work, employment opportunities in residential and commercial construction
  • History and evolution of Indian carpentry — traditional techniques, regional styles, modern adaptations in contemporary construction
  • Carpentry sector in India — growth statistics, major construction projects, demand for skilled carpenters in metros and tier-2 cities
  • Role and responsibilities of a carpenter — job duties, work environment, safety culture, importance of quality and precision
  • Types of carpentry specializations — residential carpentry, commercial carpentry, furniture making, heritage restoration, modular furniture design
  • Wood work technician vs. general carpenter — skill differences, career progression, salary expectations, specialization opportunities
  • Safety culture in carpentry — workplace hazards, accident prevention, first aid basics, importance of personal protective equipment (PPE)
  • Professional ethics and standards — quality standards (IS codes), customer service, time management, teamwork on construction sites

Module 2: Wood Types, Properties & Selection

Week 2-3 | 25 Hours

  • Classification of wood — softwood vs. hardwood, Indian timber species (teak, sal, sheesham, deodar, pine), characteristics and uses
  • Wood structure and grain — understanding wood grain direction, growth rings, sapwood vs. heartwood, impact on strength and workability
  • Physical properties of wood — density, moisture content, shrinkage, warping, expansion, seasonal variations in Indian climate
  • Mechanical properties — strength, hardness, elasticity, impact resistance, bending strength, compression strength for different wood types
  • Common Indian timber species — teak (durability, cost), sal (strength, resistance), sheesham (beauty, durability), pine (affordability, softness), bamboo alternatives
  • Wood grading and quality standards — defects detection (knots, cracks, warping), grading systems, IS standards for timber quality
  • Plywood, MDF, and engineered wood — types, advantages, disadvantages, applications in modern furniture and construction
  • Wood selection for different projects — choosing right wood for furniture, flooring, structural work, outdoor vs. indoor applications
  • Timber preservation and treatment — methods to prevent decay, termite resistance, water-proofing treatments used in India
  • Cost estimation based on wood type — understanding wood pricing, calculating material costs for projects, budget planning

Module 3: Hand Tools & Power Tools Mastery

Week 3-5 | 35 Hours

  • Essential hand tools for carpentry — hammer, saw, chisel, plane, screwdriver, wrench, measuring tape, spirit level, square, marking gauge, usage and maintenance
  • Saw types and applications — hand saw, hacksaw, coping saw, bow saw, circular saw blade selection, cutting techniques for different wood types
  • Chisels and gouges — wood chisels (bevel-edge, mortise), gouge types, sharpening techniques, proper grip and striking methods
  • Planes and rasps — smoothing plane, jack plane, block plane, rasp usage, surface finishing, grain direction awareness for smooth finishing
  • Measuring and marking tools — tape measure, folding rule, marking gauge, mortise gauge, carpenter's pencil, chalk line for accurate layouts
  • Power tools introduction — electric drill, circular saw, jigsaw, orbital sander, power planer, safety precautions, noise and dust control
  • Power drill and bits — chuck types, drill bit selection (wood, metal, masonry), drilling straight holes, countersinking, pocket hole drilling techniques
  • Circular saw operation — blade selection, cutting straight lines, crosscutting, ripping, angle cuts, safety guards, dust collection
  • Jigsaw and curved cutting — blade types, cutting curves and circles, internal cuts, compound cuts, thickness capacity
  • Sanding equipment — orbital sanders, belt sanders, finishing sanders, sandpaper grits selection, sanding sequence for smooth finish
  • Tool maintenance and safety — sharpening tools, cleaning, storage, preventing rust, electrical safety for power tools, PPE requirements
  • Cost-benefit analysis — hand tools vs. power tools, investment decisions for small vs. large projects, tool rental options available in India

Module 4: Measuring, Marking & Layout Techniques

Week 5-6 | 20 Hours

  • Measurement systems — metric and imperial units, conversion between systems, reading measurements accurately, common mistakes in measurement
  • Precision measuring tools — tape measure usage, vernier caliper, depth gauge, calipers for curved surfaces, accuracy standards (±2mm tolerance)
  • Marking tools and techniques — pencil marking, chalk line for long distances, marking gauges for parallel lines, mortise gauge for joints
  • Layout fundamentals — drawing plans on wood, transferring designs, using templates, scaling drawings for full-size work, grid method for complex designs
  • Angle measurement — protractor usage, bevel gauge for transferring angles, common angles in carpentry (45°, 30°, 60°), mitre cuts
  • Squaring and alignment — using carpenter's square, set square, checking for 90° angles, diagonal measurement for rectangular layouts
  • Centerline and reference marking — establishing reference lines, centerline marking for symmetrical work, importance for precision
  • Layout for joinery — marking mortise and tenon positions, dovetail layout, dowel hole spacing, accuracy importance for tight joints
  • Digital measuring tools — laser measure usage, digital levels, smartphone apps for measurement, advantages and limitations
  • Common layout mistakes — prevention strategies, checking work during layout phase, correction methods before cutting

Module 5: Cutting, Shaping & Joinery Techniques

Week 6-9 | 50 Hours

  • Straight cutting techniques — hand saw technique, circular saw operation, achieving clean cuts, grain direction awareness, kerf width consideration
  • Curved and shaped cutting — jigsaw usage, band saw operation, coping saw for intricate curves, shaping with plane and rasp
  • Angle and bevel cuts — 45° mitres, compound angles, using bevel gauge, circular saw angle cuts, hand plane for bevel surfaces
  • Mortise and tenon joints — traditional Indian joinery, marking mortise positions, chiselling mortises, cutting tenons, fitting and adjustment, strength advantages
  • Dovetail joints — layout and marking, chiselling technique, sawing dovetails, assembly methods, applications in drawer making and boxes
  • Dowel joints — dowel sizing, hole drilling accuracy, dowel fitting, glue application, clamping techniques, strength and simplicity advantages
  • Lap joints — marking and cutting, half-lap, full-lap, cross-lap variations, strength considerations, modern applications
  • Butt joints and reinforcement — simple butt joints, reinforcing with blocks, corner blocks, biscuit joinery, pocket hole joinery (Kreg jig method)
  • Tongue and groove joints — cutting grooves, creating tongues, applications in flooring and panelling, traditional methods vs. modern routers
  • Rabbet and rebate joints — cutting rebates, applications in frame making, door construction, strength and alignment importance
  • Joint selection for projects — matching joint type to application, strength requirements, aesthetic considerations, time and skill requirements
  • Gluing and clamping — wood glue types (PVA, polyurethane), glue application methods, clamp types and usage, clamping pressure, drying time
  • Assembly techniques — dry assembly checking, order of assembly, preventing glue squeeze-out, checking for square during assembly, clamping strategies

Module 6: Finishing, Furniture Making & Professional Practice

Week 9-12 | 20 Hours

  • Surface preparation for finishing — sanding sequence (80, 120, 180, 220 grit), grain raising and re-sanding, stain compatibility, dust removal methods
  • Wood stains and dyes — water-based stains, oil-based stains, wood dyes, colour selection for Indian timber species, application techniques, even colour achievement
  • Varnish and lacquer finishing — polyurethane varnish, French polish (traditional method), lacquer application, brush technique, spray finishing basics
  • Paint and enamel finishes — primer selection, paint types for wood, application methods, multi-coat finishing, colour matching, durability in Indian climate
  • Oil and wax finishes — linseed oil, tung oil, beeswax, application and buffing, maintenance requirements, aesthetic appeal, traditional finishes
  • Protective treatments — weather-resistant finishes for outdoor furniture, termite-proofing treatments, moisture-resistant coatings, UV protection for sunlight exposure
  • Furniture design principles — ergonomics, proportions, style (contemporary, traditional, minimalist), space utilization, Indian design aesthetics
  • Furniture project execution — designing simple furniture (chair, table, shelf), material estimation, cost calculation, timeline planning, step-by-step construction
  • Quality control and inspection — checking dimensions, joint strength, finish quality, alignment verification, defect identification and correction
  • Costing and estimation — material costs, labour hours, overhead allocation, profit margin calculation, competitive pricing in Indian market
  • Customer communication — understanding requirements, design consultation, delivery timelines, handling feedback, warranty and maintenance guidance
  • Business basics for self-employed carpenters — registration requirements, GST compliance in India, invoicing, maintaining client relationships, building reputation

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions × 2 Marks (Multiple Choice and Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Candidates must complete practical projects demonstrating carpentry skills including cutting, joinery, finishing, and furniture assembly. Assessment based on accuracy, quality, safety practices, and professional presentation.

Practical Tasks:

  • Accurate Cutting & Measurement Test (10 Marks): Candidate cuts wood pieces to exact dimensions (±2mm tolerance) using hand saw and power tools. Evaluates precision, tool handling, and safety compliance.
  • Mortise & Tenon Joint Assembly (15 Marks): Candidate marks, cuts, and assembles a mortise and tenon joint. Evaluated on accuracy of fit, joint tightness, squareness, and finish quality.
  • Simple Furniture Project (Shelf/Small Table) (20 Marks): Candidate designs and builds a simple wooden shelf or small table including cutting, joinery, assembly, and finishing. Evaluated on dimensions accuracy, joint quality, surface finish, and overall craftsmanship.
  • Wood Finishing & Surface Preparation (5 Marks): Candidate prepares wood surface through sanding sequence and applies stain/varnish finish. Evaluated on surface smoothness, finish evenness, colour consistency, and professional appearance.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and select appropriate wood types and materials based on project requirements and Indian timber availability
  • Operate hand tools and power tools safely and efficiently to cut, shape, and finish wood with precision
  • Perform accurate measurements, marking, and layout work maintaining industry-standard tolerances (±2mm)
  • Execute traditional and modern wood joinery techniques including mortise-tenon, dovetail, and dowel joints
  • Apply appropriate finishing techniques including sanding, staining, varnishing, and protective treatments for durability
  • Design and construct simple furniture pieces meeting aesthetic and functional requirements while maintaining quality standards
  • Maintain workplace safety, follow Indian building codes (IS standards), and practice professional ethics in carpentry work
  • Estimate project costs, manage timelines, and communicate effectively with clients for successful project completion
