Banking & Insurance Operations

📚 Sector: Business, Retail & Finance 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the fundamentals of banking and insurance operations with this industry-aligned certification course. Learn customer service, account management, insurance products, and compliance procedures used across Indian financial institutions. Get job-ready in just 8 weeks with hands-on training and real-world scenarios.

This comprehensive foundation course covers essential banking operations, insurance product knowledge, customer relationship management, and regulatory compliance. Designed for entry-level professionals, it combines theoretical knowledge with practical skills needed to work in banks, insurance companies, and financial service centers across India. Gain certification recognized by CETCF and enhance your employability in the fast-growing financial services sector.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior banking or insurance experience required
  • Currently employed in financial sector? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government-issued ID proof required (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, or Voter ID)
  • Basic English and Hindi language understanding required
  • Open to all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals with valid documentation

Modules

Module 1: Banking Fundamentals & Indian Financial System

Week 1-2 | 15 Hours

  • Introduction to banking sector in India — history, structure, and RBI role in regulating banks and financial institutions
  • Types of banks — Public sector banks, Private sector banks, Cooperative banks, Payment banks, and Small Finance Banks operating in India
  • Banking services overview — Deposits, loans, investments, remittance, and digital banking products offered by Indian banks
  • Role of RBI and banking regulations — Understanding monetary policy, banking guidelines, and compliance requirements
  • Bank accounts types — Savings, Current, Recurring, Fixed Deposit accounts with features and eligibility in Indian banking
  • Customer onboarding process — KYC norms, documentation requirements, and account opening procedures as per Indian banking standards
  • Basic banking terminology — IFSC code, MICR, NEFT, RTGS, UPI, and other commonly used banking terms in India
  • Digital banking in India — Internet banking, mobile banking, digital wallets, and emerging fintech solutions

Module 2: Insurance Fundamentals & Product Knowledge

Week 2-3 | 15 Hours

  • Insurance basics — Concept of insurance, risk, premium, claim, and policyholder rights in Indian insurance sector
  • Types of insurance — Life insurance, Health insurance, General insurance, and specialized insurance products available in India
  • Life insurance products — Term insurance, Endowment, Money-back, Unit-linked insurance plans (ULIPs) offered by Indian insurers
  • Health insurance overview — Individual health plans, Family floater plans, Critical illness coverage, and cashless treatment facilities
  • General insurance categories — Motor insurance, Home insurance, Travel insurance, and liability insurance products in Indian market
  • Insurance regulatory framework — IRDAI guidelines, policy document requirements, and consumer protection rules in India
  • Insurance claims process — Filing claims, documentation needed, claim settlement procedures, and grievance redressal in Indian insurance
  • Premium calculation basics — Understanding risk assessment, underwriting, and how premiums are determined in Indian insurance
  • Insurance intermediaries — Role of agents, brokers, and corporate agents in selling insurance products across India
  • Digital insurance services — Online policy purchase, claim filing through apps, and digital insurance solutions in India

Module 3: Customer Service & Relationship Management in Financial Services

Week 3-4 | 15 Hours

  • Customer service excellence — Understanding customer expectations, service quality standards, and building long-term relationships in banking and insurance
  • Communication skills for financial services — Professional communication, active listening, empathy, and handling diverse customers across India
  • Handling customer queries — Responding to account inquiries, product information requests, and complaint resolution in banking and insurance
  • Managing difficult customers — De-escalation techniques, patience, problem-solving approach, and maintaining professionalism during conflicts
  • Customer needs assessment — Identifying customer requirements, financial goals, risk profile, and recommending suitable products
  • Building customer trust — Transparency in dealings, honest advice, protecting customer information, and maintaining ethical standards
  • Cross-selling and upselling — Introducing additional banking and insurance products to existing customers based on their needs
  • Customer retention strategies — Loyalty programs, regular follow-ups, personalized service, and creating positive customer experiences
  • Handling complaints and feedback — Documenting issues, escalation procedures, and using feedback for service improvement in financial institutions
  • Multichannel customer support — Managing customer interactions through branches, phone, email, chat, and social media in modern banking

Module 4: Banking Operations & Account Management

Week 4-5 | 15 Hours

  • Account opening procedures — Complete process from application to account activation, including document verification and KYC compliance in Indian banks
  • Account maintenance — Managing account details, address changes, nominee updates, and regular account monitoring procedures
  • Deposit operations — Processing deposits, issuing receipts, maintaining deposit registers, and handling different deposit types in banking
  • Withdrawal procedures — Cheque processing, withdrawal requests, verification, and maintaining security protocols in banking operations
  • Cheque handling — Receiving cheques, verification, clearing process, and understanding cheque bounce reasons as per Indian banking standards
  • Cash management — Counting, verification, reconciliation, and maintaining cash registers with proper accountability in banking branches
  • Fund transfers — Processing NEFT, RTGS, IMPS transfers, verifying beneficiary details, and ensuring timely fund delivery
  • Loan operations basics — Loan application processing, document collection, eligibility verification, and basic loan disbursement procedures
  • Fixed deposit and recurring deposit operations — Processing FD and RD applications, issuing receipts, maturity management, and renewal procedures
  • Account reconciliation — Daily reconciliation procedures, identifying discrepancies, and maintaining accurate banking records
  • Banking documentation — Understanding and maintaining cheque books, passbooks, account statements, and other banking documents
  • Security procedures — Verifying customer identity, checking signatures, preventing fraud, and following security protocols in all banking operations

Module 5: Insurance Operations & Claims Management

Week 5-6 | 15 Hours

  • Insurance policy issuance — Receiving applications, collecting documents, issuing policy documents, and maintaining policy records in insurance companies
  • Premium collection methods — Cash collection, cheque payment, online payment, and standing instruction setup for insurance premium collection
  • Policy documentation — Understanding policy schedules, terms and conditions, coverage details, and exclusions in insurance policies
  • Premium calculation and billing — Understanding how premiums are calculated, generating bills, and managing premium payment schedules
  • Policy renewal procedures — Managing policy renewal dates, sending renewal notices, collecting renewal premiums, and avoiding policy lapse
  • Claims registration — Receiving claim information, documenting claim details, assigning claim reference numbers, and initiating claim process
  • Claims documentation — Collecting required documents like medical reports, invoices, police reports, and other supporting documents for claims
  • Claims investigation — Verifying claim authenticity, checking policy coverage, investigating suspicious claims, and ensuring compliance
  • Claims settlement — Processing approved claims, calculating settlement amounts, issuing cheques or online transfers, and maintaining claim records
  • Policy servicing — Managing policy changes, address updates, nominee modifications, and handling policy-related customer requests
  • Lapsed policy management — Identifying lapsed policies, contacting customers, processing revival applications, and maintaining policy status records
  • Grievance handling in insurance — Receiving complaints, documenting grievances, escalating to appropriate departments, and tracking resolution

Module 6: Compliance, Security & Professional Ethics in Financial Services

Week 6-8 | 15 Hours

  • Banking and insurance regulations — Understanding RBI guidelines, IRDAI regulations, SEBI rules, and compliance requirements in financial sector
  • KYC and AML compliance — Know Your Customer procedures, Anti-Money Laundering guidelines, and identifying suspicious transactions as per Indian law
  • Data protection and privacy — Safeguarding customer information, GDPR understanding, data security measures, and confidentiality in financial services
  • Information security practices — Password management, secure login procedures, preventing unauthorized access, and protecting against cyber threats
  • Fraud prevention and detection — Identifying common fraud schemes, verifying documents, checking signatures, and reporting suspicious activities
  • Document verification procedures — Authenticating identity proofs, address proofs, income documents, and detecting forged or fake documents
  • Ethical standards in banking and insurance — Maintaining integrity, avoiding conflicts of interest, honest dealing, and ethical decision-making
  • Customer rights and grievance redressal — Understanding customer rights, complaint procedures, escalation mechanisms, and regulatory grievance forums
  • Internal control and audit procedures — Understanding internal checks, audit trails, reconciliation procedures, and maintaining audit compliance
  • Code of conduct for financial services employees — Professional behavior, dress code, punctuality, confidentiality, and workplace ethics
  • Regulatory reporting and documentation — Maintaining records, filing reports, statutory compliance, and documentation standards in financial institutions
  • Crisis management and business continuity — Understanding backup procedures, disaster recovery plans, and maintaining service continuity during emergencies

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice and Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Candidates must demonstrate practical competency in banking and insurance operations through simulated real-world tasks and scenario-based assessments

Practical Tasks:

  • Account Opening & KYC Verification (10 Marks): Candidate must complete a simulated account opening process including customer verification, document checking, form filling, and KYC compliance. Evaluator assesses accuracy, completeness, and adherence to banking procedures.
  • Banking Transaction Processing (10 Marks): Candidate must process various banking transactions including deposits, withdrawals, cheque processing, and fund transfers. Must demonstrate correct procedures, proper documentation, and accurate record-keeping.
  • Insurance Product Knowledge & Customer Counseling (10 Marks): Candidate must explain different insurance products to a simulated customer, assess their needs, recommend appropriate products, and answer product-related questions accurately and confidently.
  • Claims Processing & Documentation (10 Marks): Candidate must process a simulated insurance claim including document collection, verification, calculation, and settlement. Must follow proper procedures and maintain accurate claim records.
  • Customer Service & Problem Resolution (10 Marks): Candidate must handle simulated customer interactions including complaints, queries, and service requests. Must demonstrate professional communication, empathy, and effective problem-solving skills.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the structure of Indian banking system and role of RBI in financial regulation and supervision
  • Demonstrate knowledge of various banking products, services, and account types available in Indian financial institutions
  • Identify different insurance categories, products, and claims procedures relevant to Indian insurance market
  • Execute banking operations including account management, deposits, withdrawals, and fund transfers with accuracy and compliance
  • Process insurance claims and handle policy servicing following IRDAI guidelines and company procedures
  • Deliver excellent customer service and manage relationships through professional communication and problem-solving
  • Apply KYC, AML, and data protection procedures to ensure regulatory compliance and customer information security
  • Recognize and prevent fraud through document verification, suspicious activity detection, and ethical standards
