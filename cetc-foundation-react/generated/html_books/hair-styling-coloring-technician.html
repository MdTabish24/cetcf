Hair Styling & Coloring Technician

📚 Sector: Beauty & Lifestyle 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks / 180 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the art and science of professional hair styling and coloring with this industry-focused certification course. Learn cutting-edge techniques, color theory, and client management skills to build a successful career in India's booming beauty sector. This hands-on program prepares you for immediate employment or self-employment in salons and beauty studios across the country.

This comprehensive foundation course covers all essential skills needed to become a professional Hair Styling & Coloring Technician. You will learn hair anatomy, styling techniques, color application methods, chemical treatments, safety protocols, and client communication. The curriculum combines theoretical knowledge with 80+ hours of practical training on real clients, ensuring you are job-ready upon completion.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior experience required; complete beginners welcome
  • Already working as a hair stylist? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam for direct certification
  • Valid government ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Driving License)
  • Applicable for all candidates regardless of gender, caste, religion, or economic background
  • Medical fitness: No serious hand/arm injuries or allergies to hair chemicals (as per doctor's certification)
  • Commitment to attend minimum 80% of classes for certification eligibility

Modules

Module 1: Hair Science, Anatomy & Fundamentals

Week 1-2 | 30 Hours

  • Hair structure and growth cycle — Understanding hair shaft, cortex, medulla, and cuticle layers; hair growth phases (anagen, catagen, telogen)
  • Hair types and textures — Classification of straight, wavy, curly, and coily hair; texture grading systems used in Indian salons
  • Scalp conditions and hair health — Identifying dandruff, seborrheic dermatitis, alopecia, and scalp sensitivity; basic remedies
  • pH balance and hair chemistry — Understanding acidic and alkaline products; how pH affects hair cuticle and color retention
  • Hair porosity and elasticity — Testing methods; why porous hair requires different coloring and treatment approaches
  • Common hair problems in Indian climate — Frizz, dryness, heat damage, pollution effects; prevention and care strategies
  • Hair products classification — Shampoos, conditioners, serums, oils; reading product labels and understanding ingredients
  • Safety and hygiene protocols — Salon sanitation, cross-contamination prevention, personal protective equipment (PPE) usage
  • Tools and equipment identification — Blow dryers, straighteners, curlers, combs, brushes; proper maintenance and sterilization

Module 2: Basic Hair Cutting & Styling Techniques

Week 3-4 | 30 Hours

  • Scissor and clipper handling — Grip techniques, cutting angles, and safety practices; choosing right tools for different hair types
  • Basic hair cutting terminology — Layers, graduation, blunt cuts, texturizing; understanding salon cutting patterns
  • Face shape analysis and haircut selection — Oval, round, square, oblong faces; recommending flattering cuts for Indian facial features
  • Blow-drying techniques — Directional drying, volume creation, smoothing; using blow dryer with brush and round brush techniques
  • Straightening and smoothing — Flat iron usage for different hair types; protecting hair from heat damage; achieving salon-quality finish
  • Basic curling and waves — Using hot rollers, curling irons, and waving techniques; creating loose waves, tight curls, and beachy waves
  • Braiding fundamentals — Three-strand, four-strand, and fishtail braids; practical applications in Indian wedding and daily styling
  • Updo styling basics — Buns, ponytails, twisted styles; securing with pins and products; suitable for Indian occasions and formal events
  • Styling for different occasions — Everyday looks, office-appropriate styles, festive styling; customizing based on client preferences
  • Product application and finishing — Using serums, sprays, and styling products; achieving desired hold and shine without buildup

Module 3: Hair Coloring Theory & Color Science

Week 5-6 | 30 Hours

  • Color wheel and color theory — Understanding primary, secondary, tertiary colors; complementary colors for correcting unwanted tones
  • Natural hair pigmentation — Eumelanin and pheomelanin; understanding Indian hair's natural color range and undertones
  • Levels of color and tone — Hair color levels 1-10; understanding how to read color charts and predict color outcomes
  • Permanent vs semi-permanent vs temporary colors — Differences, advantages, disadvantages; choosing right product for client needs
  • Oxidative color chemistry — How developer and color molecule interact; understanding processing time and color deposit
  • Demi-permanent coloring — Deposit-only colors; ideal for clients wanting color without permanent commitment; application methods
  • Vegetable and plant-based colors — Henna, indigo, and natural dyes popular in India; benefits, limitations, and application techniques
  • Highlights and lowlights — Balayage, foil highlights, and rooted color techniques; creating dimension and depth in Indian hair
  • Color correction and problem-solving — Fixing over-processed hair, correcting unwanted tones, salvaging failed color jobs
  • Patch testing and allergy prevention — Conducting 48-hour patch tests; recognizing allergic reactions; documentation and client safety
  • Color selection for Indian skin tones — Understanding warm, cool, and neutral undertones; recommending flattering colors for different complexions
  • Maintaining color vibrancy — Explaining color fade factors; recommending maintenance products and touch-up schedules

Module 4: Hair Coloring Application & Chemical Treatments

Week 7-8 | 30 Hours

  • Pre-coloring consultation and strand test — Assessing hair condition, previous color history, and client expectations; conducting strand tests
  • Sectioning and application techniques — Dividing hair into sections; applying color systematically to ensure even coverage and consistent results
  • Root touch-up application — Applying color to regrowth only; timing and technique to avoid overlapping and color inconsistency
  • Full-head coloring application — Systematic approach for virgin hair; managing processing time and heat distribution for even color
  • Foil highlighting technique — Placing foils, sectioning, and applying bleach or lightener; creating natural-looking highlights in Indian hair
  • Balayage hand-painting method — Free-hand application for dimensional color; creating sun-kissed and ombre effects popular in India
  • Hair bleaching and lightening — Understanding bleach strength, processing time, and damage prevention; achieving desired lightness safely
  • Toning and ash colors — Using toners to neutralize unwanted yellow and orange tones; creating ash blonde, ash brown, and cool tones
  • Smoothing and keratin treatments — Applying chemical smoothing treatments; understanding formaldehyde-free options; post-treatment care
  • Permanent wave (perm) application — Chemical restructuring of hair; understanding wave patterns and processing; aftercare instructions
  • Hair relaxing and straightening treatments — Chemical relaxers for curly hair; understanding different relaxer types; safety and processing
  • Deep conditioning and protein treatments — Intensive conditioning for damaged hair; application methods and processing times

Module 5: Client Communication, Consultation & Salon Management

Week 9-10 | 30 Hours

  • Professional communication skills — Active listening, asking clarifying questions, understanding client needs without assumptions
  • Consultation techniques — Visual reference gathering (Pinterest, magazines), discussing realistic expectations, explaining technical limitations
  • Managing difficult clients — Handling complaints, managing expectations, finding solutions when results don't match expectations
  • Pricing and service packages — Understanding salon pricing structures, explaining costs, offering add-on services (treatments, products)
  • Appointment booking and scheduling — Managing appointment slots, allowing adequate time for services, follow-up reminders
  • Product recommendations — Suggesting at-home care products, explaining maintenance routines, building retail sales alongside services
  • Hygiene and sanitation documentation — Maintaining client records, documenting allergies, previous treatments, and color history
  • Salon etiquette and professionalism — Dress code, punctuality, customer service standards, maintaining clean workstation
  • Building client loyalty — Creating positive experiences, remembering client preferences, offering loyalty programs and referral incentives
  • Social media and portfolio building — Photographing work, creating before-and-after posts, building online presence for self-employment
  • Handling chemical safety and MSDS — Understanding material safety data sheets, proper storage of chemicals, emergency procedures
  • Payment and billing practices — Handling cash and digital payments, maintaining transparent pricing, issuing receipts and invoices

Module 6: Practical Training, Trends & Career Development

Week 11-12 | 30 Hours

  • Hands-on styling practice — Minimum 20 hours of practical training on real clients; supervised styling, cutting, and coloring services
  • Portfolio development — Creating before-and-after documentation of your work; building professional portfolio for job applications
  • Current hair trends in India — Understanding popular styles (layers, undercuts, fades), color trends (balayage, ombre, pastel shades), seasonal variations
  • Bridal and occasion styling — Traditional Indian wedding hairstyles, mehendi and sangeet styling, festive updo techniques, regional variations
  • Men's grooming and styling — Fade cuts, undercuts, beard grooming, product recommendations; growing men's grooming market in India
  • Kids and senior hair care — Special considerations for children's hair, age-appropriate styles, gentle handling for senior clients
  • Entrepreneurship basics — Starting your own salon, home-based business setup, licensing requirements, initial investment, local regulations
  • Employment opportunities — Salon chains, beauty studios, wedding planning companies, film and television industry, freelance styling
  • Continuing education — Advanced courses (advanced coloring, extensions, treatments), certifications from product brands, skill upgradation
  • Business marketing and customer acquisition — Local advertising, referral programs, social media marketing, creating service packages
  • Health and wellness — Preventing occupational hazards (chemical exposure, repetitive strain), maintaining physical health, mental wellness
  • Exam preparation and final revision — Review of all modules, mock assessments, common mistakes, confidence building for certification exam

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice & Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Practical assessment includes live demonstration of styling and coloring techniques on real clients or mannequins under supervision

Practical Tasks:

  • Hair Styling Demonstration (15 Marks): Candidate must perform a complete styling service including wash, blow-dry, and styling (straightening, curling, or updo) on a live client or practice head. Assessed on technique, safety, client comfort, and final result quality.
  • Hair Coloring Application (15 Marks): Candidate must perform a complete coloring service (full-head color, root touch-up, or highlights) demonstrating proper sectioning, application technique, timing, and processing. Assessed on even color distribution, safety protocols, and color accuracy.
  • Client Consultation & Communication (10 Marks): Candidate must conduct a professional consultation with a client/assessor, understand requirements, recommend appropriate services, explain procedures, address concerns, and document information. Assessed on listening skills, professionalism, and clarity.
  • Hygiene & Safety Compliance (10 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate proper sanitation of tools, correct use of PPE, safe handling of chemicals, and maintaining a clean workstation throughout the practical assessment.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand hair anatomy, growth cycle, and various hair types to provide appropriate care and styling recommendations
  • Perform basic to intermediate hair cutting and styling techniques including blow-drying, straightening, curling, and updos
  • Apply color theory and chemistry knowledge to select, mix, and apply hair colors safely and effectively
  • Execute professional hair coloring services including highlights, lowlights, root touch-ups, and color correction with precision
  • Communicate professionally with clients, conduct consultations, manage expectations, and build long-term client relationships
  • Maintain salon hygiene standards, handle chemicals safely, follow legal requirements, and provide excellent customer service
