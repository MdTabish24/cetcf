Office Administration Professional

📚 Sector: Business, Retail & Finance 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the essential skills of modern office management and become a confident administrative professional. This foundation course equips you with practical knowledge in documentation, communication, financial basics, and office operations—all critical for success in India's growing corporate sector.

This 8-week program covers fundamental office administration practices including file management, correspondence, basic accounting, customer service, and workplace communication. Through hands-on practice and real-world scenarios, you'll develop the competencies needed to manage office operations efficiently and support business processes effectively.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (with parental consent if below 18)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior work experience required
  • Already working in office environment? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid photo ID proof required (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, or Driving License)
  • Basic English and Hindi proficiency recommended
  • Applicable to all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals
  • No medical restrictions for office-based work

Modules

Module 1: Office Administration Fundamentals & Workplace Orientation

Week 1 | 15 Hours

  • Introduction to office administration — roles, responsibilities, and career scope in India
  • Types of offices — corporate, government, NGO, startup, and their unique operational needs
  • Office hierarchy and reporting structure — understanding chain of command and communication flow
  • Workplace ethics and professional conduct — punctuality, dress code, confidentiality, and integrity
  • Health, safety, and ergonomics in office environment — workspace setup, posture, and injury prevention
  • Office rules and regulations — company policies, attendance systems, and grievance procedures
  • Workplace diversity and inclusion — respecting differences, equal opportunity, and gender sensitivity
  • Introduction to digital workplace — basic IT infrastructure, email systems, and office software overview

Module 2: Documentation, Filing & Record Management

Week 2-3 | 20 Hours

  • Types of office documents — letters, memos, reports, invoices, receipts, and forms used in Indian offices
  • Document preparation standards — formatting, font sizes, margins, and official letter writing protocols
  • Filing systems — alphabetical, numerical, chronological, and subject-based filing methods
  • Physical file management — organizing cabinets, labeling, indexing, and retrieval procedures
  • Digital document management — folder structure, naming conventions, cloud storage basics (Google Drive, OneDrive)
  • Record retention and disposal — understanding document lifecycle, legal requirements, and archiving
  • Confidentiality and data protection — handling sensitive documents, GDPR basics, and information security
  • Document tracking systems — maintaining registers, acknowledgment slips, and audit trails
  • Practical filing exercise — creating and maintaining both physical and digital filing systems
  • Introduction to DMS (Document Management System) — basic navigation and document upload procedures

Module 3: Office Communication & Correspondence

Week 3-4 | 18 Hours

  • Verbal communication skills — clarity, tone, listening, and professional telephone etiquette
  • Written communication — email drafting, formal letters, memos, and instant messaging protocols
  • Business correspondence standards — official letterhead, date formats, salutations, and closing conventions
  • Email communication best practices — subject lines, tone, attachments, CC/BCC usage, and response time expectations
  • Meeting coordination and minutes — scheduling, sending invitations, preparing agendas, and documenting meeting notes
  • Effective listening and feedback — active listening techniques, clarifying questions, and constructive feedback
  • Cross-cultural communication — understanding different communication styles in diverse Indian workplaces
  • Complaint handling and conflict resolution — addressing issues professionally and maintaining relationships
  • Presentation basics — organizing thoughts, visual aids, and delivering information clearly to groups
  • Documentation of communications — maintaining communication logs and follow-up records

Module 4: Basic Accounting, Finance & Office Operations

Week 4-5 | 22 Hours

  • Introduction to accounting basics — assets, liabilities, equity, and fundamental accounting equation
  • Invoicing and billing — creating invoices, understanding GST in India, payment terms, and invoice tracking
  • Receipt and payment management — processing receipts, maintaining payment records, and reconciliation
  • Expense tracking and reimbursement — categorizing office expenses, maintaining expense sheets, and claim procedures
  • Petty cash management — maintaining petty cash box, recording transactions, and reconciliation
  • Bank reconciliation basics — understanding bank statements, identifying discrepancies, and reconciliation process
  • Payroll basics — understanding salary components, deductions, and payroll processing overview
  • Office budgeting — preparing simple budgets, tracking expenditures, and cost control measures
  • Purchase orders and procurement — creating POs, vendor management, and purchase approval processes
  • Financial documentation — maintaining ledgers, journals, and financial records in compliance with Indian standards
  • Introduction to GST — understanding Goods and Services Tax, invoice requirements, and compliance basics
  • Basic financial analysis — understanding profit and loss, balance sheet fundamentals, and financial ratios overview

Module 5: Customer Service, Client Relations & Office Support

Week 5-6 | 18 Hours

  • Customer service principles — understanding customer needs, expectations, and service quality standards
  • Reception and visitor management — greeting visitors, maintaining visitor logs, managing appointments, and security protocols
  • Telephone handling skills — call screening, message taking, call transferring, and professional phone etiquette
  • Email management — prioritizing emails, responding promptly, managing inbox efficiently, and avoiding common mistakes
  • Complaint resolution — understanding complaints, documenting issues, following up, and maintaining customer satisfaction
  • Building professional relationships — networking, maintaining contacts, and creating positive working relationships
  • Feedback collection and documentation — gathering customer feedback, analyzing suggestions, and implementing improvements
  • Service recovery — handling dissatisfied customers, offering solutions, and rebuilding trust
  • Client database management — maintaining contact information, tracking interactions, and CRM basics
  • Follow-up procedures — ensuring tasks are completed, maintaining communication, and confirming client satisfaction

Module 6: Technology Skills & Modern Office Tools

Week 6-8 | 27 Hours

  • Microsoft Word essentials — document formatting, templates, headers/footers, mail merge, and professional document creation
  • Microsoft Excel basics — spreadsheet creation, formulas, data organization, charts, and basic data analysis
  • Microsoft PowerPoint fundamentals — presentation design, slide layouts, animations, and professional presentation delivery
  • Email systems — using Outlook or Gmail, calendar management, scheduling meetings, and managing multiple mailboxes
  • Google Workspace basics — Google Docs, Sheets, Forms, and collaborative document editing
  • Cloud storage and sharing — Google Drive, OneDrive, Dropbox usage, file sharing permissions, and security
  • Video conferencing tools — Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams basics, scheduling calls, and etiquette
  • Instant messaging and collaboration — Slack, WhatsApp Business, and professional messaging protocols
  • Online scheduling tools — Calendly, Google Calendar, and appointment scheduling systems
  • Data entry and digitization — accurate typing, speed, proofreading, and maintaining data integrity
  • Basic cybersecurity awareness — password protection, phishing awareness, data backup, and safe internet practices
  • Mobile office tools — using smartphones for work, mobile apps for productivity, and work-life balance
  • Practical projects — creating business documents, maintaining spreadsheets, and preparing presentations using real scenarios

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Multiple Choice Questions × 2 Marks each - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on demonstration of office administration tasks and document creation

Practical Tasks:

  • Document Creation & Formatting (10 Marks): Candidate must create a formal business letter, memo, and email following Indian office standards with proper formatting, language, and structure
  • Filing System Organization (10 Marks): Candidate must organize and categorize 20 sample documents into appropriate filing systems (alphabetical, chronological, subject-based) with proper labeling and indexing
  • Spreadsheet & Data Management (10 Marks): Candidate must create an Excel spreadsheet with expense tracking data, including formulas, basic calculations, and a simple chart presentation
  • Meeting Minutes & Documentation (10 Marks): Candidate must listen to a recorded meeting scenario and prepare accurate meeting minutes with action items, responsible parties, and deadlines
  • Customer Service Scenario (10 Marks): Candidate must handle a simulated customer complaint through email and telephone, demonstrating professional communication and problem-solving approach

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand and apply office administration principles, workplace ethics, and professional conduct standards
  • Create, organize, and maintain both physical and digital filing systems efficiently and securely
  • Compose professional business correspondence including letters, emails, memos, and reports following Indian standards
  • Process financial documents, manage petty cash, track expenses, and understand basic accounting concepts
  • Provide excellent customer service, manage client relationships, and resolve complaints professionally
  • Operate essential office software including MS Office, Google Workspace, email systems, and digital collaboration tools
  • Demonstrate effective communication skills in verbal, written, and digital formats
  • Manage office operations including scheduling, documentation, and administrative support tasks
