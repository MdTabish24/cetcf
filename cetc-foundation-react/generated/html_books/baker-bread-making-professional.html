Baker & Bread Making Professional

📚 Sector: Food, Catering & Hospitality 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the art and science of bread making with this foundation-level certification course. Learn traditional and modern baking techniques, understand dough fermentation, and produce quality breads that meet commercial standards. Perfect for aspiring bakers, home entrepreneurs, and hospitality professionals seeking professional credentials in India's growing baking industry.

This comprehensive 8-week program covers bakery fundamentals, ingredient knowledge, dough preparation, fermentation science, oven techniques, and professional bread making. You'll gain hands-on experience with various bread types—from basic white bread to sourdough and whole wheat varieties—and learn food safety, costing, and small business operations. The course combines classroom learning with practical bakery floor training to prepare you for employment or entrepreneurship.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior bakery experience required
  • Already working as a baker? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid government ID proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, or Voter ID) required at registration
  • Physically capable of standing for extended periods and handling heavy equipment
  • Applicable for all candidates across India regardless of background
  • Students from SC/ST/OBC categories receive CETCF support and fee concessions

Modules

Module 1: Bakery Fundamentals & Food Safety

Week 1 | 15 Hours

  • Introduction to Bakery Industry in India — Market overview, growth opportunities, and career scope in metros and tier-2 cities
  • Bakery Equipment Identification — Mixers, ovens, proofing boxes, scaling machines, and their uses
  • Personal Hygiene & Food Safety Standards — FSSAI guidelines, handwashing, uniform requirements, and cross-contamination prevention
  • Kitchen Sanitation Practices — Cleaning schedules, sanitization procedures, waste management, and pest control
  • Allergen Management & Labeling — Common allergens (gluten, nuts, dairy), labeling requirements, and customer communication
  • Fire Safety & Emergency Procedures — Fire extinguisher types, evacuation routes, and first aid basics
  • Workplace Safety & Accident Prevention — Handling hot surfaces, sharp tools, and heavy equipment safely
  • Quality Control Basics — Understanding product consistency, appearance standards, and defect identification

Module 2: Ingredients & Dough Science

Week 2 | 18 Hours

  • Flour Types & Selection — All-purpose flour, bread flour, whole wheat, multigrain; protein content and baking properties
  • Water Quality & Temperature — pH levels, hardness, temperature control for dough consistency
  • Yeast Types & Activation — Fresh yeast, dry yeast, instant yeast; blooming, hydration, and storage
  • Salt, Sugar & Fats — Role in flavor, texture, fermentation, shelf-life, and moisture retention
  • Additives & Improvers — Dough conditioners, enzymes, ascorbic acid; regulations and usage
  • Gluten Development — Understanding gluten network, extensibility, elasticity, and windowpane test
  • Hydration & Dough Consistency — Baker's percentages, calculating water ratios, achieving desired texture
  • Ingredient Weighing & Measurements — Precision scaling, converting recipes, batch calculations
  • Sourcing Quality Ingredients — Local suppliers in India, bulk purchasing, storage conditions, cost management
  • Ingredient Cost Analysis — Calculating per-loaf costs, understanding profit margins

Module 3: Basic Bread Making Techniques

Week 3 | 20 Hours

  • Dough Mixing Methods — Straight dough method, sponge & dough method, autolyse technique
  • Hand Mixing vs Machine Mixing — Advantages, disadvantages, equipment operation, and consistency
  • Kneading Techniques — Developing gluten, achieving proper texture, identifying readiness
  • Bulk Fermentation — Temperature control, fermentation time, signs of proper fermentation, punch-down techniques
  • Shaping & Molding — Round loaves, oval loaves, batards, boules; hand techniques and shaping tools
  • Proofing Process — Final proof timing, temperature, humidity control, poke test method
  • Scoring & Slashing — Techniques, tools, and impact on oven spring and crust appearance
  • Baking Process — Oven temperature management, steam injection, baking time, color development
  • Cooling & Storage — Proper cooling procedures, shelf-life extension, packaging methods
  • Troubleshooting Common Issues — Dense crumb, over-fermentation, under-fermentation, crust problems

Module 4: Bread Varieties & Specialty Breads

Week 4 | 22 Hours

  • White Bread Production — Standard loaf, sandwich bread, brioche; enriched dough handling
  • Whole Wheat Breads — Whole wheat flour properties, hydration adjustments, fermentation timing
  • Multigrain & Seed Breads — Incorporating oats, millet, sunflower seeds, flax; soaking requirements
  • Sourdough Basics — Starter culture creation, maintenance, fermentation, and flavor development
  • Quick Breads — Banana bread, zucchini bread, soda breads; chemical leavening agents
  • Rolls & Buns — Dinner rolls, burger buns, hot dog buns; shaping and portion control
  • Specialty Breads — Focaccia, ciabatta, baguettes; understanding crust development
  • Rye & Pumpernickel — Rye flour properties, sour flavors, density management
  • Flatbreads & Naan — Traditional Indian breads, tandoor baking, commercial oven adaptation
  • Enriched Breads — Panettone, challah, tea breads; sugar and fat incorporation

Module 5: Commercial Bakery Operations

Week 5-6 | 25 Hours

  • Bakery Layout & Workflow — Production sequence, space optimization, equipment placement for efficiency
  • Production Planning & Scheduling — Daily production targets, batch planning, time management
  • Inventory Management — Stock tracking, shelf-life monitoring, waste reduction, ordering systems
  • Costing & Pricing — Raw material costs, labor costs, overhead, calculating selling price, profit margins
  • Recipe Standardization — Creating standard operating procedures (SOPs), batch cards, consistency
  • Production Records — Maintaining logs, temperature records, ingredient batches, traceability
  • Quality Assurance Checks — Daily testing, weight verification, appearance grading, customer feedback
  • Packaging & Labeling — Food safety compliance, ingredient declaration, date marking, branding
  • Customer Service & Communication — Taking orders, managing expectations, handling complaints
  • Small Business Basics — Business registration, licensing, taxes, record-keeping for home-based bakeries

Module 6: Advanced Techniques & Entrepreneurship

Week 7-8 | 20 Hours

  • Fermentation Science — Temperature effects, fermentation stages, time-temperature relationships, dough conditioning
  • Crust & Crumb Development — Maillard reaction, caramelization, steam's role, achieving open crumb structure
  • Flavor Development — Slow fermentation benefits, salt impact, sourness in sourdough, ingredient selection
  • Customization & Dietary Breads — Gluten-free baking, low-sodium, sugar-free, high-protein breads
  • Seasonal Production — Managing humidity, temperature variations, ingredient adjustments across seasons
  • Equipment Maintenance — Oven care, mixer cleaning, preventive maintenance schedules, troubleshooting
  • Starting a Home Bakery — FSSAI registration, local licensing, kitchen setup, initial investment
  • Online & Direct Sales — E-commerce platforms, social media marketing, delivery logistics, customer retention
  • Scaling Production — From home to commercial kitchen, hiring staff, maintaining quality at scale
  • Industry Trends & Innovation — Artisanal breads, health-conscious options, sustainability practices, market research

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple choice, short answer, and practical scenario-based questions) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on baking assessment where candidates must produce multiple bread varieties demonstrating proper technique, food safety compliance, and quality standards

Practical Tasks:

  • White Bread / Sandwich Loaf Production (15 Marks): Candidate must prepare dough, perform mixing and kneading, manage fermentation, shape, proof, score, and bake a complete loaf meeting commercial standards (weight 450-500g, uniform crumb, proper crust color)
  • Whole Wheat or Multigrain Bread (15 Marks): Production of whole grain bread demonstrating understanding of different flour properties, hydration adjustments, and fermentation timing. Loaf must meet quality standards with good volume and texture
  • Specialty Bread (Rolls, Buns, or Flatbread) (10 Marks): Candidate must produce at least 8 uniform pieces of specialty bread (dinner rolls, burger buns, or focaccia) with consistent shaping, proper proofing, and baking. Assessment includes portion control and appearance
  • Food Safety & Hygiene Compliance (10 Marks): Demonstrated knowledge of FSSAI guidelines, proper hygiene practices, equipment sanitation, ingredient handling, and allergen management throughout the practical assessment

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand bakery industry standards, food safety regulations, and FSSAI compliance requirements applicable in India
  • Identify and correctly use bakery ingredients, understand their properties, and calculate precise measurements using baker's percentages
  • Demonstrate proper dough mixing, kneading, fermentation, shaping, and baking techniques to produce quality breads
  • Produce various bread types including white bread, whole wheat, multigrain, rolls, and specialty breads meeting commercial quality standards
  • Plan production schedules, manage inventory, calculate costs, and maintain quality control in a bakery environment
  • Start and operate a small home bakery or bread business with proper licensing, food safety compliance, and basic business management skills
