Welding & Fabrication Technician

📚 Sector: Construction & Building Trades 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks / 180 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the essential skills of welding and metal fabrication to build a strong career in India's booming construction and manufacturing sectors. This hands-on foundation course teaches you modern welding techniques, safety practices, and fabrication methods used in real-world projects across India.

This 12-week intensive program covers welding fundamentals, different welding processes (MMA, TIG, MIG), metal cutting techniques, fabrication basics, safety protocols, and quality control. You'll gain practical experience with industry-standard tools and equipment, preparing you for immediate employment in construction sites, manufacturing units, shipyards, and automotive industries across India.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (16 years recommended for shop floor work)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior welding experience required
  • Physical fitness: Ability to stand for extended periods and work at heights
  • Valid photo ID (Aadhar, PAN, Passport, Driving License) required at registration
  • Already working as welder? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Open to all candidates — no gender, caste, or community restrictions
  • Medical fitness certificate may be required by employers after certification

Modules

Module 1: Welding Fundamentals & Safety

Week 1-2 | 30 Hours

  • Introduction to welding — definition, types, and applications in Indian construction
  • Welding safety standards — PPE, fire safety, and OSHA guidelines applicable in India
  • Workshop safety protocols — machine operation, emergency procedures, first aid
  • Electrical safety — understanding current, voltage, and grounding in welding equipment
  • Material handling and storage — proper storage of welding consumables and metals
  • Health hazards — fumes, radiation, heat stress, and preventive measures
  • Fire prevention and extinguisher types — Class A, B, C, D fires and their control
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE) — helmets, gloves, aprons, shoes, and respirators
  • Safety signage and emergency exits — recognizing hazard symbols in Indian workshops
  • Incident reporting and documentation — maintaining safety records on construction sites

Module 2: Metal Properties & Welding Equipment

Week 2-4 | 35 Hours

  • Ferrous metals — iron, mild steel, stainless steel properties and applications
  • Non-ferrous metals — aluminum, copper, brass characteristics and welding challenges
  • Metal alloys — composition and behavior in Indian industrial applications
  • Heat treatment basics — annealing, tempering, hardening effects on weldability
  • Welding power sources — AC, DC transformers, inverters, and their selection
  • Electrode types and specifications — E6013, E7018, stainless steel electrodes in Indian standards
  • Welding machines — MMA, TIG, MIG machine components and operation
  • Welding cables, connectors, and ground clamps — proper sizing and maintenance
  • Cutting equipment — oxy-acetylene torches, plasma cutters, angle grinders
  • Measuring and marking tools — tape measures, calipers, try squares, chalk lines
  • Workshop layout and equipment placement — ergonomic setup for Indian workshops
  • Equipment maintenance and troubleshooting — common issues and solutions

Module 3: Manual Metal Arc (MMA) Welding

Week 4-6 | 40 Hours

  • MMA welding principle — arc formation and metal deposition process
  • Electrode selection — size, type, and coating for different materials
  • Machine settings — amperage, voltage adjustment for various thicknesses
  • Electrode holding and striking — correct grip and arc initiation technique
  • Flat position welding — horizontal beads on flat surfaces with proper technique
  • Vertical position welding — uphill and downhill welding in vertical orientation
  • Overhead position welding — safety and technique for welding above head level
  • Corner and edge preparation — beveling, gap setting for strong joints
  • Bead appearance and quality — color, ripple pattern, and spatter control
  • Common MMA defects — porosity, undercut, overlap, and remedial measures
  • Practice exercises — single pass, multi-pass welds on MS plates
  • Weld testing and inspection — visual inspection methods for Indian standards (IS 1161)

Module 4: TIG & MIG Welding Processes

Week 6-8 | 38 Hours

  • TIG welding basics — tungsten inert gas process and equipment setup
  • TIG electrode types — pure tungsten, thoriated, ceriated for different applications
  • Shielding gases — argon, helium, and mixtures for aluminum and stainless steel
  • TIG torch operation — proper angle, distance, and hand coordination
  • Filler rod selection — size and material matching for Indian applications
  • TIG welding positions — flat, vertical, overhead with foot pedal control
  • MIG welding principle — semi-automatic metal inert gas process
  • MIG gun types and operation — push and pull techniques for different materials
  • Wire feed systems — spool gun, push-pull for aluminum welding
  • MIG shielding gases — CO2, argon-CO2 mixtures for steel and stainless
  • MIG welding positions and travel speed — maintaining consistent bead profile
  • Aluminum welding challenges — porosity prevention and technique modifications
  • Comparison of MMA, TIG, MIG — advantages, disadvantages, and cost-effectiveness in India

Module 5: Fabrication Techniques & Metal Cutting

Week 8-10 | 35 Hours

  • Fabrication definition — assembly of metal components for structures and machines
  • Layout and marking — using templates, measurement, and marking for accuracy
  • Metal cutting methods — oxy-acetylene cutting, plasma cutting, angle grinder
  • Oxy-acetylene torch operation — gas selection, pressure setting, cutting technique
  • Plasma cutting equipment — advantages for stainless steel and non-ferrous metals
  • Angle grinder safety and technique — cutting and grinding metal surfaces
  • Bending and forming — manual and machine methods for metal sheets and bars
  • Drilling and tapping — hole creation and thread making in fabricated parts
  • Assembly techniques — clamping, jigging, and fixture use for accurate alignment
  • Fitment and alignment — checking squareness, straightness, and dimensional accuracy
  • Riveting — traditional and modern riveting methods in Indian shipyards and construction
  • Bolting and fastening — selection and installation of fasteners for structural work

Module 6: Quality Control, Inspection & Career Development

Week 10-12 | 22 Hours

  • Weld quality standards — Indian Standards (IS), BIS requirements for construction
  • Visual inspection methods — surface defect detection and documentation
  • Destructive testing — tensile test, bend test, impact test principles
  • Non-destructive testing introduction — ultrasonic, radiography, dye penetrant basics
  • Measurement and dimensional checking — using vernier, micrometer, height gauge
  • Weld defects classification — cracks, porosity, inclusions, and their causes
  • Defect remediation — re-welding techniques and repair procedures
  • Documentation and record-keeping — maintaining weld logs and inspection reports
  • Entrepreneurship in welding — starting small fabrication workshops in India
  • Job search strategies — resume building, interview preparation for construction sites
  • Continuing education — advanced certifications (ASME, AWS) and skill upgradation
  • Industry trends — automation in welding, digital fabrication, and future opportunities in India

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice & Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on welding and fabrication tasks evaluated on technique, safety, and output quality

Practical Tasks:

  • MMA Welding — Flat Position Bead (15 Marks): Candidate must produce a single-pass bead on mild steel plate (6mm) in flat position with proper penetration, minimal spatter, and uniform appearance
  • TIG Welding — Vertical Position Joint (15 Marks): Candidate must join two aluminum sheets (3mm) in vertical position using TIG process with filler rod, demonstrating control and quality
  • Fabrication & Assembly Task (20 Marks): Candidate must layout, cut, and assemble a simple metal structure (angle bracket or small frame) using multiple techniques with dimensional accuracy within ±2mm

Learning Outcomes

  • Operate welding equipment safely following Indian safety standards and OSHA guidelines
  • Perform MMA, TIG, and MIG welding in multiple positions with acceptable quality
  • Identify and select appropriate metals, electrodes, and gases for different applications
  • Fabricate metal structures using cutting, bending, drilling, and assembly techniques
  • Inspect welds for defects using visual and basic non-destructive testing methods
  • Maintain workshop safety, equipment, and documentation as per Indian industry standards
