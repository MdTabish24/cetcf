Auto AC Technician

📚 Sector: Automobile & Motor Trades 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the skills to diagnose, repair, and maintain automotive air conditioning systems. This hands-on certification program trains you to service all types of vehicle AC systems—from basic repairs to complex refrigerant handling. Perfect for aspiring technicians in India's growing automobile service industry.

This 120-hour foundation course covers complete AC system fundamentals, refrigeration principles, diagnostic techniques, and repair procedures. You'll learn to handle refrigerants safely, identify common faults, perform maintenance, and manage customer interactions. Includes practical training on real vehicles and industry-standard tools used in Indian service centers.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required for foundation level
  • Already working as AC technician? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, voter ID, or driving license)
  • Medical fitness certificate (for handling refrigerants and working in workshops)
  • Applicable for all candidates across India regardless of gender, caste, or religion

Modules

Module 1: Fundamentals of Automotive AC Systems

Week 1 | 15 Hours

  • Introduction to automotive air conditioning — history, importance, and types of AC systems in Indian vehicles
  • Basic refrigeration cycle — evaporator, compressor, condenser, and expansion valve functions explained
  • AC system components — compressor types (reciprocating, rotary), clutch mechanisms, and belt-driven systems
  • Refrigerants used in India — R134a, R22, R410A properties, environmental impact, and handling regulations
  • Cooling capacity and BTU concept — understanding tonnage and cooling requirements for different vehicle sizes
  • Difference between manual and automatic climate control systems — mechanical vs electronic controls
  • Safety protocols for AC work — PPE requirements, pressure gauge handling, and workshop safety rules
  • Introduction to AC system diagram reading — understanding flow charts and component connections
  • Common AC problems in Indian climate — dust clogging, moisture ingress, and seasonal issues

Module 2: AC System Components and Their Functions

Week 2 | 18 Hours

  • Compressor detailed study — working principle, displacement, rpm ratings, and failure signs
  • Condenser and fan assembly — heat rejection process, fan motor types, and thermal switches
  • Evaporator core — cooling process, frost formation, drain system, and cleaning procedures
  • Expansion valve types — orifice tube vs TXV (thermostatic expansion valve) differences and adjustment
  • Receiver-drier and accumulator — moisture absorption, desiccant replacement, and service intervals
  • Hoses and fittings — high-pressure and low-pressure lines, quick couplers, and leak points
  • Clutch and belt system — electromagnetic clutch operation, belt tension, and replacement procedures
  • Blower motor and controls — fan speed regulation, resistor networks, and motor testing methods
  • Cabin air filters — filtration importance, replacement frequency, and impact on cooling efficiency
  • Pressure switches and sensors — cut-off switches, pressure transducers, and electrical connections
  • Sight glass and pressure gauges — identifying system condition visually and through pressure readings
  • AC control panel buttons — function of each button, mode selection, and temperature adjustment mechanisms

Module 3: Diagnostic Techniques and Fault Finding

Week 3 | 16 Hours

  • Pressure gauge set usage — manifold gauge reading, high and low-side pressure interpretation for different vehicles
  • Temperature measurement methods — using thermometer and infrared guns to check evaporator and condenser temperatures
  • Visual inspection checklist — identifying oil leaks, corrosion, loose connections, and physical damage
  • Refrigerant leak detection — UV dye method, electronic leak detector, soap bubble test, and nitrogen pressure testing
  • Electrical testing basics — using multimeter for continuity, voltage, and resistance checks on AC components
  • Compressor clutch testing — checking engagement, gap measurement, and coil resistance verification
  • Blower motor troubleshooting — speed variations, noise diagnosis, and motor replacement indicators
  • Common faults and their symptoms — weak cooling, no cooling, strange noises, water leakage, and bad odors
  • Diagnostic flowcharts — step-by-step troubleshooting approach for different AC problems
  • Customer complaint documentation — recording symptoms, environmental conditions, and usage patterns
  • AC system performance testing — measuring cooling capacity, temperature drop, and compressor cycling
  • Scan tool basics — reading AC-related fault codes and sensor data (where applicable in modern vehicles)

Module 4: Refrigerant Handling and System Servicing

Week 4 | 17 Hours

  • Refrigerant properties and types — vapor pressure, boiling point, density, and miscibility with oils
  • Recovery and recycling regulations in India — EPA guidelines, legal requirements, and environmental compliance
  • AC recovery machine operation — proper connection, recovery procedure, oil separation, and safety shutoffs
  • Vacuum pump usage — evacuation process, micron level measurement, and moisture removal importance
  • Charging procedures — calculating refrigerant quantity, slow charge vs fast charge, and overcharge prevention
  • Oil management in AC systems — type selection (PAG, ester, mineral), quantity measurement, and contamination prevention
  • Nitrogen pressure testing — system integrity check, leak location, and pressure hold procedures
  • Dye injection for leak detection — UV dye application, circulation time, and inspection under UV light
  • Condenser flushing — reverse flushing technique, solvent use, and drying procedures
  • Evaporator cleaning — chemical cleaning, steam cleaning, and biological growth removal
  • Hose replacement procedures — removing old hoses, installing new ones, and pressure testing
  • System sealing techniques — proper connection methods, torque specifications, and leak prevention

Module 5: Repair and Maintenance Procedures

Week 5-6 | 32 Hours

  • Compressor replacement — removal procedure, oil draining, new unit preparation, and installation steps
  • Condenser replacement — radiator removal (if required), fan disconnection, and proper orientation
  • Evaporator replacement — dashboard access, heater core location, and reassembly precautions
  • Expansion valve replacement — identifying type, removal, cleaning, and new unit installation
  • Receiver-drier replacement — location identification, disconnection, and proper orientation installation
  • Hose and fitting replacement — size identification, proper routing, and vibration isolation
  • Clutch repair and replacement — gap adjustment, coil testing, and bearing inspection
  • Belt and pulley maintenance — tension adjustment, alignment checking, and replacement procedures
  • Blower motor replacement — access method, connector types, and speed verification after installation
  • Cabin air filter replacement — location finding, filter direction, and seal verification
  • Electrical connection repairs — wire stripping, crimping, soldering, and insulation techniques
  • AC system flush procedure — when required, method selection, and effectiveness verification
  • Seasonal maintenance checklist — pre-summer and post-winter service requirements for Indian climate

Module 6: Professional Practice and Customer Service

Week 7-8 | 22 Hours

  • Workshop layout and organization — tool arrangement, safety zones, and efficiency optimization
  • Tool and equipment management — proper storage, maintenance, calibration, and inventory control
  • Service documentation — job cards, work orders, and maintenance records for warranty and future reference
  • Quotation preparation — cost estimation, labor charges, spare parts pricing, and customer communication
  • Customer interaction skills — listening to complaints, explaining technical issues simply, and managing expectations
  • Safety and hygiene standards — workshop cleanliness, waste management, and regulatory compliance
  • Quality assurance — final testing procedures, customer handover checklist, and follow-up protocols
  • Troubleshooting complex issues — multi-symptom problems, intermittent faults, and systematic diagnosis approach
  • Warranty and guarantee terms — explaining coverage, documenting service details, and handling claims
  • Time management and efficiency — scheduling jobs, managing multiple vehicles, and meeting deadlines
  • Continuing education needs — staying updated on new AC technologies, vehicle models, and industry standards
  • Entrepreneurship basics — starting own AC service center, business planning, licensing, and customer retention strategies

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks each - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on assessment of AC system diagnosis, servicing, and repair on actual vehicles

Practical Tasks:

  • AC System Pressure Testing and Diagnosis (15 Marks): Candidate must connect manifold gauges, interpret pressure readings, identify system faults, and document findings with proper readings (high-side and low-side pressure, evaporator temperature)
  • Refrigerant Recovery and System Evacuation (12 Marks): Candidate must safely recover refrigerant using recovery machine, perform vacuum evacuation to proper micron level, and demonstrate proper equipment handling with safety protocols
  • AC System Charging and Component Replacement (15 Marks): Candidate must charge AC system with correct refrigerant quantity, replace a major component (compressor, condenser, or evaporator), test system functionality, and verify proper operation
  • Leak Detection and Hose Replacement (8 Marks): Candidate must identify refrigerant leaks using electronic detector or dye method, replace leaking hose/fitting, pressure test connection, and verify no leakage

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand automotive AC system components, their functions, and how they work together to provide cooling
  • Diagnose AC problems accurately using pressure gauges, temperature measurement, and electrical testing techniques
  • Safely handle refrigerants, recover and recycle them according to Indian environmental regulations
  • Perform complete AC system servicing including evacuation, charging, and component replacement
  • Repair and maintain AC systems to manufacturer specifications with proper documentation and quality checks
  • Communicate effectively with customers, provide quotations, and deliver professional service standards
