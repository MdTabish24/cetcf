Hand Block & Screen Printing

📚 Sector: Fashion, Tailoring & Textiles 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month (4 Weeks / 60 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the traditional art of Hand Block Printing and modern Screen Printing techniques in just 4 weeks. Learn to create stunning designs on fabric, from concept to final product, and launch your career in India's thriving textile industry worth ₹5+ lakh crore annually.

This foundation course covers both traditional hand block printing methods and contemporary screen printing processes used in Indian textile manufacturing. You will gain hands-on experience with design creation, block preparation, printing techniques, color mixing, quality control, and finishing processes. Perfect for beginners wanting to enter the textile printing industry or start their own printing business.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required — beginners welcome
  • Already working in textile industry? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid photo ID proof (Aadhar, PAN, Voter ID) required at registration
  • Physical fitness required — standing for long hours, lifting materials (up to 20kg)
  • Color blindness test may be conducted for assessment
  • Applicable to all candidates irrespective of gender, caste, or religion

Modules

Module 1: Introduction to Textile Printing & Industry Overview

Week 1 | 8 Hours

  • History and evolution of textile printing in India — from Ajrakh to modern techniques
  • Types of textile printing — hand block, screen, digital, discharge, and resist printing
  • Indian textile printing hubs — Jaipur, Ahmedabad, Varanasi, and Indigo regions
  • Career opportunities in textile printing industry
  • Safety protocols and workplace hygiene in printing units
  • Understanding fabric types — cotton, silk, linen, synthetic blends and their printability
  • Quality standards and certifications — AATCC, ISO 3175, eco-friendly standards
  • Introduction to color theory and pigment chemistry basics

Module 2: Hand Block Printing Fundamentals

Week 1-2 | 15 Hours

  • Hand block printing process workflow — from design to final product
  • Materials and tools — wooden blocks, cutting tools, mordants, natural dyes, and brushes
  • Design creation and pattern making for block printing
  • Block preparation — carving techniques, wood selection, and seasoning
  • Traditional mordants — alum, tannin, and their application methods
  • Natural dye extraction — indigo, madder, tutty, and pomegranate
  • Hand block printing technique — pressure application, registration, and repeat patterns
  • Troubleshooting common defects — uneven printing, color bleeding, and poor adhesion
  • Finishing processes — drying, steaming, and setting color
  • Cost calculation and pricing for hand block printed products

Module 3: Screen Printing Technology & Setup

Week 2 | 12 Hours

  • Screen printing process overview — mesh, emulsion, and squeegee mechanics
  • Screen mesh types — 100, 120, 150, 200, 300 mesh counts and their applications
  • Emulsion selection — water-based, plastisol, and UV-curable emulsions
  • Screen preparation — degreasing, emulsion coating, and exposure techniques
  • Design separation and color separation for multicolor printing
  • Exposure units and light sources — understanding proper exposure times
  • Ink chemistry — pigment dispersion, binder, and additives
  • Screen printing equipment setup — frames, squeegees, flood bars, and pallet jigs
  • Registration systems — manual, magnetic, and mechanical alignment
  • Screen reclamation and maintenance procedures

Module 4: Color Science & Dye Preparation

Week 2-3 | 10 Hours

  • Color theory — primary, secondary, tertiary colors and color mixing principles
  • Pigment vs dyes — differences and applications in textile printing
  • Natural dyes preparation — indigo fermentation, madder extraction, and tutty processing
  • Synthetic dye categories — acid dyes, direct dyes, reactive dyes, and vat dyes
  • Dye fixation methods — mordanting, steaming, and chemical setting
  • Color fastness testing — light fastness, wash fastness, and crocking tests
  • Shade matching and color standardization techniques
  • Dye waste management and environmental compliance
  • Ink mixing ratios and viscosity adjustment for screen printing
  • Storage and shelf life management of dyes and inks

Module 5: Practical Printing Techniques & Production

Week 3 | 12 Hours

  • Fabric preparation — washing, drying, and pre-treatment processes
  • Hand block printing execution — pressure control, repeat pattern alignment, and speed
  • Screen printing execution — squeegee angle, pressure, and snap-off distance
  • Multicolor printing — registration techniques and color layering
  • Specialty printing techniques — discharge printing, resist printing, and batik methods
  • Print quality assessment — color uniformity, edge definition, and coverage
  • Batch production management — setting up production lines and workflow optimization
  • Handling different fabric weights and textures
  • Waste reduction and fabric optimization techniques
  • Productivity tracking and output standards
  • Health and safety during printing operations — chemical handling and ventilation
  • Documentation and quality records maintenance

Module 6: Post-Printing & Business Skills

Week 3-4 | 13 Hours

  • Drying and curing processes — air drying, steam setting, and heat curing
  • Finishing operations — washing, softening, and pressing
  • Quality control inspection — defect identification and grading standards
  • Packaging and storage of printed textiles
  • Cost analysis — material costs, labor, overhead, and profit margins
  • Pricing strategies for hand block and screen printed products
  • Market trends in Indian textile printing — contemporary designs and customer preferences
  • Starting a small printing unit — space, equipment, and investment requirements
  • Customer communication and order management
  • Export standards and documentation for textile products
  • Digital marketing for textile printing business
  • Sustainable practices and eco-friendly printing methods

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (MCQ and short answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Candidates must demonstrate printing skills and submit completed samples

Practical Tasks:

  • Hand Block Printed Fabric Sample (20 Marks): Candidate must hand block print a 1m x 1m cotton fabric with minimum 3-color repeat pattern showing proper registration, color uniformity, and pattern alignment
  • Screen Printed Fabric Sample (20 Marks): Candidate must screen print a 1m x 1m fabric with 2-color design showing proper emulsion work, squeegee technique, and color separation
  • Color Mixing & Dye Preparation (10 Marks): Candidate must prepare 3 different shades of dye/ink, perform shade matching, and document color fastness observations

Learning Outcomes

  • Design and prepare wooden blocks for hand block printing with proper carving and finishing
  • Execute hand block printing on various fabric types with accurate color registration and pattern alignment
  • Prepare and coat screens with emulsion for screen printing production
  • Perform screen printing with proper squeegee technique, pressure control, and multicolor registration
  • Mix, prepare, and standardize dyes and inks according to required shades with proper documentation
  • Conduct quality control inspections, identify defects, and maintain production standards
  • Calculate production costs, pricing, and manage small-scale printing operations profitably
  • Apply safety protocols, handle chemicals responsibly, and maintain clean work environment
