Indian Cuisine — Professional Cook

📚 Sector: Food, Catering & Hospitality 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks / 180 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the art and science of authentic Indian cooking with hands-on training in traditional and modern techniques. This foundation-level course equips you with professional kitchen skills, food safety knowledge, and recipe mastery to launch a successful career in India's booming food service industry.

This comprehensive 12-week program covers essential Indian culinary techniques, regional cuisine specialties, kitchen management, food safety, and professional cooking practices. Students gain practical experience in preparing North Indian, South Indian, East Indian, and West Indian dishes while learning modern kitchen operations, cost management, and customer service standards used in restaurants, hotels, and catering businesses across India.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior cooking experience required
  • Already working as a cook? Apply for Direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government-issued ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Medical fitness certificate recommended (especially for high-heat kitchen work)
  • Open to all candidates irrespective of gender, caste, religion, or disability
  • Candidates with food allergies should inform trainers during enrollment

Modules

Module 1: Foundation of Indian Cuisine & Kitchen Basics

Week 1-2 | 30 Hours

  • History and evolution of Indian cuisine — regional variations, Mughlai influence, colonial impact, modern Indian food trends
  • Kitchen safety and hygiene standards — FSSAI regulations, personal hygiene, food contamination prevention, pest control
  • Kitchen equipment identification and use — gas stoves, ovens, grinders, mixers, pressure cookers, tandoor, tawa, Indian cooking vessels
  • Knife skills and cutting techniques — julienne, brunoise, chiffonade, dicing, mincing, safe knife handling
  • Basic cooking methods in Indian cuisine — tempering, tadka, bhunao, dum cooking, tandoori, grilling, frying
  • Understanding Indian spices — identification, storage, grinding, toasting, mixing spice blends (masalas)
  • Ingredient sourcing and quality check — selecting fresh vegetables, pulses, rice, meat, fish for Indian cooking
  • Kitchen organization and mise en place — station setup, ingredient arrangement, time management in Indian kitchen
  • Introduction to Indian food culture — festivals, dietary practices, vegetarian and non-vegetarian traditions, regional preferences
  • Cost calculation and food waste management — portion control, leftover management, economic cooking practices

Module 2: North Indian Cuisine Mastery

Week 3-4 | 30 Hours

  • Bread preparation techniques — atta kneading, resting, rolling, tawa cooking for roti, paratha, puri, naan, kulcha
  • Dal and curry foundations — soaking and cooking pulses, tadka preparation, makhani dal, dal makhani, dal fry, chana masala
  • Paneer dishes — paneer making basics, paneer tikka, matar paneer, paneer butter masala, shahi paneer, paneer dosa
  • Tandoori cooking — tandoor operation, marinating techniques, tandoori chicken, tandoori paneer, tandoori fish, seekh kebab
  • Mughlai specialties — biryani preparation (basmati cooking, dum pukht method), nihari, korma, kebab varieties
  • Gravy-based curries — onion base preparation, tomato-based curries, cream-based curries, spice balancing
  • Rice dishes — pulao preparation, fried rice, khichdi, risotto-style Indian rice, biryani variations
  • Street food and appetizers — samosa filling, pakora batter, chaat masala preparation, aloo tikki, spring rolls
  • North Indian vegetarian specialties — rajma, chole bhature, aloo gobi, baingan bharta, mixed vegetables
  • Spice blends for North India — garam masala, chaat masala, tandoori masala, biryani masala, proper grinding and storage
  • Accompaniments and condiments — raita preparation, pickle making, chutney varieties, papad frying
  • Finishing techniques — garnishing, presentation, plating for North Indian dishes, temperature control

Module 3: South Indian Cuisine & Regional Specialties

Week 5-6 | 30 Hours

  • Rice preparation for South Indian cuisine — parboiling, drying, storage, quality assessment of rice varieties
  • Idli and dosa batter — fermentation process, grinding techniques, consistency management, troubleshooting
  • Idli preparation — steaming technique, mold usage, consistency of batter, serving with sambar and chutney
  • Dosa varieties — plain dosa, masala dosa, ghee roast, cheese dosa, paneer dosa, proper pan temperature and technique
  • Sambar preparation — dal selection, spice tempering, vegetable cutting, consistency, regional variations
  • Coconut-based curries — coconut milk extraction, coconut grinding, curry leaf tempering, curry preparation
  • Chutneys and condiments — coconut chutney, mint chutney, tomato chutney, peanut chutney, grinding and storage
  • Uttapam and other breakfast items — batter preparation, topping arrangement, cooking on griddle, vegetable selection
  • Andhra and Telangana specialties — biryani variations, gongura preparations, mirchi ka salan, lentil dishes
  • Kerala cuisine basics — coconut oil usage, fish curries, appam preparation, puttu making, spice profiles
  • Tamil Nadu specialties — rasam preparation, avial, sambhar variations, traditional cooking methods
  • Presentation and service standards — traditional plating, leaf-based serving, portion sizes for South Indian meals

Module 4: East & West Indian Cuisine Plus Seafood Cookery

Week 7-8 | 30 Hours

  • Bengali cuisine fundamentals — mustard oil usage, fish preparation, luchi-alur dom, shorshe ilish, traditional cooking methods
  • Fish and seafood handling — cleaning, descaling, filleting, storage at proper temperature, freshness assessment
  • Fish curry preparation — Bengali fish curry, Goan fish curry, coastal fish preparations, sauce consistency
  • Prawn and shrimp dishes — butter garlic prawns, prawn curry, tandoori prawns, spice balance for seafood
  • Crab and lobster preparations — regional crab curries, butter crab, grilling techniques, presentation
  • Gujarati and Rajasthani cuisine — dal bhati, undhiyu, gatte ki sabzi, bajra rotli, traditional vegetarian preparations
  • Maharashtrian specialties — puran poli, misal pav, batata vada, sol kadhi, regional spice blends
  • Goan cuisine — coconut-based fish curries, pork vindaloo, bebinca, fish recheado, Goan spice profile
  • Street food and snacks — vada pav preparation, pav bhaji masala making, bhel puri assembly, regional variations
  • Seafood marinades and seasonings — ginger-garlic paste, spice rubs, lemon and vinegar usage, timing for marination
  • Cooking seafood without overcooking — temperature management, timing, texture preservation, doneness indicators
  • Regional condiments and side dishes — coconut-based side dishes, vegetable preparations for seafood meals, bread varieties

Module 5: Advanced Cooking Techniques & Menu Planning

Week 9-10 | 30 Hours

  • Pressure cooking mastery — timing for different ingredients, safety protocols, advantages and limitations, modern electric pressure cookers
  • Slow cooking and dum pukht method — heat management, sealed cooking, flavor development, traditional vs modern techniques
  • Grilling and tandoori techniques — temperature control, marinating timing, char management, health benefits
  • Deep frying and shallow frying — oil temperature management, food safety, reuse guidelines per FSSAI, oil degradation
  • Steaming and boiling — water management, salt usage, nutrient retention, timing for different foods
  • Stir-frying and tossing — wok usage, ingredient timing, heat control, maintaining texture and color
  • Sauce and gravy consistency — thickening agents, reduction techniques, emulsification, proper consistency for service
  • Flavor balancing — salt, spice, sour, sweet, bitter, umami balance, taste correction techniques
  • Menu planning for Indian restaurants — seasonal ingredients, cost management, dietary preferences, portion planning
  • Batch cooking for catering — scaling recipes, time management, food holding temperatures, quality maintenance
  • Plating and presentation — color contrast, height variation, sauce application, garnish selection, Indian restaurant standards
  • Recipe standardization — documentation, yield management, consistency across services, training new staff

Module 6: Food Safety, Hygiene & Professional Kitchen Management

Week 11-12 | 30 Hours

  • FSSAI food safety standards — compliance requirements, licensing, inspection procedures, penalties for violations
  • Personal hygiene practices — hand washing, nail care, hair covering, uniform standards, illness reporting protocols
  • Food storage and preservation — refrigeration temperatures, freezing methods, dry storage organization, labeling requirements
  • Cross-contamination prevention — separate cutting boards for vegetables and meat, cleaning procedures, allergen management
  • Pest control and sanitation — pest identification, prevention methods, cleaning schedules, pest control documentation
  • Temperature monitoring — food thermometer usage, cooking temperatures for different foods, holding temperatures, cooling procedures
  • Cleaning and sanitization — chemical safety, sanitizer concentration, equipment cleaning, kitchen deep cleaning schedules
  • Kitchen staff management — delegation, training, quality control, communication in kitchen hierarchy, conflict resolution
  • Inventory management — stock rotation (FIFO), ordering procedures, waste tracking, cost control, supplier relationships
  • Health and safety regulations — fire safety, first aid, emergency procedures, accident reporting, worker compensation
  • Nutrition and dietary requirements — macronutrients in Indian cuisine, dietary restrictions (vegan, gluten-free, diabetic), menu adaptation
  • Professional ethics and customer service — hospitality standards, handling complaints, maintaining confidentiality, teamwork in service

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice and Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on cooking assessment with standardized recipes and evaluation criteria

Practical Tasks:

  • Preparation & Mise en Place (10 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate proper kitchen setup, ingredient preparation, knife skills, and organization for a complete meal service
  • North Indian Dish Preparation (15 Marks): Prepare one assigned North Indian curry with bread (e.g., butter chicken with naan, or paneer makhani with roti) demonstrating technique, flavor balance, and presentation
  • South Indian Dish Preparation (15 Marks): Prepare dosa or idli with sambar and chutney, demonstrating batter consistency, fermentation knowledge, cooking technique, and proper accompaniments
  • Food Safety & Hygiene Compliance (10 Marks): Demonstrate proper food handling, temperature control, sanitation practices, and documentation throughout cooking process

Learning Outcomes

  • Prepare authentic North Indian, South Indian, East Indian, and West Indian dishes using traditional and modern techniques
  • Demonstrate proficiency in knife skills, spice grinding, and advanced cooking methods (tandoori, dum cooking, pressure cooking)
  • Apply FSSAI food safety and hygiene standards in kitchen operations and food handling
  • Plan menus, calculate food costs, manage inventory, and organize kitchen operations efficiently
  • Plate and present Indian dishes professionally according to restaurant standards and customer expectations
  • Work effectively in professional kitchen teams, manage time, and maintain quality during high-volume service
