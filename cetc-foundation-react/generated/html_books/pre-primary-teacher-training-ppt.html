Pre-Primary Teacher Training (PPT)

📚 Sector: Education & Early Childhood 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks / 180 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the art of teaching young children (ages 3-6) with this comprehensive Pre-Primary Teacher Training program. Learn child development, classroom management, activity planning, and modern teaching methods to create nurturing learning environments. This certification opens doors to employment in schools, playgroups, and early childhood centers across India.

This 12-week foundation course equips aspiring educators with essential skills for teaching pre-primary students. You will study child psychology, curriculum design, creative teaching methods, health and safety protocols, and parent communication strategies. The program combines theoretical knowledge with hands-on classroom experience to prepare you for immediate employment in the early childhood education sector.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior experience required - open to all
  • Already working as a teacher? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Medical fitness certificate (basic health check)
  • Applicable for all candidates across India

Modules

Module 1: Child Development & Psychology Fundamentals

Week 1-2 | 30 Hours

  • Stages of child development (Physical, Cognitive, Social, Emotional) — Understanding age-appropriate milestones from 3-6 years
  • Piaget's Theory of Cognitive Development — How young children think and learn at different ages
  • Attachment Theory and Separation Anxiety — Managing emotional needs of pre-primary students
  • Individual differences in learning — Recognizing diverse learning styles and abilities
  • Language development in early childhood — Phonetics, vocabulary building, communication skills
  • Social and emotional development — Building confidence, cooperation, and emotional intelligence
  • Play-based learning theory — Why play is essential for child development
  • Behavioral patterns and common challenges — Managing tantrums, aggression, and shyness

Module 2: Classroom Management & Safe Learning Environment

Week 3-4 | 30 Hours

  • Creating safe and inclusive classroom spaces — Room layout, furniture arrangement, safety standards
  • Health and hygiene protocols — Handwashing, sanitation, disease prevention in pre-primary settings
  • First aid basics for young children — CPR, choking, minor injuries, emergency procedures
  • Behavior management techniques — Positive reinforcement, setting boundaries, conflict resolution
  • Classroom routines and transitions — Morning assembly, lunch time, rest time, dismissal procedures
  • Managing large groups of young children — Attention-getting signals, group activities, individual attention
  • Safety regulations and compliance — NCLP guidelines, staff-child ratios, building safety standards
  • Hygiene standards for food handling — Nutrition, food allergies, dietary requirements
  • Dealing with special needs and inclusive education — Supporting children with developmental delays
  • Parent-teacher communication for behavior issues — Reporting concerns, collaborative problem-solving

Module 3: Curriculum Design & Activity Planning

Week 5-6 | 30 Hours

  • Pre-primary curriculum frameworks — National guidelines, learning outcomes, skill development areas
  • Planning age-appropriate activities — Cognitive, motor, social, and creative activities for 3-6 years
  • Arts and crafts activities — Drawing, painting, collage, sculpture, craft materials and techniques
  • Music and movement activities — Songs, rhymes, dance, physical development through play
  • Storytelling and language activities — Picture books, puppet shows, dramatic play, vocabulary building
  • Numeracy and pre-math concepts — Counting, shapes, patterns, sorting, basic arithmetic foundations
  • Science exploration activities — Nature walks, simple experiments, sensory play, environmental awareness
  • Thematic planning and project-based learning — Creating integrated lesson plans around themes
  • Adapting activities for different learning styles — Visual, auditory, kinesthetic learners
  • Resource management and DIY teaching materials — Creating low-cost, high-impact learning materials

Module 4: Teaching Methods & Interactive Learning Techniques

Week 7-8 | 30 Hours

  • Student-centered learning approaches — Child-led activities, exploration, discovery-based learning
  • Montessori method basics — Prepared environment, self-directed learning, practical life activities
  • Waldorf education principles — Imagination, creativity, holistic development, rhythm and routine
  • Play-based teaching methods — Structured and unstructured play, outdoor learning, free play benefits
  • Questioning techniques for young learners — Open-ended questions, scaffolding, encouraging thinking
  • Demonstration and modeling — Teaching through examples, observational learning, imitation
  • Group discussions and circle time — Building community, sharing experiences, listening skills
  • Use of visual aids and manipulatives — Charts, flashcards, blocks, puzzles for concrete learning
  • Technology integration in pre-primary — Educational apps, videos, interactive boards (age-appropriate use)
  • Differentiated instruction — Modifying activities for advanced and struggling learners

Module 5: Assessment, Progress Tracking & Parent Engagement

Week 9-10 | 30 Hours

  • Observation and assessment methods — Anecdotal records, checklists, rating scales, portfolios
  • Developmental screening tools — Identifying developmental delays, early intervention referrals
  • Progress documentation and record-keeping — Maintaining student files, achievement tracking
  • Reporting to parents — Progress reports, parent-teacher conferences, communicating concerns
  • Building positive parent relationships — Open communication, involvement opportunities, feedback channels
  • Handling difficult parent conversations — Addressing concerns, behavior issues, developmental delays
  • Involving parents in learning — Home activities, reading at home, parental support programs
  • Understanding diverse family structures — Inclusive language, respect for cultural differences
  • Managing expectations and setting realistic goals — Age-appropriate milestones, avoiding pressure
  • Collaboration with specialists — Coordination with speech therapists, psychologists, special educators

Module 6: Professional Development & Early Childhood Education Career

Week 11-12 | 30 Hours

  • Professional ethics and code of conduct — Child protection, confidentiality, professional boundaries
  • Child abuse recognition and reporting — Types of abuse, warning signs, mandatory reporting procedures
  • Stress management and teacher well-being — Burnout prevention, work-life balance, self-care
  • Continuing education and skill development — Pursuing higher qualifications, specialized training
  • Building a portfolio and resume — Documenting achievements, certifications, work samples
  • Job search strategies in early childhood education — School selection, interview preparation, salary negotiation
  • Understanding employment contracts and policies — Salary structures, benefits, working hours, leave policies
  • Starting your own playgroup or daycare — Business planning, licensing, staffing, marketing
  • Staying updated with latest research — Child development trends, new teaching methodologies, policy changes
  • Networking and professional communities — Joining teacher associations, attending workshops, peer learning

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice, Short Answer, Scenario-based) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate classroom readiness through activity planning, teaching demonstration, and classroom management skills assessment

Practical Tasks:

  • Activity Planning & Execution (20 Marks): Design and conduct a 15-minute classroom activity for pre-primary students (ages 3-6). Submit activity plan with learning objectives, materials, and step-by-step instructions. Must demonstrate engagement and classroom management.
  • Teaching Demonstration (15 Marks): Deliver a 10-minute lesson on any subject (language, math, science, or art). Candidate must show use of appropriate teaching methods, visual aids, and student engagement techniques.
  • Classroom Management Case Study (15 Marks): Analyze 2 real-world classroom scenarios (behavior issues, learning difficulties) and provide solutions. Submit written responses showing knowledge of child psychology and management techniques.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand child development stages and apply age-appropriate teaching methods for 3-6 year olds
  • Create safe, inclusive, and hygienic learning environments following regulatory compliance standards
  • Design and execute engaging classroom activities using play-based and student-centered approaches
  • Manage classroom behavior effectively using positive reinforcement and conflict resolution techniques
  • Assess student progress through observation and communicate findings to parents professionally
  • Pursue career advancement in early childhood education sector with professional ethics and continuous learning
