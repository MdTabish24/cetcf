Tile Fitting & Floor Work Technician

📚 Sector: Construction & Building Trades 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Become a skilled Tile Fitting & Floor Work Technician and tap into India's booming construction industry. This 8-week foundation course teaches you professional tile laying, floor preparation, and finishing techniques used across residential and commercial projects. Earn certification recognized by CETCF and start your career with hands-on expertise.

This comprehensive program covers everything from understanding different tile types and floor materials to mastering surface preparation, layout planning, adhesive application, and grouting techniques. You'll learn safety protocols, quality control standards, and professional practices followed on Indian construction sites. Through practical training and real-world projects, you'll be job-ready as a tile fitting technician or start your own flooring business.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (with parental consent if under 18)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience needed; beginners welcome
  • Already working in tile/flooring industry? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required (Aadhar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport) at registration
  • Physical fitness required — ability to work on feet for extended periods and handle tools
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals
  • No upper age limit; career changers and mature workers encouraged to apply

Modules

Module 1: Introduction to Tile Fitting & Floor Work Industry

Week 1 | 15 Hours

  • Overview of construction industry in India — growth, opportunities, and market demand for tile technicians
  • Types of flooring projects — residential, commercial, industrial, and public spaces in Indian context
  • Career pathways — employment, self-employment, and entrepreneurship opportunities in tile and floor work
  • Safety standards and regulations — OSHA guidelines, Indian building codes, and workplace safety protocols
  • Tools and equipment introduction — basic hand tools, power tools, and safety equipment overview
  • Professional ethics and communication — working with supervisors, clients, and team members
  • Cost estimation basics — understanding material costs, labor charges, and project budgeting in India
  • Quality standards — Indian Standards (IS) codes for flooring and tile installation
  • Environmental considerations — sustainable flooring materials and waste management on site

Module 2: Tile Types, Materials & Floor Preparation

Week 2 | 18 Hours

  • Ceramic tiles — glazed, unglazed, and porcelain varieties; sizes, grades, and quality standards
  • Natural stone tiles — marble, granite, slate, and sandstone; characteristics and handling precautions
  • Vitreous tiles — glass and mosaic tiles; applications in kitchens and bathrooms across Indian homes
  • Specialty tiles — anti-slip tiles, digital printed tiles, and decorative tiles used in modern Indian construction
  • Tile grading and selection — understanding ISI marks, quality certifications, and avoiding defective tiles
  • Floor substrate assessment — concrete, cement, wood, and existing tile surfaces; inspection techniques
  • Surface preparation methods — cleaning, leveling, waterproofing, and priming existing surfaces
  • Moisture testing and damp-proofing — identifying moisture issues and applying DPC (damp-proof course)
  • Adhesive types — cement-based, epoxy, and acrylic adhesives; selection based on tile and substrate
  • Tools for preparation — chisels, hammers, grinders, levels, and measuring instruments
  • Safety precautions — dust masks, gloves, and ventilation during surface preparation
  • Documentation — recording surface conditions and preparing work schedules

Module 3: Layout Planning, Cutting & Measuring Techniques

Week 3 | 16 Hours

  • Measurement and marking — using measuring tapes, chalk lines, and laser levels for accurate layout
  • Grid planning — creating balanced tile layouts to minimize cutting and avoid narrow edge pieces
  • Pattern selection — straight, diagonal, brick bond, and herringbone patterns used in Indian homes
  • Calculating tile quantities — estimating material requirements and accounting for wastage (10-15% standard)
  • Centering techniques — ensuring symmetrical layouts in bathrooms, kitchens, and living spaces
  • Edge and corner planning — handling wall-to-wall transitions and external corners professionally
  • Tile cutting tools — manual cutters, wet saws, and angle grinders; safety and maintenance
  • Cutting techniques — straight cuts, diagonal cuts, L-shaped cuts, and notching for pipes and fixtures
  • Handling delicate tiles — cutting marble, granite, and glass tiles without damage or chipping
  • Creating templates — using cardboard and paper templates for complex layouts and curved surfaces
  • Waste management — recycling cut tiles and disposing of tile waste responsibly
  • Quality checks — ensuring cuts are straight, smooth, and dimensionally accurate

Module 4: Tile Installation & Adhesive Application Methods

Week 4-5 | 25 Hours

  • Adhesive mixing — correct water-to-powder ratios, consistency, and proper mixing techniques
  • Trowel selection — understanding notch sizes (6mm, 8mm, 10mm) based on tile size and type
  • Trowel application methods — flat-bed and ridge-and-furrow techniques for full tile coverage
  • Tile pressing technique — applying consistent pressure to ensure proper adhesive contact and level surface
  • Spacer usage — maintaining uniform grout lines (3mm, 5mm, 10mm) for professional appearance
  • Vertical tile installation — techniques for walls, ensuring tiles don't slide or shift downward
  • Horizontal floor installation — working systematically from center outward or corner-to-corner
  • Large format tiles — special handling for tiles larger than 60cm x 60cm; back-buttering technique
  • Thin-set vs. thick-set application — understanding when to use each method in Indian construction
  • Moisture barrier application — using waterproofing membranes in bathrooms and wet areas
  • Checking for lippage — ensuring tiles are level and aligned; using laser levels and straightedges
  • Adhesive curing time — understanding drying periods before grouting (typically 24-48 hours)
  • Problem-solving — addressing hollow spots, uneven surfaces, and adhesive failure
  • Safety during installation — proper ventilation, handling heavy tiles, and preventing repetitive strain injuries

Module 5: Grouting, Sealing & Finishing Work

Week 6 | 18 Hours

  • Grout types — cement-based, epoxy, and urethane grouts; selecting appropriate grout for different applications
  • Grout color selection — matching with tile colors and understanding color variations in Indian tiles
  • Grout mixing — achieving correct consistency (like peanut butter) for proper application
  • Grouting application — using grout floats at 45-degree angles to fill joints completely
  • Joint width management — maintaining consistent 3-5mm joints for residential and 5-10mm for decorative work
  • Excess grout removal — timing and technique for removing excess without damaging joints
  • Grout curing and drying — understanding drying times and protecting newly grouted surfaces
  • Sealing grout joints — applying sealers in kitchens and bathrooms to prevent staining and water damage
  • Tile sealing — protecting porous tiles (marble, granite, sandstone) from moisture and stains
  • Cleaning after grouting — using sponges, cloths, and appropriate cleaning agents for final finish
  • Caulking corners — using flexible caulk at wall-to-wall and wall-to-floor junctions for movement
  • Final quality inspection — checking for uniform grout lines, color consistency, and surface cleanliness
  • Touch-up work — addressing chips, cracks, and grout deficiencies before project completion
  • Documentation and handover — preparing project completion reports and client instructions for maintenance

Module 6: Safety, Quality Control & Professional Practices

Week 7-8 | 28 Hours

  • Occupational health and safety — identifying hazards, preventing falls, and managing physical strain in tile work
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE) — proper use of helmets, gloves, masks, eye protection, and safety boots
  • Tool safety ��� safe handling and maintenance of hand tools, power tools, and cutting equipment
  • Chemical safety — handling adhesives, sealers, and cleaning chemicals; understanding SDS (Safety Data Sheets)
  • Scaffold and ladder safety — working at heights safely and securely during wall tiling projects
  • First aid and emergency procedures — basic first aid for cuts, chemical burns, and workplace injuries
  • Quality standards in India — following IS codes (Indian Standards) for flooring and tile work
  • Defect identification — recognizing common installation defects (hollow spots, lippage, cracking)
  • Troubleshooting common problems — addressing tile cracking, grout failure, and adhesive issues
  • Material testing — checking tile quality, adhesive strength, and grout durability before and after application
  • Client communication — explaining work progress, addressing concerns, and managing expectations
  • Documentation practices — maintaining site records, material invoices, and work completion certificates
  • Cost management — tracking material usage, labor time, and project profitability
  • Sustainable practices — minimizing waste, recycling materials, and using eco-friendly products
  • Continuing education — staying updated with new tile products, techniques, and building regulations in India
  • Building professional reputation — delivering quality work, meeting deadlines, and ensuring customer satisfaction
  • Insurance and legal compliance — understanding contractor liability, GST registration, and labor laws
  • Business development — building client networks, managing multiple projects, and scaling your tile business

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks each (Multiple Choice, Short Answer, and Descriptive) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on tile installation and floor work demonstration on actual or simulated surfaces under supervision

Practical Tasks:

  • Surface Preparation & Layout (15 Marks): Candidate must prepare a concrete/cement surface, check for levelness, apply waterproofing, and create accurate tile layout with proper centering and pattern planning
  • Tile Cutting & Shaping (10 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate cutting 10-12 tiles using manual cutter and wet saw, including straight cuts, diagonal cuts, and notches for fixtures; all cuts must be clean and accurate
  • Tile Installation (15 Marks): Candidate must install minimum 2 square meters of tiles on prepared surface using correct adhesive, proper trowel technique, spacers, and achieving level surface with proper joint alignment
  • Grouting & Finishing (10 Marks): Candidate must grout installed tiles with correct grout consistency, fill joints completely, remove excess grout cleanly, and apply sealer; final surface must be clean and professional

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and select appropriate tile types, materials, and adhesives based on project requirements and Indian building standards
  • Prepare floor and wall surfaces correctly, including leveling, waterproofing, and damp-proofing techniques
  • Plan tile layouts using measurement, marking, and pattern selection to minimize waste and ensure professional appearance
  • Install tiles using proper trowel techniques, spacing, and adhesive application to achieve level, aligned surfaces
  • Apply grouting and sealing techniques to complete flooring projects with durability and aesthetic quality
  • Implement safety protocols, quality control measures, and professional practices in accordance with Indian regulations and industry standards
