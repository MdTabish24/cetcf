Boutique & Tailoring Business Management

📚 Sector: Fashion, Tailoring & Textiles 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the complete business of running a successful boutique and tailoring enterprise in India. Learn from fabric selection to customer management, costing to quality control, and digital marketing to financial planning. This foundation course equips you with practical skills to launch your own boutique or manage an existing one profitably.

This comprehensive 2-month program covers boutique setup, tailoring operations, business management, and entrepreneurship. You'll learn fabric knowledge, garment construction basics, pricing strategies, inventory management, customer service, and digital marketing specific to the Indian fashion retail market. Perfect for aspiring boutique owners, tailors wanting to expand, and fashion entrepreneurs.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass
  • No prior experience needed
  • Currently working in tailoring/fashion? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid photo ID proof required (Aadhar, voter ID, driving license, passport)
  • Open to all candidates irrespective of gender, caste, religion
  • For online training: Basic internet access and smartphone/computer required

Modules

Module 1: Boutique Business Fundamentals & Startup Planning

Week 1-2 | 20 Hours

  • Boutique business models in India — retail, custom tailoring, semi-ready, online hybrid models
  • Market research and customer analysis — identifying target segments in your locality
  • Business registration and legal compliance — GST, Shop Act, labor laws, FSSAI if applicable
  • Location selection and store layout — foot traffic analysis, rent negotiation, space optimization
  • Startup investment planning — initial capital requirements, equipment costs, inventory budgeting
  • Business plan development — mission, vision, 3-year projection for Indian boutique context
  • Licensing and permits — municipal corporation, fire safety, labor department requirements
  • Competitive analysis — studying local competitors, pricing benchmarks, unique selling propositions
  • Supplier identification and vendor management — fabric wholesalers, trims, embroidery sources
  • Insurance and risk management — shop insurance, product liability, staff welfare compliance

Module 2: Fabric Knowledge & Garment Construction Basics

Week 2-3 | 18 Hours

  • Indian fabric types and their properties — cotton, silk, wool, synthetic blends, khadi, handlooms
  • Seasonal fabric selection — summer wear (cotton, linen), winter (wool, heavy cotton), monsoon
  • Fabric quality assessment — thread count, weight, shrinkage, color fastness, durability testing
  • Traditional Indian textiles — saree, salwar kameez, kurta fabrics, regional specialties
  • Western garment fabrics — formal wear, casual, stretch fabrics, suiting and shirting materials
  • Garment construction fundamentals — stitching types, seam finishes, hemming, button attachment
  • Pattern basics and measurement — taking accurate body measurements, standard sizing charts
  • Common garment defects and quality checks — loose stitches, color bleeding, shrinkage issues
  • Fabric care and maintenance — washing instructions, storage, pest prevention for boutiques
  • Embellishment and trim knowledge — laces, beads, buttons, zippers, embroidery materials
  • Cost calculation per garment — fabric wastage percentage, labor hours, overhead allocation
  • Seasonal and trend forecasting — understanding Indian fashion seasons and customer preferences

Module 3: Tailoring Operations & Quality Management

Week 3-4 | 22 Hours

  • Tailoring workshop setup — equipment needs, sewing machines, pressing tools, storage systems
  • Production planning and scheduling — order management, delivery timelines, rush order handling
  • Fitting and alteration services — common alterations, pricing, customer communication
  • Quality control procedures — inspection at cutting, stitching, finishing stages, final check
  • Garment fitting standards — bust, waist, hip, length measurements, regional fit preferences
  • Common tailoring problems and solutions — tight seams, puckering, hem issues, zipper problems
  • Workforce management — hiring tailors, skill assessment, training, productivity tracking
  • Standard operating procedures (SOPs) — documentation, order tracking, delivery checklists
  • Customization and bespoke tailoring — design consultation, fabric selection guidance, fittings
  • Speed vs. quality balance — managing customer expectations on turnaround time
  • Safety standards in tailoring — needle safety, machine maintenance, fire safety, ventilation
  • Waste management and sustainability — fabric scraps utilization, eco-friendly practices

Module 4: Pricing, Costing & Financial Management

Week 4-5 | 19 Hours

  • Cost structure understanding — fixed costs (rent, salary), variable costs (fabric, trims), overhead
  • Garment costing formula — material cost + labor cost + overhead + profit margin calculation
  • Pricing strategies for Indian market — competitive pricing, value-based, psychological pricing
  • Margin calculation and profitability — gross margin, net margin, break-even analysis
  • Labor costing — per-piece rates, hourly wages, productivity-linked incentives for tailors
  • Bulk order pricing and discounts — wholesale rates, seasonal discounts, loyalty programs
  • Inventory valuation — FIFO, LIFO methods, stock turnover ratio, dead stock identification
  • Financial record-keeping — daily cash flow, expense tracking, profit & loss statements
  • GST compliance and tax planning — invoice generation, tax filing, input tax credit management
  • Banking and payment systems — shop banking, online payments, customer credit management
  • Working capital management — cash flow forecasting, supplier credit terms, customer receivables
  • Budgeting and financial projections — monthly targets, quarterly reviews, annual planning

Module 5: Customer Service, Sales & Digital Marketing

Week 5-7 | 28 Hours

  • Customer service excellence — greeting, listening, problem-solving, complaint handling
  • Sales techniques — product knowledge, upselling, cross-selling, closing techniques
  • Customer relationship management (CRM) — maintaining customer database, follow-ups, loyalty
  • Handling difficult customers — managing expectations, refund policies, dispute resolution
  • Feedback collection and service improvement — suggestion boxes, customer surveys, reviews
  • Whatsapp and social media engagement — customer communication, order updates, promotions
  • Instagram marketing for boutiques — product photography, carousel posts, reels, hashtag strategy
  • Facebook business page management — community building, customer engagement, targeted ads
  • Google My Business optimization — local SEO, customer reviews, business information accuracy
  • Online ordering and delivery systems — integrating WhatsApp orders, payment gateways, logistics
  • Email marketing basics — customer newsletters, seasonal promotions, order confirmations
  • Influencer collaboration and local partnerships — tie-ups with fashion bloggers, event sponsorships
  • Seasonal marketing campaigns — Diwali, wedding season, summer collection launches
  • Photography and styling tips — product presentation, fitting room aesthetics, visual merchandising

Module 6: Inventory Management, Compliance & Business Growth

Week 7-8 | 13 Hours

  • Inventory management system — stock tracking, reorder points, inventory software basics
  • Supplier relationship management — negotiation, quality agreements, payment terms, communication
  • Stock rotation and seasonal management — planning for seasons, clearance sales, new arrivals
  • Shrinkage and loss prevention — theft prevention, damage control, storage best practices
  • Fabric and trim procurement — bulk buying strategies, seasonal stock planning, supplier diversification
  • Labor compliance and employee management — wages, working hours, attendance, skill development
  • Health and safety standards — ventilation, lighting, ergonomics, fire safety certifications
  • Environmental and social responsibility — waste reduction, fair wages, community engagement
  • Business expansion strategies — opening second outlet, online store, franchise model possibilities
  • Scaling operations — increasing production capacity, hiring staff, supplier network expansion
  • Technology adoption — point of sale (POS) systems, inventory software, online presence
  • Continuous learning and trend updates — fashion workshops, industry associations, networking

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions × 2 Marks (Multiple Choice & Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on boutique operations and business case study demonstration

Practical Tasks:

  • Boutique Business Plan Development (15 Marks): Candidate must create a complete business plan for a hypothetical boutique including market analysis, startup costs, pricing strategy, and 3-month financial projection
  • Fabric Quality Assessment & Costing (15 Marks): Identify fabric types, assess quality parameters, and calculate garment cost with profit margin for 3 different garments
  • Customer Service & Sales Simulation (10 Marks): Demonstrate customer interaction, product recommendation, handling objection, and closing a sale through role-play scenario
  • Digital Marketing Content Creation (10 Marks): Create social media posts (Instagram/Facebook) with product photography, captions, and hashtag strategy for boutique promotion

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand boutique business models and startup planning specific to Indian market conditions
  • Identify and assess different fabric types, quality parameters, and seasonal suitability
  • Calculate accurate garment costs and apply profit margins for sustainable business operations
  • Manage tailoring operations with quality control and workforce coordination
  • Apply digital marketing strategies and customer service techniques for boutique growth
  • Develop business expansion plans and implement compliance with legal and safety standards
