AC & Refrigeration Technician (HVAC)

📚 Sector: Electrical & Electronics Trades 🎓 Level: Intermediate ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks / 180 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the skills of air conditioning and refrigeration systems maintenance with this industry-focused HVAC technician course. Learn to install, repair, and maintain AC units, refrigerators, and commercial cooling systems used across Indian homes, offices, and industries. Become job-ready in just 12 weeks with hands-on training and certification recognized nationwide.

This intermediate-level course covers complete HVAC fundamentals including refrigeration cycles, electrical connections, system diagnostics, and troubleshooting. You will gain practical experience in servicing window ACs, split ACs, refrigerators, deep freezers, and commercial cooling units commonly used in India. The program combines classroom learning with real workshop practice to prepare you for immediate employment or self-employment in the booming HVAC sector.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (16 years preferred for better job placement)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (Class 10th preferred)
  • No prior experience needed — beginners welcome
  • Already working in HVAC field? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required — Aadhaar, PAN, or Passport at registration
  • Physically fit — able to work at heights and in confined spaces
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and foreign nationals with valid visa
  • No age upper limit — career changers welcome

Modules

Module 1: Fundamentals of Refrigeration & AC Systems

Week 1-2 | 30 Hours

  • Introduction to refrigeration and air conditioning — basic concepts, history, and applications in Indian market
  • Refrigeration cycle (Carnot cycle, vapour compression cycle) — understanding how cooling systems work
  • Properties of refrigerants — R22, R32, R410A, R134A, environmental impact and safety regulations
  • Heat and temperature basics — BTU, TR (Tonnage of Refrigeration), capacity calculations for Indian climate
  • Pressure-temperature relationships — saturation pressure, superheating, subcooling concepts
  • Components of AC and refrigeration systems — compressor, condenser, evaporator, expansion device functions
  • Types of air conditioning systems — window AC, split AC, central AC, VRF systems used in India
  • Indian HVAC market overview — residential, commercial, industrial applications and growth opportunities
  • Safety regulations and environmental standards — CFC phase-out, ozone layer protection, energy efficiency norms
  • Introduction to tools and equipment — manifold gauge set, vacuum pump, charging cylinder, leak detector basics

Module 2: Electrical Systems & Wiring for HVAC Units

Week 3-4 | 30 Hours

  • AC electrical fundamentals — voltage, current, power, single-phase vs three-phase systems in India
  • Wiring diagrams and circuit symbols — reading AC unit schematics, Indian electrical standards (IS 1866)
  • Capacitors in AC systems — starting capacitors, running capacitors, function and testing methods
  • Compressor motor protection — thermal overload, current limiting devices, soft starters
  • Contactor and relay operations — magnetic contactors, time-delay relays, function verification
  • Thermostat and temperature control devices — mechanical and electronic thermostats, sensor types
  • Wiring connections for split AC units — indoor unit, outdoor unit, copper wire sizing for Indian installations
  • Safety switches and disconnects — main switch, isolator, emergency stop, proper installation practices
  • Troubleshooting electrical faults — no power, motor not running, compressor issues diagnosis
  • Energy efficiency and power consumption — understanding star ratings, reducing electricity bills for customers
  • Indian electrical code compliance ��� earthing, grounding, MCB/ELCB requirements for AC installations
  • Common electrical problems in Indian climate — humidity effects, voltage fluctuations, rust and corrosion

Module 3: Window AC & Split AC Installation & Service

Week 5-6 | 30 Hours

  • Window AC anatomy — compressor location, condenser coil, evaporator coil, air filter, drain system
  • Window AC installation procedure — wall preparation, bracket fixing, sealing, leveling, testing
  • Split AC anatomy — indoor unit (wall-mounted/cassette), outdoor unit, copper piping, electrical connections
  • Split AC installation — outdoor unit placement, copper pipe routing, vacuum procedure, gas charging
  • Refrigerant charging methods — charging by weight, charging by superheat method, Indian standard procedures
  • Copper piping — sizing, bending, flaring, brazing, leak testing, insulation wrapping for Indian climate
  • Vacuum procedure — importance, vacuum pump operation, evacuation time, moisture removal techniques
  • Cleaning and maintenance — filter cleaning, coil cleaning, drain pipe clearing, seasonal servicing
  • Coolant top-up and recharging — identifying refrigerant leaks, safe handling, recovery procedures
  • Noise and vibration issues — compressor isolation, pipe support, common causes and solutions
  • Common AC problems — cooling not sufficient, water leakage, strange noises, ice formation diagnosis
  • After-sales service and customer care — warranty, service contracts, customer communication in Indian context

Module 4: Refrigerator & Deep Freezer Servicing

Week 7-8 | 30 Hours

  • Refrigerator components — compressor, condenser, evaporator, thermostat, defrost system, door seal
  • Refrigerator working principle — cooling cycle, thermostat operation, defrosting mechanisms
  • Types of refrigerators — top freezer, bottom freezer, side-by-side, frost-free, non-frost-free models
  • Deep freezer servicing — commercial vs home freezers, temperature maintenance, ice buildup prevention
  • Defrost systems — manual defrost, automatic defrost, timer-based defrost, hot gas defrost
  • Evaporator coil frost issues — causes, prevention, cleaning techniques for Indian humid climate
  • Condenser coil maintenance — dust accumulation, cooling fan operation, efficiency improvement
  • Refrigerant leaks in refrigerators — detection methods, repair, safe refrigerant handling
  • Thermostat replacement and adjustment — temperature setting, sensor cleaning, calibration
  • Door seal and gasket replacement — checking for leaks, replacement procedure, energy efficiency
  • Compressor problems — weak cooling, not starting, overheating, noise issues diagnosis
  • Water drainage system — drain pipe cleaning, water collection tray maintenance, mold prevention

Module 5: Diagnostic Tools, Testing & Troubleshooting

Week 9-10 | 30 Hours

  • Manifold gauge set operation — reading high-side and low-side pressure, temperature correlation
  • Digital multimeter usage — voltage testing, resistance measurement, continuity testing, safety procedures
  • Refrigerant leak detection — soap bubble method, electronic leak detector, UV dye method, safety precautions
  • Vacuum pump operation — evacuation procedure, vacuum levels, moisture removal from systems
  • Charging cylinder and weighing scales — accurate refrigerant measurement, weight-based charging method
  • Thermometer usage — digital thermometers, infrared thermometers, temperature differential measurement
  • Pressure and temperature charts — understanding P-T charts, superheat calculation, subcooling calculation
  • Compressor performance testing — current draw measurement, discharge temperature, efficiency check
  • Electrical testing — capacitor testing, compressor winding resistance, contactor operation verification
  • System blockage detection — partial blockage symptoms, filter-drier replacement, system flushing
  • Oil analysis and contamination — oil color, moisture content, acid levels, replacement procedures
  • Practical troubleshooting cases — real-world Indian AC problems, step-by-step diagnosis methodology

Module 6: Safety, Environmental Compliance & Entrepreneurship

Week 11-12 | 30 Hours

  • Refrigerant safety — handling R22, R32, R410A safely, toxicity, flammability, proper storage in India
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE) — safety goggles, gloves, apron, respiratory protection for refrigerants
  • Workplace safety — electrical hazards, pressure hazards, burn prevention, first aid for refrigerant exposure
  • Environmental regulations in India — Montreal Protocol, CFC phase-out, R22 ban implementation, penalties
  • Refrigerant recovery and recycling — recovery machine operation, proper disposal, certification requirements
  • Energy efficiency standards — BEE (Bureau of Energy Efficiency) ratings, SEER values, customer education
  • Indian HVAC certification standards — CETCF, NSDC, NCVT requirements, maintaining certification
  • Starting an AC servicing business — business registration, licensing, insurance, customer acquisition strategies
  • Pricing and cost estimation — labor charges, spare parts margin, competitive pricing in Indian market
  • Customer relationship management — service quality, warranty management, building repeat business
  • Advanced skills development — central AC systems, commercial HVAC, building management systems (BMS)
  • Continuing education and skill upgradation — staying updated with new refrigerants, IoT-enabled AC systems

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice & Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on demonstration of AC/refrigerator servicing, installation, and troubleshooting skills

Practical Tasks:

  • Split AC Installation & Evacuation (15 Marks): Candidate must complete proper installation of split AC unit including copper pipe routing, vacuum procedure, and pressure testing with correct documentation
  • Refrigerant Charging & System Testing (15 Marks): Safe refrigerant charging using weight method or superheat method, pressure verification, temperature differential measurement, and system performance validation
  • Electrical Troubleshooting & Repair (10 Marks): Diagnose and repair electrical faults using multimeter, capacitor testing, wiring connection verification, and safety compliance check
  • Refrigerator/Deep Freezer Servicing (10 Marks): Complete servicing of refrigerator including thermostat check, defrost system verification, coil cleaning, and leak detection

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand refrigeration cycles and thermodynamic principles applicable to AC and refrigeration systems
  • Install, service, and maintain window AC and split AC units following Indian safety standards
  • Diagnose and troubleshoot electrical and mechanical faults in HVAC systems using proper tools
  • Safely handle refrigerants, perform evacuation, and charge systems accurately using weight and superheat methods
  • Service refrigerators and deep freezers including defrost systems, thermostat adjustment, and leak detection
  • Start and manage an AC servicing business with proper compliance, customer service, and entrepreneurial skills
