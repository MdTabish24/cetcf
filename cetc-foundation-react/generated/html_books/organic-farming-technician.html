Organic Farming Technician

📚 Sector: Agriculture & Rural Livelihood 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the science and practice of organic farming with this hands-on certification course. Learn sustainable agricultural techniques, soil management, natural pest control, and organic crop production methods that are in high demand across rural India. Become a skilled organic farming technician and help farmers transition to profitable, chemical-free agriculture.

This foundation-level course equips you with practical knowledge of organic farming principles, certification processes, and modern sustainable techniques. You will learn soil testing, composting, natural fertilizers, organic pest management, crop rotation, and farm business planning. The curriculum emphasizes real-world application with field visits, hands-on demonstrations, and business skills for Indian agricultural contexts.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior farming experience required
  • Interested in agriculture and sustainable farming
  • Already working as a farmer? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid photo ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, voter ID, or PAN)
  • Applicable for all candidates including women, SC/ST, and differently-abled persons
  • No upper age limit — career changers welcome

Modules

Module 1: Fundamentals of Organic Farming & Soil Science

Week 1-2 | 20 Hours

  • Introduction to organic farming — definition, principles, and benefits in Indian agriculture
  • Difference between organic and conventional farming — yield, cost, and market advantages
  • Soil composition and structure — sand, silt, clay, and organic matter importance
  • Soil testing methods — pH testing, nutrient analysis, and interpretation of results
  • Soil health indicators — microbial activity, earthworm presence, and water retention
  • Soil conservation techniques — mulching, contour farming, and terrace management
  • Organic certification requirements in India — NPOP standards and documentation
  • Environmental benefits of organic farming — groundwater, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration

Module 2: Organic Manures, Composts & Natural Fertilizers

Week 2-3 | 18 Hours

  • Types of organic manures — farmyard manure, poultry manure, and green manure crops
  • Composting methods — pit composting, heap composting, and vermicomposting techniques
  • Vermicomposting setup and management — worm selection, bedding, and harvesting
  • Biofertilizers — nitrogen-fixing bacteria, phosphate-solubilizing microbes, and application
  • Preparation of liquid manures — panchagavya, jeevamrit, and microbial consortia
  • Neem-based fertilizers and soil amendments — neem cake, neem oil applications
  • Seaweed and plant-based extracts — preparation and nutrient content for organic farms
  • Nutrient management in organic systems — nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium cycling
  • Cost-benefit analysis of organic fertilizers — economical options for small farmers
  • Quality standards for organic manures — moisture content, nutrient levels, and testing

Module 3: Organic Pest & Disease Management

Week 3-4 | 19 Hours

  • Common pests in Indian crops — identification, lifecycle, and damage assessment
  • Organic pest control methods — cultural practices, mechanical control, and bio-agents
  • Beneficial insects and predators — ladybugs, spiders, parasitoid wasps, and conservation
  • Preparation of botanical pesticides — neem spray, garlic-chilli solution, and soap water
  • Microbial pest control — Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt), Beauveria, and Trichoderma applications
  • Common crop diseases — powdery mildew, leaf spot, blight, and wilt identification
  • Organic disease management — sulfur dusting, bordeaux mixture, and trichoderma use
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM) in organic farming — monitoring, thresholds, and decision-making
  • Trap crops and intercropping for pest control — marigold, sunflower, and legume integration
  • Record-keeping for pest and disease management — farm notebooks and documentation

Module 4: Organic Crop Production & Crop Rotation Systems

Week 4-5 | 20 Hours

  • Organic production of cereals — wheat, rice, maize, and millet cultivation techniques
  • Organic production of pulses — chickpea, moong, arhar, and nitrogen-fixing benefits
  • Organic vegetable production — tomato, potato, onion, brinjal, and leafy vegetables
  • Organic spice cultivation — turmeric, chilli, cumin, and coriander organic methods
  • Seed selection and germination testing — certified organic seeds and preservation
  • Crop rotation planning — 2-year, 3-year, and 4-year rotation systems for soil health
  • Legume integration in crop rotation — benefits for nitrogen fixation and soil improvement
  • Cover crops and green manuring — mustard, sunhemp, and dhaincha as soil enrichers
  • Intercropping systems — coconut-cacao, sugarcane-legume, and vegetable combinations
  • Seasonal planning and crop calendar — monsoon, winter, and summer crop selection for regions
  • Yield estimation and record management — documentation for certification and market claims

Module 5: Organic Certification, Market Access & Farm Economics

Week 5-6 | 18 Hours

  • NPOP (National Programme for Organic Production) certification process and standards
  • Organic certification bodies in India — APEDA, IFOAM-accredited agencies, and procedures
  • Documentation requirements for certification — land history, input records, and harvest data
  • Conversion period from conventional to organic — 2-3 year transition and financial planning
  • Organic farming groups and cluster certification — benefits and collective approach
  • Organic market opportunities in India — premium pricing, direct selling, and export potential
  • Organic product labeling and branding — FSSAI compliance and consumer trust building
  • Supply chain management — collection, storage, processing, and distribution channels
  • Farm economics in organic system — cost analysis, break-even point, and profitability
  • Government schemes and subsidies — PMKSY, Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) benefits
  • Direct selling platforms — farmers markets, online platforms, and cooperative marketing
  • Record-keeping for farm management — input-output registers, production logs, and audits

Module 6: Practical Field Work & Entrepreneurship Skills

Week 6-8 | 25 Hours

  • Field visit to organic farms — observation of composting, pest management, and crop systems
  • Hands-on composting demonstration — pit preparation, layering, turning, and harvesting
  • Soil testing practical — collection, preparation, and interpretation of test results
  • Preparation of botanical pesticides — neem spray, soap solution, and fermented plant extracts
  • Identification of beneficial and harmful insects — field collection and specimen observation
  • Crop monitoring and scouting techniques — pest threshold assessment and damage recording
  • Setting up demonstration plots — layout design, crop selection, and management practices
  • Vermicomposting unit setup — bin construction, worm introduction, and maintenance
  • Organic farm planning project — design a 1-hectare organic farm with rotation and economics
  • Entrepreneurship in organic farming — business plan, target market, and financial projections
  • Communication and farmer training skills — extension methods and knowledge sharing
  • Certification documentation practice — preparing records for organic certification audit

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ) x 2 Marks each - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Candidates must complete three practical assessment items demonstrating field skills and knowledge application

Practical Tasks:

  • Compost Preparation & Soil Testing (20 Marks): Candidate must prepare a composting pit or vermicomposting unit, demonstrate proper layering, and conduct a soil pH test with interpretation. Submit photographic documentation and test results.
  • Botanical Pesticide Preparation & Pest Identification (15 Marks): Prepare a batch of neem spray or garlic-chilli solution following organic standards. Identify 5 common pests and 5 beneficial insects from field samples or images. Submit preparation notes and identification chart.
  • Organic Farm Management Project (15 Marks): Design a 1-hectare organic farm layout including crop rotation plan, composting system, pest management strategy, and financial projections. Submit detailed farm plan with diagrams and cost-benefit analysis.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand organic farming principles, soil science, and certification standards applicable in India
  • Prepare and manage organic manures, composts, and biofertilizers for sustainable crop production
  • Identify and manage pests and diseases using organic and biological methods without synthetic chemicals
  • Plan and implement crop rotation systems and intercropping for soil health and productivity
  • Access organic market opportunities, navigate certification processes, and manage farm economics
  • Conduct field demonstrations, prepare documentation, and guide farmers in organic farming adoption
