GST Practitioner & Billing Professional

📚 Sector: Business, Retail & Finance 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master GST compliance, billing systems, and tax documentation to become a skilled GST Practitioner. This course trains you in India's modern taxation system, making you job-ready for retail, e-commerce, and corporate sectors. Learn practical billing software, invoice management, and GST filing procedures used in Indian businesses daily.

This foundation-level course covers GST fundamentals, billing software operations, tax calculation methods, and compliance procedures essential for modern Indian businesses. You'll gain hands-on experience with billing systems, understand GST slabs and exemptions, create proper invoices, and manage tax records. The course prepares you for entry-level roles in retail stores, corporate offices, accounting firms, and e-commerce companies across India.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (with parental consent) or 18 years (independent)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior GST or billing experience needed — course is for beginners
  • Already working in retail/billing? Apply for Direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required: Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, or Voter ID
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and foreign nationals with valid visa
  • No medical fitness certificate required
  • Candidates with disability eligible under equal opportunity provisions

Modules

Module 1: GST Fundamentals & Indian Tax System

Week 1-2 | 15 Hours

  • History of GST in India — Why GST replaced old tax system (VAT, Excise, Service Tax). How GST unified Indian taxation since July 2017.
  • GST structure and types — Understanding CGST (Central GST), SGST (State GST), IGST (Integrated GST), and Cess. Real examples of how taxes work on Rs 100 product.
  • Difference between direct and indirect taxes — What is direct tax (Income Tax) vs indirect tax (GST). Why businesses collect and deposit GST.
  • GST registration process in India — Who must register, online registration steps at www.gst.gov.in, documents needed (PAN, Aadhaar, Shop address proof).
  • GST slabs in India — 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, 28% rates explained with common product examples (medicines, clothes, electronics, luxury items).
  • Taxable and non-taxable goods — Which items have GST, which are exempted (basic food, education, healthcare), importance for billing accuracy.
  • Input Tax Credit (ITC) concept — How businesses reduce tax by claiming credit on purchased goods. Real scenario: buying Rs 1000 stock with GST.
  • GST registration certificate and GSTIN — What is GSTIN (15-digit number), how to find it, importance in invoicing and business credibility.
  • Difference between B2B, B2C, and B2G transactions — Business-to-Business, Business-to-Consumer, Business-to-Government transactions and their GST treatment.
  • Composition scheme for small businesses — 1% GST for traders with turnover below Rs 1.5 crore, benefits and limitations for small shops.

Module 2: Billing Systems & Invoice Management

Week 2-3 | 18 Hours

  • Manual vs digital billing — When to use manual billing (small shops), when digital billing is mandatory (online stores, big retailers). POS systems explained.
  • GST-compliant invoice structure — Mandatory fields: Invoice number, date, seller details, buyer details, GSTIN, HSN/SAC codes, item description, quantity, rate, tax amount, total amount.
  • Creating proper GST invoices step-by-step — Practical exercise: creating 5 sample invoices for different products (grocery, apparel, electronics) with correct tax calculation.
  • Understanding HSN and SAC codes — HSN codes for goods (8-digit), SAC codes for services (8-digit). How to find correct codes for products using GST portal.
  • Billing software training (Tally/GST-Ready POS) — Hands-on training on popular Indian billing software: data entry, invoice generation, tax calculation, report printing.
  • E-invoicing and e-way bills — E-invoice system for B2B transactions, e-way bill for goods movement between states. When they are mandatory.
  • Tax calculation methods — Simple calculation: Base Price + GST. Example: Rs 1000 item at 18% = Rs 1000 + Rs 180 = Rs 1180 total. Reverse calculation for inclusive prices.
  • Discount and offer handling in GST — How to apply discounts correctly without affecting GST calculation. Trade discount vs cash discount treatment.
  • Credit notes and debit notes — When issued (returns, price corrections), how they affect GST, proper documentation with examples.
  • Billing for different customer types — Individual customers, registered businesses, government departments. Different invoice requirements for each.
  • Digital payment integration — Linking UPI, card payments, bank transfers to billing system. Recording payment methods in invoices.
  • Invoice storage and retrieval — Digital backup systems, GST portal requirements for invoice retention (6 years), physical file organization for small shops.

Module 3: GST Compliance & Tax Filing Procedures

Week 4-5 | 16 Hours

  • Monthly GST returns filing — GSTR-1 (outward supplies), GSTR-2 (inward supplies), GSTR-3B (summary return). Deadlines: 11th, 15th, 20th of next month.
  • GSTR-1 filing step-by-step — What to include (all B2B invoices), how to upload on GST portal, common errors to avoid, deadline extensions.
  • GSTR-2 filing and ITC claims — Recording purchases, claiming Input Tax Credit, matching invoices with seller's GSTR-1, importance for business savings.
  • GSTR-3B summary return — Monthly reconciliation of tax collected vs tax paid, calculating net GST liability, payment deadlines and late fees.
  • GST payment methods — Online payment via GST portal, bank transfer, credit/debit card. Understanding payment challans and receipt generation.
  • Late filing penalties and interest — Penalties for missed deadlines (Rs 100-500 per day), interest on unpaid GST (18% per annum). Real examples of financial impact.
  • Quarterly returns for composition dealers — GSTR-4 for businesses under composition scheme, simplified filing process, lower compliance burden.
  • Annual return filing — GSTR-9 annual reconciliation (by December), reconciling monthly returns with annual figures, importance for audit trail.
  • GST audit and compliance checks — Random audits, what documents to maintain, inspection by GST officers, preparing for audit with proper records.
  • Amendments and corrections in returns — Correcting filed returns, revised return filing (GSTR-1R), time limits for amendments, penalties for false information.
  • GST portal navigation and account management — Dashboard features, downloading certificates, tracking payment status, resolving portal login issues.
  • Record-keeping requirements — Documents to maintain: invoices, purchase orders, delivery notes, payment receipts, GST returns. Digital vs physical storage norms.

Module 4: Tax Calculations & Special Transactions

Week 5-6 | 17 Hours

  • Basic GST calculation on goods — Step-by-step: Price before tax + GST % = Final price. Working with 5%, 12%, 18%, 28% rates. 10 practical examples.
  • GST on services — Service tax calculation, difference from goods tax, examples: restaurant bills, salon services, transportation, consulting fees.
  • Supply of goods vs supply of services distinction — How to classify transactions correctly, impact on GST rate, common confusion areas.
  • Reverse charge mechanism — When buyer pays GST directly to government (B2B services, imports). Circumstances: unregistered suppliers, construction services, e-commerce.
  • Intra-state vs inter-state supplies — SGST+CGST for intra-state, IGST for inter-state. Practical: selling from Delhi to Mumbai (IGST) vs Delhi to Delhi (SGST+CGST).
  • E-commerce operator GST responsibilities — Marketplace GST collection, TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) on seller payments, GST on commission charges.
  • Job work and contract manufacturing — GST on job work (processing raw materials), input tax credit on materials supplied, invoice requirements.
  • Import and export GST treatment — 0% GST on exports (with documentation), GST on imports, customs duty vs GST, export-import procedures.
  • Deemed supply transactions — Gift, free samples, personal use items. When GST is applicable even without payment, impact on ITC.
  • Special schemes: SEZ and Special Economic Zones — Zero GST for supplies within SEZ, documentation for SEZ transactions.
  • Advance payment and GST timing — When GST is due: on invoice date or payment date. Handling advance payments and partial deliveries.
  • Valuation of goods for GST — Determining taxable value when discounts applied, handling bundled products, transfer pricing in related party transactions.

Module 5: Practical Billing Software & Real-World Scenarios

Week 6-7 | 19 Hours

  • Tally ERP 9 for GST — Installation, basic setup, creating company with GST details, configuring tax rates and HSN codes.
  • Point of Sale (POS) system operations — Daily transactions, inventory management, customer billing, generating daily reports, end-of-day reconciliation.
  • Creating master data in billing systems — Product master (name, HSN code, rate, tax rate), customer master (name, GSTIN, address), supplier master setup.
  • Processing daily sales transactions — Recording cash sales, credit sales, multiple item billing, applying discounts, printing receipts with GST details.
  • Managing stock and inventory — Stock entry, stock transfer between locations, stock adjustment for damage/loss, stock reports and valuation.
  • Processing purchases and maintaining purchase records — Recording supplier invoices, verifying HSN codes and tax rates, storing for ITC claims.
  • Handling returns and adjustments — Processing customer returns, issuing credit notes, supplier returns (debit notes), impact on GST calculations.
  • Monthly reconciliation and closing — Matching invoices with GST returns, identifying discrepancies, bank reconciliation, preparing for filing.
  • GST report generation from billing software — GSTR-1 auto-generation, GSTR-2 data preparation, exporting data for portal filing.
  • Multi-location billing — Managing branches, inter-branch transfers, consolidating GST returns from multiple locations.
  • Backup and data security — Daily backup procedures, cloud backup options, protecting billing data from loss, disaster recovery planning.
  • Troubleshooting common billing errors — Duplicate invoices, missing HSN codes, tax rate mismatches, resolving portal upload failures.
  • Mobile billing and offline operations — Billing without internet, syncing data when online, mobile POS devices for retail stores.

Module 6: Professional Skills & Career Development

Week 7-8 | 15 Hours

  • Communication skills for GST professionals — Explaining GST to non-technical clients, responding to GST officer queries, professional email writing.
  • Customer service in billing operations — Handling payment disputes, addressing invoice queries, resolving billing complaints, maintaining customer relationships.
  • Ethical practices in GST compliance — Avoiding tax evasion, reporting accurate figures, maintaining confidentiality, professional conduct standards.
  • Continuous learning and GST updates — Following GST Council notifications, understanding tax changes, subscribing to GST news, staying compliant.
  • Soft skills for accounting professionals — Time management, attention to detail, accuracy in calculations, working under pressure during month-end.
  • Interview preparation for GST roles — Common questions: 'Explain GST', 'How to file GSTR-1', salary negotiations, presenting your GST knowledge confidently.
  • Building professional network — Joining accounting associations, attending GST seminars, connecting with finance professionals, online communities.
  • Self-employment and freelance GST consulting — Starting as independent GST consultant, pricing GST compliance services, managing multiple clients.
  • Entrepreneurship: Starting a billing service center — Business plan for billing bureau, investment needed (software, computer, printer), revenue model.
  • Understanding business financials beyond GST — Profit-loss concepts, basic accounting, how GST fits in financial statements, reading balance sheets.
  • Legal responsibilities and documentation — Acting as authorized signatory on GST returns, legal liability for false information, insurance for professionals.
  • Advanced certifications and upskilling paths — CA, CMA, GST certification courses, online platforms (ICAI, ICMAI), career progression opportunities.

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks each (Multiple Choice + Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on assessment of GST compliance, billing software operations, and invoice creation with real-world scenarios

Practical Tasks:

  • GST Invoice Creation & Calculation (15 Marks): Candidate must create 3 GST-compliant invoices for different products (different tax slabs), showing correct HSN codes, tax calculations, and mandatory fields. Manual or software-based submission accepted.
  • Billing Software Operation (15 Marks): Candidate must operate billing software (Tally/POS) to process 5 sample transactions (sales, returns, discounts), generate invoices, and produce daily report. Assessed on accuracy and speed.
  • GST Return Filing Simulation (10 Marks): Candidate must prepare GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B for a sample business using provided transaction data. Must identify correct tax rates, calculate ITC, and show proper filing format.
  • Real-World Problem Solving (10 Marks): Candidate must solve 3 case studies: handling inter-state supplies, processing returns with credit notes, and resolving billing discrepancies. Written answers showing GST knowledge.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand GST structure, tax slabs, and compliance requirements in India
  • Create GST-compliant invoices with correct tax calculations and HSN codes
  • Operate billing software and POS systems for daily business transactions
  • File GSTR-1, GSTR-2, and GSTR-3B returns accurately and on time
  • Calculate ITC (Input Tax Credit) and manage GST liability for businesses
  • Handle special transactions: inter-state supplies, returns, reverse charge, e-commerce
