Dairy Farming & Milk Processing

📚 Sector: Agriculture & Rural Livelihood 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the complete dairy farming and milk processing cycle from animal husbandry to final product packaging. This foundation course equips you with practical skills in dairy cattle management, milk collection, quality testing, and basic processing techniques—essential for establishing a profitable dairy business in rural India.

This comprehensive program covers dairy cattle selection and management, animal health and nutrition, milk production optimization, quality assurance, basic processing techniques like pasteurization and paneer making, and business fundamentals for dairy enterprises. You'll gain hands-on experience in dairy farm operations and processing equipment while learning to meet Indian food safety standards and build a sustainable income source.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior dairy farming experience required
  • Already working in dairy? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid photo ID proof required (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport, or Driving License)
  • Applicable for all candidates including farmers, rural youth, and agricultural workers
  • No upper age limit — career changers welcome

Modules

Module 1: Dairy Cattle Selection, Breeding & Management

Week 1-2 | 20 Hours

  • Introduction to Indian dairy industry — current production statistics, market demand, and opportunities in rural areas
  • Dairy cattle breeds in India — Holstein-Friesian, Jersey, Sahiwal, Gir, Red Sindhi, cross-breeds and their milk yield characteristics
  • Selection criteria for dairy animals — age, health, milk production history, body structure, and udder conformation
  • Breeding fundamentals — natural breeding vs artificial insemination, heat detection, breeding records maintenance
  • Cattle housing and infrastructure — shed design, ventilation, drainage, space requirements per animal, and cost estimation
  • Daily cattle management practices — feeding schedules, water management, hygiene protocols, and animal handling techniques
  • Identification and record keeping — ear tagging, individual animal records, milk production tracking, breeding history
  • Common behavioral problems — aggression, stress management, and safe handling of dairy animals
  • Introduction to dairy buffalo farming — advantages, milk composition differences, and management variations
  • Cost-benefit analysis of dairy cattle farming — investment requirements, expected returns, and profitability calculations

Module 2: Animal Nutrition, Feed Management & Health Care

Week 2-3 | 18 Hours

  • Nutritional requirements of dairy cattle — protein, energy, minerals, vitamins, and water needs for milk production
  • Types of feed — green fodder, dry fodder, concentrate feeds, mineral supplements, and their nutritional composition
  • Feed formulation basics — calculating rations for different production levels, seasonal adjustments, and cost optimization
  • Fodder cultivation — growing maize, jowar, lucerne, berseem for year-round availability and quality maintenance
  • Silage and hay preparation — techniques, storage methods, quality assessment, and shelf-life management
  • Common cattle diseases — mastitis, foot-and-mouth disease, brucellosis, tuberculosis, symptoms, prevention, and treatment basics
  • Vaccination schedules — routine immunizations, record maintenance, and government subsidy schemes in India
  • Udder health and mastitis prevention — cleaning protocols, milking hygiene, early detection of infection signs
  • Parasite control — internal and external parasites, deworming schedules, and preventive measures
  • Health monitoring — body condition scoring, milk quality indicators, veterinary care access, and emergency protocols
  • Reproduction health — postpartum care, infertility causes, dry period management, and lactation cycle optimization
  • Herd health planning — maintaining disease-free herds, isolation procedures, and coordination with veterinary services

Module 3: Milk Production, Collection & Quality Testing

Week 3-4 | 22 Hours

  • Milk production physiology — lactation cycle, peak production periods, factors affecting yield, and seasonal variations
  • Milking equipment — manual milking, bucket milking, portable milking machines, equipment maintenance and hygiene
  • Milking procedures — pre-milking preparation, proper technique, post-milking care, frequency optimization, and animal stress reduction
  • Milk collection and storage — containers, cooling methods, temperature maintenance, storage duration, and contamination prevention
  • Milk composition testing — fat percentage, Solids-Not-Fat (SNF), lactose, protein content, and Indian dairy standards
  • Specific gravity and density measurement — using lactometer, interpreting results, detecting adulteration
  • Acidity and pH testing — importance for shelf-life, testing methods, acceptable ranges, and corrective actions
  • Microbial quality testing — Standard Plate Count (SPC), coliform testing, pathogenic bacteria detection, and hygiene implications
  • Chemical adulteration detection — identifying water addition, urea, formalin, melamine, and other adulterants
  • Somatic cell count — importance for milk quality, mastitis correlation, testing procedures, and industry standards
  • Documentation and record keeping — daily production logs, quality test records, batch tracking, and regulatory compliance
  • Milk grading and quality standards — IS 1224 standards, FSSAI regulations, and market-based premium pricing

Module 4: Basic Milk Processing Techniques & Products

Week 4-5 | 24 Hours

  • Milk processing fundamentals — principles, benefits, value addition, and market demand for processed products
  • Pasteurization process — low-temperature long-time (LTLT) and high-temperature short-time (HTST) methods, equipment, and temperature control
  • Homogenization basics — purpose, equipment types, pressure requirements, and impact on product quality
  • Paneer (cottage cheese) production — curdling with acid/rennet, cutting curds, cooking, molding, and cooling procedures
  • Paneer troubleshooting — crumbly texture causes, color issues, moisture problems, and quality improvement techniques
  • Ghee production — traditional and modern methods, clarification process, impurity removal, packaging, and shelf-life extension
  • Curd/yogurt production — starter cultures, fermentation temperature, incubation period, setting techniques, and flavor variations
  • Flavored milk preparation — chocolate milk, toned milk, fortified milk, ingredient mixing, and homogenization requirements
  • Butter and buttermilk production — cream separation, churning process, salt addition, washing, and moisture content control
  • Kheer and dessert products — traditional recipes, ingredient proportions, cooking times, and packaging for retail
  • Whey management — byproduct utilization, ricotta cheese production, whey protein extraction, and waste reduction
  • Small-scale processing equipment — capacity requirements, cost analysis, space planning, and equipment maintenance protocols

Module 5: Food Safety, Hygiene & Regulatory Compliance

Week 5-6 | 18 Hours

  • Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) regulations — licensing requirements, registration process, and compliance deadlines
  • Hygiene standards for dairy farms — personal hygiene, farm sanitation, water quality, and pest control measures
  • Hygiene standards for processing units — facility design, cleaning schedules, sanitization procedures, and documentation
  • Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP) — identifying hazards, critical control points, monitoring procedures
  • Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) — process control, equipment maintenance, record keeping, and traceability systems
  • Milk quality standards — Indian Standards (IS 1224), FSSAI regulations, fat content, SNF requirements, and microbial limits
  • Labeling requirements — mandatory information, nutritional facts, allergen declarations, and expiry date formats
  • Packaging standards — material safety, barrier properties, temperature maintenance, and recyclability considerations
  • Traceability and recall procedures — batch tracking, customer notification, product withdrawal, and documentation
  • Testing and certification — third-party lab testing, certificate validity, renewal procedures, and cost management
  • Dairy cooperative compliance — registration with NDDB, milk union requirements, and government subsidy schemes
  • Record maintenance — production logs, quality test reports, cleaning schedules, and inspection readiness

Module 6: Dairy Business Planning, Marketing & Entrepreneurship

Week 6-8 | 18 Hours

  • Dairy business models — individual farming, cooperative membership, contract farming, and dairy processing units
  • Financial planning — startup costs, operational expenses, revenue projections, and break-even analysis for dairy farms
  • Loan and subsidy schemes — NABARD schemes, bank loans for dairy, government subsidies, and application procedures
  • Market analysis — local demand assessment, competitor analysis, pricing strategies, and seasonal demand variations
  • Direct-to-consumer marketing — farm gate sales, home delivery models, WhatsApp/online ordering, and customer retention
  • Cooperative marketing — milk union membership benefits, collective bargaining power, and quality premium incentives
  • Brand development — product naming, packaging design, quality consistency, and building customer trust
  • Cost management — feed cost optimization, labor efficiency, waste reduction, and profitability improvement strategies
  • Quality as competitive advantage — maintaining premium milk quality, certifications, organic farming potential, and price premiums
  • Scaling dairy operations — expansion planning, additional animals, processing equipment investment, and market readiness
  • Record keeping for business — financial records, tax compliance, GST registration, and income documentation
  • Risk management — disease prevention, market fluctuations, climate challenges, and insurance options for dairy animals

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions × 2 Marks each (Multiple Choice and Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Hands-on demonstration and product preparation under supervision

Practical Tasks:

  • Milk Quality Testing Practical (15 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate milk testing using lactometer, acidity testing, and basic adulteration detection. Submit test report with observations and interpretations.
  • Paneer Production Practical (15 Marks): Candidate must prepare 500ml paneer from raw milk, demonstrating proper curdling, cutting, cooking, and molding. Quality assessment based on texture, color, and yield.
  • Dairy Farm Record Maintenance (10 Marks): Candidate must maintain and submit 7-day dairy farm records including animal health, feed consumption, milk production, and quality data with proper documentation.
  • Hygiene Protocol Implementation (10 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate proper sanitization of milking equipment, farm cleaning, and personal hygiene practices as per FSSAI standards.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand dairy cattle breeds suitable for Indian climate and manage their health, nutrition, and reproduction effectively
  • Implement proper milking techniques and maintain milk quality standards meeting FSSAI regulations
  • Perform basic milk quality testing including composition analysis and adulteration detection
  • Prepare value-added dairy products like paneer, ghee, and curd using safe processing techniques
  • Maintain hygiene standards and ensure food safety compliance in dairy operations
  • Develop a sustainable dairy business plan with proper financial management and marketing strategies
