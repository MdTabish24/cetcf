Site Supervisor — Construction

📚 Sector: Construction & Building Trades 🎓 Level: Intermediate ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks / 180 Hours)

Course Overview

Become a skilled Site Supervisor and lead construction teams with confidence. This 12-week intensive program trains you in site management, safety protocols, quality control, and worker supervision—essential skills for managing modern construction projects across India.

This course covers complete site supervision responsibilities including daily site operations, worker management, quality assurance, safety compliance, material handling, cost control, and communication with architects and contractors. You'll learn to manage residential, commercial, and infrastructure projects while maintaining safety standards and project timelines.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 18 years
  • Minimum education: Class 10th pass (or equivalent)
  • Preferred: 2+ years experience in construction sector
  • Already working as Site Supervisor? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Driving License)
  • Medical fitness certificate may be required for on-site practical training
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals

Modules

Module 1: Construction Fundamentals & Site Organization

Week 1-2 | 30 Hours

  • Introduction to construction industry in India — types of projects, scales, and sectors
  • Role and responsibilities of Site Supervisor — daily duties, authority, and accountability
  • Site layout planning — understanding blueprints, site boundaries, and equipment placement
  • Site office setup — record-keeping systems, documentation, and communication tools
  • Understanding project timelines — critical path method, work schedules, and milestones
  • Introduction to building codes and Indian Standards — NBC, BIS, and local regulations
  • Mobilization of site resources — labor, equipment, materials, and utilities
  • Site induction program — inducting new workers, safety briefings, and orientation
  • Coordination with consultants — architects, engineers, contractors, and government officials
  • Basic project cost estimation — understanding budgets, rate analysis, and financial tracking

Module 2: Safety Management & Occupational Health

Week 3-4 | 30 Hours

  • Construction Safety Act & Rules — OSHA guidelines, Building and Other Construction Workers Act
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) — helmets, vests, gloves, footwear, and enforcement
  • Hazard identification and risk assessment — common construction hazards and mitigation
  • Fall protection systems — scaffolding safety, harnesses, and working at heights
  • Electrical safety on site — earthing, insulation, safe use of power tools, and accident prevention
  • Fire safety and emergency response — fire extinguishers, evacuation plans, and first aid
  • Excavation and trenching safety — shoring, sloping, and confined space entry protocols
  • Machine and equipment safety — operation, maintenance, and operator certification requirements
  • Health and hygiene practices — sanitation, drinking water, waste management, and health camps
  • Accident reporting and investigation — documentation, root cause analysis, and corrective actions
  • Safety audits and inspections — conducting regular checks and maintaining safety records
  • Worker welfare and insurance — ESI, accident compensation, and grievance redressal

Module 3: Quality Control & Technical Supervision

Week 5-6 | 30 Hours

  • Quality management principles — ISO standards, TQM concepts, and continuous improvement
  • Understanding construction drawings — reading plans, elevations, sections, and specifications
  • Material testing and inspection — concrete cubes, slump tests, steel testing, and aggregate quality
  • Concrete work supervision — mix design, batching, pouring, curing, and strength verification
  • Masonry and brickwork quality — brick quality, mortar strength, plumb and level checks
  • Reinforcement steel inspection — bar diameter, spacing, overlap, and placement verification
  • Formwork and shuttering — design approval, safety, and removal timing
  • Plumbing and electrical rough-in inspection — pipe sizing, cable routing, and code compliance
  • Finishing work quality — plastering, painting, tile fitting, and surface finish standards
  • Non-destructive testing methods — rebound hammer, ultrasonic testing, and visual inspection
  • Defect management — documentation, rectification, and warranty tracking
  • Quality records and documentation — daily reports, test certificates, and compliance files

Module 4: Labor Management & Workforce Supervision

Week 7-8 | 30 Hours

  • Labor laws in India — minimum wages, working hours, leave entitlements, and statutory requirements
  • Workforce planning — assessing labor requirements, skill mix, and recruitment strategies
  • Worker classification — skilled, semi-skilled, unskilled labor, and contractor vs. direct labor
  • Wage management and payroll — daily wages, piece rates, overtime, and deduction rules
  • Attendance and productivity tracking — muster rolls, time sheets, and output measurement
  • Worker motivation and performance management — incentives, appraisals, and discipline
  • Conflict resolution and grievance handling — addressing disputes and maintaining harmony
  • Training and skill development — on-site training, certification, and upskilling programs
  • Worker accommodation and facilities — temporary shelters, toilets, drinking water, and rest areas
  • Migrant worker management — documentation, registration, and welfare support
  • Team building and communication — daily toolbox talks, team meetings, and feedback sessions
  • Compliance with labor regulations — record-keeping, inspections, and legal obligations

Module 5: Materials Management & Equipment Handling

Week 9-10 | 30 Hours

  • Material procurement processes — purchase orders, supplier selection, and rate negotiation
  • Inventory management — stock tracking, storage, and wastage control
  • Cement, aggregates, and concrete — types, quality standards, and storage requirements
  • Steel and reinforcement — grades, certifications, cutting, bending, and handling
  • Bricks and blocks — quality checking, water absorption, and damage prevention
  • Timber and formwork materials — seasoning, grading, and safe handling practices
  • Equipment selection and deployment — cranes, excavators, concrete pumps, and cost optimization
  • Equipment maintenance and safety — daily checks, servicing, and operator certification
  • Material transport and logistics — site access, unloading, stacking, and organization
  • Waste management and recycling — segregation, disposal, and environmental compliance
  • Material cost control — rate analysis, variance tracking, and loss prevention
  • Documentation and billing — material receipts, consumption records, and payment processing

Module 6: Project Coordination, Reporting & Professional Development

Week 11-12 | 30 Hours

  • Daily site operations management — morning briefings, activity planning, and progress tracking
  • Communication with stakeholders — clients, consultants, contractors, and government agencies
  • Progress reporting — daily logs, weekly reports, monthly statements, and photographic records
  • Schedule management — updating timelines, identifying delays, and recovery planning
  • Cost management and budgeting — tracking expenditure, variance analysis, and financial reporting
  • Coordination with multiple trades — sequencing work, managing dependencies, and minimizing conflicts
  • Environmental management — dust control, noise management, water conservation, and green practices
  • Regulatory compliance — building permits, environmental clearances, and labor inspections
  • Documentation and record-keeping — site files, correspondence, approvals, and archives
  • Problem-solving and decision-making — addressing site issues and escalation procedures
  • Professional ethics and code of conduct — integrity, transparency, and industry best practices
  • Career advancement — certifications, further education, and professional networking opportunities

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice & Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Site-based practical assessment involving supervision tasks, quality checks, safety inspections, and documentation

Practical Tasks:

  • Site Inspection and Hazard Identification (15 Marks): Candidate must conduct a complete site safety inspection, identify at least 10 hazards, document findings, and propose corrective actions with photographic evidence
  • Quality Control Report (15 Marks): Prepare a detailed quality inspection report for concrete work, masonry, or reinforcement with test results, non-conformances, and corrective action plan
  • Daily Progress Report & Documentation (10 Marks): Create a comprehensive daily site report including labor attendance, material consumption, work completed, safety incidents, and photographs with proper formatting
  • Labor Management Case Study (10 Marks): Resolve a given conflict or labor management scenario with documented communication, policy application, and resolution strategy

Learning Outcomes

  • Plan and organize daily site operations while maintaining safety and quality standards
  • Identify, assess, and mitigate construction site hazards to prevent accidents and injuries
  • Inspect materials, workmanship, and finishes to ensure compliance with specifications and building codes
  • Manage construction workers effectively through motivation, training, and adherence to labor laws
  • Control project costs, track progress, and maintain accurate site documentation and records
  • Communicate effectively with consultants, contractors, clients, and government authorities for smooth project execution
