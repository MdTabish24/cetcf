Knitting & Crochet Professional

📚 Sector: Fashion, Tailoring & Textiles 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the art of knitting and crochet with hands-on training in traditional and modern techniques. This foundation course equips you with professional skills to create beautiful garments, accessories, and home décor items, opening doors to employment and self-employment opportunities in India's growing fashion and textile industry.

This 8-week intensive program covers essential knitting and crochet fundamentals, from basic stitches to advanced patterns and garment construction. You'll learn to read patterns, work with different yarn types, troubleshoot common mistakes, and create market-ready products. The course combines theoretical knowledge with extensive practical training, preparing you for roles in garment factories, boutiques, or starting your own home-based business.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (with parental consent for 14-18 age group)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required; beginners welcome
  • Already working in knitting/crochet? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required: Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport
  • Medical fitness: No conditions that prevent hand/finger dexterity for 8+ hours daily
  • Applicable for all genders and social categories
  • Persons with disabilities: Reasonable accommodations provided; contact center for accessibility requirements

Modules

Module 1: Fundamentals of Knitting & Crochet

Week 1 | 15 Hours

  • History and evolution of knitting and crochet in India — understanding traditional crafts like Kullu shawls, Kashmiri embroidery, and modern applications
  • Tools and equipment identification — needles, hooks, stitch markers, measuring tapes, yarn cutters, blocking tools, and their proper usage
  • Yarn types and fiber content — understanding cotton, wool, acrylic, silk, blends; weight classifications (DK, worsted, bulky); Indian yarn brands like Vardhman and Patons
  • Yarn labels and care instructions — decoding symbols, washing guidelines, storage tips to maintain quality
  • Safety and ergonomics — proper posture, hand positioning, preventing repetitive strain injury (RSI), workspace setup for 8+ hour work
  • Tension and gauge importance — why tension matters, how to measure gauge accurately, adjusting needles/hooks for consistent results
  • Introduction to basic knitting needles — straight needles, circular needles, double-pointed needles (DPN) and their applications
  • Introduction to crochet hooks — hook sizes, materials, handling techniques for different hook types
  • Casting on techniques — long-tail cast-on, cable cast-on, thumb cast-on for knitting; chain foundation for crochet
  • Binding off and finishing edges — secure cast-off methods, preventing edge curling, professional finishing

Module 2: Basic Stitches & Pattern Reading

Week 2 | 15 Hours

  • Fundamental knitting stitches — garter stitch, stockinette stitch, reverse stockinette, ribbed stitches (1x1, 2x2), moss stitch, seed stitch
  • Fundamental crochet stitches — single crochet, half double crochet, double crochet, treble crochet, slip stitch, chain variations
  • Increasing and decreasing in knitting — knit front and back (KFB), yarn over (YO), knit two together (K2tog), slip slip knit (SSK), techniques for shaping
  • Increasing and decreasing in crochet — single crochet increase, decrease stitches, working into multiple loops for shaping
  • Reading knitting patterns — understanding abbreviations (K, P, YO, K2tog), chart symbols, row counters, pattern repeat markers
  • Reading crochet patterns — understanding abbreviations (sc, dc, tr, ch), American vs UK terminology differences, chart interpretation
  • Correcting common mistakes — dropped stitches, twisted stitches, uneven tension, picking up mistakes using crochet hook
  • Stitch practice projects — creating practice swatches, maintaining consistency, documenting progress with measurements
  • Understanding pattern difficulty levels — beginner, intermediate, advanced patterns; choosing appropriate projects for skill development
  • Digital pattern resources — using websites like Ravelry, Pinterest, YouTube tutorials; Indian pattern sources and communities

Module 3: Intermediate Techniques & Garment Construction

Week 3-4 | 30 Hours

  • Cable stitches in knitting — understanding cable mechanics, cable needles, creating decorative cable patterns, cable gauge importance
  • Lace stitches and openwork — yarn overs, decreases, creating lace patterns, blocking lace items for proper drape
  • Color work techniques — stranding, carrying yarn, tension management in multi-color projects, Fair Isle patterns, tapestry crochet
  • Textured stitches — bobbles, popcorn stitches, puff stitches, creating dimensional effects for market appeal
  • Circular knitting — working with circular needles and DPNs, avoiding laddering, joining in the round, advantages for seamless garments
  • Garment shaping fundamentals ��� understanding armholes, necklines, sleeves; calculating increases and decreases for proper fit
  • Measuring and fitting — taking accurate body measurements (bust, length, sleeve length), understanding size charts, Indian sizing standards
  • Seaming techniques — mattress stitch, backstitch, grafting, weaving in ends professionally, blocking for finished appearance
  • Working with patterns — scaling patterns up or down, adjusting for different yarn weights, calculating yarn requirements
  • Common construction methods — top-down construction, bottom-up construction, in-the-round construction; advantages and disadvantages of each

Module 4: Advanced Garment Projects

Week 5 | 15 Hours

  • Sweater construction — understanding body, sleeves, neckline shaping; set-in sleeves vs raglan vs drop shoulder styles; sizing for Indian body proportions
  • Cardigan design — button bands, buttonholes, collar options, front placket techniques for professional finish
  • Shawls and wraps — triangular shawls, rectangular wraps, circular shawls; lace shawl construction; traditional Indian shawl patterns
  • Socks and small accessories — cuff, leg, heel construction; heel flap vs heel turn; creating fitted garments on small circumference
  • Hats and headwear — crown shaping, brim options, sizing for different head sizes, Indian traditional hat styles
  • Afghans and blankets — joining squares, creating repeating patterns, managing large projects, finishing edges professionally
  • Baby garments — understanding sizing, using appropriate yarn (soft, washable), creating safe closures, market demand in India
  • Market-ready finishing — professional blocking, proper packaging, care label attachment, quality control checks
  • Adapting patterns for Indian climate — choosing breathable yarns, appropriate garment weights, seasonal considerations
  • Creating original designs — simple modifications to existing patterns, sketching design ideas, calculating yarn quantities for custom projects

Module 5: Specialty Items & Business Fundamentals

Week 6-7 | 30 Hours

  • Home décor items — cushion covers, table runners, wall hangings, door hangings, creating items for Indian home aesthetics
  • Amigurumi and stuffed toys — working in the round, increasing/decreasing for shaping, stuffing techniques, safety considerations for toys
  • Jewelry and small accessories — crocheted jewelry, beaded knitting, brooches, hair accessories, market demand and pricing
  • Seasonal items — winter scarves, summer tank tops, monsoon-appropriate garments, festival-specific designs for Indian market
  • Corporate gifting items — branded items, bulk production requirements, quality consistency, minimum order quantities
  • Yarn sourcing and cost calculation — finding suppliers in India (Ludhiana, Tiruppur, Delhi), comparing prices, bulk purchasing, storage
  • Production planning — estimating time per garment, creating production schedules, managing multiple orders, quality benchmarks
  • Pricing strategy — calculating material cost, labor cost, overhead, profit margin; competitive pricing in Indian market (₹500-₹5000 per item)
  • Business registration basics — GST registration for self-employed, maintaining records, invoicing, understanding tax implications
  • Marketing and sales channels — online platforms (Etsy, Amazon Handmade, Instagram), local markets, boutiques, wholesale opportunities in India

Module 6: Quality Control, Troubleshooting & Career Development

Week 8 | 15 Hours

  • Quality standards in textile industry — understanding ASTM standards, Indian textile quality norms, durability testing, shrinkage prevention
  • Common defects and solutions — pilling, stretching, color bleeding, uneven stitches; prevention and correction techniques
  • Blocking and finishing excellence — wet blocking, steam blocking, proper drying, achieving professional appearance, storage of finished items
  • Yarn care and maintenance — preventing moth damage, humidity control, proper storage methods for finished inventory
  • Troubleshooting techniques — identifying causes of mistakes, fixing holes and tears, reinforcing weak areas, extending garment life
  • Documentation and record-keeping — maintaining project logs, tracking yarn usage, recording time spent, building portfolio
  • Portfolio development — photographing work professionally, creating lookbooks, documenting before-after of projects, building credibility
  • Continuing education — staying updated with trends, learning new techniques through online courses, joining knitting/crochet communities
  • Industry certifications and upgrades — pursuing advanced certifications, specialization options (fashion design, textile technology), career progression
  • Networking and collaboration — joining artisan groups, collaborating with designers, participating in craft fairs, building professional relationships in Indian textile industry

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks (Multiple Choice, Short Answer, Diagram-based) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Candidates must complete 3 practical projects demonstrating progressive skill development from basic to intermediate level, evaluated on technique, finish quality, and adherence to specifications

Practical Tasks:

  • Basic Knitting Swatch & Accessories (15 Marks): Create a 20cm x 20cm swatch demonstrating garter stitch, stockinette stitch, and ribbed stitch with consistent tension; submit a pair of knitted dishcloths or simple scarves showing proper casting on, binding off, and edge finishing
  • Crochet Garment or Home Décor Item (18 Marks): Complete one crochet project from: cushion cover (40cm x 40cm), table runner (30cm x 120cm), baby blanket (60cm x 80cm), or shawl (triangular, 100cm base). Must demonstrate color work, consistent stitch quality, proper shaping, and professional finishing
  • Knitted Garment Project (17 Marks): Complete one knitted garment from: fitted sweater (size S/M), cardigan, or structured shawl. Must include proper measurements, gauge swatch, appropriate sizing for Indian body proportions, seaming, blocking, and professional presentation. Garment must be wearable and market-ready

Learning Outcomes

  • Master fundamental knitting and crochet stitches with consistent tension and proper technique
  • Read and interpret knitting/crochet patterns accurately, including charts and abbreviations
  • Construct complete garments including sweaters, cardigans, and accessories with proper shaping and finishing
  • Apply color work techniques, cable stitches, and textured stitches to create market-ready designs
  • Calculate yarn requirements, manage costs, and plan production for small-scale business operations
  • Maintain quality standards, troubleshoot common defects, and deliver professional-grade finished products suitable for retail or gifting
