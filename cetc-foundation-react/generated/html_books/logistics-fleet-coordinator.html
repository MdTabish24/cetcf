Logistics & Fleet Coordinator

📚 Sector: Automobile & Motor Trades 🎓 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months (8 Weeks / 120 Hours)

Course Overview

Master the essential skills of fleet management and logistics coordination in India's fast-growing automobile sector. This 8-week foundation course prepares you to manage vehicle fleets, optimize routes, handle documentation, and coordinate logistics operations for transport companies, e-commerce logistics firms, and corporate fleet departments.

This comprehensive program covers fleet operations, vehicle maintenance coordination, route optimization, documentation management, safety regulations, and customer service in the Indian logistics industry. You'll learn practical skills used daily by logistics coordinators in companies like Amazon, Flipkart, TCS Logistics, and regional transport operators across India.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum age: 14 years (18 years preferred for driver-related roles)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required; open to all interested candidates
  • Already working in logistics/transport? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid photo ID proof required (Aadhar, PAN, Driving License, or Passport)
  • Medical fitness for physical activities (no severe mobility restrictions)
  • Applicable for all genders and candidates from any background

Modules

Module 1: Fundamentals of Fleet Management & Logistics

Week 1-2 | 15 Hours

  • Introduction to Fleet Management — types of fleets (commercial, corporate, e-commerce), fleet sizes, and Indian logistics industry overview
  • Role of Logistics & Fleet Coordinator — daily responsibilities, reporting structure, and career progression in Indian transport companies
  • Automobile Industry in India — major logistics players, growth sectors (e-commerce, FMCG, pharma), and employment opportunities
  • Types of Vehicles in Fleet Operations — HCV (Heavy Commercial Vehicles), MCV (Medium Commercial Vehicles), LCV (Light Commercial Vehicles), and passenger vehicles
  • Fleet Ownership Models — owned fleets, leased fleets, contract hiring, and third-party logistics (3PL) operations in India
  • Introduction to Logistics Chain — supply chain basics, distribution networks, warehousing, and last-mile delivery concepts
  • Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) in Fleet Management — fuel efficiency, vehicle utilization rate, on-time delivery percentage, and cost per kilometer
  • Legal Framework for Fleet Operations in India — Motor Vehicles Act 1988, CMVR (Central Motor Vehicles Rules), and National Highway Code

Module 2: Vehicle Documentation & Regulatory Compliance

Week 2-3 | 18 Hours

  • Vehicle Registration Documents — RC (Registration Certificate), understanding Part A and Part B, transfer procedures, and online registration portals
  • Insurance Documentation — comprehensive vs third-party insurance, claim procedures, fitness certificate requirements, and GST implications
  • Pollution Under Control (PUC) Certificate — emission standards, renewal cycles, testing procedures, and compliance tracking
  • Fitness Certificate — validity periods for different vehicle types, renewal process, and inspection standards for commercial vehicles
  • Driving Licenses & Permits — LMV (Light Motor Vehicle), HMV (Heavy Motor Vehicle), HPMV (Heavy Passenger Motor Vehicle) classifications, and permit requirements
  • Road Permits & Taxes — state permits, national permits, goods tax, vehicle tax, and toll documentation for interstate operations
  • Accident & Incident Documentation — FIR filing, insurance claim forms, accident report formats, and liability documentation
  • Maintenance & Service Records — logbook maintenance, service schedules, spare parts documentation, and warranty tracking
  • Digital Compliance Tools — SARTHI portal, VAHAN database, e-challan systems, and online permit management platforms used in India
  • Penalty & Violation Management — common traffic violations, fine structures, appeal procedures, and corrective action plans

Module 3: Fleet Maintenance & Vehicle Care Coordination

Week 3-4 | 16 Hours

  • Preventive Maintenance Planning — scheduled maintenance calendars, service intervals based on kilometers and months, and maintenance cost budgeting
  • Vehicle Inspection Protocols — pre-trip inspection checklists, daily vehicle condition reports, and systematic inspection procedures
  • Engine & Mechanical Systems — basic understanding of engine health, oil changes, filter replacements, cooling system maintenance, and brake system care
  • Tire Management — tire pressure monitoring, tread depth checking, rotation schedules, puncture management, and tire replacement decisions
  • Fuel System Management — fuel quality monitoring, fuel efficiency tracking, adulteration detection, and fuel cost optimization strategies
  • Electrical & Battery Systems — battery maintenance, charging systems, electrical fault identification, and basic troubleshooting
  • Coordination with Service Centers — selecting authorized dealers, managing service appointments, cost negotiation, and warranty claims
  • Maintenance Record Keeping — digital maintenance logs, spare parts inventory management, and cost analysis for maintenance budgeting
  • Emergency Breakdown Management — roadside assistance coordination, towing service arrangements, and vehicle recovery procedures
  • Green Fleet Practices — fuel-efficient driving habits, emission reduction strategies, and eco-friendly fleet management in Indian context

Module 4: Route Planning, Optimization & GPS Technology

Week 4-5 | 17 Hours

  • Route Planning Basics — understanding source, destination, stops, and delivery sequences for efficient logistics
  • Map Reading & Navigation — interpreting road maps, understanding highway networks, identifying toll points, and traffic-prone areas in India
  • GPS & Telematics Systems — GPS tracking devices, real-time vehicle monitoring, geofencing, and data interpretation from telematics platforms
  • Route Optimization Software — introduction to Google Maps, Sap Transportation Management, route optimization algorithms, and cost-benefit analysis
  • Traffic Management & Road Conditions — identifying peak traffic hours, monsoon considerations, road construction zones, and seasonal challenges in India
  • Fuel-Efficient Routing — calculating shortest routes vs. most fuel-efficient routes, speed optimization, and idling reduction strategies
  • Delivery Window Management — time slot optimization, customer availability coordination, and on-time delivery performance tracking
  • Multi-Stop Optimization — managing multiple deliveries in single route, sequence planning, and cluster-based delivery strategies
  • Highway Network Knowledge — National Highways (NH), State Highways (SH), and toll plaza locations across major Indian routes
  • Real-Time Monitoring & Alerts — speed monitoring, geofence breaches, deviation alerts, and emergency response coordination
  • Customer Communication — providing estimated time of arrival (ETA), delivery updates, and handling customer queries during transit

Module 5: Driver Management & Safety Regulations

Week 5-6 | 18 Hours

  • Driver Recruitment & Verification — checking driving license validity, background verification, medical fitness assessment, and police clearance procedures
  • Driver Training & Induction — company policies, vehicle handling, customer service, and safety protocol training for new drivers
  • Driving Hours & Rest Regulations — Motor Vehicles Act rules on maximum driving hours, mandatory rest periods, and fatigue management compliance
  • Safety Equipment & Compliance — helmets, seat belts, fire extinguishers, first-aid kits, warning triangles, and reflective jackets requirements
  • Accident Prevention & Safe Driving — defensive driving techniques, speed management, lane discipline, and weather-related driving precautions
  • Vehicle Safety Standards — ABS (Anti-lock Braking System), airbags, electronic stability control, and modern safety features in commercial vehicles
  • Road Safety Regulations — traffic rules, overtaking procedures, parking guidelines, and pedestrian safety in Indian traffic context
  • Driver Performance Monitoring — tracking driving behavior, fuel consumption patterns, accident records, and incentive-based safety programs
  • Incident & Accident Management — reporting procedures, medical assistance coordination, insurance claim initiation, and investigation process
  • Drug & Alcohol Testing — random testing protocols, company policies, legal compliance, and rehabilitation support programs
  • Driver Welfare & Incentives — overtime compensation, safety bonuses, health insurance, and career advancement opportunities

Module 6: Communication, Customer Service & Professional Skills

Week 6-8 | 36 Hours

  • Professional Communication — written communication for logistics, email etiquette, report writing, and formal documentation standards
  • Verbal Communication Skills — phone communication with drivers, customers, and vendors; clarity, professionalism, and problem-solving approach
  • Customer Service Excellence — handling delivery queries, managing complaints, tracking shipments, and maintaining customer satisfaction
  • Stakeholder Management — coordinating with drivers, warehouse staff, customers, vendors, and management for smooth operations
  • Conflict Resolution — managing disputes between parties, negotiating solutions, and maintaining professional relationships in logistics
  • Data Management & Reporting — maintaining fleet records, preparing performance reports, analyzing KPIs, and presenting insights to management
  • MS Excel & Spreadsheets — creating fleet databases, tracking vehicle expenses, calculating fuel efficiency, and generating performance charts
  • Basic Accounting for Fleet — understanding costs (fuel, maintenance, insurance), calculating per-unit costs, and budget management
  • Customer Relationship Management (CRM) — using CRM tools for tracking customer interactions, managing delivery schedules, and follow-ups
  • Problem-Solving & Decision Making — identifying issues, analyzing root causes, implementing solutions, and learning from operational challenges
  • Time Management — prioritizing tasks, managing multiple deliveries, coordinating schedules, and handling urgent situations effectively
  • Ethical Practices & Professionalism — maintaining integrity, following company policies, legal compliance, and building trust in logistics operations

Assessment & Certification

Total Marks: 100 (Pass Percentage: 50%)

Theory (50 Marks): 25 Questions x 2 Marks each (MCQ & Short Answer) - 45 minutes

Practical (50 Marks): Candidate must demonstrate practical competency in fleet coordination tasks including documentation management, route planning, vehicle inspection, and driver coordination

Practical Tasks:

  • Fleet Documentation & Compliance File (15 Marks): Create a complete documentation folder for one commercial vehicle including RC, insurance, PUC, fitness certificate, maintenance records, and permit documents. Demonstrate understanding of each document's purpose and validity period.
  • Route Planning & Optimization Exercise (15 Marks): Plan an optimized delivery route for 5 stops using a map or GPS software. Calculate distance, estimated time, fuel cost, and provide reasoning for the chosen sequence. Submit route plan with calculations.
  • Vehicle Inspection & Maintenance Report (10 Marks): Conduct a pre-trip vehicle inspection using standard checklist format. Identify at least 3 issues and document them with photographs or detailed descriptions. Recommend maintenance actions and timelines.
  • Fleet Performance Analysis (10 Marks): Analyze monthly fleet data (fuel consumption, mileage, maintenance costs, on-time delivery %) for 3 vehicles. Create graphs, identify trends, and present 3 recommendations for improvement.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand fleet management operations, vehicle types, and logistics industry structure in India
  • Manage vehicle documentation, regulatory compliance, and legal requirements for commercial fleets
  • Coordinate preventive maintenance, track vehicle health, and manage service center relationships
  • Plan optimized delivery routes using maps and GPS technology to reduce costs and improve efficiency
  • Manage drivers, ensure safety compliance, and monitor driving behavior for accident prevention
  • Communicate professionally with stakeholders, handle customer service, and prepare fleet performance reports
