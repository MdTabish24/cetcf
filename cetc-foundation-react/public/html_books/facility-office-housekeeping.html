Facility & Office Housekeeping

📂 Sector: Security & Facility Management 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the essential skills of professional facility and office housekeeping to maintain clean, safe, and organized workspaces. This foundation course equips you with industry-standard cleaning techniques, hygiene protocols, and facility management practices used in offices, hospitals, hotels, and commercial buildings across India.

Module 1: Introduction to Facility & Office Housekeeping

📚 Topics Covered

  • Overview of facility management industry in India — scope, importance, and growth opportunities in corporate offices, hospitals, malls, and hotels
  • Role and responsibilities of housekeeping staff — daily duties, reporting structure, and career progression in facility management
  • Types of facilities and their specific housekeeping needs — offices, hospitals, educational institutions, hotels, and manufacturing units
  • Professional standards and codes of conduct — dress code, punctuality, behavior, and ethical practices in workplace
  • Health, safety, and hygiene fundamentals — importance of cleanliness in preventing diseases and maintaining healthy work environment
  • Introduction to cleaning tools and equipment — basic identification, proper handling, and storage of housekeeping equipment
  • Workplace safety regulations — understanding Indian labor laws, OSHA guidelines, and facility-specific safety rules
  • Communication skills for housekeeping staff — interacting with colleagues, supervisors, and clients professionally

Module 2: Cleaning Techniques & Procedures

📚 Topics Covered

  • Daily cleaning procedures for offices — dusting, sweeping, mopping, and sanitizing workstations, desks, and common areas
  • Wet and dry cleaning methods — understanding when to use which method, proper technique, and time management for different surfaces
  • Floor care and maintenance — cleaning different floor types (tile, marble, carpet, wooden), polishing, and preventing slips and falls
  • Restroom cleaning and sanitization — toilet cleaning, sink maintenance, waste disposal, and preventing odor and bacterial growth
  • Glass and window cleaning — techniques for streak-free cleaning of windows, glass partitions, and mirrors in office buildings
  • Dusting and surface cleaning — removing dust from furniture, electronics, and hard-to-reach areas without damaging equipment
  • Carpet and upholstery cleaning — basic stain removal, vacuuming, and maintenance of carpeted areas in offices and conference rooms
  • Cleaning around electronics and sensitive equipment — safe practices for cleaning near computers, printers, and office machinery
  • Deep cleaning vs daily cleaning — understanding frequency, scope, and procedures for routine vs periodic intensive cleaning
  • Time management and efficiency — planning daily cleaning tasks, prioritizing areas, and completing work within allocated time

Module 3: Cleaning Chemicals & Safety Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Types of cleaning chemicals — detergents, disinfectants, degreasers, and specialized products for different cleaning needs in facilities
  • Understanding chemical labels and safety data sheets (SDS) — reading product information, hazard symbols, and safe usage instructions
  • Proper storage and handling of chemicals — storage conditions, segregation of incompatible products, and preventing chemical accidents
  • Dilution and mixing of cleaning solutions — correct ratios, preparation methods, and avoiding dangerous chemical combinations
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE) — selection, proper use, and maintenance of gloves, masks, aprons, and safety gear
  • Chemical safety in different facility types — special precautions for hospitals, food service areas, and sensitive environments
  • Disposal of chemical waste — proper disposal methods, environmental considerations, and compliance with Indian waste management rules
  • Health hazards and first aid — recognizing chemical exposure symptoms, basic first aid, and when to seek medical help
  • Green cleaning alternatives — eco-friendly cleaning solutions and sustainable practices in modern facility management
  • Creating and maintaining safety protocols — documenting chemical usage, accident reporting, and safety audit procedures

Module 4: Waste Management & Environmental Hygiene

📚 Topics Covered

  • Waste segregation at source — understanding dry waste, wet waste, hazardous waste, and proper segregation in facilities
  • Waste collection and storage procedures — appropriate containers, placement, frequency of collection, and preventing overflow
  • Biomedical waste management — special handling for healthcare facilities, segregation of infectious materials, and disposal protocols
  • Recyclable waste management — identifying recyclable materials, storage, and coordination with recycling agencies in India
  • Sanitary waste disposal — handling of sanitary pads, diapers, and similar items in office restrooms and public facilities
  • Composting and organic waste management — understanding composting process and its benefits in facility management
  • Pest control and prevention — identifying common pests, prevention measures, and when to call professional pest control services
  • Odor control and air quality management — ventilation practices, use of air fresheners, and maintaining pleasant facility environment
  • Spillage and emergency cleanup — procedures for handling chemical spills, food spills, and other accidents in workplace
  • Documentation and waste management audit — maintaining records, tracking waste disposal, and compliance with environmental regulations

Module 5: Infection Control & Hygiene Standards

📚 Topics Covered

  • Principles of infection control — understanding pathogen transmission, cross-contamination prevention, and hygiene best practices
  • Hand hygiene protocols — proper handwashing techniques, timing, and importance in preventing workplace infections
  • Surface disinfection and sanitation — effective disinfection procedures, contact time requirements, and coverage of high-touch areas
  • Restroom hygiene and sanitation — maintaining cleanliness standards, preventing bacterial and viral spread in shared facilities
  • Food service area cleaning — special hygiene requirements for pantries, kitchens, and food storage areas in offices
  • Post-COVID cleaning standards — enhanced disinfection protocols, sanitization frequency, and new hygiene practices in facilities
  • Handling of bodily fluids and biohazards — safe cleanup procedures, appropriate PPE, and disposal methods in healthcare facilities
  • Microfiber cloth and modern cleaning tools — benefits of microfiber, proper use, washing, and maintenance of modern cleaning equipment
  • Hygiene audit and quality checks — conducting cleanliness inspections, identifying gaps, and implementing corrective measures
  • Training staff on hygiene standards — communicating hygiene requirements, demonstrating proper techniques, and ensuring compliance

Module 6: Professional Development & Facility Operations Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Customer service and guest interaction — greeting visitors professionally, handling complaints, and maintaining positive relationships
  • Team coordination and reporting — working with supervisors, communicating issues, and coordinating with other departments in facility
  • Record keeping and documentation — maintaining cleaning logs, equipment maintenance records, and incident reporting in facilities
  • Inventory management of supplies — tracking cleaning supplies, ordering materials, and managing inventory efficiently
  • Cost control and resource optimization — using supplies efficiently, reducing wastage, and contributing to cost-effective facility operations
  • Seasonal cleaning and special events — preparing facilities for special occasions, seasonal maintenance, and handling increased cleaning demands
  • Maintenance coordination — identifying maintenance issues, reporting repairs needed, and coordinating with maintenance team
  • Quality assurance and performance standards — understanding facility standards, meeting quality benchmarks, and continuous improvement
  • Career advancement opportunities — progression to supervisor, housekeeping manager, and specialized roles in facility management
  • Latest trends in facility management — automation in cleaning, smart facility management systems, and future opportunities in India

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required — open to all candidates
  • Already working as housekeeping staff? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Driving License)
  • Medical fitness certificate may be required for some facility types
  • Applicable for all candidates across India regardless of background or location

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Demonstrate professional cleaning techniques for various office and facility surfaces using appropriate tools and chemicals
  • Apply infection control and hygiene standards to prevent disease transmission in workplace environments
  • Handle, store, and dispose of cleaning chemicals safely following Indian regulations and facility protocols
  • Segregate, manage, and dispose of different types of waste according to environmental and facility management standards
  • Perform customer service duties professionally and communicate effectively with colleagues and supervisors
  • Maintain facility cleanliness and safety standards while managing time and resources efficiently in daily operations
