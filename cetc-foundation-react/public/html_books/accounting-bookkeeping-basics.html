Accounting & Bookkeeping Basics

📂 Sector: Business, Retail & Finance 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months 🕐 Hours: 180 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the fundamentals of accounting and bookkeeping to manage financial records for small businesses, retail shops, and trading companies. This practical course teaches you how to maintain accounts, prepare financial statements, and handle daily cash transactions—essential skills for any business setup in India.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Accounting & Business Finance

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to accounting—what is it and why businesses need it — Understanding the role of accountants and bookkeepers in Indian businesses
  • Difference between accounting and bookkeeping — When to use each, and how they work together
  • Basic business concepts—assets, liabilities, equity, income, and expenses — Real examples from Indian retail and trading
  • Types of business structures in India—Sole proprietorship, Partnership, Private Limited Company — How accounting differs for each
  • Accounting principles and concepts—GAAP, materiality, consistency, going concern — Applied to Indian business context
  • Financial statements overview—Balance Sheet, Profit & Loss, Cash Flow — What each statement shows
  • Accounting equation—Assets = Liabilities + Equity — Practical applications
  • Introduction to GST and its impact on bookkeeping — How GST affects daily transactions
  • Introduction to accounting software—Tally, GST Suvidha Provider basics — Overview only at this stage
  • Career opportunities in accounting and bookkeeping in India — Job market, salary expectations, growth paths

Module 2: Double-Entry System & Journal Entries

📚 Topics Covered

  • Double-entry bookkeeping principle—why every transaction has two sides — Foundation of modern accounting
  • Debit and credit concept—understanding which accounts increase and decrease — Rules for different account types
  • Chart of Accounts—creating and organizing accounts for a business — Setting up for a small retail shop
  • Transaction analysis—identifying which accounts are affected — Step-by-step process with examples
  • Journal preparation—recording transactions in chronological order — Format and procedure
  • Journal entries for common transactions—sales, purchases, payments, receipts — Real examples from Indian businesses
  • Special journals—Sales Journal, Purchase Journal, Cash Receipt Journal, Cash Payment Journal — When and how to use them
  • Narration in journal entries—writing clear descriptions of transactions — Best practices for clarity
  • Compound journal entries—transactions affecting more than two accounts — Examples and practice
  • Recording GST in journal entries—Input Tax Credit, Output Tax, IGST, SGST, CGST — Practical application

Module 3: Ledger, Posting & Trial Balance

📚 Topics Covered

  • Ledger account structure—T-account format and modern ledger sheets — Understanding debit and credit sides
  • Posting from journal to ledger—systematic transfer of entries — Maintaining proper references
  • Balancing ledger accounts—calculating opening balance, closing balance — Monthly and yearly procedures
  • Ledger for different account types—Personal, Real, Nominal — Specific treatment for each
  • Sub-ledgers and subsidiary books—maintaining detailed records for debtors and creditors — Importance for control
  • Trial Balance preparation—extracting all account balances — Purpose and limitations
  • Errors that affect trial balance—identifying and correcting them — Common mistakes in Indian bookkeeping
  • Errors that don't affect trial balance—compensating errors, omissions — How to find and fix them
  • Suspense account—temporary account for unresolved differences — When and how to use
  • Reconciliation of subsidiary books with main ledger — Ensuring accuracy and control

Module 4: Cash & Bank Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Cash accounting—recording all cash inflows and outflows — Daily cash management in retail
  • Cash book maintenance—single column, two column, three column formats — When to use each format
  • Petty cash system—managing small daily expenses — Setting up and maintaining petty cash
  • Bank account operations—deposits, withdrawals, cheques, standing orders — Indian banking procedures
  • Cheque handling—receiving cheques, issuing cheques, cheque clearance — Banking regulations and timelines
  • Bank reconciliation—matching bank statement with cash book — Monthly reconciliation procedure
  • Identifying reconciling items—cheques in transit, deposits in transit, bank charges, interest — Common differences
  • Preparing bank reconciliation statement—step-by-step process — Practical example with Indian bank statement
  • Online banking and digital payments—UPI, NEFT, RTGS, mobile banking — Modern transaction recording
  • GST on banking services—TDS on banking transactions — Recording in accounts

Module 5: Financial Statements & Final Accounts

📚 Topics Covered

  • Trial Balance to Final Accounts—the complete process — Step-by-step flow
  • Adjusting entries—accruals, prepayments, depreciation, bad debts — When and why they're needed
  • Preparing Trading Account—calculating gross profit — For retail and trading businesses
  • Preparing Profit & Loss Account—calculating net profit — All expenses and income
  • Preparing Balance Sheet—showing financial position — Assets, Liabilities, Equity classification
  • Classification of assets—current and fixed assets — Indian accounting standards
  • Classification of liabilities—current and long-term liabilities — Balance sheet presentation
  • Understanding working capital—current assets minus current liabilities — Importance for business health
  • Schedules and notes to accounts—detailed breakdowns of balance sheet items — Required disclosures
  • Comparative financial statements—year-on-year analysis — Identifying trends and changes

Module 6: GST, Compliance & Accounting Software Basics

📚 Topics Covered

  • GST overview—what is GST, who needs to register, registration process — Indian tax system basics
  • GST rates and classifications—different rates for different goods and services — Common examples
  • Input Tax Credit (ITC)—claiming credit on purchases — Eligibility and restrictions
  • GST invoice requirements—what details must be included — Mandatory fields and formats
  • Recording GST transactions—separating tax from value — Practical journal entries
  • GST returns preparation—GSTR-1, GSTR-3B basics — Filing timelines and requirements
  • GST compliance calendar—filing deadlines, payment dates — Important dates for businesses
  • Introduction to Tally ERP 9—basic navigation and menu structure — Overview of features
  • Entering master data in Tally—creating companies, ledgers, stock items — Practical demonstration
  • Recording transactions in Tally—sales, purchases, payments — Basic data entry

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior accounting experience required
  • Basic numeracy and literacy skills essential
  • Already working in accounting? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid photo ID proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, Passport) required at registration
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and foreign nationals with valid visa
  • No upper age limit—career changers welcome

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand fundamental accounting principles and the accounting equation applicable to Indian businesses
  • Record business transactions using double-entry system with proper journal entries and GST treatment
  • Maintain ledger accounts, post transactions, balance accounts, and prepare trial balance accurately
  • Perform cash and bank reconciliation, manage petty cash, and handle cheque operations
  • Prepare final accounts including Trading Account, Profit & Loss Account, and Balance Sheet with adjustments
  • Understand GST basics, maintain GST-compliant records, and apply Input Tax Credit principles
  • Use basic accounting software (Tally) for recording transactions and maintaining accounts
  • Identify and correct accounting errors and maintain internal controls in bookkeeping
