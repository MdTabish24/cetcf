Borewell & Submersible Pump Operator

📂 Sector: Plumbing, Sanitation & Water 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the skills to operate, maintain, and troubleshoot borewells and submersible pumps - essential equipment for water supply in Indian homes, farms, and industries. This foundation course equips you with practical knowledge to become a reliable pump operator and earn ₹10,000-₹25,000 monthly in a high-demand sector.

Module 1: Borewell Systems & Water Sources Fundamentals

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to borewell technology — What is a borewell, why it's needed in India, types of borewells (shallow, medium, deep)
  • Groundwater basics — Water table, aquifers, seasonal variations, and how borewells tap into groundwater
  • Borewell drilling process — Drilling methods, bore diameter standards (4-6 inches common), depth determination, and soil testing
  • Borewell design & construction — Casing pipes, screens, gravel packing, and sealing to prevent contamination
  • Water quality assessment — Testing pH, hardness, salinity, and identifying iron/fluoride content in borewell water
  • Government regulations & permissions — CGWB guidelines, state water board approvals, and legal requirements for borewell registration
  • Site selection for borewells — Distance from septic tanks, soil composition analysis, and feasibility studies
  • Common borewell problems — Sand pumping, water depletion, clogging, and preventive maintenance strategies

Module 2: Submersible Pump Mechanics & Components

📚 Topics Covered

  • Types of submersible pumps — Centrifugal pumps, turbine pumps, jet pumps, and their applications in India
  • Pump motor basics — Single-phase vs three-phase motors, horsepower ratings (0.5 HP to 5 HP common), and power consumption
  • Internal pump components — Impeller, diffuser, suction strainer, discharge valve, and bearing assembly
  • Electrical components — Capacitor function, thermal overload protection, starter switches, and cable specifications
  • Pump specifications & performance — Understanding discharge (liters/hour), head (meters), efficiency, and duty cycles
  • Pump selection criteria — Matching pump capacity to water requirement, borewell depth, and power availability
  • Submersible vs surface pumps — Advantages, disadvantages, and when to use each type in Indian contexts
  • Quality standards & brands — ISI-marked pumps, popular Indian brands (Grundfos, Jain, Kirloskar, Crompton), and durability factors
  • Pump installation requirements — Foundation preparation, alignment, cable management, and earthing specifications
  • Warranty & documentation — Understanding pump warranty, maintenance records, and spare parts availability

Module 3: Installation, Setup & Commissioning

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pre-installation checklist — Inspecting borewell condition, verifying pump specifications, and gathering tools
  • Pump lowering techniques — Using rope/cable, avoiding cable damage, and safe handling procedures
  • Cable connection & earthing — Proper cable sizing (1.5mm² to 6mm² based on load), color coding, and safety earthing
  • Discharge pipe assembly — Selecting PVC/MS pipes, thread connections, check valves, and pressure gauges
  • Pressure tank installation — Sizing tanks (100-500 liters), connection points, and air pressure settings
  • Electrical connections — Connecting motor to switchboard, understanding starter operation, and phase checking
  • Commissioning procedure — Priming the pump, running initial test, checking discharge, and monitoring pressure
  • Water tank filling protocols — First fill water quality check, chlorination, and tank cleaning before use
  • Performance testing — Measuring discharge rate, monitoring voltage/current, and recording baseline data
  • Safety checks before operation — Checking all connections, verifying earthing, and confirming emergency stop function
  • Commissioning documentation — Completing installation certificates and recording all technical parameters

Module 4: Daily Operation & Monitoring Practices

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pre-operation checks — Inspecting cables, checking water level, verifying electrical supply, and confirming tank status
  • Starting procedures — Priming steps, starter operation, monitoring initial flow, and checking for abnormal sounds
  • Operating controls — Understanding pressure switches, timer controls, and manual on/off operations
  • Monitoring during operation — Observing discharge quality, listening for vibrations, checking temperature, and recording run time
  • Water level management — Using float switches, understanding cut-in/cut-out pressures, and preventing dry running
  • Pressure monitoring — Reading gauges correctly, understanding safe pressure ranges (2-4 kg/cm² typical), and pressure relief
  • Power management — Monitoring voltage fluctuations, understanding load demand, and managing electricity consumption
  • Seasonal adjustments — Adapting operations for summer water scarcity, monsoon overflow, and winter temperature changes
  • Record keeping — Maintaining daily operation logs, recording water quantity, and noting any irregularities
  • Safety during operation — Never opening covers while running, keeping children away, and emergency shutdown procedures

Module 5: Maintenance, Troubleshooting & Fault Diagnosis

📚 Topics Covered

  • Preventive maintenance schedule — Weekly checks, monthly servicing, seasonal inspection, and annual overhaul timing
  • Cable & electrical maintenance — Checking insulation, cleaning connections, checking earthing resistance, and replacing worn cables
  • Motor maintenance — Listening for bearing noise, checking temperature, monitoring current draw, and replacing brushes if needed
  • Pump impeller cleaning — Identifying sand/silt accumulation, flushing procedures, and when to extract pump for cleaning
  • Check valve maintenance — Testing one-way function, replacing faulty valves, and preventing backflow issues
  • Pressure tank servicing — Draining sediment, checking air pressure, replacing air valve, and inspecting for corrosion
  • Common faults & solutions — No water discharge (causes: dry borewell, blocked suction, faulty check valve), low discharge (sand in impeller, worn impeller), motor not starting (power failure, capacitor fault, thermal overload), noisy operation (cavitation, bearing wear, misalignment)
  • Electrical troubleshooting — Testing continuity, checking capacitor with multimeter, verifying phase sequence, and identifying short circuits
  • Water quality issues — Addressing sand in water, handling iron-stained water, and managing hard water problems
  • Spare parts identification — Recognizing common replacements (capacitor, check valve, suction strainer, impeller), sourcing authentic parts, and cost estimation

Module 6: Safety, Environment & Professional Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Electrical safety standards — Understanding voltage hazards, proper earthing importance, lockout-tagout procedures, and PPE requirements
  • Water safety & hygiene — Preventing contamination, chlorination basics, storage tank cleaning, and water quality testing methods
  • Environmental responsibility — Sustainable groundwater extraction, preventing over-pumping, and respecting water conservation rules
  • Occupational health & safety — Managing repetitive strain, lifting techniques, heat stress prevention, and first aid awareness
  • Personal protective equipment — Gloves, safety shoes, helmets for working at heights, and eye protection when handling chemicals
  • Emergency procedures — Handling electrical shocks, managing water overflow, shutdown during power surges, and reporting accidents
  • Professional ethics & customer service — Punctuality, honest diagnosis, transparent pricing, and maintaining confidentiality
  • Business skills for self-employment — Calculating service charges (₹500-₹2000 per service visit typical), creating quotations, maintaining client relationships, and building reputation
