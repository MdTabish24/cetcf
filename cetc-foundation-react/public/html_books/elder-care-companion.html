Elder Care Companion

📂 Sector: Child Care & Domestic Services 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Become a skilled Elder Care Companion and provide compassionate, professional support to senior citizens. This foundation course equips you with essential skills in personal care, health monitoring, mobility assistance, and emotional support—preparing you for a rewarding career in India's fast-growing elderly care sector.

Module 1: Introduction to Elder Care & Professional Ethics

📚 Topics Covered

  • Understanding elder care sector in India — current demand, employment opportunities, and growth trends
  • Role and responsibilities of an Elder Care Companion — daily duties and expectations
  • Professional ethics and code of conduct — confidentiality, respect, and dignity of elders
  • Understanding aging process — physical, emotional, and social changes in seniors
  • Legal and regulatory framework — rights of elderly persons in India, caregiver responsibilities
  • Communication skills for elder care — speaking clearly, active listening, and patience
  • Cultural sensitivity and family dynamics — working with diverse Indian families
  • Health and safety standards — infection control, hygiene protocols, and workplace safety
  • Introduction to common age-related conditions — arthritis, diabetes, hypertension, dementia basics
  • Personal grooming and professional appearance — dress code and hygiene standards for caregivers

Module 2: Personal Care & Hygiene Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Bathing and showering techniques — safe water temperature, slip prevention, dignity maintenance
  • Toileting assistance — using commodes, bedpans, and maintaining privacy
  • Hair care and grooming — shampooing, combing, nail care for seniors
  • Oral hygiene and denture care — brushing, flossing, cleaning dentures properly
  • Skin care and pressure ulcer prevention — identifying risk areas, moisturizing, massage techniques
  • Menstrual and incontinence management — handling with dignity and proper disposal methods
  • Dressing and undressing assistance — choosing appropriate clothing for comfort and mobility
  • Foot care and nail trimming — preventing infections and maintaining comfort
  • Personal hygiene during illness — managing bedridden patients and infectious conditions
  • Waste disposal and environmental hygiene — proper sanitation and cleaning protocols
  • Handling special needs — caring for paralyzed, partially disabled, or post-surgery patients

Module 3: Mobility, Safety & Physical Assistance

📚 Topics Covered

  • Body mechanics and safe lifting — preventing back injuries, proper posture, weight distribution
  • Transfer techniques — bed to chair, chair to toilet, safe handling without injury
  • Walking assistance — using walkers, canes, and crutches correctly
  • Fall prevention strategies — identifying hazards, removing obstacles, using safety equipment
  • Range of motion exercises — passive and active movements for joint flexibility
  • Positioning and turning — preventing bedsores, using pillows and cushions effectively
  • Wheelchair management — pushing, braking, transferring safely
  • Stairs and ramps navigation — assisting seniors on stairs and uneven surfaces
  • Emergency response — recognizing falls, strokes, heart attacks, and immediate first aid
  • Mobility aids selection and use — walkers, canes, grab bars, and accessibility modifications
  • Bed exercises and rehabilitation — gentle movements for bedridden patients

Module 4: Health Monitoring & Nutrition Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Vital signs monitoring — taking temperature, pulse, blood pressure, and respiration rate
  • Understanding vital signs — normal ranges and when to alert medical professionals
  • Medication administration support — reminding, organizing, and recording medication schedules
  • Common medications for seniors — understanding side effects and interactions
  • Nutrition for elderly — balanced diet, portion sizes, and age-appropriate foods
  • Feeding assistance — safe swallowing, preventing choking, managing dietary restrictions
  • Hydration importance — encouraging water intake, recognizing dehydration signs
  • Managing diabetes in seniors — diet management, recognizing hypoglycemia symptoms
  • Managing hypertension and heart conditions — low-salt diet, activity monitoring
  • Dealing with loss of appetite — encouraging eating, making food appealing
  • Special dietary needs — vegetarian, religious, and medical diet requirements in Indian context

Module 5: Emotional Support, Communication & Mental Health

📚 Topics Covered

  • Understanding loneliness and isolation in elderly — emotional needs and psychological impact
  • Building trust and rapport — creating safe, comfortable relationships with seniors
  • Active listening techniques — showing empathy, validating feelings, and responding appropriately
  • Managing difficult behaviors — patience with irritability, confusion, and mood changes
  • Recognizing depression in seniors — warning signs and when to seek professional help
  • Dementia care basics — understanding memory loss, confusion, and behavioral changes
  • Communication with dementia patients — simple language, repetition, and visual cues
  • Managing anger and frustration — staying calm, de-escalation techniques
  • Reminiscence therapy — sharing memories, storytelling, and connecting with past
  • Encouraging social activities — games, conversations, family visits, and entertainment
  • Spiritual and cultural needs — respecting religious practices, festivals, and traditions
  • Recognizing abuse and reporting — identifying signs of physical, emotional, or financial abuse

Module 6: Practical Skills, Documentation & Professional Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Care plan understanding — reading and following individualized care instructions
  • Daily care documentation — recording activities, observations, and health changes
  • Maintaining care records — keeping accurate notes on food intake, medications, activities
  • Communicating with family members — updates, concerns, and coordinating care
  • Working with healthcare professionals — nurses, doctors, and therapists collaboration
  • Infection control and hygiene protocols — handwashing, using PPE, preventing disease spread
  • Emergency preparedness — knowing emergency contacts, procedures, and first aid
  • Technology for elder care — using call bells, alert systems, and monitoring devices
  • Managing multiple seniors — time management and prioritizing care tasks
  • Self-care for caregivers — preventing burnout, managing stress, and maintaining own health
  • Career advancement opportunities — further certifications, specializations, and skill development
  • Practical role-play scenarios — real-world situations, problem-solving, and decision-making

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (18 years preferred for certain job placements)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required — beginners welcome
  • Already working as elder care staff? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Physical fitness to perform care duties (no serious mobility issues)
  • Applicable for all candidates across India regardless of caste, religion, or gender
  • Willingness to work with elderly persons and commitment to care ethics

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Provide safe, dignified personal care assistance to elderly individuals including bathing, toileting, and dressing
  • Monitor vital signs, recognize health changes, and communicate effectively with healthcare professionals
  • Assist seniors with mobility using proper body mechanics and safety techniques to prevent injuries
  • Manage nutrition, hydration, and medication support while respecting dietary preferences and restrictions
  • Offer emotional support, recognize mental health concerns like depression and dementia, and communicate with empathy
  • Maintain professional standards, documentation, and infection control while adhering to ethical and legal responsibilities

💼 Career Paths

Home-Based Elder Care Companion
Work directly in seniors' homes providing daily care, companionship, and assistance with activities of daily living
Senior Care Facility Staff Member
Work in old age homes, nursing homes, or assisted living facilities with multiple residents and structured schedules
Hospital Elder Care Assistant
Support elderly patients in hospitals during recovery, post-operative care, and rehabilitation programs
Private Elder Care Entrepreneur
Start your own elder care service, hire and train staff, manage multiple clients and build a care business
Corporate Wellness Program Coordinator
Manage elder care benefits and wellness programs for corporate employees' aging parents
Specialized Dementia Care Specialist
Provide specialized care for dementia and Alzheimer's patients with advanced training and certification
Live-in Caregiver (Urban Families)
Live with senior citizens or families providing 24/7 care support in metro cities and urban areas
Senior Care Trainer & Supervisor
Train new caregivers, supervise care teams, and ensure quality standards in care facilities after gaining experience
