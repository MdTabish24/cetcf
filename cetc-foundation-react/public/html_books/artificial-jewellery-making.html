Artificial Jewellery Making

📂 Sector: Handicrafts & Creative Arts 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹500
Master the art of creating stunning artificial jewellery pieces using traditional and modern techniques. This hands-on foundation course teaches you everything from basic wire-work to advanced stone-setting, preparing you for a rewarding career in India's booming fashion jewellery industry worth over ₹50,000 crores annually.

Module 1: Introduction to Artificial Jewellery & Industry Fundamentals

📚 Topics Covered

  • History and evolution of artificial jewellery in India — from traditional to contemporary designs
  • Indian artificial jewellery market overview — market size, growth trends, and consumer demand patterns
  • Types of artificial jewellery — costume jewellery, fashion jewellery, imitation jewellery, and ethnic wear accessories
  • Materials used in artificial jewellery — metals (brass, copper, alloy), stones (glass, acrylic, crystal), beads, and semi-precious materials
  • Quality standards and hallmarking requirements — BIS standards, purity marks, and customer expectations
  • Safety and health protocols in jewellery-making workshops — ventilation, chemical handling, and ergonomics
  • Jewellery-making tools and equipment — essential tools, their uses, maintenance, and cost-benefit analysis
  • Workspace setup and organization — designing an efficient home or small workshop setup with minimal investment
  • Career opportunities in artificial jewellery sector — employment, self-employment, and entrepreneurship options in India

Module 2: Basic Tools, Materials & Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Essential hand tools — pliers, wire cutters, files, hammers, and how to select quality tools within budget
  • Measuring and marking tools — rulers, calipers, dividers, and importance of precision in jewellery-making
  • Materials sourcing in India — wholesale markets (Delhi, Mumbai, Jaipur, Kolkata), online suppliers, and cost optimization
  • Beads and stones — types (glass, acrylic, wooden, metal, crystal), sizes, quality grading, and storage
  • Wire and metal strips — gauges, types (copper, brass, silver-plated), working properties, and selection
  • Adhesives and fasteners — appropriate glues, clasps, jump rings, findings, and their applications
  • Basic wire-working techniques — straightening, bending, coiling, and creating basic shapes
  • Cutting and filing techniques — cutting wires and materials cleanly, finishing edges, and surface preparation
  • Joining techniques — wire wrapping, twisting, crimping, and creating secure connections without soldering
  • Finishing and polishing — basic finishing methods, using sandpaper and polishing compounds for aesthetic appeal
  • Material testing and quality checks — identifying defects, checking durability, and ensuring customer satisfaction

Module 3: Jewellery Design Principles & Pattern Making

📚 Topics Covered

  • Design fundamentals — balance, symmetry, proportion, and visual hierarchy in jewellery design
  • Color theory and combinations — color psychology, trending color palettes, and Indian aesthetic preferences
  • Traditional Indian jewellery designs — studying regional styles (Rajasthani, Bengali, South Indian, Punjabi designs)
  • Contemporary and fusion designs — blending traditional with modern elements for urban Indian markets
  • Sketching and drafting — hand-drawing designs, creating technical sketches, and scale measurements
  • Digital design tools introduction — using basic software (Canva, Adobe Illustrator basics) for jewellery visualization
  • Pattern creation — making reusable patterns, templates, and production guides for consistency
  • Size and fit standards — understanding neck, wrist, ear measurements for different jewellery pieces
  • Trend forecasting — analyzing fashion weeks, social media trends, and seasonal demands in Indian markets
  • Customization techniques — adapting designs based on client preferences and cultural occasions (weddings, festivals)
  • Portfolio development — documenting designs and creating a visual record of your work
  • Sustainability in design — eco-friendly material choices and ethical jewellery-making practices

Module 4: Necklace & Pendant Making

📚 Topics Covered

  • Necklace types and styles — chokers, pendants, long chains, multi-strand, and layered necklaces
  • Chain-making techniques — creating chains using wire, beads, and ready-made components
  • Pendant creation — designing and crafting attractive focal pieces using various materials
  • Stone setting basics — secure placement of stones and beads in pendants without soldering
  • Clasp attachment — installing different types of clasps (hook, lobster, toggle) and ensuring durability
  • Beaded necklace assembly — stringing techniques, knot-tying, and preventing bead loss
  • Metal pendant work — creating metal settings, wire wrapping stones, and decorative elements
  • Tassel and fringe creation — adding movement and elegance to necklaces
  • Length and proportion calculations — determining correct lengths for different body types and occasions
  • Trending necklace designs in India — mangalsutra-inspired, temple jewellery styles, and modern adaptations
  • Quality assurance — testing clasp strength, checking for sharp edges, and ensuring comfort
  • Pricing necklaces — calculating material costs, labor, and setting competitive prices for Indian market

Module 5: Earrings, Bracelets & Ring Making

📚 Topics Covered

  • Earring types — studs, dangles, hoops, chandeliers, and jhumkas (traditional Indian earrings)
  • Earring post and back attachment — secure fastening methods and hypoallergenic options
  • Creating symmetrical pairs — techniques for making matching earrings consistently
  • Jhumka and traditional earring design — specialized techniques for Indian ethnic earrings
  • Bracelet styles — bangle, cuff, chain, beaded, and adjustable designs
  • Bracelet sizing and fitting — measuring wrist circumference and creating comfortable fits
  • Clasp mechanisms for bracelets — different closure types and their installation
  • Charm bracelet creation — adding interchangeable elements and customization options
  • Ring basics — sizing, shank creation, and stone setting
  • Ring design variations — cocktail rings, stackable rings, and adjustable rings
  • Resizing techniques — modifying ring sizes without compromising design
  • Bangle creation — working with rigid materials and decorative techniques
  • Comfort and durability — ensuring all pieces are wearable and long-lasting
  • Batch production methods — creating multiple pieces efficiently while maintaining quality

Module 6: Business Skills, Quality Control & Entrepreneurship

📚 Topics Covered

  • Business planning for jewellery makers — creating a business plan, identifying target customers, and market analysis
  • Cost calculation and pricing strategies — material costs, labor costs, overhead, and profit margins (20-40% typical in India)
  • Inventory management — sourcing materials, storage, and managing stock efficiently
  • Quality control standards — testing durability, checking for defects, and customer satisfaction metrics
  • Packaging and presentation — attractive packaging, branding, and adding value to products
  • Photography and product documentation — taking professional-looking photos for online sales and portfolios
  • Online selling platforms — using Flipkart, Amazon, Etsy, Instagram, and local marketplaces for sales
  • Customer service and feedback — handling orders, managing complaints, and building customer loyalty
  • GST and tax compliance for small businesses — understanding GST registration, invoice requirements, and record-keeping
  • Safety certifications and compliance — nickel-free testing, hypoallergenic claims, and legal requirements
  • Scaling production — moving from hobby to small business, hiring help, and increasing output
  • Marketing and branding strategies — social media presence, influencer collaborations, and word-of-mouth marketing in Indian context

  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior jewellery-making experience required
  • Already working in jewellery sector? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport, or School ID)
  • Applicable for all candidates including differently-abled persons
  • No upper age limit — senior citizens welcome
  • Must have access to basic tools and workspace (home-based setup acceptable)

💼 Career Paths

Jewellery Artisan (Self-Employed)
Create and sell your own jewellery designs through local shops, online platforms (Etsy, Instagram), or direct customer orders. Income depends on production volume and pricing strategy.
Jewellery Production Worker
Work in jewellery manufacturing units, fashion brands, or wholesalers in cities like Delhi, Mumbai, Jaipur, and Bangalore. Opportunities in mass production and quality control.
Online Jewellery Seller (E-commerce)
Sell jewellery on Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, or personal website. Build brand presence on Instagram and Facebook. Scale as demand grows.
Jewellery Trainer/Instructor
Teach jewellery-making in vocational institutes, community centers, or private studios. Conduct workshops and online courses for aspiring makers.
Retail Jewellery Staff
Work in jewellery shops, boutiques, or department stores. Assist customers, manage inventory, and handle sales. Knowledge of jewellery-making adds value.
Custom Jewellery Designer/Maker
Create bespoke pieces for weddings, festivals, and special occasions. Take custom orders and build a loyal clientele. Higher margins on personalized work.
Wholesale Jewellery Supplier
Manufacture jewellery in bulk and supply to retailers, boutiques, and online sellers. Establish relationships with shop owners and e-commerce platforms.
Fashion Jewellery Designer
Work with fashion brands, clothing companies, or jewellery houses designing collections. Combine fashion trends with jewellery-making expertise.
