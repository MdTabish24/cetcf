Arabic Language Basics

📂 Sector: Spoken Languages & Communication 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months 🕐 Hours: 180 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1200
Master the fundamentals of Arabic language with this comprehensive 12-week foundation course designed for Indian learners. Learn essential vocabulary, basic grammar, and conversational skills to communicate effectively in Arabic for business, travel, and personal growth.

Module 1: Arabic Alphabet, Pronunciation & Script Foundation

📚 Topics Covered

  • Arabic Abjad system - 28 letters and their names — Understanding the unique Arabic alphabet structure different from English
  • Letter forms - Initial, medial, final, and isolated positions — How letters change shape based on their position in words
  • Pronunciation guide - Guttural sounds, emphatic consonants, and vowels — Mastering sounds like 'Ayn, Ghayn, Kha that don't exist in English
  • Diacritical marks (Tashkeel) - Fatha, Damma, Kasra, Sukun, Shadda — Understanding vowel marks essential for correct pronunciation
  • Connecting letters and word formation — How Arabic letters connect differently than English, affecting handwriting and typing
  • Common pronunciation mistakes for Indian learners — Addressing specific challenges faced by Hindi/English speakers learning Arabic sounds
  • Introduction to Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) vs Colloquial Arabic — Understanding formal vs casual Arabic usage in different contexts
  • Writing practice - Tracing, copying, and independent writing of letters and simple words — Building muscle memory for proper letter formation
  • Listening and recognition exercises — Identifying letters and sounds in recorded audio materials
  • Cultural context of Arabic script — Historical significance and usage across 26 countries and 400+ million speakers

Module 2: Basic Vocabulary & Numbers

📚 Topics Covered

  • Greetings and politeness phrases — Assalamu Alaikum, Wa Alaikum Assalam, Sabah Al-Khair, Masaa Al-Khair with cultural context
  • Days of the week, months, and seasons — Al-Ahad through As-Sabt, calendar vocabulary for scheduling and business
  • Numbers 0-100 and counting system — Understanding Arabic number system and its differences from Western numbers
  • Time expressions - Hours, minutes, and daily routine vocabulary — Telling time in Arabic and scheduling conversations
  • Colors and common descriptive words — 20+ colors and basic adjectives for describing objects and people
  • Family members and relationships — Immediate family, extended family, and kinship terms important in Arab culture
  • Body parts and health-related vocabulary — Essential terms for medical situations and physical descriptions
  • Common food items and dining vocabulary — Traditional Arabic dishes, restaurant terms, and food-related phrases
  • Clothing and appearance vocabulary — Types of garments, fabrics, and appearance descriptions
  • Occupations and professions — Job titles relevant to Indian professionals working in Middle East or Arab countries
  • Building vocabulary through word families and root system — Understanding tri-literal roots (3-letter roots) that generate related words
  • Vocabulary retention techniques — Spaced repetition, flashcards, and mnemonic strategies for Indian learners

Module 3: Fundamental Grammar & Sentence Structure

📚 Topics Covered

  • Noun categories - Gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular, dual, plural) — Understanding how Arabic nouns change based on gender and number
  • Definite and indefinite articles - Al (the) and Tanwin (indefinite markers) — Core concept for proper Arabic sentence formation
  • Personal pronouns - Subject and object pronouns (I, you, he, she, we, they) — Essential for basic sentence construction
  • Verb basics - Present tense conjugation for common verbs — Learning how verbs change based on subject and time
  • Simple verb forms - Fa'ala (he did) and Yaf'alu (he does) patterns — Understanding basic verb conjugation patterns
  • Common verbs in daily context - To be (Kana), to have (Malaka), to go (Dhahaba), to come (Ja'a) — Most frequently used verbs in conversation
  • Basic sentence structure - SVO (Subject-Verb-Object) and VSO patterns — Understanding word order differences from English
  • Negation - Using Lam and Laysa for negative statements — How to form negative sentences correctly
  • Questions formation - Yes/no questions and question words (What, Where, When, Who, How) — Essential for conversational interaction
  • Adjective agreement - Making adjectives match nouns in gender and number — Critical for proper Arabic grammar
  • Prepositions and their uses - Fi (in), Ala (on), Min (from), Ila (to), Bi (with/in) — Directional and relational words
  • Introduction to verb tenses - Past, present, and future basic structures — Foundation for more complex grammar later

  • Self-introduction and personal information exchange — Name, nationality, profession, and background introduction phrases
  • Asking and answering questions about daily life — Family, work, hobbies, and routine activities in Arabic
  • Expressing needs and making requests politely — Asking for help, directions, information with appropriate politeness levels
  • Shopping and market vocabulary — Bargaining, prices, quantities, and merchant interactions common in Indian-Arab trade
  • Restaurant and food ordering phrases — Menu navigation, dietary preferences, and dining etiquette in Arab countries
  • Hotel and accommodation vocabulary — Booking, room types, amenities, and customer service interactions for travelers
  • Travel and transportation phrases — Taxi, bus, flight, directions, and navigation vocabulary for business travelers
  • Business communication basics — Meeting greetings, email openings, appointment scheduling relevant to Indian professionals
  • Emergency and safety phrases — Essential phrases for medical help, police, and urgent situations
  • Telephone conversation etiquette — Answering calls, leaving messages, and phone-specific vocabulary
  • Common expressions and idioms — Native-like phrases, colloquialisms, and cultural expressions for natural communication

📚 Topics Covered

  • Phonetic listening exercises - Distinguishing between similar Arabic sounds — Training ears to recognize subtle differences in pronunciation
  • Listening to native speakers - Dialogues, conversations, and authentic audio materials — Exposure to natural speech patterns and accents
  • Understanding different Arabic accents - MSA, Egyptian, Levantine, Gulf Arabic variations — Preparing for real-world interactions across regions
  • Comprehension of greetings and basic exchanges — Understanding native-speed conversations in common scenarios
  • Listening for specific information - Numbers, times, locations in spoken text — Practical skill for business and travel situations
  • Dialogue comprehension - Restaurant, hotel, market, and business scenarios — Real-world context-based listening practice
  • Accent and intonation patterns — Understanding emotional tone, stress patterns, and emphasis in Arabic speech
  • Listening to news and media content at beginner level — Building exposure to formal Arabic from news broadcasts and podcasts
  • Recognizing cognates and borrowed words — Understanding words borrowed from English or Hindi used in modern Arabic
  • Listening strategies for non-native speakers — Techniques to improve comprehension when you don't understand every word
  • Note-taking while listening — Developing skills to capture key information from audio materials
  • Audio-visual learning - Videos, documentaries, and cultural content — Combining visual context with audio for better comprehension

  • Comprehension of written Arabic - Understanding context and extracting meaning — Developing reading strategies for unfamiliar words
  • Writing simple sentences and paragraphs — Composing original content about personal topics
  • Email and formal letter writing basics — Professional correspondence format used in Arab business culture
  • Social media and informal writing — WhatsApp messages, SMS, and casual written communication in Arabic
  • Handwriting vs typed Arabic — Understanding differences between traditional handwriting and digital typing
  • Common writing mistakes and corrections — Addressing typical errors made by Indian learners
  • Vocabulary building through reading — Using context clues and dictionary skills for independent learning
  • Cultural texts and literature introduction — Reading about Arab culture, history, and traditions through simple texts

  • Certification exam preparation - Reading and writing sections overview — Understanding what to expect in final assessment
