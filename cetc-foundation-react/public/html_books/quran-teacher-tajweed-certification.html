Module 2: Makhraj and Sifaat — Articulation Points and Letter Characteristics

📚 Topics Covered

  • Makhraj definition and importance — 17 articulation points in the vocal apparatus
  • Throat letters (Halqiyyah) — detailed pronunciation of Alif, Ha, Ha-mza, Ayn, Ghain, Kha
  • Tongue letters (Lissaniyyah) — articulation of Lam, Noon, Ra, Ta, Dal, Thal, Seen, Zain, Sad, Dad, Taa, Dha
  • Lip letters (Shafawiyyah) — proper pronunciation of Ba, Fa, Meem
  • Sifaat — 11 characteristics of letters including voicing, emphasis, and nasalization
  • Heavy letters (Mufattaha) and light letters (Murahkhaha) — impact on recitation quality
  • Practical exercises for each Makhraj — mouth position, tongue placement, airflow
  • Common mispronunciation errors in Indian context — regional accent correction techniques
  • Recording and self-assessment methods — using audio tools for improvement
  • Group practice sessions — peer feedback and collaborative learning
  • Demonstration and imitation technique — learning from expert reciters
  • Individual correction sessions — personalized guidance for difficult letters

Module 3: Core Tajweed Rules — Noon, Meem, and Sukoon

📚 Topics Covered

  • Noon Sakinah and Tanween — four rules: Izhar, Idgham, Iqlab, Ikhfa
  • Izhar Halqi — when to pronounce Noon/Tanween clearly before throat letters
  • Idgham — complete merging of Noon/Tanween with following letters (Yameen, Waaw, Lam, Ra, Meem, Noon)
  • Idgham with Ghunnah — nasal resonance rules for specific letter combinations
  • Iqlab — converting Noon/Tanween to Meem before Ba letter
  • Ikhfa Shafawi — hiding Meem before Ba with nasalization
  • Meem Sakinah — three rules: Izhar Shafawi, Idgham Shafawi, Ikhfa Shafawi
  • Sukoon rules — definition and application across different letter combinations
  • Practical application in Surah recitation — identifying rules in authentic texts
  • Common mistakes in applying Noon/Meem rules — correction techniques
  • Speed and accuracy balance — maintaining Tajweed while increasing recitation pace
  • Testing competency — assessing mastery of core rules through recitation

Module 4: Advanced Tajweed Rules — Lam, Ra, and Hamza

📚 Topics Covered

  • Lam rules — Tafkheem (emphasis) and Tarqeeq (lightness) based on preceding vowel
  • Lam of Definite Article (Alif-Lam) — Izhar and Idgham rules with different letters
  • Ra rules — Tafkheem conditions and Tarqeeq requirements in various positions
  • Ra with Kasra — lightness rule and exceptions in Quranic text
  • Hamza — types, positions, and elimination rules (Hamza wasl vs Hamza qat)
  • Hamza wasl in Quran — specific surahs and word patterns where it applies
  • Hamza qat — when to pronounce the glottal stop clearly
  • Alif-Lam combination rules — comprehensive guide with examples from Quran
  • Practical recitation of complex Ayahs — applying multiple Tajweed rules simultaneously
  • Identifying rule combinations — analyzing Quranic verses for multiple overlapping rules
  • Regional variation handling — addressing dialect-influenced mispronunciations
  • Advanced practice sessions — challenging Ayahs from different Surahs

Module 5: Ghunnah, Madd, and Waqf Rules

📚 Topics Covered

  • Ghunnah — nasal resonance, its importance, and application in recitation
  • Ghunnah with Noon and Meem — timing and intensity of nasalization
  • Madd (elongation) — types and duration rules for proper vowel extension
  • Madd Asli (natural elongation) — three vowels (Alif, Waw, Ya) and their extension
  • Madd Fari (secondary elongation) — caused by Hamza or Sukoon following vowel
  • Madd Wajib Muttasil — when Hamza follows vowel in same word
  • Madd Jair Muttasil — when Hamza follows vowel in different word
  • Madd Badal — replacing vowel with Hamza in specific patterns
  • Waqf (stopping) — rules for proper pause between Ayahs and within verses
  • Waqf signs in Quran — understanding Mushaf markings (م، ج، ق، ع، ۝)
  • Proper breathing points — maintaining continuity while following Waqf rules
  • Combination practice — integrating Ghunnah, Madd, and Waqf in complete Surah recitation
