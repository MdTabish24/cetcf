Rainwater Harvesting Technician

📂 Sector: Environmental & Green Skills 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Learn to design, install, and maintain rainwater harvesting systems across residential, commercial, and agricultural properties. This hands-on course equips you with skills to capture and manage water resources efficiently, addressing India's water scarcity challenges while creating sustainable employment opportunities in the green sector.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Rainwater Harvesting & Water Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to rainwater harvesting — definition, importance, and benefits in Indian context
  • Water cycle and precipitation patterns — understanding monsoons and seasonal variations in India
  • Government policies and regulations — NRWM guidelines, state-level water policies, building codes
  • Types of rainwater harvesting systems — rooftop, surface runoff, and groundwater recharge systems
  • Assessment of water availability — calculating rainfall, catchment area, and potential yield
  • Environmental and health benefits — water conservation, groundwater recharge, reducing flooding
  • Cost-benefit analysis — investment required, maintenance costs, and long-term savings
  • Site assessment and planning — evaluating property layout, soil conditions, and water requirements
  • Common challenges in Indian regions — water quality issues, space constraints, seasonal variations
  • Case studies from India — successful RWH projects in urban and rural areas

Module 2: System Design & Components Selection

📚 Topics Covered

  • Catchment surfaces — roof materials, area calculation, slope determination, and efficiency factors
  • Conveyance systems — gutters, downpipes, filters, and material selection (PVC, GI, copper)
  • First-flush devices — design principles, automatic vs manual types, and installation techniques
  • Storage tanks — capacity calculation, types (underground, overhead, tanks), material selection
  • Filtration units — sand filters, charcoal filters, membrane filters, and multi-stage filtration systems
  • Pump selection — submersible pumps, surface pumps, capacity determination, and power requirements
  • Overflow and drain systems — design for excess water management and emergency drainage
  • Recharge systems — bore wells, recharge pits, trenches, and percolation tanks for groundwater
  • Piping and valves — sizing, layout design, quality standards, and leak prevention
  • System sizing calculator — using rainfall data, area, and water demand to design optimal systems
  • Budget planning and material sourcing — cost estimation, vendor selection, and quality assurance

Module 3: Installation & Construction Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pre-installation site preparation — ground leveling, foundation construction, and safety measures
  • Tank installation — excavation, base preparation, tank positioning, and waterproofing methods
  • Gutter and downpipe fitting — measurement, cutting, joining, slope adjustment, and securing
  • First-flush device installation — positioning, connection, and testing for proper functioning
  • Filtration unit assembly — layering materials, securing containers, inlet-outlet connections
  • Pump installation — positioning, electrical connections, priming, and initial testing
  • Piping connections — cutting, threading, jointing (solvent cement, PTFE tape), and pressure testing
  • Valve installation — check valves, isolation valves, gate valves, and proper orientation
  • Recharge pit and bore well construction — depth determination, casing, and filter media selection
  • Overflow pipe routing — safe discharge locations, anti-mosquito screening, and visibility
  • System pressure testing — water tightness checks, leak detection, and rectification
  • Final commissioning — system flushing, water quality testing, and user training

Module 4: Water Quality Management & Testing

📚 Topics Covered

  • Water quality parameters — pH, turbidity, TDS, hardness, and microbial contamination
  • Sampling and testing methods — collecting samples, field testing kits, and lab analysis procedures
  • Common contaminants — dust, bird droppings, algae, bacteria, and chemical pollutants
  • Filtration effectiveness — understanding filter media, pore size, and contaminant removal rates
  • Disinfection methods — UV treatment, chlorination, boiling, and combination approaches
  • Water quality standards — Indian Standards (IS 3025), WHO guidelines, and state regulations
  • Maintenance of filtration systems — cleaning schedules, media replacement, and performance monitoring
  • Seasonal water quality changes — monsoon effects, dry season challenges, and storage-related issues
  • Testing equipment — turbidity meters, pH meters, TDS meters, and basic microbiological tests
  • Record keeping and documentation — maintaining water quality logs and maintenance records

Module 5: Maintenance, Troubleshooting & System Optimization

📚 Topics Covered

  • Regular maintenance schedules — daily, weekly, monthly, and seasonal maintenance tasks
  • Cleaning gutters and downpipes — debris removal, algae prevention, and blockage clearing
  • Tank maintenance — sediment removal, interior cleaning, and structural integrity checks
  • Filter media replacement — identifying worn-out media, replacement procedures, and disposal
  • Pump maintenance — lubrication, seal inspection, performance checks, and troubleshooting
  • Common problems and solutions — leaks, low water pressure, algae growth, bad odors, and staining
  • Seasonal adjustments — monsoon preparation, dry season optimization, and winter considerations
  • System efficiency monitoring — water yield measurement, usage tracking, and optimization tips
  • Cost-effective maintenance practices — DIY checks, preventive care, and reducing operational costs
  • Documentation and reporting — maintenance logs, performance reports, and customer communication
  • Safety protocols — electrical safety, confined space entry, and chemical handling
  • Upgrading and retrofitting — adding features, improving efficiency, and system expansion

Module 6: Professional Practice, Safety & Entrepreneurship

📚 Topics Covered

  • Professional ethics and customer service — communication skills, reliability, and problem-solving
  • Safety standards and regulations — OSHA guidelines, Indian labor laws, and site safety protocols
  • Personal protective equipment — helmets, gloves, safety glasses, and proper usage
  • Electrical safety — handling pumps, avoiding electrocution, and grounding procedures
  • Tool safety and handling — proper use of spanners, cutters, measuring instruments, and equipment
  • Business setup for RWH technicians — registration, licensing, insurance, and compliance
  • Pricing and quotation — cost estimation, profit margins, and competitive market rates in India
  • Marketing and client acquisition — building reputation, online presence, and referral networks
  • Quality assurance and warranty — providing guarantees, handling complaints, and building trust
  • Government schemes and subsidies — AIBP, state RWH incentives, and accessing financial support

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior technical experience required
  • Already working as RWH technician? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhar, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Physical fitness to handle installation work (climbing, lifting, outdoor work)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand rainwater harvesting principles, system types, and water cycle in Indian climate conditions
  • Design appropriate RWH systems based on site assessment, rainfall data, and water requirements
  • Install and commission complete RWH systems with proper safety and quality standards
  • Test water quality and implement effective filtration and disinfection methods
  • Maintain and troubleshoot RWH systems, ensuring optimal performance and longevity
  • Establish professional practice as an independent RWH technician with business and entrepreneurship skills
