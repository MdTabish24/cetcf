Spoken English Proficiency Certificate

📂 Sector: Spoken Languages & Communication 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months 🕐 Hours: 180 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the fundamentals of English speaking with confidence and clarity. This foundation-level program equips you with essential communication skills for workplace success, customer interaction, and daily professional conversations in India's competitive job market.

Module 1: English Basics & Pronunciation Fundamentals

📚 Topics Covered

  • English alphabet, phonetics, and sound system — Understanding 44 sounds of English and Indian English accent differences
  • Vowels and consonants with practice exercises — Identifying pure vowels, diphthongs, and consonant clusters
  • Stress, intonation, and rhythm patterns — Word stress, sentence stress, and natural speech rhythm in English
  • Common pronunciation mistakes by Indian speakers — Fixing typical errors like 'th' sounds, 'r' vs 'l' distinction, and silent letters
  • Mouth positioning and articulation techniques — Physical exercises for clear and confident speech
  • Listening to native speakers and accent training — Audio exposure to British, American, and Indian English varieties
  • Self-recording and feedback practice — Using apps and devices to monitor your own pronunciation improvement
  • Phonetic transcription basics — Reading and writing phonetic symbols for accurate pronunciation
  • Tongue twisters and fluency exercises — Fun practice materials for building muscle memory in speaking
  • Introduction to English speaking confidence — Overcoming fear and building positive attitude toward English
  • Daily practice routines and habit formation — Creating sustainable learning habits for 12 weeks

Module 2: Basic Grammar & Sentence Construction

📚 Topics Covered

  • Parts of speech in simple English — Nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, prepositions, and conjunctions with examples
  • Present tense forms (Simple, Continuous, Perfect) — Understanding when and how to use each tense in daily conversation
  • Past tense forms and usage — Simple past, past continuous, and past perfect with practical examples
  • Future tense introduction — Simple future, going to, and present continuous for future plans
  • Subject-verb agreement rules — Common mistakes in matching subjects and verbs correctly
  • Basic sentence structure (SVO pattern) — Building simple, compound, and complex sentences step-by-step
  • Question formation techniques — How to ask yes/no questions, wh-questions, and tag questions naturally
  • Negation and negative sentences — Forming correct negative statements in different tenses
  • Articles (a, an, the) in context — Understanding when to use articles in English speaking
  • Prepositions of time, place, and direction — Using 'in', 'on', 'at', 'to', 'from' correctly in sentences
  • Common grammar errors and corrections — Identifying and fixing mistakes that Indian learners typically make
  • Grammar games and interactive practice — Engaging exercises to reinforce learning through play

Module 3: Vocabulary Building & Everyday Communication

📚 Topics Covered

  • Essential vocabulary for daily life (500 words) — Common words for food, clothing, family, home, and daily activities
  • Workplace vocabulary and professional terms — Office terminology, job titles, and business-related words used in India
  • Numbers, time, dates, and measurements — Speaking numbers naturally, telling time, and discussing quantities
  • Family relationships and personal information — Vocabulary for describing yourself, your family, and personal details
  • Food and dining vocabulary — Words for Indian and international cuisines, meals, and restaurant interactions
  • Shopping and money-related vocabulary — Bargaining, prices, currency, and retail transaction words
  • Travel and transportation vocabulary — Terms for vehicles, directions, booking, and journey-related communication
  • Collocations and word partnerships — Learning words that naturally go together (e.g., 'make a call', 'take a break')
  • Synonyms and antonyms for variety — Using different words to express similar meanings and avoiding repetition
  • Phrasal verbs in context — Common two-word verbs used in everyday English like 'get up', 'look after', 'put on'
  • Idioms and expressions for natural speech — Learning 30-40 common English idioms used in daily conversation
  • Vocabulary retention strategies — Memory techniques and spaced repetition for long-term vocabulary learning

Module 4: Listening Skills & Comprehension Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Active listening techniques and strategies — Focusing, noting key points, and preparing for listening tasks
  • Understanding different accents and speech speeds — Exposure to various English speakers and natural conversation pace
  • Listening to conversations and dialogues — Short exchanges in shops, offices, and social settings with comprehension questions
  • Listening for main ideas and details — Distinguishing between key information and supporting details in audio
  • Note-taking while listening — Developing shorthand techniques to capture important information during listening
  • Listening to instructions and directions — Understanding step-by-step guidance and following instructions in English
  • Audio from Indian English context — Listening to Indian news, podcasts, and conversations in Indian English
  • Movies, songs, and entertainment media — Using films, songs, and YouTube content for natural listening exposure
  • Telephone conversations and voice calls — Understanding phone etiquette and handling calls in English
  • Listening for emotion and tone — Recognizing feelings, sarcasm, and attitude in spoken English
  • Overcoming listening anxiety and building confidence — Strategies for managing listening difficulties and frustration
  • Regular listening practice schedule — Structured daily listening routines with progressive difficulty levels

Module 5: Conversational Skills & Interactive Speaking

📚 Topics Covered

  • Greeting and introduction in formal and informal settings — Meeting people, introducing yourself, and social etiquette
  • Small talk and casual conversations — Weather, news, hobbies, and light topics for social interaction
  • Asking for and giving information — Requesting directions, help, and advice in real-world situations
  • Expressing opinions and agreeing/disagreeing politely — Sharing views without being rude or offensive
  • Asking questions and clarification techniques — Saying 'I don't understand', asking for repetition, and seeking clarity
  • Storytelling and narration skills — Sharing personal experiences, anecdotes, and events in an engaging way
  • Telephone etiquette and professional calls — Making and receiving calls, taking messages, and handling business conversations
  • Role-playing common scenarios — Customer service, job interviews, doctor's office, and restaurant situations
  • Turn-taking and conversation flow — Knowing when to speak, when to listen, and keeping conversation natural
  • Handling difficult conversations — Disagreement, complaints, and sensitive topics with respect and clarity
  • Building conversation stamina — Speaking continuously for 2-3 minutes on a given topic without hesitation
  • Pair and group speaking activities — Interactive exercises with classmates and instructors for real practice

Module 6: Confidence Building & Practical Application

📚 Topics Covered

  • Overcoming speaking anxiety and nervousness — Psychological techniques and breathing exercises for calm speaking
  • Building self-confidence through positive reinforcement — Celebrating progress and managing self-doubt
  • Body language and non-verbal communication — Eye contact, posture, gestures, and facial expressions while speaking
  • Public speaking basics — Presenting ideas, speaking to groups, and managing stage fright
  • Presentation skills for workplace — Introducing topics, explaining concepts, and handling Q&A sessions
  • Interview preparation and practice — Common interview questions, answers, and professional communication
  • Handling mistakes and recovery techniques — What to do when you make errors and continuing naturally
  • Speed and fluency improvement — Speaking faster without sacrificing clarity and understanding
  • Accent reduction and clarity focus — Maintaining Indian English while improving intelligibility for diverse audiences
  • Real-world application projects — Practical tasks like recording videos, conducting interviews, and completing speaking challenges
  • Mock interviews and assessments — Full-length practice tests simulating actual speaking evaluation
  • Personal action plan for continued improvement — Setting goals and creating strategies for post-course learning

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior English speaking experience required
  • Open to all candidates including working professionals
  • Already working in a related field? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) direct exam
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport) required at registration
  • Access to basic audio equipment (headphones, microphone) for practice and assessment
  • Commitment to attend 80% of classes (minimum 144 hours out of 180)

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Speak English with clear pronunciation and natural intonation in everyday conversations
  • Understand and respond to spoken English at normal conversation speed
  • Use correct grammar and appropriate vocabulary in professional and social contexts
  • Introduce yourself, share information, and engage in meaningful dialogue confidently
  • Handle common workplace and customer service interactions in English effectively
  • Tell stories, express opinions, and explain ideas clearly without hesitation
