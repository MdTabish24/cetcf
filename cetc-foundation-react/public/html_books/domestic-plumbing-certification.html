Domestic Plumbing Certification

📂 Sector: Plumbing, Sanitation & Water 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the essential skills of domestic plumbing and become a certified professional ready to install, maintain, and repair water supply and drainage systems in residential buildings across India. This foundation course equips you with hands-on knowledge of pipes, fittings, tools, and safety practices used in everyday plumbing work.

Module 1: Introduction to Plumbing & Safety

📚 Topics Covered

  • Plumbing industry overview in India — scope, opportunities, and career growth in residential and commercial sectors
  • Types of plumbing work — water supply, drainage, sanitation, maintenance, and repair services
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) — safety helmets, gloves, masks, aprons, and footwear for plumbing tasks
  • Workplace safety rules — preventing accidents, handling hazardous materials, and emergency procedures
  • Tool safety and handling — proper use of wrenches, pipe cutters, drills, and hand tools
  • Common plumbing hazards — water-borne diseases, chemical exposure, electrical hazards, and confined spaces
  • First aid basics — treating cuts, burns, and minor injuries common in plumbing work
  • Health and hygiene practices — maintaining cleanliness while working with water and drainage systems
  • Legal compliance — understanding building codes and regulations for domestic plumbing in India
  • Communication and professionalism — customer interaction, punctuality, and work ethics

Module 2: Plumbing Materials & Tools

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pipe materials — GI pipes, PVC pipes, CPVC pipes, copper pipes, and their properties and uses
  • Pipe sizes and specifications — understanding nominal bore (NB) and wall thickness standards in India
  • Fittings and connectors — elbows, tees, unions, couplings, and their applications in water systems
  • Valves — ball valves, gate valves, check valves, and their functions in plumbing systems
  • Pipe cutting tools — manual cutters, hacksaw, and electric pipe cutters with safe operation
  • Pipe bending and threading — techniques for bending PVC and GI pipes, manual threading methods
  • Hand tools for plumbing — adjustable wrenches, pipe wrenches, pliers, screwdrivers, and measuring tools
  • Power tools introduction — electric drills, impact drivers, and safety precautions for powered equipment
  • Testing and measuring equipment — water pressure gauges, spirit levels, measuring tapes, and leak detection methods
  • Sealants and jointing materials — PTFE tape, plumber's putty, silicone sealants, and proper application techniques
  • Storage and maintenance of tools — organizing tools, preventing rust, and extending tool life
  • Sourcing materials locally — understanding Indian plumbing suppliers, quality checking, and cost estimation

Module 3: Water Supply Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Water supply sources in India — municipal water, bore wells, tankers, and rainwater harvesting systems
  • Types of water supply systems — gravity-fed systems, pump-based systems, and overhead tank arrangements
  • Piping layout and design — understanding branch lines, distribution pipes, and connection points in homes
  • Water pressure and flow rate — calculating pressure requirements, managing low-pressure issues in Indian homes
  • Pipe sizing calculation — selecting appropriate pipe diameters based on demand and flow rate
  • Installation of water supply lines — routing pipes through walls, under floors, and concealing techniques
  • Connection of water meters — understanding meter reading, installation, and maintenance
  • Taps and fixtures — kitchen taps, bathroom taps, washing machine connections, and garden hose connections
  • Overhead tanks and storage — tank capacity calculation, inlet and outlet pipe connections, overflow management
  • Water purification systems — basic understanding of filters, RO systems, and plumbing connections for these
  • Pressure regulators and boosters — managing water pressure in multi-story buildings and low-pressure areas
  • Troubleshooting water supply issues — addressing leaks, low pressure, contamination, and blockages

Module 4: Drainage & Sanitation Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Drainage system types — open drains, underground pipes, and septic tank systems used in Indian homes
  • Pipe materials for drainage — PVC pipes, cast iron pipes, and concrete pipes for different applications
  • Gradient and slope calculation — ensuring proper water flow in drainage pipes (minimum 1:50 slope)
  • Soil pipes and waste pipes — differentiating between grey water and black water drainage systems
  • Toilet connections — connecting water closets to drainage systems with proper sealing and slope
  • Sink and basin drainage — kitchen sink, bathroom basin, and shower drainage installation
  • Trap systems — understanding S-traps, P-traps, and their role in preventing foul odors
  • Ventilation pipes — installing vent stacks for proper drainage function and odor control
  • Septic tanks — basic working, maintenance, and plumbing connections for septic systems
  • Sump pits and pumping stations — installation and connection for basement drainage in urban homes
  • Manhole covers and inspection points — placement and installation for system maintenance access
  • Water-proofing around drains — preventing leaks and water seepage in bathrooms and kitchens

Module 5: Practical Pipe Fitting & Installation

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pipe cutting techniques — cutting GI pipes, PVC pipes, and copper pipes accurately with different tools
  • Threading GI pipes — manual threading process, die selection, and creating leak-proof connections
  • PVC pipe joining — solvent welding process, dry fitting, and achieving strong permanent joints
  • Pipe bending methods — cold bending for PVC, heat bending techniques, and avoiding pipe damage
  • Fitting connections — assembling elbows, tees, and couplings with proper tightening and alignment
  • Leak-proof jointing — using PTFE tape, pipe dope, and ensuring water-tight connections
  • Testing water supply lines — pressure testing procedures and identifying leaks before completion
  • Installation of kitchen plumbing — sink connection, tap installation, and under-counter pipe routing
  • Bathroom plumbing layout — toilet, basin, shower, and drainage pipe installation in bathrooms
  • Concealing pipes in walls — chasing walls, installing pipes in conduits, and aesthetic finishing
  • Connection to existing systems — joining new pipes to old systems and managing transitions
  • Quality checking and finishing — ensuring smooth operation, proper alignment, and professional appearance

Module 6: Maintenance, Troubleshooting & Career Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Routine maintenance procedures — regular inspection, cleaning, and preventive care for plumbing systems
  • Identifying leaks — detecting visible leaks, hidden leaks in walls, and water meter testing methods
  • Fixing common leaks — repairing leaking taps, joints, pipes, and tank connections
  • Clearing blockages — using plungers, pipe snakes, and chemical cleaners safely for blocked drains
  • Pipe corrosion and replacement — identifying corroded pipes and replacement procedures
  • Water quality issues — addressing discolored water, bad odor, and contamination problems
  • Winter maintenance — protecting pipes from freezing in cold regions and seasonal care
  • Emergency repairs — responding to burst pipes, major leaks, and water damage situations
  • Customer service skills — communicating with clients, explaining problems, and providing quotations
  • Business setup for plumbers — registering as self-employed, pricing services, and building customer base
  • Safety compliance documentation — maintaining records, certificates, and following regulations
  • Continuing education — staying updated with new materials, techniques, and industry standards in India

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or 18 years for employment in certain states)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior plumbing experience needed
  • Already working as a plumber? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required — Aadhar, PAN, voter ID, or driving license
  • Physical fitness — ability to work in various postures and carry tools
  • Applicable for all candidates including SC/ST/OBC and persons with disabilities

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand plumbing systems, materials, and tools used in Indian domestic installations
  • Safely cut, bend, thread, and join pipes using appropriate techniques and materials
  • Design and install water supply systems with proper pressure and flow management
  • Install drainage and sanitation systems with correct slope and ventilation
  • Identify and repair common plumbing problems including leaks and blockages
  • Provide professional service and build a sustainable plumbing business in India
