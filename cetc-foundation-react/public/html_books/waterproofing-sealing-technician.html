Waterproofing & Sealing Technician

📂 Sector: Construction & Building Trades 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the essential skills of waterproofing and sealing to become a highly demanded construction professional in India. This 1-month foundation course equips you with practical knowledge of modern waterproofing materials, techniques, and safety protocols used in residential, commercial, and industrial projects across the country.

Module 1: Introduction to Waterproofing & Sealing Industry

📚 Topics Covered

  • Overview of waterproofing in Indian construction — why it's critical for building longevity and safety
  • Types of water ingress problems — dampness, seepage, leakage in basements, roofs, and walls
  • Role of waterproofing technician — responsibilities and career scope in construction sector
  • Indian building codes and standards — IS 2645, IS 3689, IS 12627 and their requirements
  • Climate considerations in India — monsoon impact, temperature variations, and regional challenges
  • Common causes of water damage in buildings — poor construction, material failure, design flaws
  • Career opportunities in residential, commercial, industrial, and infrastructure projects
  • Safety culture in waterproofing work — importance of protective gear and work practices

Module 2: Waterproofing Materials & Products

📚 Topics Covered

  • Bituminous materials — coal tar, bitumen, modified bitumen and their properties
  • Liquid waterproofing membranes — acrylic, polyurethane, cement-based types and applications
  • Sheet membranes — EPDM, PVC, TPO, APP modified membranes and installation methods
  • Sealants and caulks — silicone, polyurethane, acrylic types and suitable applications
  • Admixtures and additives — waterproofing chemicals for concrete and mortar
  • Primers and bonding agents — purpose and application in different scenarios
  • Indian brands and suppliers — Pidilite, Sika, Basf, Fosroc, Cico and their product ranges
  • Material specifications and quality checks — testing for viscosity, coverage, durability
  • Storage and handling — proper methods to maintain material integrity on-site
  • Cost-benefit analysis — selecting right materials for different project budgets
  • Environmental and health considerations — eco-friendly products and safe handling
  • Reading product datasheets — understanding technical specifications and usage instructions

Module 3: Surface Preparation & Pre-Application Assessment

📚 Topics Covered

  • Moisture testing methods — calcium carbide test, moisture meter usage, damp assessment
  • Surface inspection techniques — identifying cracks, efflorescence, loose materials
  • Cleaning and preparation — removing dust, loose paint, algae, and contaminants
  • Crack repair methods — hairline cracks, structural cracks, and expansion joints
  • Substrate compatibility — concrete, brick, stone, metal surfaces and preparation needs
  • Leveling and smoothing — filling holes, leveling surfaces for uniform application
  • Primer application — choosing and applying correct primer for different substrates
  • Weather conditions assessment — temperature, humidity, wind effects on application
  • Concrete curing requirements — waiting periods before waterproofing application
  • Documentation and checklists — recording surface conditions and preparation done

Module 4: Application Techniques & Practical Methods

📚 Topics Covered

  • Brush application technique — proper strokes, coverage rates, thickness for liquid coatings
  • Roller application — selecting roller types, pressure, speed, and achieving uniform coverage
  • Spray application — equipment setup, pressure settings, safety precautions, and waste control
  • Trowel and putty knife methods — for sealants, fillers, and thick membrane application
  • Torch-on membrane installation — heating techniques, overlap management, safety protocols
  • Adhesive-based sheet membrane installation — using contact adhesive, bitumen adhesive
  • Mechanical fixing methods — using fasteners, caps, and washers for sheet membranes
  • Joint and corner treatment — special attention to vulnerable areas, overlaps, edges
  • Multi-coat application — layering techniques, drying times, number of coats required
  • Slope and drainage consideration — ensuring water flows away from protected areas
  • Thickness measurement — using wet film thickness gauge and dry film thickness gauge
  • Quality control during application — visual inspection, coverage verification
  • Common application mistakes — puddles, thin spots, missed areas and corrections
  • Time management on-site — productivity rates and scheduling work phases

Module 5: Waterproofing Specific Areas & Problem Solving

📚 Topics Covered

  • Roof waterproofing — flat roofs, sloped roofs, terraces and different techniques
  • Basement waterproofing — internal and external methods, sump pit design, drainage
  • Bathroom and wet area protection — shower areas, toilet floors, wall protection
  • Foundation and basement walls — external waterproofing, internal tanking methods
  • Expansion joints and movement joints — design, sealing, and maintenance
  • Pipe penetrations and service entries — sealing around pipes, conduits, and ducts
  • Window and door frames — preventing water ingress around openings
  • Terrace gardens and planters — root barrier waterproofing, drainage management
  • Parking decks and podiums — heavy-duty waterproofing for vehicle traffic
  • Troubleshooting common problems — blistering, peeling, seepage, efflorescence
  • Repair of failed waterproofing — identifying root cause and remedial solutions
  • Seasonal challenges — monsoon preparation, heat expansion, frost damage prevention

Module 6: Safety, Quality Control & Professional Practice

📚 Topics Covered

  • Personal protective equipment (PPE) — gloves, masks, goggles, safety shoes, clothing
  • Hazard identification — chemical exposure, fall risks, heat stress, toxic fumes
  • Safe handling of chemicals — storage, mixing, disposal of waterproofing materials
  • Ventilation and respiratory protection — working in enclosed spaces safely
  • First aid for chemical exposure — burns, inhalation, skin contact and immediate care
  • Fire safety — flammable materials handling, torch safety, emergency procedures
  • Working at heights — scaffolding safety, harness use, fall prevention on roofs
  • Tool and equipment safety — proper use and maintenance of application tools
  • Site housekeeping — waste management, spill cleanup, organized work area
  • Quality assurance checklist — coverage verification, thickness checks, visual inspection
  • Water testing and leak detection — simple methods to verify waterproofing effectiveness
  • Documentation and record-keeping — maintaining work logs and material records
  • Warranty and guarantee terms — understanding product guarantees and customer expectations
  • Professional ethics — punctuality, honest communication, respecting customer property
  • Customer communication — explaining work, managing expectations, handling complaints

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required — foundation level course
  • Already working as waterproofing technician? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhar, voter ID, or driving license)
  • Physical fitness required — ability to work at heights and with chemicals
  • Indian citizen or valid work permit holder
  • Applicable for all candidates regardless of gender, caste, or religion

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand waterproofing principles, Indian building codes, and climate-specific challenges in construction
  • Identify and select appropriate waterproofing materials and products based on project requirements and budget
  • Properly assess surfaces, conduct moisture testing, and prepare substrates for waterproofing application
  • Apply waterproofing coatings, membranes, and sealants using correct techniques and achieving quality standards
  • Troubleshoot common waterproofing failures and implement corrective solutions for different building areas
  • Follow safety protocols, maintain quality control, and communicate professionally with clients and team members
