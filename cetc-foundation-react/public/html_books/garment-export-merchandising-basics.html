Garment Export & Merchandising Basics

📂 Sector: Apparel Design, Pattern Making & Fashion Technology 📊 Level: Intermediate ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months 🕐 Hours: 180 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the complete cycle of garment merchandising and export operations in India's booming apparel industry. This course equips you with practical skills in costing, quality control, supplier management, and international shipping—essential for working with major export houses across Delhi, Tiruppur, Bangalore, and Mumbai.

Module 1: Garment Industry Basics & Export Framework

📚 Topics Covered

  • Indian apparel industry overview — production, exports, and major clusters (Tiruppur, Bangalore, Delhi NCR, Surat)
  • Types of garments — woven, knitted, and blended fabric garments
  • Export destinations — USA, EU, UK, Australia, and emerging markets
  • Key stakeholders — manufacturers, exporters, buyers, compliance agencies (DGFT, APEDA)
  • Global quality standards — ISO 9001, SEDEX, BSCI, and WRAP certifications
  • Tariff codes and HS classification — understanding 6-digit and 10-digit codes for garments
  • India's trade agreements — FTA benefits with ASEAN, RCEP, and bilateral partners
  • Role of merchandiser in export supply chain — responsibilities and career growth

Module 2: Garment Costing & Cost Sheet Preparation

📚 Topics Covered

  • Cost sheet structure — raw materials, labor, overheads, and profit margins
  • Fabric costing — GSM calculation, yield percentage, wastage allowance (5-8%)
  • Trims and accessories costing — buttons, zippers, labels, thread, elastic
  • Labor cost breakup — cutting, stitching, finishing, quality check time standards
  • Overhead allocation — factory rent, utilities, depreciation, supervisory cost
  • Price negotiation with buyers — margin analysis and competitive pricing
  • Cost sheet templates for different garment types — t-shirts, shirts, trousers, dresses
  • Currency fluctuation impact — USD/INR and hedging basics
  • Profitability analysis — FOB pricing, CIF pricing, and net profit calculation
  • Cost reduction techniques — process optimization and waste minimization
  • Costing software tools — basic Excel templates and industry-used systems
  • Case study — real cost sheets from Tiruppur and Delhi export houses

Module 3: Fabric Selection, Sourcing & Quality Standards

📚 Topics Covered

  • Fabric types — cotton, polyester, blends, and specialty materials (linen, modal)
  • Fabric specifications — GSM (grams per square meter), thread count, shrinkage percentage
  • Supplier evaluation — quality records, delivery reliability, certifications
  • Fabric testing standards — tensile strength, color fastness (ISO 105), shrinkage (ISO 6330)
  • Sustainable fabrics — organic cotton, recycled polyester, and eco-certifications (GOTS, OEKO-TEX)
  • Fabric dyeing and printing — types, color matching, and repeat accuracy
  • Supplier agreements — terms, penalties, and quality clauses
  • Bulk fabric inspection — sampling methodology and defect identification
  • Fabric storage and inventory management — humidity, temperature, and shelf life
  • Documentation — invoices, certificates of origin, and traceability records

Module 4: Production Planning & Sampling Process

📚 Topics Covered

  • Order-to-delivery timeline — lead times and critical path analysis
  • Production planning — batch sizing, capacity planning, and bottleneck identification
  • Sampling process — proto sample, approval sample, and bulk sample stages
  • Sample costing and approval cycles — typical 2-3 week timelines
  • Fit standards — size grading, measurement tolerance, and fit approval
  • Technical specifications — stitch type, seam strength, and construction details
  • Color approval process — Pantone matching and batch consistency
  • Pattern making for bulk production — marker making and fabric utilization
  • Production capacity assessment — hourly output and resource planning
  • Quality gates at each stage — pre-production, in-process, and final inspection
  • Delay management — causes and mitigation strategies
  • Communication with production team — specifications, timelines, and problem-solving

Module 5: Quality Control & Inspection Standards

📚 Topics Covered

  • AQL (Acceptable Quality Level) — 0, 1, 2.5, 4 levels and their application
  • Inspection methodology — random sampling and statistical quality control
  • Common defects — stitching, color variation, measurement deviation, pilling
  • Testing equipment — tensile tester, pilling machine, color fastness chamber
  • Lab testing standards — shrinkage, color fastness, tensile strength, tear strength
  • Final inspection checklist — measurement, appearance, and packaging quality
  • Defect documentation — photo evidence and corrective action reports
  • Buyer audit preparation — documentation and compliance records
  • Third-party lab certifications — SGS, INTERTEK, and local labs in India
  • Compliance with buyer standards — Walmart, H&M, Zara, and retailer requirements
  • Quality metrics reporting — defect rate percentage and trending
  • Continuous improvement — Kaizen and lean manufacturing principles

Module 6: Export Documentation & Compliance

📚 Topics Covered

  • Commercial Invoice — details, quantities, pricing, and buyer information
  • Packing List — itemization, carton numbering, and weight details
  • Certificate of Origin — Form A (GSP), Form B, and country-specific requirements
  • Bill of Lading — ocean freight documentation and shipping terms (FOB, CIF, DDP)
  • Export License — DGFT registration and product-specific licenses (if applicable)
  • Customs clearance — documentation at origin port and destination
  • Compliance certifications — SEDEX, BSCI, WRAP for ethical manufacturing
  • Child labor and forced labor declarations — buyer compliance requirements
  • Restricted substances testing — Azo dyes, heavy metals, and chemical compliance
  • Shipping terms — understanding Incoterms 2020 (FOB, CIF, DDP, CPT)
  • Insurance and risk management — cargo insurance and liability coverage
  • Port operations — container booking, documentation submission, and loading
  • Import-export codes — IEC registration and HS code classification

Module 7: Customer Communication & Order Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Order confirmation — PO details, specifications, and timeline agreement
  • Specification sheets — technical drawings, measurements, and material details
  • Communication protocols — email etiquette, response time standards, and escalation
  • Sample submission and feedback — approval cycles and revision management
  • Production updates — weekly progress reports and milestone tracking
  • Problem escalation — delay communication and alternative solutions
  • Shipment tracking — ETD (Estimated Time of Departure) and ETA updates
  • Invoice and payment terms — pro-forma invoice, LC (Letter of Credit), and payment conditions
  • Post-shipment support — claim handling and quality issue resolution
  • Customer relationship management — long-term buyer retention strategies

Module 8: Practical Project & Industry Simulation

📚 Topics Covered

  • End-to-end order simulation — from PO to shipment documentation
  • Complete cost sheet preparation — real buyer specifications and pricing
  • Supplier evaluation case study — selecting fabric and trim suppliers
  • Production timeline creation — Gantt chart and critical path analysis
  • Quality inspection report preparation — defect identification and corrective actions
  • Export document compilation — all required paperwork for a single shipment
  • Buyer communication exercise — responding to quality issues and delays
  • Costing optimization challenge — reducing cost while maintaining quality
  • Compliance audit preparation — documentation review and gap analysis
  • Industry visit debrief — insights from real export houses (Tiruppur, Delhi, or Bangalore)
  • Capstone project — managing a mock order from order receipt to final shipment
  • Presentation and viva — explaining decisions and demonstrating learning

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the Indian apparel export industry structure, major clusters, and global trade framework
  • Prepare accurate cost sheets with fabric, labor, and overhead calculations for competitive pricing
  • Source quality fabrics and trims, evaluate suppliers, and ensure compliance with international standards
  • Plan production timelines, manage sampling cycles, and coordinate between buyers and manufacturers
  • Conduct quality inspections using AQL standards, identify defects, and implement corrective actions
  • Prepare and manage complete export documentation including invoices, CoO, BoL, and compliance certifications
  • Communicate effectively with international buyers, manage orders, and resolve quality/delivery issues
  • Apply costing optimization and lean manufacturing principles to improve profitability and efficiency
