Barista & Coffee Making

📂 Sector: Food, Catering & Hospitality 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the art and science of coffee making with this intensive 1-month foundation course. Learn espresso extraction, milk frothing, latte art, and customer service skills to launch your career in India's rapidly growing specialty coffee industry. Get certified and start earning within 4 weeks.

Module 1: Coffee Fundamentals & Equipment Knowledge

📚 Topics Covered

  • Coffee plant origin and global varieties — Arabica vs Robusta, growing regions in India (Coorg, Chikmagalur, Nilgiris), flavor profiles and characteristics
  • Coffee bean processing methods — Washed, natural, pulped natural processes and how they affect taste and quality
  • Coffee roasting levels — Light, medium, dark roasts, understanding roast dates, freshness indicators, and storage best practices
  • Grinder types and operation — Burr grinders vs blade grinders, grind size consistency, maintenance and cleaning procedures
  • Espresso machine anatomy — Group head, portafilter, boiler, steam wand, water filtration systems, and safety features
  • Coffee scales and measurement tools — Using digital scales for precision, understanding grams and ratios for consistent shots
  • Water quality and filtration — Hardness, pH levels, importance of filtered water, descaling procedures for machines
  • Personal hygiene and food safety standards — FSSAI regulations, hand washing, apron care, preventing cross-contamination in coffee preparation

Module 2: Espresso Extraction & Shot Pulling Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Espresso fundamentals — Definition, pressure requirements (9 bars), extraction time (25-30 seconds), yield ratios (1:2 or 1:3)
  • Dosing and tamping — Correct dose measurements (18-20g for double shot), tamp pressure consistency, angle correction, channeling prevention
  • The perfect pull — Reading flow rate, identifying underextraction vs overextraction, adjusting grind size, recognizing crema quality
  • Single vs double shots — Difference in flavor, preparation techniques, when to use each for different drinks
  • Ristretto and lungo shots — Short strong shots vs long diluted shots, customer preferences, preparation methods
  • Shot timing and troubleshooting — Diagnosing weak shots, bitter shots, sour shots, and corrective measures
  • Consistency and repeatability — Maintaining same quality across 50+ shots daily, tracking variables, machine calibration
  • Pressure profiling basics — Understanding pre-infusion, maintaining consistent pressure, impact on extraction quality
  • Espresso machine operation — Daily startup procedures, temperature stability, group head flushing, safety protocols

Module 3: Milk Steaming, Frothing & Latte Art

📚 Topics Covered

  • Milk selection and properties — Whole milk vs low-fat, plant-based alternatives (oat, almond, soy), fat content impact on microfoam
  • Steam wand operation — Positioning, depth control, angle adjustment, preventing sputtering and water ejection
  • Microfoam creation — Aeration phase, heating phase, texture achievement, ideal temperature (60-65°C), thermometer usage
  • Common milk steaming mistakes — Scorching milk, large bubbles, insufficient texture, temperature overshoot and correction
  • Pitcher selection and technique — Stainless steel pitcher importance, size selection, grip and movement patterns
  • Temperature control and thermometers — Inserting thermometer correctly, reading temperatures, preventing burns, steam wand cleaning between shots
  • Latte art basics — Pouring technique, pitcher height control, cup positioning, simple patterns (heart, rosetta, tulip)
  • Practice patterns and progression — Starting with hearts, advancing to rosettas, understanding milk flow and espresso visibility
  • Milk steaming for different drinks — Cappuccino (more foam), latte (less foam), flat white (velvety microfoam), macchiato proportions
  • Cleaning and maintenance — Purging steam wand, wiping immediately, deep cleaning procedures, preventing milk buildup

Module 4: Specialty Coffee Drinks & Menu Preparation

📚 Topics Covered

  • Espresso-based drinks — Americano, macchiato, cortado, lungo, ristretto, their definitions and proportions
  • Classic milk-based drinks — Cappuccino, latte, flat white, differences in milk-to-espresso ratios and presentation
  • Contemporary specialty drinks — Cortado (equal parts), Gibraltar (specific glass), piccolo (concentrated), affogato (ice cream + espresso)
  • Cold coffee beverages — Iced latte, iced cappuccino, cold brew basics, cold foam preparation, serving temperatures
  • Alternative brewing methods — Pour-over (V60, Chemex), French press, Aeropress, Moka pot, when to recommend each
  • Flavored and seasonal drinks — Vanilla latte, caramel macchiato, mocha, adding syrups safely, measuring and consistency
  • Non-coffee beverages — Steamed milk drinks, hot chocolate, chai latte, herbal teas, expanding menu offerings
  • Portion control and pricing — Standard shot sizes (single 30ml, double 60ml), milk quantities, profit margins in Indian cafes
  • Presentation standards — Cup selection, saucer usage, garnishes (cocoa powder, cinnamon), serving protocols
  • Speed and efficiency — Preparing multiple drinks simultaneously, managing queue during rush hours, time management skills

Module 5: Customer Service, Sales & Cafe Operations

📚 Topics Covered

  • Customer greeting and engagement — Welcoming customers warmly, eye contact, remembering regular customers, building rapport in Hindi/English
  • Taking orders effectively — Listening skills, clarifying preferences (sugar level, milk type, temperature), upselling techniques
  • Handling difficult customers — Managing complaints professionally, resolving issues (wrong order, temperature), maintaining composure
  • Menu knowledge and recommendations — Describing drinks accurately, suggesting based on preferences, explaining specialty options
  • Payment handling and transactions — Cash management, card payments, giving correct change, POS system basics, maintaining honesty
  • Cafe hygiene and cleanliness — Daily cleaning schedules, sanitizing work areas, maintaining customer-facing spaces, trash management
  • Inventory management basics — Tracking coffee stock, milk usage, ordering supplies, preventing wastage, expiry date awareness
  • Rush hour management — Prioritizing orders, maintaining quality under pressure, team coordination, staying calm during peak times
  • Health and safety compliance — Fire safety, first aid basics, handling hot water/steam safely, preventing accidents, incident reporting
  • Professional appearance and behavior — Dress code adherence, punctuality, maintaining composure, representing cafe brand positively

Module 6: Coffee Tasting, Quality Control & Career Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Coffee tasting fundamentals — Cupping methodology, aroma evaluation, flavor notes identification, acidity vs bitterness distinction
  • Flavor wheel and tasting vocabulary — Recognizing fruit notes, floral notes, chocolate, nuts, spices in different coffees
  • Quality assessment — Identifying defects (stale beans, over-roasted, under-roasted), freshness indicators, bean appearance evaluation
  • Comparing different origins — Ethiopian vs Colombian vs Indian coffee characteristics, seasonal variations, price-quality relationship
  • Espresso shot quality indicators — Crema color (golden-brown ideal), consistency, taste profile, identifying extraction issues
  • Consistency checking procedures — Daily calibration, maintaining standards across different baristas, quality control metrics
  • Customer education — Explaining coffee origin to customers, describing flavor profiles simply, building coffee appreciation
  • Upselling specialty coffees — Single-origin vs blends, premium beans, explaining value proposition, increasing average transaction
  • Career progression in coffee industry — Barista roles, coffee shop manager, coffee trainer, roastery operator, coffee consultant positions
  • Advanced skill development — Espresso certification programs (SCA Level 1-3), competing in barista competitions, specializing in latte art
  • Entrepreneurship opportunities — Starting own cafe in tier-2/3 Indian cities, coffee cart business, home-based coffee subscription service
  • Continuous learning resources — Following coffee trends, joining barista communities in India, online courses, attending coffee festivals and workshops

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or as per local regulations)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior barista experience required - suitable for complete beginners
  • Already working as barista? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam for direct certification
  • Valid government ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Voter ID)
  • Medical fitness certificate may be required (no communicable diseases, fit for standing 8+ hours)
  • Applicable for all candidates regardless of gender, caste, religion, or background

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Operate espresso machines safely and prepare consistent, high-quality espresso shots following industry standards
  • Steam milk correctly to create microfoam and prepare milk-based specialty drinks with proper proportions
  • Demonstrate latte art basics and prepare visually appealing specialty coffee beverages
  • Apply food safety, hygiene, and sanitation standards in coffee preparation and cafe operations
  • Provide excellent customer service, handle payments, and manage cafe operations professionally
  • Identify coffee quality issues, understand different origins and roasts, and recommend appropriate drinks to customers
