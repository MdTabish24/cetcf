Electric Vehicle (EV) Basics

📂 Sector: Automobile & Motor Trades 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1200
Master the fundamentals of Electric Vehicles and launch your career in India's fastest-growing automotive sector. This hands-on certification program equips you with essential knowledge about EV technology, battery systems, charging infrastructure, and maintenance—preparing you for immediate employment in the rapidly expanding EV industry across India.

Module 1: Introduction to Electric Vehicles and Industry Overview

📚 Topics Covered

  • History and evolution of Electric Vehicles globally and in India — from early concepts to modern Tesla, Tata Nexon EV, Mahindra XUV400, and local brands
  • Types of Electric Vehicles — Battery Electric Vehicles (BEV), Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEV), Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEV), and Fuel Cell Vehicles (FCV)
  • Advantages and disadvantages of EVs — cost savings, environmental benefits, performance, range anxiety, and infrastructure challenges in India
  • Indian EV market landscape — government policies, FAME India scheme, subsidies, and growth projections for 2024-2030
  • Comparison between traditional ICE (Internal Combustion Engine) vehicles and Electric Vehicles — efficiency, emissions, operating costs
  • Global EV manufacturers vs Indian EV manufacturers — understanding market leaders like Tata, Mahindra, BYD, MG Motor, and emerging startups
  • Career opportunities in EV sector — technician, service engineer, charging station operator, battery specialist, and entrepreneurship options
  • Environmental impact and sustainability — carbon footprint reduction, recycling, and circular economy in EV industry
  • Government initiatives and incentives — FAME-II scheme, state subsidies, GST benefits, and charging infrastructure development programs

Module 2: Electrical Fundamentals and EV Power Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Basic electrical concepts — voltage, current, resistance, power, and Ohm's Law with practical EV applications
  • AC (Alternating Current) vs DC (Direct Current) — understanding three-phase AC, single-phase AC, and DC systems used in EVs
  • Electrical circuits in EVs — series circuits, parallel circuits, and mixed circuits with real-world EV examples
  • Safety in electrical systems — earthing, grounding, circuit protection, fuses, circuit breakers, and MCBs used in EV charging
  • Power distribution systems in EVs — high-voltage systems (400V+), low-voltage systems (12V), and isolation between them
  • Electrical measuring instruments — multimeter, clamp meter, insulation tester, and how to use them safely on EV systems
  • Common electrical faults in EVs — short circuits, open circuits, ground faults, and diagnostic procedures
  • Wiring and cable standards for EVs — IEC standards, Indian standards, color coding, and cable sizing calculations
  • Protection systems — relays, contactors, solenoids, and their role in EV safety and control
  • Practical hands-on — measuring voltage and current in EV charging systems, testing circuits, and safe handling of tools

Module 3: Battery Technology and Energy Storage Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Battery fundamentals — how batteries work, electrochemistry basics, and energy conversion principles
  • Lithium-ion battery technology — cell structure, chemistry, advantages over lead-acid, and why it's standard in modern EVs
  • Battery specifications and parameters — voltage, capacity (Ah/kWh), energy density, power density, and C-rating explained
  • Battery pack architecture — series and parallel cell arrangements, cell balancing, and thermal management in EV batteries
  • Battery Management System (BMS) — monitoring, balancing, temperature control, voltage regulation, and safety functions
  • State of Charge (SOC) and State of Health (SOH) — calculation methods, importance, and how BMS maintains battery health
  • Temperature management in batteries — cooling systems, thermal sensors, and why overheating reduces battery life
  • Battery degradation and cycle life — understanding capacity fade, calendar aging, and extending battery lifespan through proper charging
  • Alternative battery technologies — solid-state batteries, LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate), and emerging technologies for future EVs
  • Battery safety and hazards — thermal runaway, overcharging, deep discharge, and emergency procedures
  • Battery recycling and second-life applications — environmental responsibility, circular economy, and emerging Indian recycling startups
  • Practical hands-on — disassembling battery modules, identifying cell types, measuring battery voltage, and understanding BMS display readings

Module 4: EV Charging Infrastructure and Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Charging standards in India — Type 2 connectors, CCS (Combined Charging System), CHAdeMO, and Indian standards for AC/DC charging
  • Charging levels explained — Level 1 (household 230V), Level 2 (wall-mounted 32A), DC fast charging (50-350 kW), and charging time comparisons
  • AC charging systems — onboard chargers, power electronics, and how vehicles convert AC to DC for battery charging
  • DC fast charging systems — charging station components, power conversion, cooling systems, and grid integration challenges
  • Home charging setup — installing 7.4 kW and 11 kW chargers, electrical requirements, safety considerations, and cost in India
  • Public charging networks in India — NITI Aayog charging stations, private networks (Fortum, Shell, Tata Power), and coverage across states
  • Charging station operation and maintenance — monitoring systems, payment methods, troubleshooting common issues, and user support
  • Grid integration and smart charging — load management, vehicle-to-grid (V2G) technology, and demand response programs
  • Battery charging optimization — fast charging vs slow charging, optimal charging strategies, and impact on battery longevity
  • Safety protocols during charging — PPE requirements, electrical safety, emergency procedures, and customer safety guidelines
  • Practical hands-on — connecting EV to different charger types, reading charging display data, identifying charger faults, and safe disconnection procedures

Module 5: EV Components, Systems, and Vehicle Architecture

📚 Topics Covered

  • Electric motor types — AC induction motors, permanent magnet motors, and switched reluctance motors used in Indian EVs
  • Motor specifications — power output (kW), torque (Nm), RPM range, efficiency, and thermal characteristics
  • Motor control systems — inverters, motor controllers, and how they manage speed and torque delivery
  • Power transmission in EVs — single-speed reduction gearbox, direct drive, and advantages over multi-gear transmissions
  • Regenerative braking system — how EVs recover energy during braking, efficiency gains, and impact on range
  • Braking systems in EVs — friction brakes, brake-by-wire technology, ABS integration, and safety considerations
  • Steering and suspension — modifications for EV weight distribution, lower center of gravity, and handling characteristics
  • HVAC systems in EVs — cabin heating (heat pump technology), cooling, defogging, and energy consumption management
  • Vehicle Control Unit (VCU) and onboard electronics — central processing, sensor integration, and communication protocols (CAN bus)
  • Safety systems — airbags, stability control, collision detection, and EV-specific safety features
  • Auxiliary systems — 12V power distribution, lighting systems, windshield wipers, and low-voltage power management
  • Practical hands-on — identifying major EV components, understanding component layouts, reading system diagrams, and tracing electrical connections

Module 6: EV Maintenance, Troubleshooting, and Career Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Preventive maintenance in EVs — service intervals, fluid checks (coolant, brake fluid), filter replacements, and tire maintenance
  • Battery health monitoring — SOH assessment, capacity testing, cell voltage measurement, and when to recommend battery service
  • Troubleshooting electrical faults — using diagnostic tools, reading error codes, interpreting BMS messages, and systematic fault-finding
  • Common EV problems and solutions — range degradation, slow charging, battery imbalance, motor noise, and brake issues
  • Software updates and diagnostics — OBD-II readers for EVs, manufacturer diagnostic tools, and remote diagnostics via cloud platforms
  • Safety protocols during EV service — high-voltage safety, lockout-tagout procedures, insulation testing, and emergency disconnection
  • Tire and wheel maintenance — specific tire requirements for EVs, pressure monitoring, and impact on range
  • Cabin climate control troubleshooting — heat pump diagnostics, refrigerant handling, and energy efficiency optimization
  • Brake system maintenance — brake fluid flushing, pad replacement, and regenerative braking system calibration
  • Electrical connector maintenance — corrosion prevention, contact cleaning, and waterproofing for charging connectors
  • Career pathways in EV sector — EV technician, service advisor, charging station operator, battery specialist, and entrepreneurship opportunities
  • Skill development and certifications — advanced EV courses, manufacturer training programs, and international certifications (IMR, IMERC)
  • Entrepreneurship in EV sector — starting a charging station business, mobile repair service, battery reconditioning, and franchising opportunities
  • Practical hands-on — complete vehicle inspection checklist, battery health assessment, charging system diagnostics, fault code interpretation, and customer communication skills

💼 Career Paths

EV Service Technician
Work at authorized EV service centers, dealerships, or multi-brand workshops. Diagnose, maintain, and repair electric vehicles. High demand across major cities like Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi, and Pune.
EV Charging Station Operator
Operate and maintain public/private EV charging stations. Ensure smooth customer experience, monitor equipment, and perform regular maintenance. Growing opportunities with NITI Aayog and private networks.
Battery Specialist/Technician
Specialize in battery diagnostics, health monitoring, and replacement. Work with battery manufacturers or service centers. Highest growth potential as battery technology becomes critical.
EV Charging Station Entrepreneur
Start your own charging station business in residential societies, commercial complexes, or highways. Government subsidies available under FAME-II scheme. Initial investment: ₹5-15 lakhs depending on location and charger type.
Mobile EV Repair Service Owner
Provide on-site EV repair and maintenance services. Visit customer locations for diagnostics and repairs. Growing demand in metro cities where convenience is valued.
EV Service Advisor/Sales
Work at EV dealerships as service advisor or sales consultant. Explain EV technology to customers, manage service bookings, and handle customer relationships. Excellent communication skills required.
EV Training Instructor
After gaining 2-3 years of experience, become a trainer at vocational institutes, manufacturer training centers, or corporate training programs. Teach next generation of EV technicians.
Battery Recycling Technician
Work with battery recycling startups and facilities. Extract valuable materials from used EV batteries. Emerging sector with high growth potential and environmental impact.
