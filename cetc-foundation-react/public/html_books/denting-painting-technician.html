Denting & Painting Technician

📂 Sector: Automobile & Motor Trades 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months 🕐 Hours: 180 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the art and science of vehicle body restoration through this intensive 12-week foundation course. Learn professional denting and painting techniques used in authorized service centers, independent garages, and body shops across India. Gain hands-on expertise in metal work, surface preparation, spray painting, and quality finishing to launch a rewarding career in the automotive aftermarket industry.

Module 1: Automotive Industry Fundamentals & Safety Practices

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to Indian automotive industry — market overview, growth trends, job opportunities in denting & painting sector
  • Types of vehicles in India — two-wheelers, three-wheelers, four-wheelers, commercial vehicles and their body structures
  • Workshop safety regulations — OSHA standards, Indian labor laws, personal protective equipment (PPE) requirements
  • Fire safety and hazard management — handling flammable materials, fire extinguisher types, emergency procedures
  • Electrical safety in workshops — proper grounding, handling power tools, preventing electrical accidents
  • Health and hygiene practices — respiratory protection, skin care, handling toxic chemicals safely
  • Tool safety and equipment handling — proper storage, maintenance, safe operation of pneumatic and electric tools
  • First aid basics — common workshop injuries, basic treatment, emergency contact procedures
  • Environmental regulations — waste disposal of paint materials, solvent handling, compliance with Pollution Control Board norms

Module 2: Vehicle Body Structure & Damage Assessment

📚 Topics Covered

  • Vehicle body types — monocoque, ladder frame, space frame construction and their characteristics
  • Body panel components — doors, fenders, hood, trunk, bumpers, side panels and their assembly
  • Metal types used in vehicles — mild steel, high-strength steel, aluminum panels, composite materials and their properties
  • Understanding dent types — simple dents, crease dents, stretched metal, creases, sharp bends and identification methods
  • Damage assessment techniques — visual inspection, measuring dent depth, identifying paint damage, rust assessment
  • Using measurement tools — measuring tape, depth gauge, straight edge, ruler for damage quantification
  • Estimating repair costs — labor hours, material requirements, customer communication about damage
  • Documenting damage — photography, written descriptions, creating repair estimates for insurance claims
  • Paint damage classification — chips, scratches, fading, peeling, rust spots and their severity levels
  • Structural damage evaluation — identifying frame damage, suspension impact, alignment issues requiring specialist attention
  • Customer interaction skills — explaining damage to vehicle owners, managing expectations, professional communication

Module 3: Hand Tools, Equipment & Workshop Setup

📚 Topics Covered

  • Denting tools overview — hammers (ball peen, cross peen, finishing), dollies, spoons, picks and their uses
  • Specialized denting equipment — slide hammers, suction cups, pneumatic dent pullers, hydraulic equipment operation
  • Measuring and marking tools — calipers, dividers, center punch, marking gauge, chalk for layout work
  • Cutting and grinding tools — angle grinders, die grinders, sanders, cutoff wheels and safety procedures
  • Welding equipment basics — MIG welders, spot welders, oxy-acetylene equipment for panel joining
  • Surface preparation tools — scrapers, wire brushes, sandpaper varieties (grit sizes 80-2000), sanding blocks
  • Spray painting equipment — spray guns (HVLP, conventional, gravity feed), compressors, regulators, moisture traps
  • Masking and protection materials — masking tape, plastic sheets, drop cloths, protective coverings for windows
  • Workshop layout and organization — tool storage systems, material organization, workspace ergonomics
  • Equipment maintenance — cleaning spray guns, draining compressors, tool sharpening, preventive maintenance schedules
  • Personal protective equipment — respirators, safety glasses, gloves, aprons, steel-toed shoes, hearing protection
  • Workshop ventilation systems — exhaust systems, air flow requirements, dust collection for health and safety

Module 4: Denting Techniques & Metal Working

📚 Topics Covered

  • Basic metal properties — elasticity, plasticity, work hardening, annealing and how they affect repair techniques
  • Hammer and dolly technique — proper striking methods, angle control, sequential hitting patterns for dent removal
  • Working with simple dents — removing shallow dents, maintaining panel shape, avoiding over-stretching metal
  • Crease dent removal — techniques for sharp creases, preventing metal splitting, using specialized tools
  • Stretched metal treatment — shrinking stretched areas, heat application methods, cold shrinking techniques
  • Access hole creation — drilling holes for slide hammer attachment, proper hole placement, plug welding after repair
  • Door and fender repair — panel-specific techniques, hinge considerations, alignment after straightening
  • Hood and trunk lid repair — lightweight panel handling, support techniques, spring mechanism protection
  • Bumper and trim repair — plastic bumper dent removal, metal bumper straightening, chrome trim handling
  • Panel replacement techniques — when to replace vs. repair, panel removal, installation, welding and bonding methods
  • Metal finishing — final shaping with files, planishing hammers, achieving smooth surfaces before painting
  • Quality checking — using straight edge, visual inspection, ensuring proper panel alignment and fit

Module 5: Surface Preparation & Primer Application

📚 Topics Covered

  • Surface cleaning methods — degreasing, removing old paint, rust removal techniques and chemical solvents
  • Sandpaper selection and use — grit size selection (80, 120, 220, 400, 600, 1000, 2000), wet vs. dry sanding
  • Sanding techniques — orbital sanding, hand sanding, block sanding, achieving uniform surface texture
  • Rust treatment — identifying rust types, surface rust removal, deep rust treatment, rust converter chemicals
  • Plastic filler application — body filler selection, mixing ratios, application techniques, curing times
  • Filler sanding and shaping — achieving desired contour, preventing filler cracking, final smoothness
  • Primer types — epoxy primer, polyester primer, urethane primer and their properties and applications
  • Primer mixing and thinning — correct ratios, solvent selection, achieving proper spray consistency
  • Primer application techniques — spray gun handling, distance and angle, multiple coats, drying times
  • Masking procedures — protecting adjacent areas, using masking tape and paper, creating clean edges
  • Surface inspection before painting — checking for imperfections, final touch-ups, ensuring readiness for topcoat
  • Environmental conditions for primer — temperature, humidity, ventilation requirements for proper curing

Module 6: Paint Application, Finishing & Quality Control

📚 Topics Covered

  • Paint types and systems — solid colors, metallic paints, pearl finishes, matte finishes and their characteristics
  • Paint composition — pigments, binders, solvents, additives and their roles in paint performance
  • Color matching techniques — using color codes, spectrophotometer use, manual color matching methods
  • Paint mixing procedures — base coat and clear coat ratios, activator and hardener proportions, proper stirring
  • Spray gun setup — nozzle selection, fluid needle adjustment, air pressure settings (typically 25-35 PSI)
  • Spray painting techniques — gun distance (6-8 inches), speed control, overlap patterns, wet vs. dry spray
  • Base coat and clear coat application — layering techniques, flash-off times between coats, achieving gloss finish
  • Handling metallic and pearl paints — special techniques, preventing color variation, proper blending
  • Blending and feathering — seamless color transitions, blending into adjacent panels, edge control
  • Common paint defects — orange peel, runs, drips, sags, dust, overspray and prevention methods
  • Paint curing and drying — ambient temperature effects, humidity control, dry time vs. cure time, bake oven use
  • Quality inspection and touch-up — checking for defects, wet sanding, buffing and polishing for final finish

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (15 years for certain states)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience needed — course is designed for beginners
  • Already working as denting/painting technician? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, Driving License)
  • Applicants from all educational and economic backgrounds welcome
  • No medical restrictions except those affecting ability to handle tools safely

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Identify different types of vehicle damage and assess repair requirements using proper measurement techniques
  • Operate hand tools, power tools, and specialized denting equipment safely and effectively
  • Remove dents and straighten metal panels using hammer, dolly, and modern dent removal techniques
  • Prepare surfaces through cleaning, sanding, and filling for professional paint application
  • Mix and apply primers, base coats, and clear coats using spray equipment with proper technique
  • Perform quality inspections, identify paint defects, and execute touch-ups and buffing for professional finishes
  • Follow workshop safety protocols, handle hazardous materials responsibly, and maintain a clean work environment
  • Communicate effectively with customers about damage assessment and repair timelines
