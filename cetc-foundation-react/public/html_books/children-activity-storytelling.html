Children Activity & Storytelling

📂 Sector: Child Care & Domestic Services 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the art of engaging children through creative activities and storytelling. Learn to plan, execute, and manage age-appropriate activities that develop children's cognitive, emotional, and social skills. This foundation course prepares you for roles in daycare centers, schools, and home-based childcare services across India.

Module 1: Child Development & Activity Planning Basics

📚 Topics Covered

  • Child development stages (0-8 years) — understanding physical, cognitive, emotional and social milestones at each age group
  • Age-appropriate activity selection — matching activities to developmental levels and learning needs
  • Safety guidelines for children's activities — identifying hazards, preventing accidents, and maintaining safe spaces
  • Activity planning framework — setting objectives, duration, materials, and outcomes for structured activities
  • Observing and assessing children — noting behavior, progress, and individual learning styles during activities
  • Special needs awareness — adapting activities for children with developmental delays or disabilities
  • Cultural sensitivity in childcare — respecting diverse family backgrounds and celebrating Indian festivals through activities
  • Parent communication — sharing activity reports and developmental progress with families
  • Documentation and record-keeping — maintaining activity logs and child participation records
  • Time management in group settings — organizing multiple activities and transitions smoothly

Module 2: Storytelling Fundamentals & Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Types of stories for children — folk tales, fairy tales, moral stories, modern picture books, and Indian classics
  • Story selection criteria — choosing age-appropriate, engaging, and culturally relevant stories
  • Voice modulation and expression — using tone, pitch, and pace to capture children's attention
  • Storytelling posture and body language — maintaining engagement through physical presence and movement
  • Narrative techniques — building suspense, using repetition, and creating memorable story moments
  • Interactive storytelling — encouraging participation, asking questions, and involving children in narratives
  • Props and visual aids — using puppets, flannel boards, pictures, and objects to enhance stories
  • Story adaptation — modifying stories for different age groups and time constraints
  • Regional storytelling traditions — incorporating Hindi, Tamil, Bengali, Gujarati and other Indian storytelling styles
  • Digital storytelling tools — using age-appropriate apps, videos, and multimedia for story presentation
  • Story comprehension activities — checking understanding through discussions, drawings, and re-enactments
  • Building a story library — curating and organizing diverse story collections for different occasions

Module 3: Creative & Educational Activities

📚 Topics Covered

  • Arts and crafts activities — drawing, painting, cutting, pasting, and modeling with clay and recyclables
  • Music and movement activities — singing, dancing, rhythm instruments, and gross motor skill development
  • Sensory play activities — texture exploration, water play, sand play, and taste-safe sensory bins
  • Language development activities — phonics games, word building, storytelling, and vocabulary exercises
  • Math and logic activities — counting games, shape recognition, sorting, and problem-solving activities
  • Science exploration activities — simple experiments, nature observation, and hands-on discovery learning
  • Role-play and dramatic play — setting up play corners, props, costumes, and guided imaginative play
  • Outdoor activities — games, sports, nature walks, and physical fitness for different age groups
  • Rainy day and indoor activities — structured games, puzzles, and quiet time activities
  • Seasonal and festival activities — Diwali, Holi, Christmas, Independence Day themed crafts and celebrations
  • Inclusive activity adaptation — modifying activities for children with different abilities and learning styles
  • Transition activities — quick games and songs for moving between different parts of the day

Module 4: Puppet Making & Performance

📚 Topics Covered

  • Types of puppets — hand puppets, finger puppets, stick puppets, shadow puppets, and marionettes
  • DIY puppet making — creating puppets from socks, paper bags, foam, felt, and recycled materials
  • Puppet design and decoration — painting, adding features, clothing, and making puppets expressive
  • Puppet theater setup — creating simple puppet stages using cardboard, fabric, and household items
  • Puppet voice and characterization — developing distinct voices for different puppet characters
  • Puppet movement techniques — making puppets walk, dance, gesture, and interact naturally
  • Puppet show scripting — writing short scripts suitable for children's performances
  • Interactive puppet shows — involving children as audience participants and co-performers
  • Traditional Indian puppetry — understanding Kathputli, Gombeyatta, and other regional puppet forms
  • Puppet shows for learning — using puppets to teach moral lessons, social skills, and academic concepts
  • Managing puppet shows with groups — handling logistics, timing, and child engagement
  • Safety in puppet theater — preventing accidents and ensuring child-safe performance spaces

Module 5: Behavior Management & Group Dynamics

📚 Topics Covered

  • Understanding child behavior — recognizing developmental behavior, attention-seeking, and emotional expressions
  • Positive discipline techniques — setting boundaries, using positive reinforcement, and logical consequences
  • Attention and engagement strategies — keeping children focused during activities and stories
  • Managing group transitions — moving smoothly between activities without chaos or resistance
  • Handling conflicts between children — teaching problem-solving and peaceful resolution skills
  • Managing difficult behaviors during activities — addressing aggression, refusal, and disruptive behavior
  • Building classroom routines — creating predictable structures that help children feel secure
  • Encouraging participation — drawing out shy children and managing over-energetic participants
  • Inclusive group activities — ensuring all children, including those with special needs, feel included
  • Emotional regulation support — helping children manage frustration, disappointment, and strong emotions
  • Creating a positive classroom culture — fostering cooperation, kindness, and mutual respect

Module 6: Practical Application & Professional Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Lesson planning for activities — creating detailed activity plans with clear learning objectives
  • Resource management — sourcing, organizing, and maintaining activity materials and supplies on budget
  • Creating activity schedules — balancing structured activities, free play, meals, and rest times
  • Assessment and evaluation — measuring activity effectiveness and child learning outcomes
  • Documentation practices — photographing activities, maintaining portfolios, and creating progress records
  • Health and hygiene in activities — preventing disease spread, sanitizing materials, and food safety
  • First aid basics for childcare — responding to minor injuries and knowing when to seek medical help
  • Professional communication — maintaining confidentiality, documenting concerns, and reporting issues
  • Building relationships with children — creating trust, providing emotional support, and showing care
  • Collaborating with parents and colleagues — teamwork, sharing information, and professional conduct
  • Continuing professional development — staying updated with new techniques, resources, and child psychology research
  • Entrepreneurial opportunities — starting home-based activity centers, freelance storytelling, or activity planning services

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (18 years preferred for employment in formal centers)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience needed — foundation level course
  • Already working in childcare? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam to skip training
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, Pan Card, Voter ID, or Driving License)
  • Applicable for all candidates regardless of gender, caste, or religion
  • Physical fitness to engage in activities with children recommended
  • Criminal background check may be required by employers

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand child development stages and select age-appropriate activities and stories
  • Plan, organize, and execute engaging children's activities that support learning and development
  • Master storytelling techniques including voice modulation, expression, and interactive engagement
  • Create and perform with puppets to enhance learning and maintain child interest
  • Manage group behavior, handle conflicts, and create inclusive learning environments
  • Maintain safety, hygiene, and documentation standards in childcare activity settings
  • Communicate effectively with children, parents, and colleagues in professional childcare roles
