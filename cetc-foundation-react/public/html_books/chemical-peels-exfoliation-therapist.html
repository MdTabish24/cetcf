Chemical Peels & Exfoliation Therapist

📂 Sector: Advanced Beauty, Cosmetology & Aesthetics 📊 Level: Intermediate ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months 🕐 Hours: 180 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1200
Master the art and science of professional chemical peels and advanced exfoliation techniques. This intermediate certification course equips you with expertise in skin analysis, peel formulations, safety protocols, and client management to become a sought-after chemical peel specialist in India's booming aesthetic industry.

Module 1: Skin Anatomy, Physiology & Chemistry Fundamentals

📚 Topics Covered

  • Skin structure: Epidermis, dermis, hypodermis layers — understanding thickness, cell composition, and function
  • Skin cell turnover cycle: Stratum corneum renewal, desquamation process, and aging factors
  • pH balance of skin: Normal pH range (4.5-5.5), acid mantle, and its importance in peel safety
  • Skin barrier function: Lipid layer composition, moisture retention, and how peels affect barrier integrity
  • Types of skin: Oily, dry, combination, sensitive, and ethnic variations in Indian skin types
  • Common skin conditions: Acne, hyperpigmentation, melasma, photoaging, texture issues, and scars
  • Collagen and elastin: Structure, degradation, and how chemical peels stimulate regeneration
  • Chemistry basics: pH, concentration, penetration depth, and chemical reactions on skin surface
  • Skin sensitivity factors: Genetics, medications, sun exposure, and pre-existing conditions
  • Melanin production: Mechanisms of hyperpigmentation, melasma triggers, and peel effectiveness
  • Practical: Microscopic skin analysis using magnifying lamp and Wood's lamp examination

Module 2: Chemical Peel Types, Formulations & Safety Protocols

📚 Topics Covered

  • Alpha Hydroxy Acids (AHA): Glycolic acid, lactic acid, mandelic acid — concentration levels, penetration depth, and best uses
  • Beta Hydroxy Acids (BHA): Salicylic acid chemistry, oil-solubility, acne treatment application, and strength variations
  • Trichloroacetic Acid (TCA): Strength classifications (10%, 20%, 35%, 50%), application techniques, and risk management
  • Jessner's solution: Composition, depth of peel, combination effects, and ideal candidates
  • Combination peels: Layering techniques, synergistic effects, and advanced formulations used in premium salons
  • Phenol peels: Deep peeling mechanism, risks, contraindications, and when to refer to dermatologists
  • Natural and gentler peels: Enzyme peels, fruit acid peels, and their role in sensitive skin treatment
  • Peel strength classification: Superficial (epidermal), medium (dermal), and deep peels — depth and healing time
  • Neutralization techniques: Sodium bicarbonate, water, and timing protocols for safe peel cessation
  • Storage and shelf-life: Proper handling, temperature control, contamination prevention, and expiry management
  • Safety data sheets (SDS): Understanding chemical hazards, first aid procedures, and regulatory compliance
  • Practical: Preparing peel solutions, testing pH levels, and conducting patch tests on dummy skin

Module 3: Client Consultation, Assessment & Contraindication Screening

📚 Topics Covered

  • Consultation framework: Building rapport, active listening, and understanding client expectations and concerns
  • Skin assessment techniques: Visual examination, tactile assessment, and using diagnostic tools (magnifying lamp, Wood's lamp)
  • Fitzpatrick skin type classification: Understanding all 6 types, ethnic considerations, and peel suitability for each
  • Baumann skin type system: Oily vs. dry, sensitive vs. resistant classification for personalized peel selection
  • Contraindications and precautions: Pregnancy, active infections, eczema, psoriasis, open wounds, and immunosuppression
  • Medication interactions: Accutane/Isotretinoin, photosensitizing drugs, anticoagulants, and timing considerations
  • Allergy history and patch testing: Identifying sensitivity to specific peel ingredients and conducting safe pre-treatment tests
  • Sun exposure and tanning assessment: Recent sun damage, spray tans, and timing recommendations before peels
  • Realistic expectations setting: Before-after photo discussions, healing timeline, and achievable outcomes for different concerns
  • Consent and documentation: Written agreements, liability waivers, and maintaining detailed client records
  • Special populations: Treating darker skin types safely, managing post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation risks, and customized protocols
  • Practical: Conducting full consultations with mock clients, performing skin analysis, and creating personalized treatment plans

Module 4: Pre-Treatment Preparation, Application Techniques & Timing

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pre-treatment skincare regimen: 2-week preparation with hydroquinone, retinoids, and sunscreen for optimal results
  • Skin cleansing protocols: Removing makeup, oils, and dead skin without irritating before peel application
  • Degreasing techniques: Acetone or alcohol-based prep solutions, timing, and ensuring clean peel penetration
  • Anesthesia options: Topical numbing creams, cooling systems, and pain management for deeper peels
  • Application tools and equipment: Brushes, cotton pads, gauze, timer devices, and safety equipment setup
  • Layering techniques: Single layer, multiple layers, and timing intervals between applications for medium/deep peels
  • Depth control methods: Monitoring frosting (white precipitation), erythema (redness), and endpoint recognition
  • Application sequence: Face zones, sensitive areas (around eyes, lips), and avoiding overlap patterns
  • Timing protocols: Exposure time for different peel types, strength variations, and client comfort monitoring
  • Neutralization process: When and how to neutralize AHA/BHA peels, TCA peels, and combination formulations
  • Post-application care: Cooling, moisturizing, sunscreen application, and immediate aftercare instructions
  • Practical: Live application on training dummies and supervised practice on volunteer clients with various skin types

Module 5: Post-Treatment Care, Healing Protocols & Complication Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Immediate post-peel care: First 24 hours — cooling, moisturizing, sun protection, and activity restrictions
  • Healing timeline: Day-by-day expectations for superficial, medium, and deep peels including redness, flaking, and peeling
  • Skincare products for recovery: Gentle cleansers, hydrating serums, barrier repair creams, and recommended brands
  • Sun protection protocols: SPF 50+, reapplication frequency, protective clothing, and UV avoidance during healing
  • Activity restrictions: Exercise, swimming, sauna, and heat exposure guidelines based on peel depth
  • Makeup and cosmetic use: When safe to reapply, product recommendations, and avoiding irritating ingredients
  • Hydration and nutrition: Internal healing support, water intake, antioxidants, and foods promoting collagen synthesis
  • Common side effects: Redness, sensitivity, mild swelling, and temporary hyperpigmentation — duration and management
  • Complications identification: Severe burns, infection signs, prolonged erythema, and when to refer to dermatologist
  • Infection prevention: Signs of bacterial/fungal infection, appropriate topical treatments, and antibiotic recommendations
  • Post-peel maintenance: Follow-up treatments, timing between peels, and building a long-term skincare routine
  • Practical: Creating detailed aftercare instruction sheets, managing mock complication scenarios, and client follow-up protocols

Module 6: Professional Practice, Client Management & Business Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Salon setup and equipment: Chemical peel station requirements, safety equipment, ventilation, and hygiene standards
  • Sterilization and sanitation: Instrument sterilization, workspace cleaning, waste disposal, and infection control protocols
  • Record-keeping and documentation: Client files, treatment records, consent forms, complication logs, and legal compliance
  • Pricing strategies: Cost analysis, market research, competitive pricing, and package deals for repeat clients in India
  • Client communication: Pre-treatment expectations, during-treatment comfort, post-treatment follow-up, and handling complaints
  • Building client loyalty: Referral programs, loyalty discounts, seasonal promotions, and social media engagement
  • Marketing and brand building: Instagram/Facebook presence, before-after portfolio, testimonials, and local partnerships
  • Upselling complementary services: Facials, serums, LED therapy, microneedling, and creating treatment packages
  • Managing difficult clients: Setting boundaries, handling unrealistic expectations, and professional conflict resolution
  • Continuing education: Staying updated on new peel formulations, attending workshops, and certifications in advanced techniques
  • Self-employment vs. salon employment: Pros/cons, income potential, client base building, and business registration requirements
  • Practical: Creating business plans, developing marketing materials, role-playing client scenarios, and setting up mock treatment booking system

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 16 years (beauty industry standard in India)
  • Minimum education: Class 10th pass (recommended for chemical safety understanding)
  • Prior experience: Basic beauty/cosmetology knowledge helpful but not mandatory
  • Already working as beautician/therapist? Apply for Direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required: Aadhaar, PAN, or Passport at registration
  • Medical clearance: No severe skin conditions that prevent hands-on practice
  • Applicable for: All candidates including self-employed beauticians, salon staff, and aspiring entrepreneurs
  • No age upper limit: Career changers and experienced professionals welcome

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Demonstrate comprehensive understanding of skin anatomy, physiology, and chemical mechanisms involved in peeling processes
  • Accurately identify skin types, conditions, and contraindications to select appropriate peel formulations and safety protocols
  • Perform professional-grade chemical peel applications using multiple techniques (AHA, BHA, TCA) with proper timing and neutralization
  • Manage post-treatment care, identify complications, and provide appropriate aftercare guidance to ensure optimal client outcomes
  • Establish safe salon practices including sterilization, documentation, and regulatory compliance for chemical peel services
  • Build sustainable client base through professional consultation, realistic expectation-setting, and effective communication skills
