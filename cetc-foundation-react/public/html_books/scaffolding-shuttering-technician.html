Scaffolding & Shuttering Technician

📂 Sector: Construction & Building Trades 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the essential skills of scaffolding and shuttering work - one of the most in-demand trades in India's booming construction sector. This 1-month intensive course trains you to safely erect, maintain, and dismantle temporary structures that support construction projects, making you job-ready for India's growing real estate and infrastructure development.

Module 1: Introduction to Scaffolding & Shuttering Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Definition and importance of scaffolding in construction — Understanding temporary structures used to support workers and materials during building construction
  • Definition and importance of shuttering in construction — Learning about temporary formwork systems that hold concrete in shape until it hardens
  • Types of scaffolding systems — Bamboo scaffolding, steel tubular scaffolding, cuplock systems, and mobile scaffolding used across Indian projects
  • Types of shuttering systems — Timber shuttering, plywood shuttering, steel shuttering, and aluminum formwork systems commonly used in India
  • Indian construction standards and regulations — OSHA guidelines, IS 3696 (Code of practice for safety of workers in construction), and local municipal rules
  • Safety culture in scaffolding and shuttering work — Understanding accident prevention, hazard identification, and worker protection measures
  • Career opportunities in scaffolding and shuttering — Job roles, salary expectations, and growth paths in Indian construction industry
  • Tools and equipment overview — Introduction to spanners, hammers, levels, measuring tapes, safety harnesses, and other essential tools

Module 2: Scaffolding Materials, Design & Load Calculations

📚 Topics Covered

  • Bamboo scaffolding materials and properties — Selection, grading, and quality checks for bamboo used in Indian construction sites
  • Steel tubular scaffolding materials — Understanding mild steel tubes, couplers, standards, and quality certifications required in India
  • Accessories and fastening systems — Couplers, clips, braces, base plates, and connection methods used in modern scaffolding
  • Load capacity and weight distribution — Calculating safe working loads, understanding safety factors, and preventing overloading on scaffolds
  • Reading and interpreting technical drawings — Understanding architectural plans, elevation drawings, and scaffolding design specifications
  • Basic structural calculations for scaffolding — Simple load calculations, spacing requirements, and stability principles for temporary structures
  • Timber and plywood selection for shuttering — Quality grading, thickness selection, and durability factors for formwork materials
  • Concrete pressure calculations — Understanding lateral pressure on shuttering, depth factors, and reinforcement requirements
  • Material cost estimation and procurement — Calculating material quantities, budgeting, and supplier selection for projects
  • Quality standards and certifications — Understanding Indian Standards (IS codes), BIS certifications, and quality assurance in materials

Module 3: Practical Installation Techniques - Scaffolding

📚 Topics Covered

  • Site preparation and ground assessment — Checking soil bearing capacity, leveling ground, and preparing foundation for scaffolding installation
  • Base plate installation and leveling — Proper placement of base plates, using spirit levels, and ensuring stability on uneven surfaces
  • Vertical standard erection — Step-by-step process of installing vertical poles, maintaining alignment, and checking plumb with plumb bob
  • Horizontal ledger and cross-brace installation — Installing ledgers at correct heights, spacing, and securing cross-braces for lateral stability
  • Coupler and fastening techniques — Proper tightening of couplers, checking connections, and ensuring load transfer without slippage
  • Bamboo lashing techniques — Traditional Indian method of securing bamboo using natural rope, achieving proper tension and knot security
  • Guardrails and safety netting installation — Installing protective barriers, safety nets, and warning signs at height to prevent falls
  • Mobile and modular scaffolding assembly — Quick installation of pre-fabricated systems and mobile towers on construction sites
  • Inspection and testing procedures — Checking installed scaffolding, load testing, and documentation before allowing worker access
  • Dismantling and removal procedures — Safe removal of scaffolding components, proper storage, and site cleanup after project completion
  • Common installation mistakes and corrections — Identifying defects, making repairs, and preventing failures on active construction sites

Module 4: Practical Installation Techniques - Shuttering

📚 Topics Covered

  • Formwork design for different structural elements — Understanding shuttering requirements for slabs, beams, columns, and walls in Indian construction
  • Timber shuttering assembly — Cutting timber pieces, assembling frames, and creating modular units for quick installation on site
  • Plywood shuttering systems — Selecting plywood grades, cutting panels, and assembling robust formwork for concrete structures
  • Propping and support systems — Installing vertical and diagonal props, adjusting heights, and ensuring uniform load distribution
  • Bracing and lateral support — Installing diagonal braces, lateral supports, and temporary connections for formwork stability
  • Concrete release agents and surface finishes — Understanding mold oils, surface treatments, and achieving desired concrete finishes
  • Shuttering for columns and walls — Specific techniques for vertical formwork, corner details, and preventing concrete leakage
  • Shuttering for slabs and beams — Creating supports for horizontal elements, managing deflection, and achieving level concrete surfaces
  • Shuttering for stairs and ramps — Specialized formwork for inclined structures, step details, and finish requirements
  • Formwork striking (removal) techniques — Safe removal of shuttering after concrete curing, timing considerations, and sequential dismantling
  • Reusing and maintaining shuttering materials — Cleaning, storing, and refurbishing timber and plywood for multiple uses
  • Quality checks and surface finish inspection — Verifying concrete quality, checking surface finish, and documenting defects

Module 5: Safety, Health & Environmental Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Occupational Safety and Health (OSH) in construction — Understanding workplace hazards, risk assessment, and control measures specific to scaffolding work
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) — Correct usage of helmets, safety harnesses, gloves, safety shoes, and respiratory protection on construction sites
  • Fall prevention and protection systems — Understanding fall hazards at height, using safety nets, guardrails, and harness systems correctly
  • Electrical safety on construction sites — Avoiding contact with electrical lines, safe use of tools, and preventing electrocution hazards
  • Manual handling and ergonomics — Proper lifting techniques, preventing back injuries, and safe material handling practices
  • Accident reporting and emergency procedures — Documenting incidents, first aid response, and evacuation procedures on construction sites
  • Environmental protection in construction — Dust control, waste management, noise reduction, and protecting surrounding areas from construction impact
  • Weather-related safety considerations — Working safely in rain, wind, heat, and cold conditions common in different Indian regions
  • Scaffolding inspection checklists — Daily and weekly inspection routines, identifying defects, and maintaining safety records
  • Legal compliance and worker rights — Understanding labor laws in India, worker compensation, and employee rights in construction sector

Module 6: Professional Competency & Advancement

📚 Topics Covered

  • Communication and teamwork on construction sites — Coordinating with supervisors, engineers, and other workers for safe and efficient work
  • Reading and interpreting construction documents — Understanding site plans, specifications, and work schedules provided by project management
  • Basic mathematics for construction — Measurements, area calculations, percentage, and simple geometry used in daily work
  • Estimation and material planning — Calculating material requirements, cost estimation, and inventory management for projects
  • Problem-solving and decision-making — Addressing on-site challenges, adapting to design changes, and suggesting improvements
  • Quality assurance and inspection skills — Checking workmanship, identifying defects, and maintaining quality standards on projects
  • Documentation and record-keeping — Maintaining work logs, inspection records, and safety documentation as per site requirements
  • Supervision and mentoring skills — Leading junior workers, training new team members, and progressing toward supervisory roles
  • Entrepreneurship in construction trades — Starting independent contracting business, managing projects, and building client relationships
  • Continuous learning and skill upgradation — Pursuing advanced certifications, learning new systems, and staying updated with industry practices
  • Professional ethics and work discipline — Punctuality, honesty, reliability, and maintaining professional conduct on construction sites
  • Career progression pathways — Moving from technician to supervisor, site engineer, or project manager roles in construction industry

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (with parental consent for candidates below 18 years)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required - Foundation level course for beginners
  • Already working in construction? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam for faster certification
  • Valid photo ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, or Voter ID)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and migrant workers with valid documentation
  • Medical fitness certificate may be required for work at height (obtained during training)

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand scaffolding and shuttering systems used in Indian construction and their applications in different project types
  • Select appropriate materials and design temporary structures according to Indian Standards and safety regulations
  • Install, inspect, and maintain scaffolding systems safely using bamboo lashing and steel couplers
  • Assemble, prop, and strike shuttering formwork for different structural elements with proper load calculations
  • Identify hazards, use personal protective equipment, and implement safety procedures to prevent accidents at height
  • Read technical drawings, perform basic calculations, and communicate effectively with supervisors and team members on construction sites
  • Progress toward supervisory roles and entrepreneurship by developing advanced skills and professional competencies in construction trades
