Food Safety & Hygiene — FSSAI Basics

📂 Sector: Food, Catering & Hospitality 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master food safety standards set by FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India) and become a certified professional in food handling, hygiene, and contamination prevention. This foundation course equips you with essential knowledge to work safely in kitchens, food processing units, restaurants, and catering businesses across India.

Module 1: Introduction to Food Safety & FSSAI Regulations

📚 Topics Covered

  • What is Food Safety? — Definition, importance, and global standards
  • FSSAI Overview — Role, responsibilities, and regulatory framework in India
  • Food Safety Act 2006 — Key provisions and compliance requirements for food businesses
  • License & Registration — Understanding FSSAI license types: Basic, State, and Central
  • Food Business Operator (FBO) Responsibilities — Duties and obligations under FSSAI
  • Hazard Analysis & Critical Control Points (HACCP) — Basic principles and application
  • Food Safety Management Systems — Documentation, record-keeping, and traceability
  • Penalties & Enforcement — Consequences of non-compliance and inspection procedures
  • Indian Food Safety Standards — Specific standards for different food categories
  • Allergen Management — Identifying, labeling, and preventing allergen cross-contamination

Module 2: Microbiology & Food Contamination

📚 Topics Covered

  • Microorganisms in Food — Bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites affecting food safety
  • Pathogenic Bacteria — E. coli, Salmonella, Listeria, and Staphylococcus aureus in Indian context
  • Foodborne Illness Outbreaks — Common diseases, symptoms, and prevention in India
  • Food Poisoning vs Food Infection — Differences and identification in food service settings
  • Contamination Sources — Biological, chemical, and physical contaminants in kitchens
  • Cross-Contamination Prevention — Separation of raw and cooked foods in Indian kitchens
  • Temperature Control & Danger Zone — 5°C to 60°C safe food storage principles
  • Spoilage Indicators — Visual, olfactory, and tactile signs of food deterioration
  • Spice & Condiment Safety — Managing contamination in dry goods and Indian spices
  • Water Quality & Sanitation — Testing, treatment, and safe water use in food preparation

Module 3: Personal Hygiene & Food Handler Practices

📚 Topics Covered

  • Hand Hygiene Protocols — Proper washing techniques, frequency, and critical times
  • Personal Cleanliness Standards — Uniform, footwear, and grooming requirements
  • Disease & Illness Management — Reporting procedures for sick food handlers
  • Jewelry & Accessories — Restrictions and safety concerns in food preparation areas
  • Hair & Nail Care — Preventing contamination from personal appearance
  • Handwashing Stations — Design, placement, and maintenance in commercial kitchens
  • Sanitation Supplies — Soaps, sanitizers, and their proper use in Indian kitchens
  • Behavioral Hygiene — Eating, drinking, smoking, and spitting restrictions in food areas
  • Health Screening — Medical checkups and fitness certificates for food handlers
  • Training & Awareness — Continuous education and hygiene culture in food businesses
  • PPE Requirements — Gloves, masks, and protective equipment usage and limitations
  • Hygiene Audit Checklist — Self-inspection tools for food handlers and supervisors

Module 4: Food Storage, Preservation & Temperature Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Cold Storage Systems — Refrigerators, freezers, and walk-in coolers: maintenance and monitoring
  • Temperature Monitoring — Thermometers, data logging, and maintaining cold chain in India
  • Dry Storage Management — Organization, ventilation, and pest prevention in pantries
  • Storage Duration & Shelf Life — FIFO (First In, First Out) principle and expiry management
  • Freezing & Thawing Procedures — Safe methods for Indian food items and meat products
  • Canned & Preserved Foods — Storage conditions and spoilage detection
  • Spice & Flour Storage — Moisture control and pest prevention in Indian ingredients
  • Dairy Products Safety — Milk, paneer, yogurt storage and contamination prevention
  • Meat & Seafood Storage — Separation, packaging, and temperature requirements
  • Produce Storage — Vegetables, fruits, and herbs: segregation and shelf life management
  • Ready-to-Eat Foods — Storage separation and prevention of cross-contamination
  • First Aid & Emergency Response — Handling food contamination incidents and spoilage

Module 5: Kitchen Sanitation, Cleaning & Pest Control

📚 Topics Covered

  • Kitchen Layout & Design — Work zones, flow patterns, and hygiene-friendly design principles
  • Cleaning Procedures — Daily, weekly, and monthly cleaning schedules for commercial kitchens
  • Cleaning Agents & Sanitizers — Types, dilution, and safe handling of chemicals
  • Equipment Sanitation — Cleaning cutting boards, knives, cooking vessels, and appliances
  • Utensil & Crockery Washing — Manual and mechanical dishwashing standards
  • Surface Cleaning — Countertops, floors, walls, and ceiling maintenance protocols
  • Drain & Waste Management — Prevention of blockages and contamination from waste areas
  • Pest Control Measures — Rodents, insects, and birds: prevention and management
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM) — Non-chemical and chemical pest control strategies
  • Pest Identification & Monitoring — Signs of infestation and reporting procedures
  • Waste Segregation & Disposal — Organic, inorganic, and hazardous waste management
  • Cleaning Records & Documentation — Maintaining hygiene logs for FSSAI compliance

Module 6: Food Preparation Safety & Best Practices in Indian Kitchens

📚 Topics Covered

  • Raw Food Preparation — Washing, cutting, and safe handling of vegetables and meat
  • Cooking Temperatures — Safe internal temperatures for different Indian food items
  • Cooking Methods & Safety — Boiling, frying, grilling, and steaming in commercial settings
  • Spice Blending Safety — Preventing contamination in spice mixes and masalas
  • Fermented Foods — Safe preparation of dosa batter, idli, and other fermented items
  • Oil & Fat Management — Storage, reuse, and disposal of cooking oils
  • Seasoning & Condiments — Safe addition and contamination prevention during service
  • Plating & Presentation — Food safety during garnishing and plate assembly
  • Hot & Cold Food Service — Maintaining temperatures during buffet and counter service
  • Leftover Management — Storage, reheating, and safe disposal of unused food
  • Recipe Documentation — Recording ingredients, cooking times, and food safety procedures
  • Customer Communication — Allergen disclosure and dietary requirement management

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (16 years preferred for employment)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior food safety experience required
  • Valid government ID proof required (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Medical fitness certificate recommended (no communicable diseases)
  • Already working in food industry? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) assessment
  • Applicable for all candidates: students, homemakers, entrepreneurs, and working professionals
  • Physical ability to stand for extended periods and handle food items

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand and apply FSSAI regulations, food safety acts, and compliance requirements in food businesses
  • Identify food contamination sources and implement effective prevention and control measures
  • Demonstrate proper personal hygiene practices and maintain food handler health standards
  • Manage safe food storage, temperature control, and maintain cold chain integrity
  • Execute effective kitchen sanitation, cleaning procedures, and pest control measures
  • Apply safe food preparation practices specific to Indian cuisine and prevent foodborne illnesses
