Pipe Fitting & Valve Maintenance

📂 Sector: Plumbing, Sanitation & Water 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the essential skills of pipe fitting and valve maintenance to become a skilled professional in India's growing plumbing sector. This 1-month intensive program covers practical techniques, safety standards, and hands-on training to prepare you for immediate employment in residential, commercial, and industrial water systems.

Module 1: Introduction to Plumbing Systems & Safety

📚 Topics Covered

  • Overview of plumbing systems in India — water supply, drainage, and sanitation basics
  • Indian plumbing codes and standards — NBC (National Building Code) compliance and BIS standards
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) — proper use of safety gear, helmets, gloves, and footwear
  • Workplace safety and hazard identification — recognizing electrical, chemical, and physical hazards
  • First aid and emergency procedures — basic first aid for common plumbing injuries
  • Tool safety and equipment handling — safe storage and usage of hand and power tools
  • Health and hygiene practices — preventing water-borne diseases and contamination
  • Fire safety and emergency evacuation — understanding fire risks in plumbing work

Module 2: Pipe Materials, Sizing & Selection

📚 Topics Covered

  • Types of pipes — GI (Galvanized Iron), PVC, CPVC, PPR, copper, and cast iron pipes used in India
  • Pipe specifications and standards — IS codes for different pipe materials and their applications
  • Pipe sizing calculations — understanding diameter, pressure ratings, and flow capacity
  • Selecting pipes for different applications — cold water, hot water, drainage, and sewage systems
  • Pipe fittings and connections — elbows, tees, reducers, unions, and couplings
  • Understanding pressure ratings — nominal pressure (PN) and its importance in system design
  • Cost comparison and durability — advantages and limitations of each pipe material in Indian climate
  • Storage and handling of pipes — preventing damage and contamination before installation
  • Reading technical specifications — interpreting manufacturers' data sheets and Indian standards
  • Common defects in pipes — identifying cracks, corrosion, and manufacturing defects

Module 3: Hand Tools, Power Tools & Equipment

📚 Topics Covered

  • Essential hand tools — pipe wrenches, adjustable wrenches, screwdrivers, hammers, and chisels
  • Cutting tools — hacksaw, pipe cutter, and angle grinder operation and maintenance
  • Threading tools — manual and electric threading machines for GI pipes
  • Measuring instruments — measuring tape, level, try square, and pipe thickness gauges
  • Power tools — electric drills, impact drivers, and their safe operation
  • Specialized tools — pipe benders, flaring tools, and crimping tools for different materials
  • Tool maintenance and care — cleaning, sharpening, and storage practices
  • Identifying tool defects — recognizing worn-out or damaged tools that need replacement
  • Rental and procurement — where to source tools in your local area
  • Cost estimation for tool kits — building a professional toolkit for independent work
  • Safety features in modern tools — understanding kill switches, guards, and safety mechanisms
  • Troubleshooting tool problems — handling common tool malfunctions on-site

Module 4: Pipe Fitting Techniques & Installation Methods

📚 Topics Covered

  • Threaded pipe connections — cutting, threading, and joining GI pipes using traditional methods
  • Solvent cement jointing — preparing, applying, and curing PVC/CPVC pipe connections
  • Compression fittings — installing and tightening compression connections for copper and plastic pipes
  • Push-fit connections — understanding and installing modern push-fit systems like PPR and MDPE
  • Flange connections — bolting and sealing flanged pipe joints for industrial applications
  • Elbow and tee installation — proper positioning and alignment of directional fittings
  • Reducing and expanding connections — joining pipes of different diameters correctly
  • Pipe bending techniques — cold bending, hot bending, and using pipe benders for different materials
  • Slope and gradient — maintaining proper slope for drainage and sewage systems (1:50 to 1:100)
  • Support and clipping — installing pipe clamps, brackets, and supports at correct intervals
  • Underground pipe laying — depth, bedding, and protection for buried water and sewage lines
  • Pressure testing — conducting hydrostatic tests to verify joint integrity before commissioning

Module 5: Valves: Types, Functions & Maintenance

📚 Topics Covered

  • Ball valves — construction, operation, and applications in water supply systems
  • Gate valves — understanding sluice gates and their use in isolation and control
  • Check valves — preventing backflow in water supply and sewage systems
  • Butterfly valves — large-capacity flow control in main supply lines
  • Needle valves — precision control in metering and instrumentation applications
  • Pressure relief valves — protecting systems from overpressure and burst risks
  • Float valves — automatic water level control in overhead tanks and cisterns
  • Solenoid valves — electromagnetic control for automated water systems
  • Valve body materials — brass, cast iron, and plastic valves for different water conditions
  • Valve maintenance procedures — cleaning, lubrication, and replacement of worn parts
  • Identifying valve leaks — detecting and fixing internal and external leakage
  • Valve sizing and selection — choosing correct valve sizes for flow requirements

Module 6: Troubleshooting, Repair & System Commissioning

📚 Topics Covered

  • Common plumbing problems — leaks, blockages, low pressure, and water quality issues
  • Diagnosing pipe leaks — locating hidden leaks using visual inspection and sound detection
  • Repairing leaks — patching, wrapping, and replacing damaged pipe sections
  • Clearing blockages — using plungers, drain snakes, and high-pressure jets safely
  • Corrosion issues — identifying and managing rust in GI pipes and brass fittings
  • Water hammer prevention — understanding pressure surges and installing expansion tanks
  • Pressure regulation — adjusting pressure reducing valves for optimal system performance
  • Flow rate testing — measuring and troubleshooting inadequate water supply
  • System flushing and cleaning — removing construction debris and sediment from new installations
  • Pressure testing procedures — conducting safety tests before handover to customers
  • Documentation and handover — preparing maintenance manuals and user instructions
  • Preventive maintenance schedules — planning regular inspections and servicing routines
  • Emergency repairs — responding quickly to burst pipes and water damage situations

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or 16 years for certain states following local regulations)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required — open to all candidates
  • Already working as a plumber? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) direct exam
  • Valid identity proof required — Aadhaar, PAN, voter ID, or driving license
  • Medical fitness — able to perform physical work including climbing and bending
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered migrant workers
  • No upper age limit — candidates of all ages welcome

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and select appropriate pipe materials and fittings based on application and Indian standards
  • Execute proper pipe cutting, threading, and joining techniques for different connection types
  • Install, operate, and maintain various valve types in water supply and drainage systems
  • Diagnose common plumbing problems and perform effective repairs using professional techniques
  • Conduct pressure testing and system commissioning following safety and quality standards
  • Apply workplace safety practices and follow Indian plumbing codes in all work activities
