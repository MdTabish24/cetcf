Auto Electrician

📂 Sector: Automobile & Motor Trades 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the essential skills of automotive electrical systems and become job-ready in 8 weeks. This hands-on foundation course teaches you to diagnose, repair, and maintain electrical components in cars, bikes, and commercial vehicles used across India. Get certified and start earning immediately in the booming automobile sector.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Automotive Electricity

📚 Topics Covered

  • Basics of electricity — voltage, current, resistance, and power in simple terms
  • Ohm's Law and its application in vehicle electrical circuits
  • Types of electrical circuits — series, parallel, and series-parallel combinations
  • AC and DC current — understanding alternating and direct current in vehicles
  • Electrical safety — precautions, earthing, and preventing electrical shocks
  • Basic electrical symbols and circuit diagrams used in Indian vehicles
  • Multimeter usage — measuring voltage, current, and resistance in circuits
  • Common electrical faults and their identification methods

Module 2: Vehicle Battery Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Battery construction — lead-acid battery components and working principle
  • Battery types — conventional, maintenance-free, and sealed batteries used in India
  • Battery capacity and rating — Ah (Ampere-hour) and CCA (Cold Cranking Amps)
  • Battery charging and discharging cycles — how batteries work in vehicles
  • Battery terminals and connections — positive, negative, and earth connections
  • Starting procedure — battery testing with hydrometer and multimeter
  • Battery maintenance and care — water level, terminal cleaning, and corrosion removal
  • Troubleshooting battery problems — dead battery, low charge, and terminal issues
  • Battery replacement and disposal — safe handling and environmental practices
  • Modern battery management systems in new vehicles

Module 3: Starting and Charging Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Starter motor construction and working principle in Indian vehicles
  • Starter motor components — armature, field coils, commutator, and brushes
  • Starter circuit operation — how battery powers the starter motor
  • Alternator basics — AC generator principle and construction
  • Alternator components — rotor, stator, rectifier, and regulator functions
  • Alternator output and regulation — maintaining correct charging voltage (13.5-14.5V)
  • Charging circuit operation — how alternator charges battery while engine runs
  • Common starter problems — no-start conditions, grinding noise, and slow cranking
  • Common alternator problems — low charge, overcharge, and noise issues
  • Testing starter and alternator — using multimeter and load testing methods
  • Replacement procedures for starter motor and alternator
  • Modern smart charging systems in new Indian car models

Module 4: Ignition and Fuel System Electrical Components

📚 Topics Covered

  • Ignition system overview — mechanical and electronic ignition in Indian vehicles
  • Spark plugs — types, gap setting, and replacement procedures
  • Ignition coil function and testing methods
  • Distributor and ignition timing — how spark timing works in engines
  • Electronic ignition systems — advantages over mechanical ignition
  • Fuel pump electrical operation — 12V fuel pump circuits and relays
  • Fuel pump relay and fuel pump fuse — protection and circuit control
  • Fuel injector electrical operation — solenoid-type injectors and pulse control
  • Injector circuit testing — checking resistance and signal patterns
  • Engine management sensor circuits — MAF, MAP, and oxygen sensors
  • Common ignition and fuel system electrical faults
  • Diagnostic procedures for no-start and hard-start conditions

Module 5: Lighting, Wiper, and Accessory Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Headlight system — halogen, LED, and HID types used in Indian cars
  • Headlight circuit operation — dipped and main beam switching
  • Tail lights, brake lights, and indicator lights — circuit operation and bulbs
  • Interior lighting — dome lights, reading lights, and courtesy switches
  • Wiper motor circuit — two-speed and intermittent wiper operation
  • Wiper switch and relay operation — controlling wiper speed and park position
  • Windshield washer pump circuit — electrical operation and troubleshooting
  • Horn circuit — relay operation and common horn problems
  • Electric window motors — power window switch and regulator circuits
  • Door lock actuators — central locking system operation
  • Air conditioner compressor clutch — electromagnetic operation
  • Accessory circuits — cigarette lighter, power outlets, and infotainment power

Module 6: Wiring, Fuses, Relays, and Practical Troubleshooting

📚 Topics Covered

  • Automotive wiring — copper conductors, insulation, and wire gauges used in India
  • Wire color codes and identification — understanding Indian vehicle wiring standards
  • Connectors and terminals — crimp terminals, connectors, and proper connection methods
  • Soldering techniques — proper soldering for reliable electrical connections
  • Fuse types and ratings — cartridge fuses, blade fuses, and circuit protection
  • Fuse box location and identification — finding correct fuses in Indian vehicles
  • Relay function and operation — electromagnetic relay working principle
  • Relay circuits — understanding relay control and power circuits
  • Earthing and grounding — importance of proper earth connections in vehicles
  • Wiring harness repair — splicing, joining, and insulating wires safely
  • Troubleshooting methodology — systematic approach to finding electrical faults
  • Using wiring diagrams — reading and interpreting vehicle electrical schematics
  • Common electrical faults — open circuits, short circuits, and high resistance issues
  • Practical hands-on troubleshooting — diagnosing real vehicle problems
  • Safety practices in workshop — preventing accidents and electrical hazards
  • Tool handling and equipment maintenance — caring for electrical diagnostic tools

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or 18 for some states)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior experience required — beginners welcome
  • Already working as auto electrician? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Medical fitness certificate may be required
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand basic electrical principles and their application in vehicles
  • Diagnose and troubleshoot battery, starter, and charging system faults
  • Test and repair ignition and fuel system electrical components
  • Maintain and repair lighting, wiper, and accessory electrical systems
  • Read wiring diagrams and identify electrical circuits in vehicles
  • Perform safe electrical repairs using proper tools and techniques
  • Apply systematic troubleshooting methodology to find electrical faults
  • Maintain workshop safety standards and handle electrical hazards properly
