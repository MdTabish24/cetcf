Tyre Fitting & Wheel Balancing

📂 Sector: Automobile & Motor Trades 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the essential skills of tyre fitting and wheel balancing—critical services in India's booming automotive aftermarket. This foundation course equips you with hands-on expertise to work in tyre shops, service centers, and petrol pumps across the country.

Module 1: Automobile Fundamentals & Tyre Basics

📚 Topics Covered

  • Vehicle types in India (2-wheeler, 3-wheeler, 4-wheeler, commercial) — classifications and tyre requirements for each
  • Tyre construction — understanding radial vs bias ply, sidewall, tread, bead, and carcass components
  • Tyre nomenclature & size coding — reading tyre markings like 185/65R15, speed ratings, load index, manufacturing date
  • Types of tyres — all-season, monsoon, summer, tubeless, tube-type, and run-flat tyres used in India
  • Tyre defects & damage identification — punctures, bulges, cracks, uneven wear patterns, dry rot, and chunking
  • Wheel types and materials — steel wheels, alloy wheels, spoke wheels, and their maintenance requirements
  • Safety standards in India — BIS certification, ISI marks, and quality parameters for tyres
  • Tyre storage and inventory management — proper storage conditions, rotation, and shelf-life tracking

Module 2: Tyre Fitting Tools, Equipment & Safety Procedures

📚 Topics Covered

  • Tyre fitting tools — tyre levers, bead breakers, tyre mounting machines, air compressors, and manual vs electric options
  • Wheel balancing equipment — static balancers, dynamic balancers, laser wheel alignment machines, and computerized systems
  • Tyre repair tools — plugging kits, patching materials, buffing machines, and vulcanization equipment
  • Safety equipment required — safety gloves, goggles, steel-toed shoes, aprons, and face protection
  • Workshop safety standards — fire safety, first aid box, chemical storage, and electrical safety compliance
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE) — correct usage and maintenance of safety gear
  • Machine operation manuals — understanding specifications, capacity limits, and maintenance schedules
  • Safety procedures for tyre removal and fitting — preventing accidents, proper lifting techniques, and emergency protocols
  • Environmental safety — handling waste tyres, old tubes, and chemical disposal according to Indian regulations
  • Tool maintenance — cleaning, calibration, and preventive maintenance of equipment

Module 3: Tyre Fitting Process & Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Vehicle lifting and securing — using jacks, jack stands, and vehicle ramps safely for different vehicle types
  • Wheel removal procedure — using correct socket sizes, lug nut removal sequence, and wheel storage
  • Tyre removal from rim — using bead breakers, tyre levers, and tyre machines without damaging rims
  • Rim cleaning and inspection — removing old rubber residue, checking for cracks, dents, and corrosion
  • Tyre mounting on rim — applying tyre lubricant, seating the bead, and using mounting machines correctly
  • Tubeless tyre fitting — valve stem installation, sealing, and pressure testing for air-tightness
  • Tube-type tyre fitting — tube inspection, powder application, and proper tube positioning
  • Initial inflation and bead seating — using air compressors, achieving proper pressure, and confirming bead seating
  • Wheel reinstallation — correct lug nut torque sequences, cross-pattern tightening, and final pressure check
  • Common fitting errors — over-inflation, under-inflation, improper bead seating, and misalignment issues
  • Two-wheeler and three-wheeler specific procedures — working with smaller wheels and different mounting systems
  • Quality check after fitting — visual inspection, pressure verification, and balance readiness

Module 4: Wheel Balancing & Alignment Fundamentals

📚 Topics Covered

  • Wheel balancing principles — understanding static and dynamic imbalance, centrifugal forces, and vibration causes
  • Static wheel balancing — using bubble balancers, weight placement, and manual balancing techniques
  • Dynamic wheel balancing — using computerized balancing machines, reading weight specifications, and adhesive weight application
  • Weight types and materials — lead weights, zinc weights, self-adhesive weights, and clip-on weights used in India
  • Balancing machine operation — mounting wheels, entering specifications, interpreting readings, and safety procedures
  • Identifying imbalance symptoms — steering wheel vibration, uneven tyre wear, and noise during driving
  • Wheel alignment basics — understanding toe-in, camber, caster, and their effect on tyre life
  • Visual alignment checks — using string method, laser alignment, and professional alignment equipment
  • Alignment issues in Indian vehicles — common problems due to road conditions, potholes, and speed bumps
  • Balancing for different wheel sizes — adjusting techniques for 12-inch to 20-inch wheels
  • Re-balancing procedures — when and how to rebalance tyres after repairs or adjustments
  • Documentation — recording balancing data, weights applied, and customer records

Module 5: Tyre Repair, Maintenance & Customer Service

📚 Topics Covered

  • Puncture repair — identifying puncture location, temporary vs permanent repairs, and patch/plug techniques
  • Tyre buffing and patching — using buffing machines, applying rubber patches, and vulcanization process
  • Tyre plugging — using plug kits, inserting plugs, and sealing for tubeless tyres
  • Valve stem replacement — removing old valves, installing new ones, and pressure testing
  • Tyre pressure maintenance — checking pressure with gauges, recommended PSI for Indian vehicles, and seasonal adjustments
  • Tyre rotation schedules — cross-rotation, front-to-back rotation, and maintenance intervals
  • Tyre cleaning and conditioning — removing dirt, applying protectants, and extending tyre life
  • Customer communication — explaining tyre conditions, repair options, and cost estimates in simple language
  • Billing and documentation — creating invoices, maintaining service records, and warranty information
  • Handling customer complaints — troubleshooting issues, offering solutions, and building customer loyalty
  • Upselling and cross-selling — recommending tyre upgrades, alignment services, and preventive maintenance

Module 6: Business Operations, Quality Standards & Career Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Starting a tyre shop — location selection, licensing, registration, and compliance with local authorities
  • Inventory management — stock tracking, supplier relationships, seasonal demand planning, and waste management
  • Pricing strategies — cost calculation, profit margins, competitive pricing, and seasonal adjustments in Indian market
  • Quality standards and certifications — BIS standards, ISO compliance, and customer satisfaction metrics
  • Record keeping and documentation — service logs, customer data, warranty tracking, and GST compliance
  • Health and hygiene standards — workshop cleanliness, waste disposal, and regulatory compliance
  • Customer relationship management — maintaining client database, follow-ups, and loyalty programs
  • Digital tools and software — using billing software, mobile apps for bookings, and online presence
  • Troubleshooting common issues — vibration problems, pulling to one side, uneven wear, and noise complaints
  • Advanced skills — wheel straightening, tyre repair on specialty vehicles, and custom wheel fitting
  • Career progression — becoming a supervisor, service manager, or shop owner with experience
  • Entrepreneurship opportunities — franchising, mobile tyre fitting services, and specialized services in tier-2 cities
  • Continuous learning — staying updated with new tyre technology, vehicle models, and industry trends
  • Professional networking — joining tyre dealer associations, attending trade shows, and building industry connections

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or 16 years for some states)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass
  • No prior experience needed for foundation level
  • Physical fitness required — ability to lift and work with heavy wheels
  • Already working in tyre fitting? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhar, PAN, or voter ID)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered workers
  • Medical fitness certificate may be required (check with your state CETCF office)

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and classify different tyre types, sizes, and quality standards used in Indian vehicles
  • Safely operate tyre fitting tools and wheel balancing equipment following all safety protocols
  • Remove, fit, and balance tyres on 2-wheelers, 3-wheelers, and 4-wheelers with precision
  • Diagnose tyre defects, perform repairs, and provide appropriate maintenance recommendations
  • Communicate effectively with customers, create service records, and maintain professional standards
  • Plan and manage a tyre fitting business operation with proper inventory, pricing, and quality control
