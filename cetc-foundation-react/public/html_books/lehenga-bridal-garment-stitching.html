Lehenga & Bridal Garment Stitching

📂 Sector: Fashion, Tailoring & Textiles 📊 Level: Intermediate ⏱️ Duration: 4 Months 🕐 Hours: 240 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1200
Master the art of creating stunning lehengas and bridal garments with professional-grade stitching techniques. This intermediate course trains you to produce high-quality traditional and contemporary bridal wear, from pattern making to final embellishment, preparing you for employment or entrepreneurship in India's booming wedding fashion industry.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Bridal Garment Construction & Indian Textiles

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to bridal wear industry in India — market trends, regional variations, and career opportunities
  • Types of lehengas — A-line, mermaid, sharara, peplum, and contemporary fusion styles
  • Understanding ghagra-choli, lehenga-saree, and bridal sharara construction basics
  • Indian textile types — silk, georgette, net, organza, velvet, and their properties for bridal wear
  • Fabric weight, drape, and stretch characteristics for different lehenga styles
  • Introduction to embellishment fabrics — brocade, zari, sequins, beads, and mirrors used in Indian bridal wear
  • Measurement taking for lehengas — waist, hip, length, bust, and custom fitting points
  • Tools and equipment for bridal garment stitching — scissors, needles, measuring tapes, and specialized tools
  • Workplace safety and hygiene standards in garment production units

Module 2: Pattern Making & Drafting for Lehengas

📚 Topics Covered

  • Basic pattern drafting for A-line lehenga — waist, hip, and length calculations
  • Creating circular and semi-circular lehenga patterns for maximum volume and drape
  • Mermaid lehenga pattern drafting — fitted waist to hip with flared bottom
  • Sharara pattern making — wide leg pants with fitted waist and flare from knee
  • Peplum lehenga construction — adding structured layers and balance
  • Choli (blouse) pattern drafting for bridal wear — fitted bust, armhole, and neckline design
  • Dupatta (scarf) pattern variations — rectangular, triangular, and shaped duppatta designs
  • Grading patterns for different sizes — scaling measurements proportionally
  • Pattern marking and cutting layouts for fabric efficiency and minimal wastage
  • Using commercial patterns vs. custom drafting for client requirements
  • Digital pattern making tools and traditional methods comparison

Module 3: Fabric Cutting, Marking & Preparation Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pre-cutting fabric preparation — washing, drying, and shrinkage control for silk and georgette
  • Laying out fabric — nap consideration, grain line alignment, and directional prints
  • Pattern placement strategies for lehengas — maximizing fabric usage and minimizing waste
  • Marking techniques — tailor chalk, tracing wheel, and carbon paper for bridal wear
  • Cutting methods — hand cutting with scissors, rotary cutters, and precision tools
  • Managing delicate fabrics — handling net, organza, and embellished materials without damage
  • Quality checks during cutting — identifying defects, stains, and weaving issues
  • Organizing cut pieces — labeling, bundling, and preparing for stitching process
  • Handling embellished and heavy fabrics — special cutting techniques for zari and sequined materials
  • Cost calculation based on fabric wastage and yield percentage

Module 4: Core Stitching Techniques for Lehengas & Bridal Wear

📚 Topics Covered

  • Hand stitching vs. machine stitching — when to use each method for bridal garments
  • Basic machine stitching — straight stitch, backstitch, and tension adjustment for silk fabrics
  • Seam finishing techniques — French seams, flat-fell seams, and bound seams for durability
  • Constructing lehenga panels — joining multiple pieces while maintaining grain and drape
  • Waistband construction — adding elastic, drawstring, or fitted waistbands for comfort
  • Hemming techniques — hand-stitched hems, machine hems, and decorative hemlines for lehengas
  • Creating flares and gores — adding volume to lehenga bottom while maintaining balance
  • Pleat and tuck construction — knife pleats, box pleats, and structured tucks in bridal wear
  • Godets and inserts — adding triangular or shaped fabric pieces for volume and design
  • Choli (blouse) construction — armholes, necklines, and fitting techniques
  • Dupatta attachment methods — stitching to choli, creating flowing drape
  • Quality stitching standards — stitch length consistency, seam straightness, and professional finish

Module 5: Embellishment, Decoration & Finishing Methods

📚 Topics Covered

  • Hand embroidery techniques — French knots, stem stitch, chain stitch, and cross stitch for bridal wear
  • Zari and gota work — stitching metallic threads and ribbons on lehengas and cholis
  • Sequin and bead application — hand-sewn and machine methods for embellishment
  • Mirror work (shisha) — stitching small mirrors on fabric for traditional Indian bridal look
  • Thread embroidery designs — creating floral patterns and border designs on lehengas
  • Lace attachment — stitching lace borders, panels, and overlays on bridal garments
  • Fringe and tassel creation — adding decorative fringes to lehenga hems and dupatta borders
  • Appliqué work — stitching fabric pieces for contrast and design elements
  • Beadwork patterns — creating intricate bead designs on choli and lehenga bodice
  • Combining multiple embellishment techniques — creating cohesive bridal designs
  • Embellishment placement and balance — proportional design for different body types
  • Durability testing — ensuring embellishments stay secure after wearing and dry cleaning
  • Finishing touches — pressing, final quality checks, and garment presentation

Module 6: Fitting, Customization, Costing & Professional Practice

📚 Topics Covered

  • Taking accurate measurements — standard points for lehenga fitting and custom requirements
  • Fitting trials and adjustments — testing lehenga fit on different body shapes
  • Alterations and modifications — adjusting length, waist, hip, and volume based on customer feedback
  • Creating custom designs — translating client sketches and Pinterest ideas into wearable garments
  • Regional customization — adapting lehenga styles for North Indian, South Indian, and regional preferences
  • Bridal wear fitting standards — ensuring comfort for 8-12 hour wear during wedding events
  • Working with difficult fabrics — managing stretch, slippage, and sheer materials during fitting
  • Creating sample garments — making prototypes for design approval and testing
  • Material costing — calculating fabric, thread, embellishment, and labor costs
  • Pricing strategies — market research, competitor analysis, and profit margin calculation
  • Creating quotations and work orders — documenting specifications and delivery timelines
  • Client communication and expectation management — discussing designs, timelines, and revisions
  • Production timeline planning — scheduling multiple orders and managing deadlines
  • Quality assurance — final inspection checklist for completed garments
  • Packaging and presentation — professional delivery of bridal wear to clients
  • Building portfolio — documenting completed work for marketing and business growth

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience in stitching required — course suitable for beginners
  • Already working as tailor or in garment industry? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, or voter ID)
  • Applicable for all candidates including women, minorities, and persons with disabilities
  • Basic understanding of Hindi or English helpful but not mandatory

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Successfully measure, draft, and cut patterns for various lehenga styles including A-line, mermaid, sharara, and peplum designs
  • Construct professional-quality lehengas and cholis using appropriate stitching techniques and fabric handling methods
  • Apply traditional and contemporary embellishment techniques including hand embroidery, zari work, beadwork, and sequin attachment
  • Customize and fit bridal garments to individual client specifications and body types
  • Calculate material costs, create quotations, and manage production timelines for bridal wear orders
  • Maintain quality standards and professional practices in garment construction and client communication

💼 Career Paths

Lehenga Stitching Technician
Work in bridal wear production units, boutiques, or designer studios. Responsible for stitching lehengas, cholis, and bridal garments according to specifications.
Bridal Garment Specialist
Work with high-end bridal boutiques or designer houses. Handle premium bridal wear stitching and customization for wealthy clients.
Independent Tailor/Stitching Expert
Start your own tailoring business from home or rented space. Take custom orders for lehengas, bridal wear, and party garments.
Bridal Wear Entrepreneur
Start a bridal boutique or designer brand specializing in lehengas and bridal wear. Build clientele and expand to multiple locations.
Embellishment Specialist
Specialize in hand embroidery, zari work, beadwork, and decorative techniques for bridal garments. Work with multiple boutiques or freelance.
Garment Production Manager
Manage production of bridal wear in manufacturing units. Oversee quality, timelines, and team of stitchers.
Online Bridal Wear Business
Create and sell custom lehengas through e-commerce platforms (Etsy, Instagram, personal website). Reach customers across India and abroad.
Bridal Wear Trainer/Instructor
Train aspiring tailors and garment workers in bridal wear stitching. Work with vocational institutes, NGOs, or online platforms.
