DTH & Cable TV Technician

📂 Sector: Electrical & Electronics Trades 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the skills to install, maintain, and troubleshoot DTH and Cable TV systems across India. This foundation course trains you to become a certified technician capable of handling customer installations, signal quality checks, and technical repairs in the rapidly growing broadcast television sector.

Module 1: Fundamentals of DTH & Cable TV Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to DTH (Direct-to-Home) technology — satellite-based TV delivery system working principles
  • Cable TV network basics — cable distribution, headend systems, and signal transmission over coaxial cables
  • Difference between DTH and Cable TV — comparison of service models, coverage, and customer base in India
  • Broadcasting standards in India — PAL/NTSC formats, digital standards, and regulatory framework by Ministry of Information & Broadcasting
  • Frequency bands and spectrum allocation — C-band, Ku-band usage, and Indian satellite positions (INSAT series)
  • Signal flow diagram — from satellite/headend to customer TV set, understanding each component's role
  • Role of set-top boxes — analog vs digital STBs, encryption, and conditional access systems
  • Major DTH and Cable operators in India — Tata Sky, Airtel Digital TV, Dish TV, Den Networks, and local cable operators
  • Safety regulations and standards — electrical safety, RF radiation limits, and TRAI guidelines for technicians

Module 2: DTH System Components & Installation Hardware

📚 Topics Covered

  • Satellite dish types and specifications — dish sizes (60cm, 75cm, 90cm), gain calculations, and Indian weather considerations
  • Dish mounting structures — wall mount, pole mount, roof mount, and structural load calculations for Indian homes
  • LNB (Low Noise Block) converter — types, frequency conversion, noise figure, and polarization (vertical/horizontal)
  • Coaxial cables — RG-6, RG-59 specifications, impedance (75 ohms), shielding quality, and Indian brand standards
  • Connectors and adapters — F-type connectors, BNC, RCA types, crimping techniques, and waterproofing for Indian monsoon
  • Set-top box types — HD boxes, 4K boxes, recording boxes, and compatibility with different TV models
  • Cable distribution amplifiers — signal boosters, specifications, power requirements, and placement in cable networks
  • Grounding and earthing systems — lightning protection, bonding requirements, and safety for rooftop installations
  • Cable splitters and combiners — passive components, insertion loss, return loss, and proper usage in home networks
  • Power supply systems — backup power needs, UPS requirements for DTH headends, and customer premises equipment
  • Installation tools and equipment — signal meter, multimeter, cable stripper, crimper, compass, inclinometer, and measurement tape

Module 3: DTH Dish Alignment & Signal Reception

📚 Topics Covered

  • Satellite positioning — orbital slots of Indian satellites (INSAT 4A, 4B, 4CR at 93.5°E, 55°E, 74°E), azimuth and elevation calculations
  • Geographical coordinates and dish pointing — latitude/longitude of Indian cities, elevation angle formulas for different regions
  • Compass usage and magnetic declination — true north vs magnetic north, declination values for Indian cities
  • Elevation angle calculation — step-by-step procedure using satellite position and customer location
  • Azimuth angle calculation — directional finding using compass and mathematical formulas specific to Indian geography
  • Dish alignment procedure — step-by-step installation, fine-tuning for maximum signal strength
  • Signal strength measurement — using signal meter, reading dBm values, acceptable signal levels (above -65 dBm for good reception)
  • Signal quality assessment — BER (Bit Error Rate), MER (Modulation Error Ratio), and visual quality checks
  • Polarization adjustment — vertical and horizontal polarization, skew angle adjustment for Indian satellite positions
  • Weather impact on signals — rain fade, cloud cover effects in different Indian regions, seasonal variations
  • Troubleshooting weak signals — repositioning dish, checking connections, LNB replacement, and cable damage assessment
  • Documentation and customer handover — signal strength records, dish orientation details, and maintenance guidelines

Module 4: Cable TV Network Installation & Maintenance

📚 Topics Covered

  • Cable TV network architecture — headend, trunk lines, distribution lines, drop lines, and tap points in Indian cable networks
  • Headend equipment — signal reception, modulation, multiplexing, and scrambling systems used by Indian cable operators
  • Cable distribution planning — network design for residential societies, commercial areas, and rural Indian villages
  • Trunk cable installation — laying underground cables, aerial cable routes, and protection against theft in Indian context
  • Tap installation and connection — signal splitting, subscriber connection points, and indoor wiring standards
  • In-home cable wiring — routing cables through walls, use of conduits, wall-mounted boxes, and aesthetic considerations
  • Signal level optimization — maintaining proper signal levels at each tap point, amplifier placement and settings
  • Cable splitter and combiner usage — passive vs active components, proper impedance matching, and cascade limitations
  • Grounding and bonding in cable networks — safety standards, lightning protection, and compliance with electrical codes
  • Cable maintenance schedules — regular signal checks, amplifier servicing, corrosion prevention, and seasonal maintenance
  • Subscriber connection and disconnection — proper procedures, documentation, and security sealing of connections
  • Billing and customer management — connection documentation, service activation, and disconnect procedures

Module 5: Set-Top Box Configuration & Troubleshooting

📚 Topics Covered

  • Set-top box types and models — understanding different STB models from major manufacturers, Indian market variants
  • Initial setup and activation — unpacking, connection to TV and power, first-time boot procedures
  • Menu navigation and basic settings — language selection, picture settings, audio configuration, and regional preferences
  • Channel scanning and tuning — auto-scan procedures, manual channel addition, favorite channel management
  • Conditional Access System (CAS) — smart card insertion, activation procedures, and understanding encryption
  • Subscription and recharge — online recharge methods, balance checking, package selection for Indian operators
  • Remote control operation — standard functions, learning remote codes, and troubleshooting non-responsive remotes
  • Video output options — HDMI, composite video, RF output selection based on TV type and cable quality
  • Audio output configuration — stereo, surround sound, and audio sync issues with older TV sets
  • Common STB problems — no signal, no picture, no sound, frozen screen, and step-by-step troubleshooting procedures
  • Software updates and firmware — when and how to update, common update issues, and rollback procedures
  • Customer support and warranty — understanding warranty terms, service center locations in India, and complaint registration

Module 6: Professional Skills, Safety & Career Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Electrical safety fundamentals — understanding voltage, current, grounding, and protection against electric shock
  • RF radiation safety — understanding electromagnetic fields, safe exposure limits, and protective measures for technicians
  • Height and fall safety — working on rooftops and poles, safety harness usage, and accident prevention protocols
  • Customer communication skills — explaining technical concepts in simple language, handling complaints professionally
  • Work ethics and punctuality — reliability, honesty in diagnosis, and building customer trust in Indian market
  • Documentation and record-keeping — maintaining installation records, service logs, and customer contact information
  • Entrepreneurship opportunities — starting own DTH/Cable service business, franchise models, and local market analysis
  • Licensing and certification — CETCF certification benefits, renewal procedures, and industry recognition in India
  • Continuous learning — staying updated with technology changes, attending refresher courses, and professional development
  • Quality assurance practices — checking installation quality, customer satisfaction surveys, and feedback handling

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (with parental consent if below 18)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience in DTH/Cable TV required
  • Already working as technician? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) direct exam
  • Valid government ID proof required (Aadhar, PAN, Driving License, or Passport)
  • Medical fitness certificate for safety-sensitive work (optional but recommended)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Install and configure DTH satellite dishes with proper azimuth and elevation angles for Indian satellite positions
  • Perform complete cable TV network connections and in-home wiring with correct signal levels and grounding
  • Measure and optimize signal strength using signal meters and achieve acceptable reception quality standards
  • Set up, activate, and troubleshoot set-top boxes and resolve common customer issues independently
  • Apply electrical and RF safety practices and work safely at heights during rooftop installations
  • Provide professional customer service, maintain documentation, and support business growth in DTH/Cable sector
