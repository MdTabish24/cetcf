CCTV Monitoring & Control Room Operator

📂 Sector: Security & Facility Management 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the essential skills to operate CCTV systems and manage control rooms in India's fastest-growing security sector. This foundation course trains you to monitor multiple camera feeds, respond to alerts, and maintain critical surveillance infrastructure for banks, malls, offices, and industrial facilities.

Module 1: Introduction to CCTV Systems & Security Fundamentals

📚 Topics Covered

  • Overview of CCTV systems in India — types, applications, and industry standards
  • Security sector regulations and CETCF certification framework
  • Role and responsibilities of CCTV monitoring operators in different sectors
  • Types of surveillance cameras — analog, IP, thermal, and 360-degree cameras
  • Understanding camera specifications — resolution, megapixels, focal length, and field of view
  • Basic principles of video surveillance and privacy laws in India
  • Health and safety protocols in control room environments
  • Introduction to control room layout and equipment arrangement
  • Professional ethics and code of conduct for security operators
  • Career opportunities and skill requirements in CCTV operations

Module 2: CCTV Equipment & Recording Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • DVR (Digital Video Recorder) systems — architecture, specifications, and models used in India
  • NVR (Network Video Recorder) systems — IP-based recording and storage
  • Hard disk management — storage capacity calculation, redundancy, and RAID systems
  • Camera installation and cabling — coaxial cables, network cables, and power requirements
  • Connectors and terminations — BNC, RJ45, and power connectors
  • Video compression standards — H.264, H.265, and MJPEG for bandwidth optimization
  • Frame rate and resolution settings for different surveillance scenarios
  • Backup systems and cloud storage integration for recorded footage
  • Troubleshooting common equipment failures and maintenance procedures
  • Comparative analysis of popular brands used in Indian security industry
  • Cost-benefit analysis of analog vs. IP-based CCTV systems
  • Power backup systems — UPS, inverters, and generator requirements

Module 3: Control Room Operations & Monitoring Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Control room setup — monitor arrangement, lighting, ergonomics, and workspace design
  • Multi-camera monitoring techniques — split-screen viewing, sequence switching, and PTZ control
  • Live monitoring best practices — attention span management and fatigue prevention
  • Incident detection and response protocols — identifying suspicious activities and anomalies
  • Recognizing criminal behavior patterns — theft, trespassing, loitering, and unauthorized access
  • Alert systems and alarm management — responding to sensor triggers and false alarms
  • Communication procedures — radio protocols, intercom usage, and emergency reporting
  • Shift management and duty rotation — handover procedures and logbook maintenance
  • CCTV monitoring during different times — day, night, and low-light conditions
  • Handling high-stress situations — crowd management, emergencies, and crisis response
  • Coordination with on-site security personnel and law enforcement agencies

Module 4: Video Recording, Storage & Footage Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Recording modes — continuous, motion-triggered, and event-based recording
  • Storage planning — calculating required disk space based on resolution and retention period
  • Retention policies — legal requirements for different sectors (banks, retail, offices)
  • Footage retrieval and playback techniques — searching by date, time, and camera
  • Video export and archiving — creating evidence copies for legal proceedings
  • Chain of custody procedures for recorded evidence — documentation and security
  • Handling data requests from law enforcement and legal authorities
  • Protecting sensitive footage — access control and encryption methods
  • Regular backup schedules and disaster recovery planning
  • Storage device maintenance and replacement procedures
  • Compliance with data protection regulations and privacy acts
  • Creating incident reports with timestamp and video evidence references

Module 5: Incident Response, Reporting & Legal Compliance

📚 Topics Covered

  • Types of security incidents — theft, intrusion, vandalism, and suspicious behavior
  • Incident detection and initial response procedures
  • Documentation standards — incident reports, witness statements, and photographic evidence
  • Communication with security supervisors and incident escalation procedures
  • Coordination with police and emergency services — FIR filing and evidence submission
  • Legal requirements under Indian Penal Code relevant to CCTV operations
  • Privacy laws and restrictions on recording — GDPR awareness and Indian data protection
  • Workplace harassment and discrimination prevention in control rooms
  • Cybersecurity basics — protecting CCTV systems from hacking and unauthorized access
  • CCTV regulations in different sectors — banking, retail, hospitality, and industrial
  • Documentation of system maintenance and regular audits
  • Handling false alarms and nuisance calls professionally

Module 6: Practical Skills & Professional Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Hands-on operation of DVR/NVR systems — menu navigation and settings adjustment
  • Live monitoring simulation exercises — detecting incidents in recorded footage
  • PTZ (Pan-Tilt-Zoom) camera operation — manual and preset positioning
  • Incident reporting practical exercises — writing professional incident reports
  • Mock emergency response scenarios — communication and coordination drills
  • Footage retrieval and evidence preparation — practical exercises
  • System troubleshooting — identifying and resolving common operational issues
  • Professional communication skills — radio protocol and formal reporting language
  • Time management in control room — managing multiple tasks and alerts simultaneously
  • Stress management and mental health awareness for security professionals
  • Continuous learning and skill upgrade pathways in CCTV technology
  • Preparing for CETCF certification exam — revision and practice tests

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (18+ preferred for direct employment)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior CCTV experience needed — foundation level course
  • Already working as security personnel? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Driving License, or Passport)
  • Medical fitness for shift work and extended monitoring duties
  • Ability to communicate in English or Hindi (or local language where applicable)
  • Sound physical and mental health — no serious visual or hearing impairments
  • Applicable to all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals
  • No criminal background check issues (security clearance may be required for certain employers)

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Operate DVR and NVR systems independently with proficiency in all standard functions and settings
  • Monitor multiple CCTV camera feeds simultaneously and identify suspicious activities and security threats
  • Respond to security incidents following established protocols and communicate effectively with supervisors and emergency services
  • Retrieve, archive, and manage recorded footage while maintaining chain of custody and legal compliance
  • Write professional incident reports with accurate documentation and evidence references for legal proceedings
  • Maintain control room operations professionally while managing stress and following health and safety standards
