Solar Panel Installation Technician

📂 Sector: Electrical & Electronics Trades 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the skills to install, maintain, and troubleshoot solar photovoltaic systems. This foundation course trains you to become a certified Solar Panel Installation Technician, meeting India's growing renewable energy demands. Earn ₹15,000-35,000 monthly with opportunities across residential, commercial, and industrial solar projects.

Module 1: Solar Energy Fundamentals & System Basics

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to renewable energy and India's solar mission — Overview of MNRE (Ministry of New and Renewable Energy) targets and government schemes like PM-Kusum and SURYA schemes
  • Solar radiation and photovoltaic effect — How sunlight converts to electricity, solar irradiance measurement in India (W/m²), seasonal variations across regions
  • Types of solar PV systems — Grid-connected, off-grid, and hybrid systems; suitable applications for Indian climate zones
  • Components of a solar PV system — Modules, inverters, charge controllers, batteries, mounting structures, and balance of system (BOS)
  • System sizing and capacity calculation — Determining system size based on daily load, roof space, and geographical location
  • Safety fundamentals for solar technicians — Electrical hazards, fall protection, heat stress, and PPE requirements
  • Indian solar standards and certifications — IS 12990, IEC 61215, IEC 61730 compliance and MNRE certification requirements
  • Career opportunities in India's solar sector — Job roles, employment trends, and salary progression pathways

Module 2: Solar Panel Components & Module Selection

📚 Topics Covered

  • Solar photovoltaic module types — Monocrystalline, polycrystalline, and thin-film technologies; efficiency ratings and durability
  • Module specifications and datasheet reading — Understanding Wattage (W), Voltage (V), Current (A), Temperature Coefficient, and performance ratings
  • Module quality assessment and testing — Visual inspection, IV curve testing, thermal imaging for defect detection
  • Mounting structures and racking systems — Fixed tilt, adjustable tilt, and tracking systems; selection based on roof type (RCC, metal, concrete)
  • Inverter types and selection — String inverters, micro-inverters, hybrid inverters; MPPT vs PWM technology comparison
  • Charge controllers for off-grid systems — MPPT and PWM controllers; sizing for battery banks in rural Indian installations
  • Battery storage systems — Lithium-ion, lead-acid, and gel batteries; capacity calculation and maintenance requirements
  • Cables, connectors, and electrical components — DC and AC cable sizing, MC4 connectors, breakers, and fuses for Indian climate
  • Balance of system components — Disconnect switches, monitoring equipment, earthing systems, and surge protection devices
  • Supplier selection and procurement — Identifying certified suppliers, warranty terms, and cost-benefit analysis for Indian market

Module 3: Installation Techniques & Mounting Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Site assessment and survey procedures — Roof condition evaluation, shade analysis, structural load capacity, and orientation optimization for Indian latitudes
  • Roof types and structural compatibility — RCC, metal, tile, and concrete roofs; weight distribution and load-bearing calculations
  • Mounting structure installation — Racking assembly, roof penetration sealing, and corrosion prevention in coastal and industrial areas
  • Panel orientation and tilt angle optimization — Calculating optimal angle for different Indian states (latitude-based), seasonal adjustments
  • Mechanical fastening and weatherproofing — Bolt torque specifications, sealant application, and waterproofing for monsoon-prone regions
  • Module arrangement and string configuration — Series and parallel connections, string optimization for inverter compatibility
  • Ground-mounted system installation — Foundation preparation, structural alignment, and anti-theft measures for rural installations
  • Rooftop safety during installation — Scaffolding setup, fall arrest systems, weather precautions, and on-site safety drills
  • Installation timeline and project planning — Scheduling across monsoon and summer seasons in India, material logistics
  • Quality checks during installation — Mechanical integrity verification, alignment accuracy, and documentation procedures
  • Common installation mistakes and corrections — Improper tilt angles, inadequate spacing, water pooling, and shading issues

Module 4: Electrical Wiring, Connections & Safety

📚 Topics Covered

  • DC wiring fundamentals — Voltage drop calculations, cable sizing tables, and Indian electrical code (IE Rules 2016) compliance
  • AC wiring and grid connection — Three-phase and single-phase connections, net metering setup, and DISCOM (Distribution Company) procedures
  • String configuration and combiner boxes — Parallel string wiring, fuse protection, and current limiting devices for safety
  • Grounding and earthing systems — Equipment grounding, lightning protection, earthing resistance testing (<5Ω requirement), and rod installation
  • Surge protection and lightning arrestors — SPD (Surge Protective Devices) installation, Type 1 and Type 2 protection for Indian lightning zones
  • Electrical safety devices — Isolators, MCBs, RCBOs, AC and DC disconnect switches, and their proper installation locations
  • Cable management and labeling — Color-coded wiring, conduit routing, UV-resistant cable selection, and thermal management in hot climates
  • Inverter commissioning and setup — Grid parameters, frequency synchronization, voltage regulation, and load testing procedures
  • Monitoring system installation — Energy meters, data loggers, WiFi connectivity, and cloud-based monitoring platforms used in India
  • Electrical testing and verification — Megger testing, continuity checks, insulation resistance testing, and performance validation
  • Grid interconnection procedures — DISCOM approval process, net metering registration, and compliance documentation for Indian states
  • Electrical safety protocols and emergency procedures — Circuit isolation, lockout-tagout procedures, and first aid for electrical injuries

Module 5: Maintenance, Troubleshooting & Performance Optimization

📚 Topics Covered

  • Preventive maintenance schedules — Monthly, quarterly, and annual checks; seasonal maintenance for Indian climate conditions
  • Panel cleaning and performance restoration — Dust removal techniques, water quality for cleaning, frequency optimization for Indian regions
  • Inverter maintenance and firmware updates — Battery health checks, cooling system verification, and software updates for improved efficiency
  • Monitoring system interpretation — Reading energy generation data, identifying underperformance, and trend analysis for troubleshooting
  • Common system faults and diagnostics — Low output, grid disconnection, inverter errors, and systematic troubleshooting approach
  • Electrical testing during maintenance — Insulation resistance, ground continuity, and voltage/current measurements using multimeters and clamp meters
  • Performance ratio analysis — Calculating PR (Performance Ratio), identifying losses (soiling, temperature, mismatch), and optimization strategies
  • Shade analysis and mitigation — Identifying new shade sources, partial shading solutions, and system redesign recommendations
  • Battery maintenance and health monitoring — State of charge monitoring, equalization procedures, and battery replacement timelines
  • Documentation and record keeping — Maintenance logs, spare parts inventory, warranty tracking, and customer communication
  • Safety during maintenance work — Electrical hazards during service, roof access safety, and working at heights procedures
  • Customer training and handover — Operating instructions, emergency procedures, and performance expectation management

Module 6: Professional Competency & Industry Compliance

📚 Topics Covered

  • Indian electrical safety standards and regulations — IE Rules 2016, National Building Code (NBC) compliance, and state-specific regulations
  • Environmental health and safety (EHS) practices — Waste management, hazardous material handling, and sustainability in solar installations
  • Professional ethics and customer relations — Communication skills, problem-solving approach, and maintaining professional integrity
  • Business basics for self-employed technicians — Pricing strategies, cost estimation, client acquisition, and financial management in Indian market
  • Insurance and liability awareness — Professional indemnity, worksite insurance, and legal responsibilities for technicians
  • Government schemes and subsidy programs — PM-Kusum, SURYA, state subsidies, and documentation for customer benefit realization
  • Quality management systems — ISO 9001 concepts, defect prevention, and continuous improvement in service delivery
  • Digital tools and software — Design software (PVsyst, HOMER), mobile apps for monitoring, and digital documentation systems
  • Certification and continuous learning — CETCF certification value, advanced training pathways, and industry certifications (NSDC, IGNOU)
  • Interview preparation and job readiness — Resume building, technical interview questions, and placement opportunities in India
  • Entrepreneurship in solar sector — Starting a solar installation business, licensing requirements, GST registration, and scaling strategies
  • Case studies from Indian solar projects — Successful installations, lessons learned, and real-world problem-solving examples

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or 18 years for rooftop work as per OSHA-equivalent Indian labor laws)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior experience needed; beginners welcome
  • Already working as solar technician? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Physical fitness required (ability to work at heights and in outdoor conditions)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand solar energy fundamentals, photovoltaic technology, and India's renewable energy targets
  • Identify and select appropriate solar components (modules, inverters, batteries) based on system requirements
  • Install solar PV systems safely using proper mounting structures and following Indian electrical standards
  • Perform DC and AC electrical wiring with correct cable sizing, grounding, and safety device installation
  • Commission and test solar systems using multimeters, clamp meters, and megger equipment
  • Diagnose system faults, perform preventive maintenance, and optimize performance for Indian climate conditions
  • Ensure compliance with IE Rules 2016, NBC, and state-specific electrical regulations
  • Communicate effectively with customers and document work professionally for warranty and maintenance purposes
