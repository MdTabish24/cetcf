Permanent Makeup (Microblading & PMU)

📂 Sector: Advanced Beauty, Cosmetology & Aesthetics 📊 Level: Advanced ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months 🕐 Hours: 180 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1500
Master the art and science of Permanent Makeup (PMU) and Microblading—one of India's fastest-growing beauty specializations. This advanced certification course trains you to become a skilled PMU artist, capable of creating natural-looking eyebrows, eyeliner, and lip tints using cutting-edge techniques and equipment. Learn from industry experts and build a profitable career in premium beauty services.

Module 1: Foundations of Permanent Makeup & Professional Standards

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to Permanent Makeup (PMU) — history, evolution, and current trends in India — understanding difference between microblading, nano blading, ombre, and powder brows
  • Professional Standards & Regulatory Framework — CETCF guidelines, Indian beauty industry regulations, salon licensing requirements, and insurance for PMU artists
  • Health & Safety Protocols — infection control, sterilization standards, PPE requirements, and compliance with Indian health department guidelines
  • Hygiene & Sanitation — salon setup standards, equipment sterilization using autoclaves, single-use needle protocols, and waste disposal management
  • Client Confidentiality & Consent Forms — importance of written consent, medical history documentation, patch test procedures, and liability protection
  • Professional Ethics & Code of Conduct — client respect, boundaries, handling difficult clients, and maintaining professional image on social media
  • Career Overview in Indian PMU Market — employment opportunities in metro cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore), salary expectations, and entrepreneurial pathways
  • Introduction to PMU Equipment & Tools — overview of microblading pens, digital PMU machines, needles, pigments, and equipment maintenance basics

Module 2: Skin Anatomy, Physiology & Color Theory for PMU Artists

📚 Topics Covered

  • Skin Structure & Layers — epidermis, dermis, hypodermis, and understanding how PMU pigments settle in the dermal layer
  • Skin Types & Conditions — oily, dry, combination, sensitive skin, and how to adapt PMU techniques for each type
  • Common Skin Conditions — acne, rosacea, eczema, psoriasis, melasma, and contraindications for PMU procedures
  • Skin Healing Process — inflammatory phase, proliferative phase, remodeling phase, and why proper aftercare is critical for pigment retention
  • Color Theory Fundamentals — primary, secondary, tertiary colors, color mixing, and how to create natural-looking eyebrow shades for Indian skin tones
  • Pigment Science & Selection — organic vs inorganic pigments, FDA-approved pigments used in India, pigment behavior on different skin types, and color fading patterns
  • Indian Skin Tone Considerations — how to select appropriate shades for warm, cool, and neutral undertones common in Indian clients
  • Aging & Skin Changes — how skin changes with age, impact on PMU longevity, and adjusting techniques for mature clients
  • Allergic Reactions & Sensitivities — identifying potential allergic responses, patch testing protocols, and emergency response procedures
  • Skin Assessment Tools — using magnifying lamps, Wood's lamp, and visual analysis for proper client evaluation before PMU procedures

Module 3: Microblading Techniques & Eyebrow Design

📚 Topics Covered

  • Microblading Fundamentals — definition, mechanism, needle configurations (single needle, double needle, stacked needles), and depth control techniques
  • Microblading Equipment Deep Dive — manual microblading pens, semi-automatic devices, hand tools, needle holders, and proper equipment handling
  • Eyebrow Anatomy & Structure — understanding natural eyebrow growth patterns, arch placement, tail direction, and how to work with existing hair
  • Eyebrow Design Principles — golden ratio, face shape analysis (oval, round, square, heart, oblong), and designing eyebrows that suit Indian facial features
  • Eyebrow Design Consultation — client communication, showing design options, adjusting designs based on client preferences, and documenting designs with photos
  • Shading Techniques — creating depth with microblading strokes, combination techniques (microblading + shading), and achieving natural gradient effects
  • Stroke Techniques — directional strokes, feathering, hair-like strokes, angle variation, and pressure control for consistent results
  • Pigment Application & Saturation — proper pigment loading, saturation levels, multiple passes for color depth, and avoiding over-saturation
  • Combination Brows Technique — hybrid method combining microblading strokes with powder/shading for enhanced durability and definition
  • Corrective Microblading — working with over-plucked brows, asymmetrical brows, sparse brows, and covering previous poor-quality PMU work
  • Practice on Synthetic Skin — extensive hands-on practice on practice skins before moving to live models, developing muscle memory and consistency
  • Live Model Microblading — supervised practice on volunteer clients with proper consent, feedback from trainers, and portfolio building

Module 4: Digital PMU Machines & Advanced Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Digital PMU Machine Overview — how machines work, machine types (rotary, electromagnetic, pneumatic), and advantages over manual methods
  • Machine Settings & Controls — voltage adjustment, speed settings, needle depth control, and optimization for different PMU procedures
  • Needle Cartridges & Configurations — understanding cartridge types, needle configurations (round, flat, bugpin), and selecting appropriate needles for each technique
  • Eyeliner PMU Techniques — upper eyeliner application, lower eyeliner, winged eyeliner, and creating definition without harsh appearance
  • Lip PMU Techniques — full lip color, lip blush, ombre lips, lip lining, and color selection for Indian lip tones
  • Eyebrow PMU with Machines — powder brows, ombre brows, gradient brows, and achieving softer appearance compared to microblading
  • Machine Maintenance & Troubleshooting — daily cleaning, sterilization protocols, identifying equipment problems, and preventive maintenance
  • Safety Features in Digital Machines — needle depth limiters, automatic shut-off, and understanding machine specifications to prevent complications
  • Transitioning from Manual to Machine — developing proficiency with digital equipment, understanding when to use manual vs digital techniques, and hybrid approaches

Module 5: Client Consultation, Pain Management & Aftercare Protocols

📚 Topics Covered

  • Comprehensive Client Consultation — detailed health history intake, medication review, allergies, previous PMU experience, and realistic expectation setting
  • Contraindications & Medical Screening — pregnancy, breastfeeding, certain medications (blood thinners, immunosuppressants), diabetes, and when to refer clients to doctors
  • Patch Testing Procedures — importance of patch tests, 24-48 hour waiting period, identifying allergic reactions, and documentation requirements
  • Pain Management Techniques — topical anesthetics (numbing creams), oral pain relief options, breathing techniques, and communicating with anxious clients
  • Anesthetic Products Used in India — lidocaine-based creams, prilocaine products, brand names available in Indian market, and proper application timing
  • Managing Client Discomfort — positioning for comfort, distraction techniques, taking breaks during long sessions, and empathetic communication
  • Immediate Aftercare Instructions — first 24 hours care, avoiding water/sweat, not touching the treated area, and managing redness/swelling
  • Long-term Aftercare Protocol — weeks 1-4 care instructions, moisturizing recommendations, sun protection, and avoiding makeup over fresh PMU
  • Touch-up Appointments — scheduling second appointment (4-6 weeks post-procedure), explaining pigment settling and color adjustments
  • Troubleshooting Aftercare Issues — excessive scabbing, poor color retention, infections, and when to recommend client see a dermatologist
  • Aftercare Documentation — providing written aftercare guides in Hindi/English, creating reminder systems, and maintaining client communication
  • Managing Client Expectations Post-Procedure — explaining healing timeline, color changes during healing, and preventing dissatisfaction through clear communication

Module 6: Business Development, Client Management & Professional Growth

📚 Topics Covered

  • Starting Your PMU Business — registering as self-employed, GST registration requirements, salon setup costs, and initial investment breakdown for Indian market
  • Salon Setup & Equipment Investment — cost of microblading pens, PMU machines, sterilization equipment, furniture, and location selection in Indian cities
  • Pricing Strategy in Indian Market — understanding local market rates (₹3,000-15,000 per procedure depending on city and expertise), value-based pricing, and package offerings
  • Client Acquisition & Marketing — Instagram/Facebook strategies for beauty services, before-after portfolio building, word-of-mouth referrals, and local partnerships
  • Building Your Portfolio — documenting client work with consent, creating case studies, handling portfolio for different client demographics, and ethical photography practices
  • Client Management Systems — appointment booking systems (WhatsApp, Google Calendar, dedicated apps), payment collection, and maintaining client database
  • Handling Client Reviews & Reputation — responding to reviews on Google/Instagram, managing expectations to ensure positive feedback, and handling negative reviews professionally
  • Advanced Skills for Career Growth — specializing in corrective work, celebrity/bridal clients, training other artists, and scaling to multiple locations
  • Continuing Education & Certification — staying updated with new techniques, international certifications (optional), attending workshops, and professional development
  • Financial Management — tracking income/expenses, maintaining records for tax purposes, calculating profit margins, and managing seasonal fluctuations in business
  • Networking & Professional Community — joining beauty associations, attending industry events, collaborating with other beauty professionals, and building referral networks
  • Insurance & Legal Protection — professional liability insurance, understanding client rights, maintaining proper documentation, and protecting your business legally

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 18 years (must be adult to work with clients)
  • Minimum education: Class 10th pass (basic literacy required for understanding safety protocols and client documentation)
  • No prior PMU experience required (beginners welcome)
  • Valid government ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, or Voter ID)
  • Medical fitness certificate (no serious skin conditions, good hand-eye coordination)
  • Already working in beauty industry? Apply for Direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam to get certified faster
  • Applicable for Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals with valid visa
  • Commitment to professional standards and ethical practices mandatory

💼 Career Paths

Independent PMU Artist / Microblading Specialist
Run your own PMU studio in metro cities like Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore. Charge ₹5,000-15,000 per procedure. High earning potential with repeat clients and referrals.
PMU Artist in Premium Salons & Spas
Work in high-end salons, beauty chains, and luxury spas in major Indian cities. Steady income with salon benefits and access to premium clients.
Bridal & Celebrity PMU Specialist
Specialize in bridal makeup, wedding events, and celebrity clients. Command premium rates during wedding season (October-March). Requires excellent portfolio and reputation.
PMU Trainer & Academy Owner
After 2-3 years experience, open your own PMU training academy. Train new artists, conduct workshops, and earn through course fees. Highly scalable business.
PMU Artist in Corporate Beauty Services
Work with corporate beauty chains, dermatology clinics, or aesthetic centers offering PMU services. Stable employment with professional environment.
Mobile PMU Service Provider
Provide on-demand PMU services at clients' homes or events. Requires portable equipment and excellent scheduling. Growing trend in metro cities.
PMU Product Distributor / Pigment Supplier
Distribute PMU equipment, pigments, and supplies to salons and artists. Requires business registration and supplier connections. Recurring revenue model.
Content Creator / Beauty Influencer (PMU Niche)
Build Instagram/YouTube presence teaching PMU techniques. Monetize through sponsored content, online courses, and affiliate marketing for beauty products.
