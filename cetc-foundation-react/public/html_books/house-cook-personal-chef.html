House Cook / Personal Chef

📂 Sector: Child Care & Domestic Services 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Learn professional cooking skills for household kitchens and become a skilled House Cook or Personal Chef. This foundation course covers Indian and international cuisines, food safety, nutrition, and kitchen management—preparing you for immediate employment in urban households, corporate residences, and hospitality settings across India.

Module 1: Kitchen Fundamentals & Food Safety

📚 Topics Covered

  • Kitchen layout and equipment — Understanding professional kitchen design, equipment types (gas stoves, microwaves, pressure cookers, mixers), and their proper use in Indian households
  • Food safety and hygiene standards — FSSAI guidelines, personal hygiene, cross-contamination prevention, and safe food storage practices
  • Knife skills and cutting techniques — Proper knife handling, chopping, slicing, dicing, and mincing vegetables and meat safely
  • Ingredient selection and quality check — Identifying fresh vegetables, fruits, grains, spices, and proteins; checking for quality and shelf life
  • Food storage and preservation — Proper storage of dry goods, refrigeration, freezing, and preservation techniques for Indian ingredients
  • Kitchen safety protocols — Fire safety, burn prevention, electrical safety, and first aid in the kitchen
  • Cleaning and sanitation procedures — Daily kitchen cleaning routines, sanitizing surfaces, and maintaining hygiene standards
  • Kitchen waste management — Segregating wet and dry waste, composting, and following environmental practices

Module 2: Indian Cuisine Basics & Cooking Methods

📚 Topics Covered

  • Indian spice knowledge — Understanding 40+ common Indian spices (turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala), their properties, health benefits, and proper storage
  • Tempering and tadka techniques — Mastering the art of tempering spices in hot oil/ghee to release flavors for curries and dal preparations
  • Cooking methods in Indian cuisine — Boiling, steaming, frying, deep frying, shallow frying, pressure cooking, slow cooking, and tandoori methods
  • Dal and legume preparation — Cooking various dals (moong, masoor, chana, rajma) with different spice combinations and regional variations
  • Rice and grain cooking — Preparing basmati rice, jasmine rice, brown rice, millets, and understanding proper water ratios and texture
  • Vegetable curries and preparations — Making regional vegetable dishes like aloo gobi, baingan bharta, mixed vegetable curry, and seasonal vegetables
  • Non-vegetarian basics — Cooking chicken, mutton, fish, and egg dishes using Indian methods and spice blends
  • Bread and roti making — Preparing chapati, paratha, puri, naan, and regional breads by hand and using modern equipment
  • Gravy and sauce preparation — Making tomato-based gravies, cream-based sauces, coconut curries, and regional gravies
  • Pressure cooker and modern appliance use — Efficient cooking using pressure cookers, microwave, and other kitchen gadgets common in Indian homes

Module 3: Meal Planning, Nutrition & Dietary Requirements

📚 Topics Covered

  • Balanced meal planning — Creating nutritionally complete meals with proteins, carbohydrates, fats, vitamins, and minerals for daily consumption
  • Nutritional requirements for different age groups — Understanding dietary needs for infants, children, teenagers, adults, and elderly persons
  • Special dietary requirements — Preparing meals for diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, obesity, and common health conditions
  • Vegetarian and vegan meal planning — Ensuring complete nutrition without animal products using pulses, nuts, seeds, and vegetables
  • Allergen management — Identifying common food allergens (nuts, dairy, gluten, shellfish) and preparing safe meals for allergic family members
  • Meal preparation for children — Age-appropriate nutrition, finger foods, soft foods, and introducing new ingredients safely
  • Weight management meals — Preparing healthy, low-calorie, high-nutrition meals for weight loss or maintenance
  • Festival and celebration meals — Planning special occasion meals, festive cooking, and traditional celebration dishes
  • Weekly meal planning and grocery budgeting — Creating 7-day meal plans, calculating costs, and shopping efficiently
  • Food combinations and food pairing — Understanding which foods work well together, seasonal eating, and traditional food combinations

Module 4: Breakfast, Snacks & Light Meals

📚 Topics Covered

  • Traditional Indian breakfast dishes — Idli, dosa, upma, poha, paratha, aloo puri, and regional breakfast specialties
  • Egg-based breakfast items — Scrambled eggs, omelettes, boiled eggs, egg curry, and egg fried rice variations
  • Cereal and porridge preparations — Making oatmeal, semolina porridge, rice porridge, and fortified breakfast cereals for children
  • Bread and toast variations — Buttered toast, French toast, sandwiches, open-faced toasts with various toppings
  • Smoothies and beverages — Fruit smoothies, lassi, milkshakes, fresh juices, and healthy drink preparations
  • Savory snacks — Samosa, pakora, cutlets, fritters, spring rolls, and quick snack preparations
  • Sweet snacks and desserts — Laddu, barfi, kheer, halwa, and simple dessert preparations
  • Quick lunch options — Sandwich fillings, wraps, quick curries, leftover meal preparations, and time-saving recipes
  • Tiffin and lunch box packing — Preparing meals that stay fresh, proper food storage for office and school tiffins
  • Tea-time snacks — Biscuits, cookies, cakes, pastries, and accompaniments for afternoon tea

Module 5: Main Course Cooking & Restaurant-Style Dishes

📚 Topics Covered

  • North Indian curries — Butter chicken, paneer tikka masala, rogan josh, nihari, and Mughlai preparations
  • South Indian specialties — Sambar, rasam, coconut-based curries, fish curries, and regional South Indian dishes
  • East and West Indian cuisine — Bengali fish curry, Goan coconut dishes, Maharashtrian gravies, and regional specialties
  • Tandoori and grilled preparations — Tandoori chicken, paneer tikka, kebabs, grilled fish, and marinated preparations
  • Biryani and pilaf preparations — Making fragrant biryani, pulao, fried rice, and one-pot meals
  • Seafood and fish cooking — Fish curry, prawn preparations, crab masala, and seafood handling and storage
  • Meat and poultry specialties — Chicken curry, mutton preparations, keema dishes, and meat-based gravies
  • International dishes for Indian homes — Pasta, pizza base, Chinese fried rice, hakka noodles, and Indo-Chinese fusion dishes
  • Vegetarian main courses — Paneer dishes, tofu preparations, vegetable biryani, and protein-rich vegetarian curries
  • Slow-cooked and pressure-cooked meals — Overnight curries, slow cooker preparations, and efficient cooking for busy households
  • Gravy consistency and flavor balancing — Achieving perfect gravy thickness, salt balance, spice adjustment, and final seasoning

Module 6: Professional Skills, Serving & Career Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Professional etiquette and behavior — Dressing appropriately, punctuality, respectful communication, and maintaining professional boundaries with employers
  • Table setting and serving etiquette — Proper plate presentation, serving styles (left side serving), clearing plates, and table manners
  • Guest management and entertaining — Cooking for guests, preparing multiple dishes simultaneously, managing time, and maintaining kitchen composure
  • Recipe documentation and menu planning — Writing recipes, maintaining recipe books, creating weekly menus, and taking feedback
  • Cost management and budgeting — Calculating per-meal costs, reducing food waste, bulk buying, and financial planning for kitchen operations
  • Communication with employers — Taking dietary preferences, understanding family health conditions, dietary restrictions, and special requests
  • Kitchen management for households — Organizing kitchen space, inventory management, ordering supplies, and maintaining equipment
  • Cooking for special occasions — Wedding menus, birthday celebrations, festival meals, and large gathering preparations
  • Health and wellness awareness — Understanding food allergies, food poisoning prevention, nutritional deficiencies, and health-conscious cooking
  • Career advancement opportunities — Training for higher positions, specialization in cuisines, entrepreneurship options, and continuous skill development
  • Documentation and record-keeping — Maintaining meal records, dietary tracking for health conditions, shopping lists, and expense tracking
  • Personal branding and client management — Building reputation, handling feedback, managing multiple clients as freelance chef, and maintaining confidentiality

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior professional cooking experience required
  • Already working as a cook? You can apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhar, PAN, Voter ID, or Driving License)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals
  • Physical fitness required for standing and working in kitchen for extended hours
  • No criminal record required

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Prepare safe, hygienic, and nutritionally balanced meals following FSSAI food safety standards
  • Master Indian cooking techniques including tempering, pressure cooking, and traditional methods for various cuisines
  • Plan weekly menus and manage kitchen budgets while meeting dietary requirements of different age groups and health conditions
  • Demonstrate professional knife skills, cooking methods, and kitchen equipment operation for efficient meal preparation
  • Create restaurant-quality dishes including curries, breads, rice preparations, and international dishes commonly prepared in Indian households
  • Maintain professional behavior, communication with employers, and provide courteous service while managing multiple cooking responsibilities
