Sanitation Worker Certification

📂 Sector: Plumbing, Sanitation & Water 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master essential sanitation skills to build a stable career in India's growing water and waste management sector. This foundation course trains you in safe cleaning practices, waste handling, and basic plumbing maintenance—skills in high demand across municipalities, hospitals, schools, and residential complexes.

Module 1: Health, Hygiene & Occupational Safety

📚 Topics Covered

  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) — Understanding gloves, masks, aprons, boots, and when to use them
  • Bloodborne Pathogens & Disease Prevention — Identifying hazards like hepatitis A/B, typhoid, and cholera transmission
  • Hand Hygiene & Sanitation Standards — Proper handwashing techniques and maintaining personal cleanliness
  • Chemical Safety & Handling — Safe use of disinfectants, bleach, acid cleaners, and disposal methods
  • Occupational Health Hazards — Recognizing risks like toxic gas exposure, needle-stick injuries, and back strain
  • First Aid Basics — Treating minor cuts, chemical burns, and responding to emergencies on-site
  • Legal Rights & Workplace Safety Regulations — Understanding labor laws and worker protection under Indian law
  • Mental Health & Stress Management — Coping with stigma and maintaining psychological well-being in the profession

Module 2: Waste Management & Segregation

📚 Topics Covered

  • Waste Classification System — Segregating wet waste, dry waste, hazardous waste, and e-waste as per Solid Waste Management Rules 2016
  • Biomedical Waste Handling — Proper disposal of medical waste in hospitals, clinics, and diagnostic centers
  • Segregation at Source — Training in color-coded bins (green, blue, red, yellow) and collection procedures
  • Storage & Transportation — Safe storage duration, container requirements, and transportation protocols
  • Waste Segregation Tools & Equipment — Using tongs, shovels, containers, and weighing scales efficiently
  • Composting & Recycling Basics — Understanding aerobic decomposition and recyclable materials sorting
  • Hazardous Waste Documentation — Maintaining records and understanding manifests for hazardous waste
  • Sanitary Landfill Operations — Overview of final disposal sites and environmental impact awareness
  • Waste Management in Different Settings — Residential societies, hospitals, schools, markets, and industrial areas
  • Swachh Bharat Mission Standards — Alignment with national cleanliness standards and reporting procedures

Module 3: Basic Plumbing & Drain Maintenance

📚 Topics Covered

  • Plumbing System Basics — Understanding water supply lines, drainage systems, and pipe materials (PVC, GI, copper)
  • Common Plumbing Tools — Proper use of wrenches, pipe cutters, plungers, drain snakes, and measuring tapes
  • Drain Cleaning Techniques — Manual and mechanical methods to clear blockages in pipes and sewers
  • Pipe Identification & Sizing — Recognizing different pipe diameters and materials used in Indian homes and buildings
  • Leak Detection & Repair — Identifying water leaks, understanding causes, and temporary repair solutions
  • Septic Tank Maintenance — Cleaning, desludging, and maintaining septic systems in non-sewered areas
  • Toilet & Urinal Cleaning — Proper disinfection and maintenance of sanitary fixtures
  • Water Trap & U-Bend Cleaning — Clearing blocked traps and understanding their importance in preventing odor
  • Basic Pipe Joining Techniques — Introduction to threading, soldering basics, and using PVC adhesives
  • Sewage Line Inspection — Understanding camera inspection and identifying damage in underground pipes
  • Water Harvesting Awareness — Basic knowledge of rainwater harvesting systems and maintenance

Module 4: Cleaning & Disinfection Procedures

📚 Topics Covered

  • Cleaning Chemicals & Their Uses — Understanding detergents, disinfectants, degreasers, and acid cleaners
  • Dilution & Concentration — Calculating proper ratios for effective yet safe chemical use
  • Wet Cleaning Methods — Floor washing, surface wiping, and pressure washing techniques
  • Dry Cleaning Methods — Sweeping, dusting, and vacuum cleaning in different environments
  • Disinfection Protocols — Step-by-step procedures for sanitizing bathrooms, kitchens, and common areas
  • Odor Control & Management — Using deodorants, ventilation, and identifying sources of foul smells
  • Grease Trap Cleaning — Maintenance of grease traps in restaurants and commercial kitchens
  • Floor & Surface Restoration — Removing stains, stubborn dirt, and restoring cleanliness
  • Equipment Cleaning & Maintenance — Proper care of brooms, mops, brushes, and cleaning machines
  • Eco-Friendly Cleaning Alternatives — Using vinegar, baking soda, and natural disinfectants

Module 5: Water Supply & Quality Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Water Supply Systems in India — Understanding municipal water distribution, tanker supply, and bore wells
  • Water Storage Tanks — Types, cleaning procedures, and maintenance schedules
  • Water Quality Testing Basics — Understanding pH, turbidity, and microbial contamination indicators
  • Chlorination & Purification Methods — Basic knowledge of water treatment and disinfection
  • Overhead & Underground Tank Maintenance — Cleaning, inspection, and leak prevention
  • Water Meter Reading & Leakage Detection — Identifying water waste and reporting to authorities
  • Seasonal Water Management — Handling water scarcity and excess during monsoons
  • Water Conservation Practices — Fixing leaks, preventing wastage, and promoting awareness
  • Tanker-Based Water Supply Operations — Procedures for water tanker management and delivery

Module 6: Professional Skills & Career Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Communication & Customer Service — Interacting professionally with residents, supervisors, and colleagues
  • Record Keeping & Documentation — Maintaining daily logs, incident reports, and maintenance records
  • Problem-Solving & Troubleshooting — Identifying issues and implementing practical solutions
  • Time Management & Work Scheduling — Prioritizing tasks and managing multiple assignments
  • Team Work & Coordination — Collaborating with other sanitation workers and departments
  • Entrepreneurship Opportunities — Starting small sanitation services, contract cleaning, or maintenance business
  • Government Schemes & Benefits — Understanding ESIC, PF, and social security benefits for sanitation workers
  • Continuing Education & Skill Upgradation — Pathways to advanced certifications in plumbing and water management

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (16 years preferred for industrial sites)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass
  • No prior experience required
  • Already working as sanitation worker? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government ID proof (Aadhaar, PAN, or voter card) required at registration
  • Medical fitness certificate recommended for heavy-duty sanitation work
  • Applicable for all candidates irrespective of gender, caste, or religion
  • Migrant workers welcome with valid identification

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand and apply occupational safety standards, PPE usage, and health hazard prevention in sanitation work
  • Segregate waste correctly according to Indian Solid Waste Management Rules 2016 and handle biomedical waste safely
  • Perform basic plumbing maintenance including drain cleaning, leak detection, and septic tank care
  • Execute professional cleaning and disinfection procedures using appropriate chemicals and equipment
  • Maintain water supply systems, detect contamination, and practice water conservation
  • Develop professional communication skills, maintain documentation, and identify entrepreneurship opportunities in sanitation sector
