Fire Extinguisher & Evacuation Basics

📂 Sector: Security & Facility Management 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master fire safety fundamentals and emergency evacuation procedures essential for protecting lives and property in Indian workplaces. This hands-on certification equips you with practical skills to handle fire emergencies, operate extinguishers, and lead safe evacuations in any facility.

Module 1: Fire Science Fundamentals & Fire Triangle Concept

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to fire science — What is fire and how it starts in Indian buildings
  • The Fire Triangle — Heat, fuel, and oxygen explained with real examples
  • Combustion process — How different materials catch fire at different temperatures
  • Classes of fire (A, B, C, D, F) — Indian context examples from homes, offices, factories
  • Fire behavior in confined spaces — How fire spreads in Indian apartment complexes and buildings
  • Common fire hazards in Indian workplaces — Electrical, gas, cooking, chemical fires
  • Fire temperature zones — Understanding flashpoint and ignition temperature
  • Role of ventilation and air pressure in fire spread — Building design impact
  • Fire load calculation — What causes fires to burn faster or slower
  • Prevention vs. suppression — Why prevention is better than fighting fire
  • Indian fire safety standards and codes — NBC, NFPA, local municipal rules
  • Case studies of major fires in India — Learning from real incidents

Module 2: Fire Extinguishers: Types, Selection & Operation

📚 Topics Covered

  • Water extinguishers (Type A) — Best practices and when to use in Indian buildings
  • Foam extinguishers — Effective for petroleum and liquid fires at petrol pumps
  • Dry powder extinguishers (ABC type) — Most common in Indian offices and factories
  • Carbon dioxide (CO2) extinguishers — Safe for electrical and sensitive equipment
  • Wet chemical extinguishers (Type F) — For commercial kitchens and cooking oil fires
  • Halogenated extinguishers — Ozone-safe alternatives available in India
  • Powder classifications — Monoammonium phosphate, sodium bicarbonate, potassium chloride
  • Extinguisher pressure gauges — Reading and maintaining correct pressure
  • PASS technique — Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep method demonstration
  • Selecting correct extinguisher for fire class — Decision-making in emergency
  • Maintenance and inspection schedules — Annual certification requirements in India
  • Safety distance and wind direction — Practical handling considerations

Module 3: Hands-On Fire Extinguisher Practice & Safety Protocols

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pre-operation safety checks — Inspecting extinguisher before use
  • Correct grip and stance — Ergonomics for safe operation
  • Aiming techniques — Directing spray at base of fire effectively
  • Sweep patterns — Left-right motion for complete coverage
  • Distance management — Staying safe while fighting small fires
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE) — Gloves, eye protection, respiratory safety
  • When NOT to use extinguisher — Knowing when to evacuate instead
  • Extinguisher limitations — Maximum fire size that can be tackled
  • Team coordination during fire fighting — Communication and safety
  • Post-fire cleanup and disposal — Handling chemical residue safely
  • Mock fire drills with actual extinguishers — Practical hands-on training
  • Common mistakes and how to avoid them — Learning from errors

Module 4: Evacuation Planning & Emergency Procedures

📚 Topics Covered

  • Evacuation route planning — Creating safe exit pathways in Indian buildings
  • Emergency assembly points — Designating safe zones away from building
  • Exit signage and markings — Indian standards for emergency signs
  • Fire alarm systems — Types and how to activate in Indian offices
  • Communication during evacuation — Using public address systems and sirens
  • Evacuation for people with disabilities — Accessibility and assistance protocols
  • Stairwell evacuation procedures — Never using elevators during fire
  • Smoke management and safe movement — Crawling low under smoke
  • Buddy system implementation — Pairing people for accountability
  • Headcount procedures — Ensuring nobody is left behind
  • Evacuation time calculations — How long should evacuation take
  • Re-entry procedures — When it's safe to return to building

Module 5: Emergency Response & First Aid for Fire Injuries

📚 Topics Covered

  • Emergency call procedures — Contacting fire brigade (101) in India
  • Providing location information — Clear directions for fire trucks
  • Incident reporting protocols — Documenting what happened
  • Burn injury classification — First, second, third degree burns
  • Immediate burn care — Cool water treatment and bandaging
  • Smoke inhalation symptoms — Recognizing respiratory distress
  • Recovery position for unconscious persons — CPR basics
  • Shock management — Keeping injured person warm and calm
  • Transporting injured persons — Safe movement without worsening injury
  • Hospital coordination — Ensuring injured reach medical care quickly
  • Psychological first aid — Supporting traumatized persons after fire
  • Documentation for insurance — Recording incident details for claims
