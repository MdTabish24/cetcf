Fashion Design — Foundation Certificate

Master the fundamentals of fashion design, pattern making, and garment construction in just 12 weeks. This foundation certificate equips you with essential skills in sketching, draping, tailoring, and fabric knowledge to launch a career in India's thriving apparel industry worth ₹4+ lakh crores annually.

Module 1: Fashion Design Fundamentals & Design History

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to Fashion Design Industry in India — Overview of Indian fashion market, organized vs unorganized sector, major fashion hubs (Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Jaipur), employment opportunities
  • Design Elements & Principles — Line, shape, color, texture, balance, proportion, emphasis, harmony, contrast, movement in garment design
  • Fashion History & Trends — Evolution of Indian fashion, traditional vs modern wear, regional styles (Saree draping, Kurta styles, Lehenga designs), contemporary global trends
  • Color Theory for Fashion — Color psychology, color combinations, seasonal palettes, skin tone matching, cultural significance of colors in Indian fashion
  • Design Inspiration & Mood Boards — Creating mood boards using fabric swatches, color samples, reference images, developing design concepts
  • Fashion Illustration Basics — Human figure proportions (9-head canon), stance variations, pose drawing, quick sketching techniques
  • Understanding Fashion Markets — Fast fashion vs premium wear, target audience analysis, occasion-wear vs casual-wear design differences, Indian festive wear market
  • Design Software Introduction — Basic knowledge of Adobe Illustrator/CorelDRAW, digital design tools used in Indian fashion studios, CAD basics
  • Sustainability in Fashion — Eco-friendly practices, ethical manufacturing, waste reduction, Indian handloom and sustainable fashion initiatives
  • Professional Portfolio Development — Building a design portfolio, presentation standards, digital and physical portfolio formats

Module 2: Technical Pattern Making & Grading

📚 Topics Covered

  • Body Measurements & Sizing Standards — Indian sizing standards (XS to XXL), measurement techniques, bust, waist, hip, length measurements, creating measurement charts
  • Pattern Making Fundamentals — Block pattern development, basic straight-cut patterns, understanding grain lines, seam allowances, notches and markings
  • Drafting Basic Garments — Simple shirt block, trouser block, basic skirt block, sleeve patterns, collar and cuff patterns using Indian measurement standards
  • Dart Manipulation & Fitting — Understanding dart functions, dart placement, dart manipulation techniques, fitting adjustments, creating fitted patterns
  • Advanced Pattern Modifications — Yoke designs, panel lines, princess seams, waist shaping, creating ease for comfort, design variations from basic blocks
  • Pattern Grading for Multiple Sizes — Grading rules, proportional grading, grading tables, creating size 32 to 42 from master size, Indian clothing size increments
  • Specialized Pattern Making — Saree blouse patterns, Kurti patterns, Salwar patterns, Lehenga patterns, traditional Indian wear adaptations
  • Collar & Cuff Construction — Flat collar, shirt collar, mandarin collar, peter pan collar, cuff variations, facing techniques
  • Sleeve Variations & Armhole Design — Set-in sleeve, cap sleeve, flutter sleeve, bell sleeve, kimono sleeve, raglan sleeve, understanding armhole depth
  • Pattern Grading Using CAD — Introduction to pattern grading software, digital marker making, nesting patterns for fabric efficiency, reducing fabric wastage
  • Quality Control in Pattern Making — Pattern checking, tolerance standards, consistency across sizes, documentation and archiving patterns
  • Practical Pattern Development — Creating 3-4 complete patterns from scratch, grading patterns to multiple sizes, preparing patterns for production

Module 3: Fabric Science & Material Selection

📚 Topics Covered

  • Fiber Classification — Natural fibers (cotton, silk, wool, linen), synthetic fibers (polyester, nylon, acrylic), semi-synthetic fibers, Indian fiber production
  • Yarn Types & Properties — Yarn construction, single vs ply yarns, yarn count, twist, durability, Indian textile mills and yarn suppliers
  • Fabric Weaves & Knits — Plain weave, twill, satin, jersey knit, rib knit, fleece, understanding fabric behavior and drape
  • Fabric Finishes & Treatments — Mercerization, pre-shrinking, dyeing processes, water-resistant finishes, crease-resistant treatments, Indian fabric finishing standards
  • Fabric Identification & Testing — Visual and tactile identification, burn test, strength testing, shrinkage testing, color fastness, fabric quality assessment
  • Seasonal Fabric Selection — Summer fabrics (cotton, linen, lightweight synthetics), winter fabrics (wool, fleece, heavier knits), monsoon considerations in India
  • Specialty & Technical Fabrics — Denim, corduroy, velvet, silk, georgette, crepe, jacquard, embroidered fabrics, stretch fabrics, Indian traditional textiles
  • Fabric Cost Analysis & Sourcing — Understanding fabric pricing, GST impact, fabric sourcing from Indian mills, wholesale vs retail costs, negotiation skills
  • Sustainability in Textiles — Organic cotton, eco-friendly dyes, fair trade certifications, Indian organic textile initiatives, reducing environmental impact
  • Fabric Draping & Behavior — How different fabrics drape, stretch properties, recovery, weight impact on design, matching fabric to design intent

📚 Topics Covered

  • Sewing Machine Operation & Maintenance — Types of sewing machines (domestic, industrial, overlock), threading, tension adjustment, needle selection, basic maintenance
  • Hand Stitching Techniques — Running stitch, backstitch, slip stitch, whip stitch, cross stitch, embroidery basics, Indian hand embroidery traditions
  • Seam Types & Construction — Flat seam, French seam, welt seam, bound seam, safety stitch, selecting appropriate seams for different fabrics
  • Hemming Techniques — Straight hem, curved hem, rolled hem, blind hem, machine hemming, hand hemming, finishing hems for different garment types
  • Collar Construction — Interfacing selection, collar assembly, undercollar stitching, topstitching, collar pressing, collar quality standards
  • Sleeve Setting & Armhole Finishing — Sleeve cap ease, setting sleeves, armhole binding, sleeve hem techniques, adjusting for fit
  • Buttonholes & Button Attachment — Machine buttonholes, bound buttonholes, button placement, shank buttons, reinforcement techniques, Indian traditional closures
  • Zipper Installation — Centered zipper, lapped zipper, invisible zipper, fly front zippers, zipper selection for different garments
  • Pocket Construction — Patch pockets, set-in pockets, welt pockets, functional and decorative pockets, pocket placement and proportions
  • Garment Pressing & Finishing — Pressing techniques for different fabrics, using pressing equipment, final garment finishing, quality checks before delivery
  • Garment Assembly Sequence — Understanding assembly order, efficiency in production, quality control at each stage, reducing rework
  • Special Techniques — Pleating, gathering, smocking, embellishment attachment, embroidery application, beading and sequin work, Indian traditional embellishments

💼 Career Paths

Fashion Designer (Entry-Level)
Work in design studios, fashion houses, or apparel companies creating designs, developing patterns, and overseeing sample production
Pattern Maker / Grader
Specialize in creating accurate patterns, grading to multiple sizes, and ensuring consistency in garment production for manufacturing units
Custom Tailor / Master Tailor
Run your own tailoring business offering custom-made garments, alterations, and bespoke designs for individual clients
Sample Maker / Sample Technician
Create sample garments for design presentations, fashion shows, and client approvals in design studios and fashion houses
Freelance Fashion Designer
Work independently taking design projects from multiple clients, creating custom designs, and managing own business operations
Fashion Entrepreneur (Own Brand)
Launch your own fashion brand creating and selling original designs through retail shops, online platforms, or wholesale channels
Garment QA/QC Executive
Ensure quality standards in garment production, inspect finished products, identify defects, and maintain quality documentation
Fashion Instructor / Trainer
Teach fashion design, tailoring, and pattern making at vocational institutes, fashion schools, or community centers
Ethnic Wear Designer
Specialize in designing traditional Indian wear (sarees, lehengas, kurtis, sherwanis) for individual clients or brands
Online Fashion Store Owner
Create and manage an online fashion business selling custom-designed or mass-produced garments through e-commerce platforms
