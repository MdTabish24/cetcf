Sports Nutrition & Diet Basics

📂 Sector: Sports, Fitness & Recreation 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the fundamentals of sports nutrition and learn how to design personalized diet plans for athletes and fitness enthusiasts. This foundation course equips you with essential knowledge about macronutrients, micronutrients, and practical dietary strategies used in Indian sports and fitness industry.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Nutrition Science

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to nutrition and its role in sports — Understanding macronutrients and micronutrients, basic calorie concepts
  • Carbohydrates in sports nutrition — Types of carbs, glycemic index, timing for athletic performance
  • Proteins for muscle building and recovery — Amino acids, protein requirements, quality protein sources
  • Fats and their importance — Essential fatty acids, role in hormone production, healthy fat sources
  • Vitamins and minerals essentials — Key vitamins, minerals for athletes, deficiency signs in Indian population
  • Water and hydration science — Fluid requirements, electrolytes, dehydration effects on performance
  • Digestive system basics — How body processes food, nutrient absorption, digestive health
  • Energy systems in human body — ATP production, aerobic vs anaerobic metabolism, fuel utilization

Module 2: Nutritional Requirements for Different Sports

📚 Topics Covered

  • Endurance sports nutrition — Marathon, cycling, long-distance running specific requirements
  • Strength and power sports — Weightlifting, powerlifting, bodybuilding nutritional needs
  • Team sports nutrition — Cricket, football, hockey, basketball dietary requirements
  • Combat sports nutrition — Boxing, wrestling, martial arts, weight management strategies
  • Racquet sports nutrition — Badminton, tennis, table tennis energy and hydration needs
  • Calculating individual caloric needs — Using activity level, body composition, training intensity
  • Macronutrient distribution — Carb-protein-fat ratios for different sports, periodization concepts
  • Pre-competition nutrition strategy — Timing meals, avoiding GI issues, mental preparation through diet
  • Post-competition recovery nutrition — Refueling, rehydration, muscle repair nutrition
  • Special populations — Nutrition for junior athletes, female athletes, masters athletes

Module 3: Practical Meal Planning and Diet Design

📚 Topics Covered

  • Dietary assessment methods — 24-hour recall, food frequency questionnaires, Indian food composition
  • Creating balanced meal plans — Using Indian food groups, portion control, seasonal availability
  • Indian staple foods in sports nutrition — Rice, wheat, dal, vegetables, dairy products optimization
  • Meal timing strategies — Pre-workout, intra-workout, post-workout meal timing principles
  • Budget-friendly nutrition planning — Cost-effective protein sources, seasonal vegetables, local foods
  • Vegetarian and vegan athlete nutrition — Plant-based protein combinations, nutrient density optimization
  • Food labels and nutrition facts — Reading labels, understanding claims, identifying hidden sugars
  • Creating sample meal plans — Day plans for different sports, different caloric requirements
  • Hydration planning — Fluid intake schedules, electrolyte solutions, homemade sports drinks
  • Managing dietary restrictions — Allergies, intolerances, cultural preferences in meal planning

Module 4: Sports Supplements and Ergogenic Aids

📚 Topics Covered

  • Supplement categories and classifications — What are supplements, regulatory framework in India
  • Protein supplements — Whey, casein, plant-based proteins, usage timing and dosage
  • Carbohydrate supplements — Sports drinks, energy gels, maltodextrin products
  • Creatine supplementation — Mechanism, dosing, safety, benefits for strength athletes
  • Caffeine and performance — Effects on endurance, dosing guidelines, side effects awareness
  • Branched-chain amino acids (BCAAs) — Benefits, timing, effectiveness research
  • Vitamins and mineral supplements — When needed, deficiency testing, supplementation protocols
  • Natural supplements — Ashwagandha, Shilajit, beetroot juice, turmeric benefits
  • Banned substances in sports — WADA banned list, testing protocols, anti-doping awareness
  • Supplement quality and safety — Checking certifications, avoiding contaminated products, regulatory bodies

Module 5: Body Composition and Weight Management in Sports

📚 Topics Covered

  • Body composition assessment methods — BMI, waist circumference, skinfold measurement, bioelectrical impedance
  • Healthy weight loss for athletes — Safe calorie deficit, maintaining muscle mass, gradual approach
  • Weight gain strategies for muscle building — Caloric surplus, nutrient timing, progressive overload
  • Rapid weight loss risks — Dehydration dangers, performance impact, health consequences
  • Sport-specific weight categories — Boxing, wrestling, weightlifting weight class management
  • Female athlete nutrition — Menstrual cycle effects, iron needs, energy availability
  • Eating disorders in athletes — Recognition, risk factors, referral procedures
  • Nutrition for injury recovery — Anti-inflammatory foods, protein needs, micronutrient support
  • Age-specific considerations — Growing athletes, teenage nutrition needs, aging athlete adaptation
  • Mental health and nutrition — Relationship between diet and mood, stress management through nutrition

Module 6: Professional Practice and Client Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Ethics in sports nutrition counseling — Scope of practice, referral to registered dietitians, confidentiality
  • Client assessment and goal setting — Initial consultations, SMART goals, baseline measurements
  • Communication skills for nutrition professionals — Explaining concepts simply, motivational interviewing techniques
  • Behavior change strategies — Habit formation, overcoming barriers, sustainable lifestyle changes
  • Documentation and record-keeping — Maintaining client records, progress tracking, compliance with regulations
  • Working with different age groups — Pediatric athletes, college athletes, professional athletes, masters
  • Collaboration with coaching staff — Communicating with coaches, aligning nutrition with training plans
  • Nutrition education delivery — Group workshops, one-on-one counseling, using visual aids and demonstrations
  • Indian sports nutrition landscape — Available resources, career opportunities, professional organizations
  • Building a nutrition coaching business — Setting up practice, pricing services, marketing in India
  • Legal compliance and certifications — Insurance, GST registration, maintaining professional standards
  • Current trends in sports nutrition — Evidence-based practices, technology use, personalized nutrition approaches

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required in nutrition field
  • Interest in sports, fitness, or health sector
  • Already working as fitness professional? Apply for direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Voter ID)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and foreign nationals with valid visa
  • Physical ability to assess clients and demonstrate exercises (if applicable)

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand fundamental nutrition science and how nutrients support athletic performance
  • Assess individual nutritional requirements based on sport type, training intensity, and body composition goals
  • Design practical, culturally appropriate meal plans using Indian food sources within budget constraints
  • Apply evidence-based supplement recommendations safely and ethically
  • Manage body composition changes and weight in sports using healthy, sustainable approaches
  • Communicate nutrition concepts effectively and support behavior change in athletes and fitness clients
