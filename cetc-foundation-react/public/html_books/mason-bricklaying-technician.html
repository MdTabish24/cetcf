Mason & Bricklaying Technician

📂 Sector: Construction & Building Trades 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the art and science of bricklaying and masonry work with hands-on training designed for India's booming construction industry. This 2-month foundation course transforms you into a skilled mason capable of building residential, commercial, and industrial structures with precision and safety. Gain industry-recognized certification and start earning ₹10,000-25,000 monthly within weeks of completion.

Module 1: Construction Industry Fundamentals & Safety

📚 Topics Covered

  • Indian construction industry overview — sectors, employment opportunities, growth trends, and market demand for skilled masons
  • Construction site organization — site layout, material storage, equipment placement, and site management practices
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) — hard hats, safety shoes, gloves, masks, harnesses, and correct usage in Indian climate
  • Workplace hazards identification — falls, electrical hazards, chemical exposure, dust inhalation, and heat-related illnesses
  • Safety regulations in India — OSHA guidelines, Building & Other Construction Workers Act, 1996, and state-specific rules
  • Emergency procedures — first aid, accident reporting, evacuation routes, and rescue protocols on construction sites
  • Health and hygiene on site — sanitation facilities, water supply, disease prevention, and worker wellness
  • Tool safety and equipment handling — proper storage, maintenance, inspection, and safe operation of hand and power tools
  • Environmental safety — waste management, pollution control, and eco-friendly construction practices in India
  • Safety signage and communication — understanding warning signs, safety briefings, and incident reporting procedures

Module 2: Materials Science & Selection for Masonry

📚 Topics Covered

  • Brick types and classifications — clay bricks, concrete blocks, fly ash bricks, interlocking bricks, and regional variations in India
  • Brick quality standards — IS 1077 specifications, strength grades, water absorption, durability, and defect identification
  • Cement types and properties — Portland cement, fly ash cement, slag cement, setting time, and applications in Indian construction
  • Sand and aggregates — particle size distribution, fineness modulus, contamination testing, and regional sand availability
  • Water quality for mortar — potable water requirements, testing for salinity and impurities, and impact on mortar strength
  • Mortar composition and ratios — cement:sand ratios (1:3, 1:4, 1:6), additives, and selection based on application
  • Admixtures and additives — plasticizers, water reducers, accelerators, and their effects on mortar properties
  • Lime and lime mortar — traditional lime mortar, lime properties, advantages in heritage structures, and modern applications
  • Material testing and quality control — compression testing, water absorption tests, and on-site quality checks
  • Cost estimation of materials — calculating quantities, market rates, wastage factors, and budget optimization
  • Storage and handling — proper storage of bricks, cement, sand, and protection from weather and contamination
  • Sourcing materials locally — identifying suppliers, quality verification, bulk purchasing, and cost negotiation strategies

Module 3: Tools, Equipment & Site Preparation

📚 Topics Covered

  • Hand tools for masonry — trowels (pointing, laying, gauging), chisels, hammers, levels, plumb bobs, and maintenance
  • Measuring and leveling instruments — tape measures, measuring scales, spirit levels, laser levels, and precision requirements
  • Scaffolding systems — types of scaffolding, safe erection, inspection procedures, and load-bearing capacity calculations
  • Mortar mixing equipment — manual mixing, mechanical mixers, capacity selection, and cleaning procedures
  • Cutting and shaping tools — brick cutting tools, diamond saws, grinders, and safety protocols for power tools
  • Lifting equipment — pulleys, hoists, ropes, slings, and safe rigging practices on Indian construction sites
  • Site layout and marking — understanding building lines, setting out foundations, using theodolite basics, and chalk marking
  • Foundation preparation — excavation depths, soil compaction, leveling, and checking foundation readiness for masonry
  • Damp-proof course (DPC) preparation — materials, placement, importance, and inspection before brick laying
  • Scaffold assembly and safety inspection — step-by-step erection, tie-backs, guard rails, and competent person certification
  • Tool maintenance and storage — cleaning, sharpening, rust prevention, and organized storage on construction sites
  • Equipment rental and logistics — understanding rental costs, transport arrangements, and equipment accountability systems

Module 4: Mortar Preparation & Brick Laying Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Mortar mixing procedures — manual and mechanical methods, water addition, consistency checking, and workability tests
  • Mortar strength and curing — achieving design strength, curing time, temperature effects, and quality assurance
  • Brick soaking and preparation — water absorption, pre-wetting requirements, and timing before laying
  • Brick laying patterns — running bond, Flemish bond, English bond, header bond, and selection based on structural requirements
  • Stretcher and header courses — understanding brick orientation, load distribution, and pattern repetition
  • Mortar bed thickness — standard thicknesses (10-12mm), consistency maintenance, and impact on joint quality
  • Vertical joint filling — proper filling techniques, avoiding voids, and achieving uniform joint appearance
  • Plumbing and alignment — using levels and plumb bobs, checking vertical accuracy, and correcting misalignment
  • Horizontal leveling — ensuring level courses, using string lines, and maintaining consistent heights across walls
  • Brick cutting and fitting — cutting bricks for corners, openings, and irregular spaces using proper techniques
  • Joint finishing — raked joints, flush joints, weather-struck joints, and pointing techniques for aesthetic finish
  • Facing brick laying — special techniques for exposed bricks, maintaining uniform color, and avoiding efflorescence
  • Cavity wall construction — double-wall systems, cavity width, tie placement, and insulation in modern Indian homes

Module 5: Structural Masonry & Advanced Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Wall types and structural systems — load-bearing walls, non-load-bearing walls, cavity walls, and their construction differences
  • Openings in masonry — lintels, arches, door frames, window frames, and proper load distribution above openings
  • Lintel installation — concrete lintels, steel lintels, bearing length requirements, and prevention of cracks
  • Arch construction — segmental arches, flat arches, circular arches, and traditional arch-building techniques
  • Reinforced masonry — placement of steel reinforcement, spacing, cover requirements, and bond beam construction
  • Seismic considerations — earthquake-resistant masonry, lateral tie placement, and construction practices in seismic zones
  • Damp-proof course installation — placement height, material selection, and inspection for water resistance
  • Waterproofing techniques — external coatings, sealants, and preventing water ingress in Indian monsoon climates
  • Brick masonry in different climates — hot-dry regions, monsoon zones, cold regions, and adaptation strategies
  • Restoration and repair masonry — matching old bricks, repointing techniques, and heritage structure conservation
  • Decorative masonry — ashlar facing, exposed aggregate, colored bricks, and architectural detailing
  • Thermal and acoustic properties — insulation considerations, sound dampening, and energy efficiency in masonry construction
  • Composite wall systems — combining masonry with concrete, steel, and modern materials in contemporary Indian construction

Module 6: Quality Control, Professional Practice & Career Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Quality standards in masonry — IS codes (IS 1077, IS 2250, IS 1905), workmanship standards, and inspection checklists
  • Defect identification and rectification — common faults (hollow joints, uneven mortar, misalignment) and correction methods
  • Testing and inspection procedures — compression tests, water absorption tests, visual inspection, and documentation
  • Blueprint reading for masons — interpreting architectural drawings, understanding dimensions, symbols, and construction details
  • Estimating and costing — calculating brick quantities, mortar requirements, labor costs, and project budgeting
  • Wastage factors and optimization — typical wastage percentages (5-10%), reducing waste, and cost-effective practices
  • Documentation and record-keeping — daily work logs, quality certificates, material receipts, and project documentation
  • Communication with architects and engineers — understanding specifications, clarifying doubts, and reporting progress
  • Teamwork and site hierarchy — working under supervisors, coordinating with other trades, and site discipline
  • Professional ethics and responsibility — quality commitment, safety culture, punctuality, and client satisfaction
  • Career progression pathways — advancing from mason to foreman, supervisor, or starting own contracting business
  • Entrepreneurship opportunities — starting a masonry contracting firm, managing teams, and business management basics
  • Continuous learning and skill upgradation — advanced certifications, specialized techniques, and staying current with industry trends

  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required — open to all candidates
  • Already working as a mason? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam for direct certification
  • Valid ID proof required (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Driving License)
  • Physical fitness required — able to work at heights and perform manual labor
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and migrant workers with valid documentation
  • No age upper limit — experienced workers of any age can enroll

  • Identify common defects in masonry work, implement corrective measures, and maintain quality standards per IS codes

  • Estimate material quantities, calculate costs, and optimize resources to minimize wastage in masonry projects
  • Plan career progression in construction industry and identify opportunities for entrepreneurship in masonry contracting business
