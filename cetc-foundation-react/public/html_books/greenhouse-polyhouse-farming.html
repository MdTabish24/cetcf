Greenhouse & Polyhouse Farming

📂 Sector: Agriculture & Rural Livelihood 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹500
Learn modern greenhouse and polyhouse farming techniques to grow vegetables, flowers, and herbs year-round with minimal water and maximum yield. This hands-on course equips you with skills to start your own protected cultivation business and earn ₹15,000-50,000 monthly from a small plot of land.

Module 1: Introduction to Protected Cultivation & Greenhouse Basics

📚 Topics Covered

  • What is protected cultivation? — Understanding greenhouse, polyhouse, shade net, and net house farming methods
  • Advantages of protected cultivation — Higher yield, water savings, pesticide reduction, year-round production, quality improvement
  • Types of greenhouse structures — Glass greenhouse, plastic greenhouse, polyhouse, walk-in tunnel, net house, shade net structure
  • Climate control basics — Understanding temperature, humidity, light, and ventilation requirements for crops
  • Protected cultivation in Indian agriculture — Success stories from Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and Himachal Pradesh
  • Cost-benefit analysis — Investment required vs expected returns for small-scale farmers
  • Government schemes and subsidies — PMKSY, state-level polyhouse subsidies, and financial assistance programs
  • Safety and health considerations — Personal protective equipment, ventilation hazards, and farm safety protocols
  • Role of technology in modern farming — Use of sensors, drip irrigation, and automation in protected cultivation

Module 2: Greenhouse & Polyhouse Structure Design & Installation

📚 Topics Covered

  • Site selection and land preparation — Soil testing, water availability, sunlight exposure, and location analysis
  • Polyhouse design and dimensions — Standard sizes (500 sq.m, 1000 sq.m), orientation, and spacing calculations
  • Materials used in construction — UV-stabilized plastic (200-250 micron), bamboo/GI pipe framework, cost comparison
  • Foundation and base preparation — Leveling, drainage system, and concrete base construction
  • Ventilation systems — Side vents, roof vents, louvers, and natural ventilation design for temperature control
  • Door and entry design — Double-door systems to prevent pest entry, size specifications, and placement
  • Structural safety — Wind resistance, weight distribution, and reinforcement requirements for Indian climate
  • Installation process step-by-step — Frame assembly, plastic covering, ventilation setup, and quality checking
  • Cost estimation and budgeting — Material costs, labor, and total investment for 500-1000 sq.m polyhouse
  • Common construction mistakes to avoid — Improper slope, poor ventilation, weak foundations, and plastic quality issues
  • Maintenance of structures — Plastic replacement schedule, frame rust prevention, and seasonal repairs

Module 3: Soil Preparation, Nutrient Management & Growing Media

📚 Topics Covered

  • Soil testing and analysis — pH levels, NPK content, organic matter, and micronutrient assessment methods
  • Soil preparation techniques — Plowing, harrowing, composting, and sterilization methods for disease control
  • Organic matter incorporation — Farmyard manure, vermicompost, and coir pith usage and benefits
  • Raised bed and container gardening — Advantages, dimensions, and material selection for protected cultivation
  • Growing media selection — Soil mixes, coconut coir, peat moss, vermiculite, and perlite combinations
  • Nutrient requirements for vegetables — NPK ratios for tomato, capsicum, cucumber, lettuce, and herbs
  • Fertilizer application methods — Basal dressing, top dressing, fertigation (drip-fed fertilizer), and timing
  • Organic vs inorganic fertilizers — Comparison, benefits, limitations, and certification requirements
  • Micronutrient deficiency identification — Symptoms of zinc, boron, iron, and magnesium deficiency in crops
  • Soil pH management — Lime and sulfur application for acidic/alkaline soil correction
  • Mulching techniques — Benefits, materials used, and impact on soil moisture and weed control
  • Soil health monitoring — Regular testing schedules, record-keeping, and sustainable practices

Module 4: Water Management & Irrigation Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Water requirement calculation — Crop water needs based on season, growth stage, and climate conditions
  • Drip irrigation system design — Lateral lines, drippers, filters, regulators, and layout planning
  • Drip system installation and maintenance — Spacing of drippers, clogging prevention, and seasonal cleaning
  • Micro-sprinkler and sprinkler systems — Comparison with drip, coverage area, and pressure requirements
  • Water source assessment — Well water, bore water, tank water, and water quality testing
  • Fertigation setup — Fertilizer tank connection, proportioners, and application timing for efficient nutrient delivery
  • Water conservation techniques — Mulching, soil moisture sensors, and scheduling to reduce water waste by 40-50%
  • Automation in irrigation — Timer-based systems, soil moisture sensors, and smartphone-controlled irrigation
  • Common irrigation problems — Uneven water distribution, salt accumulation, and troubleshooting methods
  • Water quality and salinity management — Effects of saline water, leaching requirements, and remedial measures
  • Seasonal irrigation planning — Summer, monsoon, and winter water scheduling for different crops
  • Cost of irrigation systems — Budget estimation for drip setup, maintenance costs, and ROI calculation

Module 5: Crop Selection, Seed Management & Plant Propagation

📚 Topics Covered

  • High-value crops for polyhouse — Tomato, capsicum, cucumber, brinjal, lettuce, spinach, mint, basil, and flowers
  • Seasonal crop calendar — Planning crops for summer, monsoon, and winter seasons in different regions
  • Variety selection — Hybrid vs open-pollinated, disease-resistant varieties, and yield characteristics
  • Seed quality and certification — Certified seed sources, germination testing, and seed storage methods
  • Seed treatment — Fungicide treatment, hot water treatment, and biological seed treatment for disease prevention
  • Nursery management — Seed bed preparation, germination care, hardening, and transplant readiness
  • Seedling production techniques — Tray nurseries, plug trays, and direct seeding in polyhouse
  • Propagation methods — Cuttings, layering, and tissue culture basics for vegetative propagation
  • Transplanting guidelines — Spacing, depth, timing, and post-transplant care for successful establishment
  • Grafting and budding — Techniques for disease-prone crops like tomato and brinjal
  • Hybrid seed production — Understanding F1 hybrids and their advantages in protected cultivation
  • Seed rate and plant population — Calculating correct seeding density for different crops and spacing

Module 6: Pest & Disease Management, Harvesting & Marketing

📚 Topics Covered

  • Common pests in protected cultivation — Aphids, thrips, whiteflies, mites, and caterpillars identification and damage symptoms
  • Disease identification — Fungal, bacterial, and viral diseases common in greenhouses (early blight, powdery mildew, leaf curl)
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM) — Combining cultural, biological, and chemical methods for sustainable control
  • Cultural practices for pest prevention — Sanitation, crop rotation, resistant varieties, and proper spacing
  • Biological control agents — Ladybugs, parasitic wasps, Trichoderma, and Pseudomonas for natural pest management
  • Organic pest management — Neem oil, sulfur dust, copper fungicide, and botanical extracts for disease control
  • Chemical pesticide application — Spraying techniques, safety precautions, and residue management
  • Pesticide selection and dosage — Reading labels, calculating spray volume, and timing of applications
  • Disease management strategies — Pruning infected parts, improving air circulation, and fungicide application schedule
  • Monitoring and record-keeping — Pest scouting, disease incidence tracking, and treatment documentation
  • Harvesting techniques — Maturity indicators, harvesting time, and handling to prevent damage and maintain quality
  • Post-harvest management — Grading, sorting, washing, cooling, and packaging methods for different crops
  • Market linkages and pricing — Direct selling, farmer markets, wholesale, and contract farming opportunities
  • Packaging and labeling — Food safety standards, labeling requirements, and branding for organic/premium produce
  • Record-keeping and profitability analysis — Cost tracking, yield records, and profit calculation for business planning

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior farming experience required
  • Own land or access to land for practical training (minimum 200 sq.m recommended)
  • Already working as a farmer? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Driving License)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and residents
  • No age limit for self-employed or experienced farmers seeking certification

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand and explain different types of protected cultivation structures and their advantages in Indian farming context
  • Design and plan a functional polyhouse or greenhouse with appropriate site selection, dimensions, and ventilation systems
  • Prepare soil, manage nutrients, and select appropriate growing media for healthy crop production in protected environments
  • Install, operate, and maintain efficient drip irrigation and water management systems to conserve water and improve yields
  • Select suitable high-value crops, manage seeds, and implement proper nursery and transplanting practices
  • Identify pests and diseases, implement Integrated Pest Management strategies, and apply sustainable control methods
  • Harvest crops at optimal maturity, manage post-harvest quality, and connect with markets for profitable sales
  • Maintain farm records, calculate profitability, and plan a sustainable greenhouse farming business with proper financial management
