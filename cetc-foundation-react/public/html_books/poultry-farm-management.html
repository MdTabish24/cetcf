Poultry Farm Management

📂 Sector: Agriculture & Rural Livelihood 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the complete art and science of poultry farming with this hands-on certification course. Learn to manage layer farms, broiler units, and hatcheries while building a profitable poultry business from scratch. This foundation course equips you with practical skills to start earning within weeks of completion.

Module 1: Introduction to Poultry Farming & Farm Setup

📚 Topics Covered

  • Poultry farming overview in India — types of farming (layer, broiler, backyard) and market demand
  • Poultry breeds and their characteristics — Desi, Leghorn, Rhode Island Red, Broiler breeds (Vencobb, Hubbard)
  • Site selection and farm location — soil type, water availability, climate, distance from city
  • Farm infrastructure planning — poultry shed design, ventilation system, nesting boxes, perches
  • Equipment and tools required — feeders, waterers, brooding equipment, weighing scales
  • Biosecurity measures and farm hygiene protocols — entry points, disinfection zones, visitor management
  • Cost estimation and farm layout design — space requirements per bird, initial investment calculation
  • Government schemes and subsidies — PMKSY, state poultry schemes, loan eligibility criteria
  • Legal registration and licensing — FSSAI registration, animal husbandry department permits, GST registration
  • Farm record-keeping systems — production logs, expense tracking, mortality records, vaccination charts

Module 2: Poultry Breeds, Selection & Procurement

📚 Topics Covered

  • Indian poultry breeds — Aseel, Kadaknath, Tellicherry, local Desi birds and their egg/meat production
  • Exotic commercial breeds — Leghorn (eggs), Broiler strains (meat), characteristics and profitability
  • Breed selection based on farm objectives — egg production vs meat production vs dual-purpose birds
  • Quality assessment of chicks — health indicators, weight standards, mortality rates, hatchery selection
  • Chick procurement process — ordering from registered hatcheries, transport arrangements, quality checks
  • Gender identification and sorting — manual sexing for layer chicks, importance for profitability
  • Vaccination at hatchery level — Marek's disease, Newcastle disease, importance of vaccination records
  • Quarantine procedures for new birds — isolation period, health monitoring, gradual introduction to flock
  • Supplier relationships and contracts — negotiating prices, bulk orders, seasonal availability planning
  • Replacement strategies — culling old birds, maintaining flock age distribution for consistent production

Module 3: Nutrition, Feed Management & Water Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Poultry nutrition fundamentals — protein, carbohydrates, fats, vitamins, minerals requirements by age
  • Feed types and formulations — starter feed (0-6 weeks), grower feed (6-16 weeks), layer feed, broiler finisher
  • Local feed ingredients in India — maize, soybean meal, fish meal, groundnut cake, rice bran usage
  • Feed cost optimization — bulk buying, mixing own feed, calculating cost per kg, profit margins
  • Feeding schedules and quantities — daily feed requirements by age, weight-based feeding calculations
  • Water management and quality — clean water systems, drinker types, water-to-feed ratio, contamination prevention
  • Watering systems — manual waterers, nipple drinkers, bell drinkers, cleaning and maintenance
  • Mineral and vitamin supplementation — calcium for layers, phosphorus, trace minerals, premix selection
  • Feed storage and preservation — moisture control, pest management, temperature maintenance, shelf life
  • Common feed-related problems — malnutrition signs, mycotoxin contamination, feed refusal, disease prevention
  • Supplier selection and quality control — feed analysis, batch testing, regulatory compliance, certification
  • Organic and specialty feeds — non-GMO feeds, antibiotic-free feeds, premium market positioning

Module 4: Health Management, Diseases & Vaccination

📚 Topics Covered

  • Common poultry diseases in India — Newcastle disease, Avian influenza, Ranikhet disease, Coccidiosis
  • Disease identification and symptoms — respiratory signs, digestive issues, paralysis, sudden death indicators
  • Vaccination schedule for layer and broiler birds — timing, routes (oral, injection, spray), record-keeping
  • Biosecurity practices — preventing disease entry, cleaning protocols, quarantine procedures, staff hygiene
  • Parasite management — internal parasites (worms), external parasites (lice, mites), deworming schedule
  • Stress management in poultry — heat stress, cold stress, crowding effects, environmental control measures
  • Antibiotic usage and withdrawal periods — responsible use, legal restrictions, residue prevention
  • Mortality management — daily health checks, sick bird isolation, necropsy basics, disposal methods
  • Veterinary support and professional help — finding good veterinarian, emergency contacts, disease reporting
  • Health records and documentation — vaccination certificates, treatment logs, mortality tracking, insurance claims
  • Poultry insurance schemes — government schemes, private insurance options, claim procedures in India
  • Emergency disease management — quarantine procedures, mass vaccination drives, government support systems

Module 5: Production Management & Egg/Meat Quality

📚 Topics Covered

  • Egg production cycle in layers — age at first egg, peak production, decline phase, production graphs
  • Factors affecting egg production — nutrition, light hours, temperature, stress, age of flock
  • Egg grading and quality standards — size grading (jumbo, large, medium), shell quality, internal quality
  • Egg collection and storage — handling practices, storage temperature, shelf life, contamination prevention
  • Broiler production timeline — growth rates, weight targets, feed conversion ratio (FCR) optimization
  • Broiler meat quality — color, texture, tenderness, processing standards, food safety requirements
  • Record-keeping for production — daily egg counts, mortality rates, feed consumption, weight gain tracking
  • Production efficiency metrics — FCR (feed conversion ratio), livability percentage, egg production percentage
  • Seasonal variations and planning — summer/winter production changes, demand fluctuations, inventory management
  • Flock health monitoring — daily observation routine, weight monitoring, behavioral changes, early warning signs
  • Environmental control for production — lighting programs, temperature management, humidity levels, ventilation
  • Culling and replacement planning — identifying low producers, maintaining optimal flock age, economic calculations

Module 6: Marketing, Economics & Business Planning

📚 Topics Covered

  • Market analysis for poultry products in India — local demand, seasonal trends, price fluctuations, competition
  • Sales channels for eggs — retail shops, wholesale markets, direct to consumer, e-commerce platforms
  • Sales channels for broiler meat — local butchers, hotels, restaurants, meat shops, online delivery platforms
  • Pricing strategies — cost-plus pricing, market-based pricing, seasonal adjustments, competitive analysis
  • Packaging and branding — egg cartons, meat packaging, hygiene standards, brand creation for premium products
  • Quality certification and standards — FSSAI compliance, organic certification, traceability systems, food safety
  • Direct marketing and farm visits — farm tourism, farm store, direct sales to consumers, relationship building
  • Cost analysis and profitability — fixed costs, variable costs, break-even analysis, profit margins by product
  • Financial planning — initial investment breakdown, working capital requirements, cash flow management
  • Loan and credit options — bank loans, government schemes (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana variants), interest rates
  • Record-keeping for business — income and expense tracking, profit/loss calculations, tax filing basics, GST compliance
  • Business scaling strategies — expansion planning, adding new product lines, market diversification, growth projections
  • Risk management — disease outbreaks, market price crashes, seasonal variations, insurance and contingency planning

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (18 years preferred for business loans)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass
  • No prior poultry farming experience required
  • Already working in poultry farms? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, voter ID, driving license)
  • Open for all candidates including SC/ST/OBC and women farmers (special incentives available)
  • Agricultural background preferred but not mandatory

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand poultry farming fundamentals including breed selection, farm setup, and infrastructure requirements
  • Manage poultry nutrition, feed quality, and water systems effectively to optimize production
  • Implement comprehensive health management, disease prevention, and vaccination protocols
  • Monitor and maintain production records for eggs and meat with quality standards compliance
  • Analyze market trends, pricing strategies, and develop sustainable business plans with profitability calculations
  • Apply biosecurity measures, regulatory compliance, and food safety standards in poultry farming operations
