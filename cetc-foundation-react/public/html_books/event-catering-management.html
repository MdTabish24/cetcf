Event Catering Management

📂 Sector: Food, Catering & Hospitality 📊 Level: Intermediate ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months 🕐 Hours: 180 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the complete art and science of managing catering operations for events in India. This intermediate course trains you to handle everything from menu planning and food safety to team management and client coordination for weddings, corporate events, and celebrations. Become job-ready for the booming Indian event catering industry.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Event Catering & Industry Overview

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to Indian event catering industry — wedding catering, corporate events, social gatherings, destination events
  • Types of catering services — full-service catering, drop-off catering, partial catering, on-site catering
  • Understanding client requirements and event briefing — venue assessment, guest count, dietary preferences, budget constraints
  • Catering business models in India — small home-based operations, medium catering houses, large event management companies
  • Role and responsibilities of catering manager — planning, execution, quality control, team supervision
  • Safety and hygiene standards in Indian food service — FSSAI regulations, food storage, contamination prevention
  • Introduction to event types and their catering needs — weddings (mehndi, sangeet, baraat, reception), corporate conferences, birthday parties, religious events
  • Professional communication with clients — taking orders, managing expectations, handling complaints
  • Introduction to costing and pricing in catering — calculating food cost, labour cost, overhead, profit margin
  • Sustainability and waste management in events — reducing food waste, eco-friendly practices, composting

Module 2: Menu Planning & Food Selection for Events

📚 Topics Covered

  • Understanding dietary requirements — vegetarian, non-vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, jain, religious restrictions (halal, kosher)
  • Seasonal menu planning — using seasonal ingredients for cost efficiency and freshness
  • Regional Indian cuisine — North Indian, South Indian, East Indian, West Indian specialties and their catering applications
  • Fusion and international cuisines — Chinese, Continental, Italian, and their adaptation for Indian events
  • Menu design for different event types — wedding menus (multi-course), corporate buffets, cocktail parties, casual gatherings
  • Quantity calculation and portion planning — calculating portions per person, accounting for waste, food preferences
  • Recipe standardization and recipe cards — maintaining consistency, training staff on portions and presentation
  • Food pairing and balance — nutritional balance, colour balance, texture variety on the plate
  • Beverage selection and pairing — alcoholic and non-alcoholic options, mocktails, traditional drinks, water and juice services
  • Presentation and plating techniques — garnishing, plate arrangement, colour contrast, height variation, professional presentation
  • Special dietary accommodations — managing allergies, religious dietary laws, medical conditions (diabetes, heart disease)
  • Cost-effective menu options — high-margin dishes, low-waste preparations, seasonal savings

Module 3: Food Preparation, Cooking & Kitchen Operations

📚 Topics Covered

  • Food preparation basics — washing, cutting, peeling, marinating, pre-cooking techniques
  • Cooking methods used in catering — boiling, steaming, grilling, frying, baking, slow-cooking, pressure cooking
  • Batch cooking and production planning — preparing large quantities, timing for service, keeping food warm
  • Kitchen equipment for catering — industrial stoves, ovens, steamers, food warmers, serving vessels, cutting tools
  • Mise-en-place and station setup — organizing ingredients and tools before service, efficient workflow
  • Quality control during cooking — taste testing, consistency checks, colour and texture assessment
  • Time management in kitchen — preparing dishes in correct sequence, managing multiple dishes simultaneously
  • Handling common catering challenges — cooking for 100+ people, maintaining food temperature, managing last-minute changes
  • Vegetarian and non-vegetarian preparation separation — preventing cross-contamination, maintaining separate cooking areas
  • Garnishing and final plating — professional presentation techniques, speed plating for large events
  • Food holding and transport — maintaining food safety during storage and transport, temperature control
  • Kitchen hygiene and sanitation — cleaning protocols, pest control, proper waste disposal, staff hygiene practices

Module 4: Food Safety, Hygiene & FSSAI Compliance

📚 Topics Covered

  • FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India) regulations — licensing requirements, food safety standards, documentation
  • Food poisoning and foodborne diseases — common pathogens (bacteria, viruses), symptoms, prevention methods
  • Personal hygiene for food handlers — handwashing, nail care, hair covering, illness reporting, uniform standards
  • Premises hygiene — kitchen cleanliness, storage area maintenance, pest control, waste management
  • Food storage and preservation — temperature control for different foods, refrigeration, freezing, dry storage
  • Cross-contamination prevention — separate storage for vegetarian and non-vegetarian, raw and cooked foods
  • Cooking temperatures and food safety — safe internal temperatures for different foods, using food thermometers
  • Allergen management and labelling — identifying common allergens (nuts, shellfish, dairy, gluten), proper labelling, staff awareness
  • Water quality and supply — safe drinking water, water storage, testing and purification methods
  • Documentation and record-keeping — maintaining food safety logs, supplier information, temperature records, incident reports
  • Health and safety certificates — training certificates for staff, medical check-ups for food handlers
  • Handling food complaints and recalls — identifying contaminated food, customer communication, proper disposal

Module 5: Event Planning, Costing & Business Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Event planning process — initial consultation, site visit, proposal preparation, confirmation and final planning
  • Vendor management — coordinating with decorators, musicians, photographers, transportation providers
  • Logistics and timeline planning — delivery schedules, setup time, cooking timeline, service schedule, breakdown time
  • Budgeting and costing for events — calculating total cost per person, profit margins, pricing strategies
  • Food cost analysis — ingredient costs, portion costs, waste percentage, pricing menus competitively
  • Labour cost management — calculating staff requirements, wage planning, overtime management
  • Quotation and proposal writing — professional quotation format, terms and conditions, payment terms
  • Negotiation and contract management — client negotiations, written contracts, cancellation policies, liability clauses
  • Cash flow and payment collection — advance payment policies, payment schedules, handling late payments
  • Inventory management — ordering supplies, stock management, expiry date tracking, minimizing waste
  • Financial record-keeping — maintaining invoices, expense records, profit and loss statements, tax compliance
  • Business registration and licensing — GST registration, FSSAI license, food handler licenses, business insurance

Module 6: Team Management, Customer Service & Professional Excellence

📚 Topics Covered

  • Team structure in catering — roles of head chef, sous chef, cooks, helpers, servers, coordinators
  • Staff recruitment and training — hiring skilled and unskilled staff, onboarding, skill development programs
  • Leadership and motivation — managing teams, handling conflicts, creating positive work environment, performance feedback
  • Service standards and etiquette — professional behaviour, dress code, punctuality, respect for clients and guests
  • Customer service excellence — greeting guests, understanding expectations, handling special requests, complaint resolution
  • Communication skills — clear instructions to staff, listening to clients, written communication, presentation skills
  • Problem-solving during events — handling food shortages, equipment failures, guest complaints, last-minute changes
  • Event day execution — pre-event checklist, setup coordination, service supervision, quality monitoring, post-event breakdown
  • Building client relationships — follow-up after events, testimonials and referrals, repeat business, customer retention
  • Professional ethics and integrity — honesty in pricing, quality commitment, respecting client confidentiality, maintaining reputation
  • Marketing and business development — creating portfolio, using social media, networking, word-of-mouth marketing, online presence
  • Continuous improvement — gathering feedback, analyzing events, updating menus, adopting new trends, skill upgradation

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior experience required; beginners welcome
  • Currently employed in catering? Apply for Direct RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid photo ID proof required (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, Passport, or Driving License)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and foreign nationals with valid visa
  • Physical fitness required for standing, cooking, and event management tasks
  • Basic English and Hindi language understanding helpful

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Plan and design professional menus for various event types (weddings, corporate events, celebrations) considering dietary requirements, budget, and client preferences
  • Prepare food in large quantities maintaining consistent quality, taste, and professional presentation standards
  • Implement food safety and hygiene protocols as per FSSAI standards and prevent foodborne contamination
  • Calculate accurate food costs, determine competitive pricing, and manage budgets for profitable catering operations
  • Coordinate and manage catering teams, supervise kitchen operations, and ensure smooth event execution
  • Provide excellent customer service, handle client communication, manage expectations, and build long-term client relationships
