Two Wheeler Mechanic Certification

📂 Sector: Automobile & Motor Trades 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months 🕐 Hours: 180 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the skills to repair, maintain, and service motorcycles, scooters, and mopeds across India. This hands-on certification program trains you to become a competent two-wheeler mechanic, ready to work in service centers, dealerships, or start your own repair shop. Learn from industry experts and gain practical experience with real vehicles.

Module 1: Two Wheeler Fundamentals & Safety Practices

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to two-wheeler industry in India — market overview, types of vehicles (motorcycles, scooters, mopeds, auto-rickshaws), and employment opportunities
  • Safety in the workshop — personal protective equipment (PPE), fire safety, electrical safety, proper lifting techniques, and hazard identification
  • Hand tools and their uses — spanners, wrenches, screwdrivers, pliers, hammers, chisels, measuring instruments, and proper tool maintenance
  • Power tools and equipment — electric drills, angle grinders, impact wrenches, tire changing machines, and safety protocols
  • Two-wheeler anatomy — frame, chassis, engine location, fuel system, cooling system, and major components overview
  • Basic mechanical principles — friction, pressure, torque, horsepower, and how they apply to two-wheelers
  • Workshop organization and cleanliness — maintaining work areas, managing spares inventory, and tool storage
  • Reading technical manuals and service bulletins — understanding diagrams, specifications, and manufacturer guidelines
  • Environmental safety and waste management — proper disposal of oil, batteries, and hazardous materials
  • First aid basics for workshop injuries — minor cuts, burns, and when to seek medical help

Module 2: Engine Systems & Fuel Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Four-stroke and two-stroke engine fundamentals — working principle, differences, and when each is used in Indian two-wheelers
  • Engine components — cylinder block, piston, connecting rod, crankshaft, camshaft, valves, and their functions
  • Fuel system components — fuel tank, fuel pump, carburetor, fuel injectors, and fuel filters in petrol and diesel two-wheelers
  • Carburetor working and adjustment — float mechanism, jet sizing, idle speed adjustment, and common carburetor problems
  • Fuel injection systems — electronic fuel injection (EFI) in modern scooters, sensor types, and basic diagnostics
  • Air intake system — air filters, air box design, and importance of clean air for engine performance
  • Engine cooling systems — air-cooled and liquid-cooled engines, radiators, fans, and coolant maintenance
  • Engine lubrication system — oil circulation, oil filters, oil grades (SAE 10W-30, 15W-40), and oil change procedures
  • Common engine problems — starting issues, rough idling, overheating, knocking sounds, and troubleshooting methods
  • Engine tuning and maintenance — spark plug selection, compression testing, valve clearance adjustment, and timing settings
  • Emission control systems — catalytic converters, emission standards in India (BS-IV, BS-VI), and their maintenance
  • Practical: Carburetor cleaning, fuel filter replacement, spark plug inspection, and oil change on actual two-wheelers

Module 3: Transmission, Clutch & Drivetrain Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Transmission types in two-wheelers — manual transmission, automatic transmission (CVT), and semi-automatic systems
  • Manual clutch system — clutch plate, pressure plate, release bearing, and clutch cable operation
  • Clutch adjustment and troubleshooting — cable tension, slipping clutch, dragging clutch, and replacement procedures
  • Gear system — gear ratios, primary and secondary gears, gear shifting mechanism, and gear selection
  • Automatic transmission (CVT) in scooters — pulley system, belt mechanism, and how CVT adjusts gear ratios automatically
  • Gearbox components and assembly — gears, bearings, shafts, and synchronization in modern two-wheelers
  • Chain and sprocket system — chain maintenance, lubrication, tension adjustment, and wear indicators
  • Belt drive systems — belt condition checking, tension adjustment, and when to replace belts
  • Final drive systems — rear axle assembly, wheel hub, and power transmission to the wheel
  • Common transmission problems — gear grinding, chain slipping, belt breaking, and noise diagnosis
  • Practical: Clutch cable adjustment, chain cleaning and lubrication, sprocket inspection, belt tension checking, and gearbox oil change

Module 4: Electrical Systems & Battery Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Two-wheeler electrical system overview — battery, alternator, starter motor, ignition system, and lighting circuits
  • Battery types and specifications — lead-acid batteries, lithium batteries, capacity ratings (Ah), and voltage (12V)
  • Battery maintenance and charging — checking water level, terminal corrosion cleaning, charging procedures, and battery replacement
  • Alternator function and output — generating electricity, voltage regulation, and common alternator faults
  • Starter motor operation — starting mechanism, solenoid function, and starter troubleshooting
  • Ignition system — spark plug types, ignition coil, distributor (in older models), CDI (Capacitor Discharge Ignition) in modern bikes
  • Lighting systems — headlight, taillight, indicators, brake lights, and bulb replacement
  • Wiring harness and connectors — wire gauge selection, connector types, waterproofing, and corrosion prevention
  • Electrical troubleshooting — using multimeter, testing circuits, identifying open circuits and short circuits
  • Horn, switches, and control systems — horn function, handlebar switches, kill switches, and safety mechanisms
  • Electric start vs. kick start — advantages and disadvantages in Indian two-wheelers
  • Practical: Battery testing and charging, spark plug inspection and replacement, headlight and taillight replacement, multimeter usage for circuit testing

Module 5: Suspension, Braking & Steering Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Suspension system types — telescopic forks (front), swing arm (rear), and rigid suspension in budget two-wheelers
  • Suspension components — springs, dampers (shock absorbers), and how they work together for comfort and stability
  • Front fork maintenance — oil level checking, seal inspection, and fork oil replacement procedures
  • Rear shock absorber service — preload adjustment, damping settings, and replacement when worn
  • Suspension troubleshooting — bouncing, sagging, harsh ride, and noise diagnosis
  • Braking system types — drum brakes, disc brakes, and anti-lock braking system (ABS) in modern scooters
  • Drum brake components — brake shoes, linings, springs, and adjustment mechanisms
  • Disc brake components — brake pads, rotors, calipers, and hydraulic lines in hydraulic disc brakes
  • Brake fluid and hydraulic system — fluid types (DOT 3, DOT 4), bleeding air from lines, and fluid replacement
  • Brake adjustment and maintenance — brake lever free play, brake pad wear indicators, and stopping distance testing
  • Steering system — handlebar assembly, steering head bearing adjustment, and steering smoothness
  • Practical: Brake pad replacement, brake fluid bleeding, suspension oil change, wheel bearing greasing, and brake testing

Module 6: Diagnostics, Service Planning & Professional Skills

📚 Topics Covered

  • Diagnostic troubleshooting methodology — systematic problem-solving, symptom analysis, and root cause identification
  • Using diagnostic tools — multimeter, compression tester, fuel pressure gauge, and basic scanning equipment
  • Reading service manuals — torque specifications, service intervals, fluid capacities, and technical specifications
  • Vehicle inspection checklist — pre-service inspection, identifying all serviceable items, and documenting findings
  • Service planning and scheduling — routine maintenance, periodic service, major overhaul, and cost estimation
  • Common two-wheeler problems and solutions — starting issues, overheating, poor fuel efficiency, unusual noises, and vibrations
  • Customer communication skills — explaining problems in simple language, providing service estimates, and building customer trust
  • Invoicing and documentation — service records, spare parts inventory, warranty terms, and customer receipts
  • Professional ethics and conduct — punctuality, cleanliness, honesty in diagnosis, and respecting customer property
  • Career development — certification value, skill enhancement, entrepreneurship opportunities, and continuous learning
  • Quality assurance and testing — road testing after repair, safety checks, and customer satisfaction verification
  • Practical: Complete vehicle inspection, diagnostic report writing, service estimate preparation, and customer interaction simulation

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or 18 years for employment in certain states)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior mechanical experience required
  • Already working as a two-wheeler mechanic? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhar, Voter ID, PAN, or Driving License)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals
  • Medical fitness for physical work (no serious disabilities preventing tool usage)
  • Commitment to complete all 180 hours of training and practical sessions

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and use hand tools, power tools, and diagnostic equipment safely and correctly in a workshop environment
  • Diagnose and repair engine problems in four-stroke and two-stroke two-wheelers including fuel system issues
  • Service transmission systems, clutches, and drivetrain components while maintaining proper adjustments
  • Test, maintain, and troubleshoot electrical systems including batteries, alternators, and lighting circuits
  • Repair and adjust suspension, braking, and steering systems according to manufacturer specifications
  • Perform complete vehicle inspections, create service reports, and communicate effectively with customers about repairs
