Islamic Finance & Banking Basics

📂 Sector: Religious & Spiritual Education 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1200
Master the fundamentals of Islamic Finance and Banking, a rapidly growing sector in India and globally. This course teaches you Sharia-compliant financial principles, Islamic banking products, and ethical investment practices. Perfect for students, professionals, and entrepreneurs wanting to enter this high-demand field.

Module 1: Foundations of Islamic Finance & Sharia Principles

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to Islamic Finance — History, scope, and relevance in modern India and global economy
  • Quranic & Hadith Foundations — Key Islamic teachings on money, trade, and economic justice
  • Five Pillars of Islamic Economics — Tawheed, Khalifah, Amanah, Adl, and Ihsan principles
  • Prohibition of Riba (Interest) — Understanding usury ban and its practical implications in banking
  • Concept of Halal & Haram in Finance — What is permissible and forbidden in Islamic commerce
  • Sharia Governance & Fatwa System — How Islamic scholars validate financial products
  • Maqasid al-Sharia (Objectives of Sharia) — Protection of wealth, religion, life, intellect, and family
  • Comparison with Conventional Finance — Key differences in ethics, risk-sharing, and profit models
  • Islamic Finance in Indian Context — Current status, regulatory framework, and growth opportunities
  • Ethical Investment Principles — Social responsibility and sustainable finance in Islam

Module 2: Islamic Banking Products & Services

📚 Topics Covered

  • Overview of Islamic Banks in India — Major players like Bank Alfalah, Al Baraka Bank, and NBFCs
  • Current Account (Al-Wadiah) — Safe-keeping principles and zero-interest accounts
  • Savings Account (Mudaraba) — Profit-sharing model between bank and depositors
  • Murabaha Financing — Cost-plus markup sales contract for asset purchases
  • Ijara (Islamic Leasing) — Equipment and property leasing with ownership transfer options
  • Musharaka (Equity Partnership) — Joint venture financing for business expansion
  • Mudaraba (Profit-Sharing) — Bank as investor in business ventures
  • Salam & Istisna — Forward contracts for agricultural and manufacturing goods
  • Takaful Insurance — Islamic insurance based on mutual cooperation, not gambling
  • Trade Finance Products — Letters of credit, guarantees, and bill discounting in Islamic way
  • Sukuk Bonds — Islamic bonds backed by real assets, not just debt obligations
  • Working Capital Solutions — Inventory financing and receivables management

Module 3: Islamic Investment & Portfolio Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Halal Stock Screening — Identifying Sharia-compliant companies in India's stock market
  • Prohibited Industries — Alcohol, pork, gambling, conventional banking, weapons, interest-based finance
  • Shariah Screening Criteria — Financial ratios, debt limits, and business ethics evaluation
  • Islamic Mutual Funds in India — Available options and how they work
  • Equity Investment Principles — Ownership stake vs. debt-based returns
  • Real Estate Investment in Islam — Property ownership, rental income, and development financing
  • Islamic Microfinance — Small loans for poor and underserved communities without interest
  • Crowdfunding & P2P Lending (Islamic Model) — Ethical peer-to-peer finance platforms
  • Gold & Commodity Investment — Sharia-compliant precious metal trading
  • Portfolio Diversification Strategy — Risk management within Islamic principles
  • ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) Investing — Alignment with Islamic values
  • Zakat Investment — Using obligatory alms for community development and business

Module 4: Compliance, Risk Management & Regulatory Framework

📚 Topics Covered

  • RBI Guidelines for Islamic Finance in India — Current regulatory status and future roadmap
  • Sharia Compliance Officer Role — Responsibilities and certification requirements
  • Anti-Money Laundering (AML) in Islamic Banking — KYC norms and due diligence
  • Basel III Compliance — Capital adequacy standards for Islamic banks
  • Liquidity Management — Handling cash flow in Islamic banking without interest
  • Credit Risk Assessment — Evaluating borrower creditworthiness without conventional scoring
  • Operational Risk — Fraud prevention, internal controls, and audit procedures
  • Profit & Loss Sharing (PLS) Risks — Managing uncertainty in Musharaka and Mudaraba
  • Sukuk Default & Recovery — Legal remedies and restructuring options
  • Regulatory Bodies — IFSB (Islamic Financial Services Board), AAOIFI (Accounting Standards)
  • Documentation & Contracts — Proper Islamic contract drafting and enforcement
  • Dispute Resolution — Arbitration and Islamic finance courts in India
