Toilet & Bathroom Renovation Work

📂 Sector: Plumbing, Sanitation & Water 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the complete skills of modern toilet and bathroom renovation, from pipe fitting and tile work to fixture installation and waterproofing. This hands-on course trains you to handle residential and commercial bathroom projects with professional standards, making you job-ready in India's booming real estate and renovation sector.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Plumbing & Sanitation Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Indian plumbing codes and IS standards — Understanding Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) guidelines for bathroom plumbing installations
  • Water supply systems — Cold water, hot water, and dual pipe systems used in Indian homes
  • Drainage and sewage systems — DWV (Drain-Waste-Vent) system basics and septic tank connections
  • Pipe materials and selection — PVC, CPVC, copper, and galvanized iron pipes; when and why to use each
  • Water pressure and flow rate calculations — Basic math for sizing pipes correctly for residential bathrooms
  • Sanitation principles — Toilet seat heights, water closet types, and hygiene standards in India
  • Common plumbing problems — Leaks, blockages, low pressure, and their causes
  • Safety equipment and personal protective gear — Helmets, gloves, masks, and safety shoes for bathroom work

Module 2: Tools, Equipment & Materials Handling

📚 Topics Covered

  • Essential hand tools — Wrenches, spanners, pipe cutters, pipe benders, plungers, and their proper use
  • Power tools for renovation — Angle grinders, drills, and hammer drills with safety precautions
  • Measuring and marking tools — Tape measures, spirit levels, and chalk lines for accurate work
  • Pipe fitting tools — Adjustable wrenches, basin wrenches, and specialized fitting tools
  • Tile cutting and installation tools — Tile cutters, wet saws, notched trowels, and spacers
  • Materials identification — Cement, sand, adhesive, grout, and waterproofing compounds
  • Tool maintenance and storage — Keeping tools rust-free and organized on job sites
  • Hazardous materials handling — Asbestos identification in old pipes and safe removal procedures
  • Inventory management — Calculating material quantities for bathroom projects
  • Supplier relationships — Finding reliable vendors for pipes, fixtures, and materials in your city

Module 3: Pipe Installation & Water Supply Work

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pipe routing and layout planning — Drawing bathroom pipe layouts and planning fixture locations
  • Cutting and joining pipes — Proper cutting techniques for PVC, CPVC, and copper pipes
  • PVC solvent welding — Applying primer and cement for strong, leak-free PVC joints
  • Threaded pipe connections — Using PTFE tape and thread sealant for metal pipe joints
  • Compression fittings — Installing and tightening compression fittings for water supply
  • Elbow, tee, and coupling installations — Proper angles and positioning for bathroom fixtures
  • Water inlet valve fitting — Installing stop valves and isolation taps near fixtures
  • Hot water pipe insulation — Wrapping pipes with foam insulation to prevent heat loss
  • Pressure testing — Using pressure gauges to test for leaks before walls are closed
  • Water meter installation — Connecting meters to main supply lines correctly
  • Fixture connection — Attaching wash basins, toilets, and shower heads to supply pipes
  • Troubleshooting water supply issues — Identifying and fixing pressure drops and flow problems

Module 4: Drainage, Waste & Waterproofing Systems

📚 Topics Covered

  • Drainage system design — Understanding slope requirements (1:40 ratio) for proper water flow
  • Trap installations — P-traps, S-traps, and bottle traps for wash basins and floor drains
  • Soil pipes and waste pipes — Installing vertical and horizontal drainage pipes correctly
  • Floor drain and gully fitting — Proper installation for water to flow away from toilet and shower areas
  • Anti-siphon devices — Understanding and installing vent pipes to prevent foul smells
  • Waterproofing materials — Liquid membranes, sheet membranes, and cement-based coatings
  • Waterproofing application methods — Brush, roller, and spray techniques for bathroom walls and floors
  • Waterproofing at critical joints — Corners, pipe penetrations, and edges that need extra protection
  • Curing and drying times — Waiting periods before tiling after waterproofing
  • Testing waterproofing — Checking for leaks with water retention tests
  • Slope creation for bathrooms — Creating proper gradients for water drainage
  • Dealing with existing moisture — Drying out damp walls before renovation work

Module 5: Tile Work & Surface Finishing

📚 Topics Covered

  • Tile types and selection — Ceramic, vitreous, porcelain, and natural stone tiles for bathrooms
  • Substrate preparation — Leveling, cleaning, and priming surfaces before tiling
  • Tile adhesive selection — Using cement-based and ready-mix adhesives correctly
  • Trowel techniques — Holding trowels at correct angles for proper adhesive coverage
  • Tile cutting techniques — Using manual cutters and wet saws for straight and curved cuts
  • Laying tiles in straight lines — Using spacers and levels to ensure even grout lines
  • Tile patterns and designs — Creating borders, mosaics, and decorative patterns
  • Grouting process — Mixing grout, filling joints, and finishing grout lines smoothly
  • Caulking vs grouting — When to use silicone caulk instead of grout
  • Sealing tiles — Applying sealant to protect porous tiles from water damage
  • Toilet and fixture placement — Tiling around pipes and fixtures without damage
  • Finishing touches — Trim tiles, edge profiles, and corner pieces for professional look

Module 6: Fixture Installation & Project Completion

📚 Topics Covered

  • Water closet (toilet) installation — Mounting on floor or wall, sealing with wax rings, and connecting water supply
  • Wash basin installation — Wall-mounted and pedestal sinks with proper support and connections
  • Shower fitting installation — Mixing valves, shower heads, and hand shower connections
  • Bathtub installation — Positioning, leveling, and waterproofing around tub edges
  • Mirror and accessory fitting — Installing towel racks, soap dispensers, and mirrors securely
  • Ventilation installation — Exhaust fans and vents to prevent moisture buildup
  • Lighting fixture installation — Waterproof lights and switches for bathroom use
  • Door and hardware fitting — Installing bathroom doors with proper hinges and latches
  • Final plumbing checks — Testing all fixtures for leaks and proper water flow
  • Cleaning and site restoration — Removing dust, debris, and protecting newly completed work
  • Quality inspection — Checking workmanship against BIS standards and client expectations
  • Project documentation — Creating completion reports, warranty details, and maintenance guides for clients
