Solar Energy Technician — Basic

📂 Sector: Environmental & Green Skills 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Learn to install, maintain, and troubleshoot solar photovoltaic (PV) systems in this hands-on foundation course. Gain practical skills in renewable energy technology that are in high demand across India's rapidly growing solar sector. This certification opens doors to employment in residential, commercial, and industrial solar installations.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Solar Energy & Renewable Energy Sources

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to renewable energy — solar, wind, hydro, biomass, geothermal sources in India
  • Solar radiation basics — sunlight, wavelength, intensity, and seasonal variations in Indian climate
  • Photovoltaic (PV) effect — how solar cells convert sunlight into electricity
  • Types of solar systems — grid-connected, off-grid, hybrid systems and their applications
  • Government policies and incentives — MNRE subsidies, net metering, PM-KUSUM scheme
  • Environmental benefits of solar energy — carbon footprint reduction and sustainability
  • Economics of solar energy — cost-benefit analysis, ROI calculation for Indian households
  • Solar energy statistics in India — current capacity, growth trends, and employment opportunities
  • Comparison of solar with conventional energy sources — cost and efficiency analysis
  • Introduction to MNRE (Ministry of New and Renewable Energy) guidelines and standards

Module 2: Solar PV System Components & Technology

📚 Topics Covered

  • Solar photovoltaic (PV) cells — monocrystalline, polycrystalline, thin-film technologies and efficiency ratings
  • Solar panel structure — glass cover, anti-reflective coating, cells, backsheet, frame materials
  • Panel specifications — wattage, voltage (Voc, Vmpp), current (Isc, Impp), temperature coefficient, efficiency
  • Inverters — string inverters, central inverters, micro-inverters; specifications and selection criteria
  • Charge controllers — PWM vs MPPT technology, sizing, and applications in off-grid systems
  • Battery storage systems — lithium-ion, lead-acid batteries; capacity, voltage, and lifespan
  • Balance of System (BOS) components — combiner boxes, disconnects, fuses, breakers, wiring, connectors
  • Mounting structures — roof-mounted, ground-mounted, pole-mounted systems and materials (aluminum, steel)
  • Earthing and lightning protection — grounding rods, surge arrestors, lightning conductors
  • Monitoring systems — data loggers, WiFi monitors, performance tracking software and apps
  • Safety equipment — DC switches, AC switches, isolators, safety disconnects as per IS standards
  • Quality standards — BIS certification, ISI marks, international standards for solar components

Module 3: Solar System Design & Site Assessment

📚 Topics Covered

  • Site assessment basics — roof condition, orientation, tilt angle, shading analysis, space requirements
  • Solar irradiance measurement — using solar meters, understanding peak sun hours in different Indian regions
  • Roof evaluation — structural load capacity, age of building, material compatibility, leak prevention
  • Shading analysis — trees, buildings, chimneys, poles; impact on system performance
  • Orientation and tilt — optimal angles for different Indian latitudes (North, South, East, West India)
  • System sizing calculations — load assessment, daily energy consumption, capacity planning
  • Capacity selection — 1kW, 2kW, 3kW, 5kW, 10kW systems; typical household and commercial setups
  • Design considerations — safety distances, ventilation requirements, cable routing, junction box placement
  • Documentation and sketches — site plan drawing, electrical diagrams, load calculations
  • Feasibility report preparation — assessing technical and financial viability for Indian customers
  • Customer requirements gathering — budget, energy needs, space constraints, future expansion plans
  • Compliance with local regulations — building bylaws, electricity board approvals, NOC requirements

Module 4: Installation, Wiring & Electrical Safety

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pre-installation checklist — material verification, safety equipment, tool inspection, weather assessment
  • Mounting structure installation — fixing brackets, ensuring structural integrity, waterproofing, alignment
  • Panel installation — handling, positioning, securing to mounting structures, ensuring proper spacing
  • DC wiring — cable sizing, connector types (MC4, Amphenol), polarity marking, parallel and series connections
  • AC wiring — from inverter to AC distribution board, breaker sizing, cable routing, conduit protection
  • Grounding and earthing — copper earthing conductor installation, earth pit preparation, resistance measurement
  • Combiner box wiring — string combination, fuse sizing, overcurrent protection, proper labeling
  • Inverter installation — wall mounting, ventilation space, input/output terminal connections, cooling
  • Battery installation (if applicable) — placement, ventilation, terminal connections, cell balancing
  • Disconnect switches — DC disconnect, AC disconnect positioning, accessibility, labeling requirements
  • Cable management — conduit installation, cable tray usage, protection from rodents and weather
  • Safety protocols during installation — PPE usage, fall protection, electrical safety, tool safety, first aid

Module 5: Testing, Commissioning & Performance Monitoring

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pre-commissioning checks — visual inspection, continuity testing, insulation resistance testing
  • DC side testing — voltage measurement, current measurement, polarity verification, grounding resistance
  • AC side testing — voltage, frequency, phase sequence, earthing resistance, leakage current measurement
  • String testing — individual string performance, voltage and current under load conditions
  • Panel performance testing — I-V curve measurement, efficiency calculation, temperature coefficient verification
  • Inverter testing — startup sequence, input/output voltage verification, frequency check, error codes
  • Battery testing (if applicable) — voltage, capacity check, internal resistance measurement, charge/discharge cycles
  • Load testing — system performance under typical household or commercial loads
  • Grid synchronization testing — for grid-connected systems, frequency and phase matching
  • Performance monitoring setup — configuring monitoring apps, setting alerts, baseline data recording
  • Commissioning documentation — test reports, performance certificates, warranty registration
  • Customer handover — operation training, troubleshooting basics, maintenance schedule, emergency contacts

Module 6: Maintenance, Troubleshooting & Professional Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Preventive maintenance — cleaning schedules, inspection frequency, seasonal maintenance tasks
  • Panel cleaning — methods, frequency, water quality requirements, safety during cleaning on roofs
  • Inverter maintenance — cooling fan cleaning, firmware updates, capacitor life assessment
  • Battery maintenance — terminal cleaning, specific gravity checking (lead-acid), cell balancing (lithium)
  • Electrical system checks — connector inspection, cable condition, grounding continuity testing
  • Common faults and diagnosis — low output, inverter errors, battery issues, wiring problems
  • Troubleshooting tools — multimeters, clamp meters, solar analyzers, thermal imaging cameras
  • Repair procedures — connector replacement, cable repair, component replacement, safety protocols
  • Documentation and reporting — maintenance logs, service records, performance trends, customer reports
  • Safety in maintenance — lockout/tagout procedures, PPE requirements, electrical safety standards
  • Professional development — industry certifications, advanced training opportunities, career progression
  • Business aspects — cost estimation, service pricing, customer communication, quality assurance

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or 18 years for certain practical components involving heights)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (basic literacy in Hindi and English required)
  • No prior experience needed — open to all candidates
  • Already working in solar sector? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Driving License, Voter ID)
  • Physical fitness required for installation work (climbing heights, carrying equipment)
  • Medical fitness certificate recommended for candidates with health conditions
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals with valid visa

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand solar energy fundamentals and India's renewable energy policies
  • Identify and select appropriate solar PV components and system configurations
  • Conduct site assessments and design basic solar systems based on customer requirements
  • Install solar panels, wiring, inverters, and balance-of-system components safely
  • Test and commission solar systems according to Indian standards and safety regulations
  • Perform preventive maintenance and troubleshoot common faults in solar installations
