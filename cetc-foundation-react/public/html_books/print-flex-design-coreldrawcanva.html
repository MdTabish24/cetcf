Print & Flex Design (CorelDRAW/Canva)

📂 Sector: Media, Photography & Content Creation 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the art of creating stunning print and flex designs using industry-standard tools like CorelDRAW and Canva. This foundation course equips you with practical skills to design banners, pamphlets, business cards, and flex materials used by businesses across India. Start your career in the booming print media and advertising sector today.

Module 1: Design Fundamentals & Software Basics

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to Print & Flex Design industry in India — job market overview, current trends, and career opportunities in print shops, advertising agencies, and digital marketing firms
  • Design principles — composition, balance, contrast, emphasis, and alignment used in professional print materials
  • Color theory for print — understanding RGB vs CMYK, color psychology, and how colors impact print quality and brand identity
  • Typography fundamentals — font families, kerning, leading, font pairing, and how to choose fonts for different print materials
  • Introduction to CorelDRAW — interface navigation, toolbars, panels, workspace customization, and basic shortcuts for faster workflow
  • Introduction to Canva — account setup, template library, drag-and-drop interface, and when to use Canva vs CorelDRAW
  • File formats for print — understanding PDF, EPS, TIFF, PNG, and JPG; which formats to use for different printing purposes
  • Resolution and DPI — why 300 DPI is essential for print quality, understanding screen resolution vs print resolution, and avoiding pixelation
  • Bleed and margin setup — understanding safe zones, bleed areas, and crop marks critical for professional printing
  • Working with color profiles — setting up CMYK profiles, managing color accuracy, and avoiding color shifts during printing

Module 2: CorelDRAW Mastery for Print Design

📚 Topics Covered

  • Creating new documents — setting up correct page size, orientation, resolution, and color mode for Indian standard print sizes (A4, A3, A5, custom flex sizes)
  • Drawing tools — using pen tool, pencil tool, shape tools, and Bezier curves to create vector illustrations and design elements
  • Object manipulation — selecting, grouping, aligning, distributing objects with precision; using snap-to-grid and guides for accuracy
  • Text tools and formatting — adding text, formatting fonts, adjusting text properties, creating text boxes, and working with paragraph styles
  • Working with layers — organizing design elements in layers, managing layer visibility, and using layers for complex multi-element designs
  • Effects and filters — applying shadows, glows, bevels, and other effects; understanding when effects work in print vs screen
  • Creating custom shapes and logos — using shape tools to build brand logos, icons, and design elements from scratch
  • Working with imported images — placing images, cropping, adjusting transparency, and converting images to vector using tracing tools
  • Gradients and fills — creating linear and radial gradients, custom fills, pattern fills, and using them effectively in print designs
  • Advanced selection and editing — using node editing, converting objects to paths, and creating complex shapes through Boolean operations
  • Exporting for print — exporting to PDF with print-ready settings, creating color separations, and preparing files for commercial printing
  • Practical project — Design a professional business card (90x50mm) with company logo, contact details, and color variations

Module 3: Canva & Quick Design Solutions

📚 Topics Covered

  • Canva Pro features — understanding free vs Pro features, when to invest in Pro subscription, and maximizing template library access
  • Template customization — selecting appropriate templates, customizing colors, fonts, and layouts for brand consistency
  • Creating from scratch in Canva — using blank canvas, adding elements, uploading custom images, and building designs without templates
  • Canva's design elements — accessing stock photos, icons, illustrations, and design elements; understanding licensing for commercial use
  • Typography in Canva — font selection, text effects, and maintaining readability across different print materials
  • Creating multiple designs — batch creating variations of designs for A/B testing in marketing campaigns
  • Collaboration features — sharing designs with clients, getting feedback, and managing version control in team projects
  • Exporting from Canva — downloading in different formats (PDF, PNG, JPG), understanding quality settings, and preparing files for print
  • Canva limitations vs CorelDRAW — understanding when Canva is sufficient and when professional tools are needed for complex designs
  • Time management — using Canva for quick turnarounds, meeting tight deadlines, and balancing speed with quality
  • Cost-effective design solutions — using Canva for startups and small businesses with limited design budgets
  • Practical project — Create 5 social media graphics and 1 printable poster using Canva templates and custom elements

Module 4: Print Materials Design (Banners, Pamphlets, Flex)

📚 Topics Covered

  • Banner design specifications — understanding dimensions for different banner types (standees, hoardings, digital displays), Indian standard sizes, and safety margins
  • Flex banner design — working with flex material specifications, understanding durability requirements, color fastness, and UV resistance considerations
  • Creating eye-catching headlines — typography hierarchy, font sizing, color contrast, and ensuring readability from distance (for outdoor banners)
  • Pamphlet and brochure design — tri-fold and bi-fold layouts, page flow, content hierarchy, and designing for printing on A4 and A3 sizes
  • Business card design — understanding 90x50mm standard size, designing front and back, adding QR codes, and creating memorable card layouts
  • Flyer design — single-page promotional designs, effective use of white space, call-to-action placement, and designing for distribution
  • Poster design — creating high-impact visual designs for events, promotions, and awareness campaigns used in Indian markets
  • Label and sticker design — designing product labels, warning labels, and stickers with correct dimensions and material considerations
  • Envelope and letterhead design — corporate identity design, maintaining brand consistency, and designing complete stationery sets
  • Menu card design — designing menus for restaurants, cafes, and food delivery services with typography and food photography
  • Invitation card design — designing wedding, corporate, and event invitations following Indian design preferences and cultural elements
  • Practical project — Design a complete marketing package: banner (3x2 ft), pamphlet (tri-fold), and business card for an Indian retail business

Module 5: Brand Identity & Professional Portfolio Building

📚 Topics Covered

  • Brand identity fundamentals — understanding logo design, color palettes, typography systems, and creating consistent brand guidelines
  • Logo design process — brainstorming, sketching, digitizing, and refining logos using CorelDRAW for vector perfection
  • Creating brand guidelines — documenting logo usage, color codes (Pantone, CMYK, RGB), typography standards, and spacing rules
  • Color palette creation — selecting complementary colors, understanding cultural color meanings in Indian context, and creating versatile palettes
  • Typography systems — selecting primary and secondary fonts, creating font hierarchies, and ensuring readability across all materials
  • Mockup creation — using mockup templates to showcase designs on real products (banners on buildings, flex on vehicles, cards in hand)
  • Portfolio organization — selecting best work, presenting case studies, explaining design decisions, and creating a professional portfolio

  • Before-and-after presentations — comparing initial concepts with final designs to demonstrate design thinking and iteration process
  • Client testimonials and case studies — documenting project outcomes, client feedback, and measurable results (increased sales, brand awareness)
  • Pricing your work — understanding market rates in India (₹2,000-₹10,000 per design), calculating project costs, and creating quotations
  • Practical project — Create a complete brand identity for a fictional Indian startup including logo, color palette, typography system, and 3 sample applications

Module 6: Industry Practices, Software Tools & Career Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Print production workflow — understanding the journey from design to final print, working with printers, and quality control checkpoints
  • Working with print vendors — communicating specifications, understanding print costs, managing timelines, and handling revisions with printing partners
  • Pre-press requirements — preparing files for commercial printing, creating color separations, and ensuring print-ready specifications
  • Common printing issues and solutions — understanding registration, color bleeding, dot gain, and how to prevent these in design phase
  • Digital printing vs offset printing — understanding differences, cost implications, and when to recommend each method to clients
  • Eco-friendly design practices — using sustainable materials, reducing ink usage, and designing with environmental responsibility
  • Alternative software tools — exploring Adobe Illustrator alternatives, free tools like Inkscape, and choosing tools based on budget
  • Automation and batch processing — creating templates, using scripts, and automating repetitive design tasks to increase productivity
  • Freelancing fundamentals — finding clients on Fiverr, Upwork, and local Indian platforms; managing projects, invoicing, and building reputation
  • Building client relationships — communication skills, managing expectations, handling feedback, and ensuring client satisfaction for repeat business
  • Staying updated with design trends — following design blogs, attending workshops, and continuous learning in rapidly evolving design industry
  • Entrepreneurship in print design — starting a print design business, managing operations, scaling services, and building a team
  • Practical project — Complete real-world brief: Design a promotional campaign for an Indian restaurant including banner, menu card, flyer, and social media graphic

💼 Career Paths

Print Designer (In-house)
Work with advertising agencies, print shops, or design studios creating marketing materials, banners, and promotional designs. Typical roles in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, and other metro cities.
Freelance Print Designer
Work independently on Fiverr, Upwork, local Indian platforms, and direct clients. Build your own client base and scale as you grow. Flexibility in working hours and project selection.
Print Shop Owner/Operator
Start your own print design and production business. Partner with local printing vendors, manage client projects, and build a brand. High growth potential in tier-2 and tier-3 cities.
E-commerce Product Designer
Design product packaging, labels, and promotional materials for e-commerce businesses and startups. Work with companies like Amazon, Flipkart, or direct D2C brands.
Marketing & Advertising Specialist
Create marketing collaterals, social media graphics, and promotional designs for marketing teams in corporations, agencies, and startups. Hybrid role combining design and marketing.
Design Studio Co-founder
Partner with other designers to establish a full-service design studio offering print, digital, and branding services. Potential for rapid growth and employee management.
Corporate In-house Designer
Join large corporations, retail chains, or hospitality groups as in-house designer managing all print and visual communication needs. Stable role with benefits.
Design Trainer/Educator
After gaining experience, teach print design at vocational institutes, coaching centers, or online platforms. Create courses on Udemy, Skillshare, or YouTube.
