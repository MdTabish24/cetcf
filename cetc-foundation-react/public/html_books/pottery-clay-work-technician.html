Pottery & Clay Work Technician

📂 Sector: Handicrafts & Creative Arts 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the ancient art of pottery and clay work with modern techniques. This foundation course trains you to create beautiful handcrafted pottery items, from decorative pieces to functional tableware. Perfect for those wanting to start a career in India's thriving handicrafts sector or launch their own pottery business.

Module 1: Introduction to Pottery & Clay Materials

📚 Topics Covered

  • History of Indian pottery and its cultural significance — Understanding traditional pottery from Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Tamil Nadu
  • Types of clay and their properties — Earthenware, stoneware, and porcelain clay characteristics and uses
  • Clay sourcing and testing — How to identify quality clay, local suppliers in India, and clay testing methods
  • Workspace setup and safety — Pottery studio layout, ventilation requirements, and safety equipment needed
  • Basic pottery tools and equipment — Hand tools, pottery wheels, kilns, and their proper usage
  • Health and safety regulations — Dust protection, proper handling of chemicals, and workplace safety standards
  • Understanding clay consistency and plasticity — How to prepare clay for different techniques
  • Introduction to pottery terminology — Common terms used in pottery industry and workshops

Module 2: Clay Preparation & Hand-Building Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Clay preparation and wedging — Removing air bubbles, achieving proper consistency for different projects
  • Pinch pot technique — Creating small vessels using pinching method, suitable for beginners
  • Coil building method — Rolling and stacking clay coils to create larger vessels and decorative pieces
  • Slab construction — Creating flat clay slabs and assembling them for boxes, tiles, and wall pieces
  • Hand-building sculptural forms — Creating decorative animals, figurines, and artistic sculptures
  • Joining techniques and slip application — How to properly attach clay pieces using slip and scoring
  • Texture creation and surface decoration — Adding patterns using tools, stamps, and natural materials
  • Drying techniques and crack prevention — Proper drying methods to avoid warping and cracking
  • Trimming and finishing hand-built pieces — Smoothing edges and preparing pieces for glazing
  • Common mistakes and corrections — Identifying and fixing common hand-building errors

Module 3: Wheel Throwing & Centering Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pottery wheel operation and safety — Understanding wheel mechanics, speed control, and safety precautions
  • Centering clay on the wheel — Fundamental technique for achieving balanced, round forms
  • Opening the clay — Creating the interior space of vessels using thumb and fingers
  • Pulling walls and raising height — Gradually expanding clay walls to create tall forms
  • Creating different vessel shapes — Bowls, cylinders, vases, and plates using wheel techniques
  • Trimming the foot and base — Creating stable bases and decorative feet on thrown pieces
  • Throwing multiple pieces — Efficiency techniques for producing similar items
  • Troubleshooting wheel throwing problems — Fixing wobbling, collapsing, and uneven walls
  • Combining hand-building with wheel-thrown forms — Creating composite pieces
  • Production speed and consistency — Tips for faster, more consistent throwing
  • Creating lids and matching forms — Throwing lids that fit wheel-thrown vessels properly
  • Decorating thrown pieces on the wheel — Adding grooves, ridges, and patterns while spinning

Module 4: Glazing, Decoration & Surface Treatments

📚 Topics Covered

  • Understanding glazes and their chemistry — Basic glaze composition and how glazes work
  • Types of glazes used in Indian pottery — Matte, glossy, crackle, and specialty glazes
  • Glaze preparation and consistency — Mixing glazes, achieving proper thickness, and storage
  • Glaze application methods — Dipping, brushing, spraying, and pouring techniques
  • Color in pottery — Understanding oxides, underglazes, and achieving desired colors
  • Traditional Indian pottery designs and patterns — Madhubani, Warli, and regional design styles
  • Contemporary glaze techniques — Layering, blending, and creating special effects
  • Underglaze decoration and painting — Applying designs before final glaze coating
  • Sgraffito and incised decoration — Carving designs into leather-hard and dry clay
  • Creating special finishes — Burnishing, raku effects, and textured surfaces
  • Glaze safety and handling — Proper use of toxic materials and environmental considerations
  • Testing and experimentation — Creating glaze test tiles and maintaining glaze records

Module 5: Firing, Kiln Operation & Quality Control

📚 Topics Covered

  • Types of kilns and their operation — Electric, gas, and traditional Indian kilns
  • Kiln loading and stacking techniques — Maximizing kiln space safely and efficiently
  • Bisque firing process — First firing, temperature control, and timing
  • Glaze firing procedures — Second firing at different temperatures for different glazes
  • Temperature measurement and pyrometric cones — Understanding cone numbers and firing temperatures
  • Firing schedules and timing — Creating proper firing curves for different clay types
  • Cooling and unloading procedures — Safe removal of fired pieces and proper cooling
  • Common firing defects and solutions — Underfiring, overfiring, bloating, and cracking issues
  • Quality control and inspection standards — Checking for defects, uniformity, and market readiness
  • Environmental and safety regulations — Proper kiln ventilation and emission control
  • Maintaining kiln equipment — Regular maintenance and troubleshooting
  • Documenting firing records — Keeping records for consistency and improvement
  • Cost calculation for firing — Understanding fuel costs and efficiency

Module 6: Production, Marketing & Entrepreneurship

📚 Topics Covered

  • Production planning and workflow — Creating efficient production schedules and managing inventory
  • Costing and pricing pottery products — Calculating material, labor, and overhead costs
  • Quality assurance and standardization — Maintaining consistent quality across batches
  • Packaging and presentation — Proper packing techniques to prevent damage during shipping
  • Market trends in Indian pottery — Understanding current demand for traditional and modern designs
  • Selling pottery locally — Setting up at craft fairs, local markets, and exhibitions
  • Online selling platforms — Using Amazon Handmade, Etsy, Instagram, and WhatsApp for sales
  • Government schemes and subsidies — Accessing PMEGP, MUDRA loans, and handicraft grants
  • Starting a pottery business — Registration, licensing, and legal requirements in India
  • Building a pottery brand — Creating unique designs, developing brand identity and customer loyalty
  • Networking and collaboration — Joining pottery associations, craft cooperatives, and artist communities
  • Export opportunities — Understanding pottery export regulations and international markets
  • Customer service and feedback — Handling orders, complaints, and building repeat business
  • Documentation and record-keeping — Maintaining business accounts and GST compliance

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (school students can enroll with parental consent)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass (or equivalent)
  • No prior pottery experience required
  • Already working as potter? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid government ID proof required (Aadhar, PAN, or voter ID)
  • Medical fitness certificate for kiln operation roles
  • Applicable to all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals
  • Women candidates get priority in government schemes and subsidies

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Prepare clay properly and use hand-building techniques to create functional and decorative pottery pieces
  • Operate pottery wheel safely and throw centered, symmetrical vessels with consistent quality
  • Mix and apply glazes using traditional and contemporary methods to achieve desired colors and finishes
  • Operate kilns, manage firing schedules, and troubleshoot common firing defects
  • Identify quality standards, implement quality control, and maintain production consistency
  • Calculate product costs, price pottery competitively, and understand market trends in Indian handicrafts sector
  • Start and manage a pottery business, including online selling, branding, and accessing government support schemes
  • Apply safety procedures, maintain workshop hygiene, and comply with environmental regulations
