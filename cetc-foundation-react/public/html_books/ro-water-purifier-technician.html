RO Water Purifier Technician

📂 Sector: Plumbing, Sanitation & Water 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Learn to install, maintain, and repair Reverse Osmosis (RO) water purifiers—one of India's fastest-growing household appliances. This hands-on certification trains you to become a skilled technician capable of serving thousands of families across urban and semi-urban areas seeking clean drinking water solutions.

Module 1: Water Quality & Purification Fundamentals

📚 Topics Covered

  • Water contamination in India — common sources like bacteria, chemicals, heavy metals, and salts affecting Indian households
  • Water quality parameters — understanding TDS (Total Dissolved Solids), pH, hardness, turbidity, and conductivity with Indian water standards
  • Purification methods comparison — boiling, filtration, chlorination, UV, UF, and RO technology explained simply
  • How Reverse Osmosis (RO) works — membrane technology, pressure dynamics, and why RO is popular in India
  • RO vs other systems — advantages and disadvantages of RO, UF, and gravity-based filters in Indian context
  • Water testing basics — using TDS meters, pH strips, and understanding test results for customer confidence
  • Government water standards in India — Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) guidelines and IS 10500:2012 norms
  • Health benefits of pure water — connecting water quality to health issues common in India
  • Seasonal water quality changes — how monsoon and summer affect water contamination in different regions
  • Customer awareness — explaining water problems to customers in simple Hindi/local language
  • Water conservation through RO — managing waste water and recycling concepts
  • Regional water challenges — understanding water quality issues in different Indian states

Module 2: RO System Components & Assembly

📚 Topics Covered

  • RO system anatomy — detailed breakdown of sediment filter, carbon filter, RO membrane, storage tank, and faucet
  • Sediment filters (Pre-filter 1) — purpose, micron ratings, replacement frequency, and Indian brand specifications
  • Carbon filters (Pre-filter 2) — removing chlorine and organic compounds, lifespan, and cost-effective options
  • RO membrane — how it works, rejection rates (95-99%), lifespan (2-3 years), and maintenance tips
  • Storage tank types — plastic vs stainless steel, capacity options (10L-20L), and placement in Indian homes
  • Pressure gauge and regulator — maintaining 40-60 PSI, safety limits, and adjustment procedures
  • Auto shut-off valve — preventing water wastage when tank is full, common issues and fixes
  • Faucet and dispenser — installation, leakage prevention, and replacement with Indian market options
  • Pipe connections and fittings — quarter-inch tubing, connectors, adapters used in Indian RO systems
  • Mineralizer cartridge — optional add-on for adding essential minerals back to purified water
  • TDS controller — how it works, when to use it, and adjusting mineral content for taste
  • Pump and motor specifications — understanding wattage, voltage, and noise levels in Indian homes

Module 3: Installation & Setup Procedures

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pre-installation site assessment — checking water source, pressure, space, and electrical outlet availability
  • Measuring water pressure — using pressure gauges and identifying low-pressure issues common in Indian areas
  • Inlet water connection — tapping into kitchen pipe, using ball valves, and preventing cross-contamination
  • Mounting the system — wall-mounted vs under-sink installation, choosing locations in small Indian kitchens
  • Filter cartridge installation sequence — proper order of sediment, carbon, and RO membrane installation
  • Tank connection and placement — securing the storage tank, ensuring stability, and accessibility for cleaning
  • Outlet faucet installation — drilling holes in countertops, sealing, and ensuring proper water flow
  • Waste water drain connection — routing RO reject water to sink or garden, following plumbing standards
  • Electrical connection safety — grounding, voltage stabilizers, and protection from power fluctuations in India
  • System pressurization and testing — initial flushing, pressure checks, and water quality testing at installation
  • Customer handover — demonstrating operation, explaining maintenance, and providing warranty documentation
  • Documentation and record-keeping — maintaining service records and customer contact details for follow-ups

Module 4: Maintenance, Troubleshooting & Repairs

📚 Topics Covered

  • Regular maintenance schedule — filter replacement timeline (6-12 months), tank cleaning (6 months), and membrane care
  • Filter replacement procedure — safe removal, cartridge disposal, and installation of new filters without contamination
  • Tank cleaning and sanitization — disinfecting storage tanks quarterly to prevent bacterial growth and odor
  • Membrane flushing — maintaining RO membrane efficiency through proper flushing techniques and frequency
  • Common problems diagnosis — low flow, high TDS in output, leaks, noise, and taste issues with solutions
  • Low water pressure troubleshooting — identifying blockages, checking inlet valve, and addressing pressure-related issues
  • Membrane rejection problems — when TDS in output increases, indicating membrane degradation or bypass
  • Leakage detection and repair — identifying source of leaks (connections, tank, or membrane), tightening, or replacement
  • Noise and vibration issues — pump problems, loose connections, and water hammer prevention in Indian plumbing
  • Electrical troubleshooting — non-functioning pump, indicator light issues, and voltage stabilizer requirements
  • Water quality monitoring — regular TDS testing, explaining results to customers, and advising on filter changes
  • Service call management — responding to complaints, diagnosing issues quickly, and maintaining customer satisfaction

Module 5: Customer Service & Business Skills

📚 Topics Covered

  • Communication skills — explaining technical concepts in simple Hindi/regional language to non-technical customers
  • Building customer trust — honest assessment of water quality, transparent pricing, and warranty clarity
  • Handling complaints professionally — listening to customer issues, providing solutions, and follow-up service
  • Maintenance reminders — creating service reminders for filter replacement and tank cleaning to ensure repeat business
  • Pricing and quotation — understanding cost of filters, labor, and providing competitive quotes in different regions
  • Warranty and guarantee — explaining warranty terms, documenting purchases, and handling warranty claims fairly
  • Safety during service — wearing gloves, maintaining hygiene, protecting customer property, and safe waste disposal
  • Time management — completing installations efficiently, scheduling multiple appointments, and respecting customer time
  • Building referral network — encouraging word-of-mouth through quality service and offering referral incentives
  • Record-keeping and invoicing — maintaining service records, issuing proper invoices, and tracking customer data
  • Upselling complementary products — suggesting mineralizer, UV add-ons, or water testing services appropriately
  • Online presence basics — responding to calls, messages, and managing bookings through common Indian platforms

Module 6: Safety, Standards & Entrepreneurship

📚 Topics Covered

  • Workplace safety — handling tools safely, protecting from electrical hazards, and first-aid basics for minor injuries
  • Chemical safety — handling chlorine, cleaning agents, and disposal of filter cartridges responsibly
  • Water safety standards — BIS certification, NSF standards, and ensuring systems meet Indian regulatory requirements
  • Environmental responsibility — proper disposal of plastic cartridges, managing waste water, and sustainability practices
  • Tools and equipment — essential tools (wrenches, screwdrivers, pressure gauges, TDS meter), maintenance, and cost
  • Business registration — GST registration, local permits, and legal requirements for starting RO service business in India
  • Startup costs and profitability — initial investment (tools, inventory, transportation), profit margins, and break-even analysis
  • Building your service territory — identifying high-demand areas, door-to-door marketing, and establishing local presence
  • Franchise vs independent — understanding RO brand franchises available in India, support provided, and independence trade-offs
  • Growth strategies — expanding to multiple service areas, hiring additional technicians, and scaling the business
  • Digital tools for business — using WhatsApp for customer communication, online payment systems, and simple accounting apps
  • Continuous learning — staying updated with new RO models, attending manufacturer training, and improving skills

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or as per local regulations)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience required — ideal for fresh learners
  • Already working as RO technician? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) direct exam
  • Valid ID proof required — Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Driving License
  • Applicable for all candidates regardless of gender, caste, religion, or background
  • Medical fitness — ability to lift and carry (up to 15kg) required for installation work

  • Understand water quality parameters, contamination sources, and RO purification technology in Indian context
  • Identify and assemble all RO system components correctly with proper understanding of their functions
  • Install complete RO systems in residential settings following safety and quality standards
  • Perform routine maintenance including filter replacement and tank cleaning to ensure system longevity
  • Diagnose and troubleshoot common RO problems and implement effective solutions independently
  • Provide excellent customer service, manage business operations, and build a sustainable career in water purification sector
