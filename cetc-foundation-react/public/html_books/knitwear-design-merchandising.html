Knitwear Design & Merchandising

📂 Sector: Apparel Design, Pattern Making & Fashion Technology 📊 Level: Intermediate ⏱️ Duration: 3 Months 🕐 Hours: 180 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1200
Master the art and science of knitwear design and merchandising with this industry-focused certification course. Learn to create market-ready designs, manage production, and develop successful knitwear collections from concept to retail.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Knitwear & Textile Technology

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to knitwear industry in India — Market overview, growth statistics, major hubs (Tiruppur, Bangalore, Delhi NCR), export opportunities
  • Yarn structure and classification — Fibre types (cotton, polyester, wool, blends), yarn count, twist, ply, and their impact on fabric properties
  • Knitting fundamentals — Warp knitting vs weft knitting, plain knit, rib, purl, jacquard, and specialty stitches
  • Fabric construction and properties — Gauge, course, wales, density, weight, shrinkage, elasticity, and dimensional stability
  • Fabric testing and quality standards — GSM measurement, tensile strength, colour fastness, pilling resistance, shrinkage tests
  • Types of knitwear fabrics — Jersey, interlock, fleece, thermal, moisture-wicking, sustainable knits
  • Fibre content and sustainability — Organic cotton, recycled polyester, bamboo, hemp, and eco-friendly knitwear trends
  • Indian textile regulations and standards — BIS standards, GOTS certification, export compliance, labelling requirements

Module 2: Knitwear Design Principles & Sketching

📚 Topics Covered

  • Design fundamentals for knitwear — Silhouette, proportion, balance, visual hierarchy, and how knitwear differs from woven garments
  • Body measurement and fit — Standard sizing charts (XS to 5XL), Indian body measurements, ease allowances for knits, fit testing
  • Garment design sketching — Technical drawing, front/back views, flat sketches, detailed specification sheets using Adobe Illustrator or CorelDRAW
  • Colour theory and palettes — Seasonal colour trends, Indian market preferences, colour combinations for knitwear, trend forecasting
  • Pattern design for knits — Drafting basics, dart placement, neckline variations, sleeve types, waistband and cuff design
  • Design inspiration and mood boards — Creating mood boards, trend research, competitor analysis, Pinterest and fashion blogs
  • Design specifications document — Detailed specs including measurements, seam allowances, stitch types, fabric weight, care instructions
  • Software for design — Introduction to Adobe Creative Suite, CAD software (Gerber, Lectra), digital sampling tools
  • Embellishments and details — Embroidery on knits, appliqué, beading, printing on knits, and their production feasibility
  • Design for different seasons — T-shirts, tanks, hoodies, cardigans, sweaters, thermal wear, seasonal adaptations

Module 3: Pattern Making & Grading for Knitwear

📚 Topics Covered

  • Pattern making basics — Block patterns, measurement conversion, seam allowances specific to knits (0.5 inches typically)
  • Creating basic blocks — T-shirt block, round neck block, V-neck block, polo block, sweatshirt block from measurements
  • Sleeve design and construction — Set-in sleeves, raglan sleeves, kimono sleeves, cap sleeves, sleeve length variations
  • Collar and neckline details — Ribbed collars, crew necks, V-necks, henley necklines, hood construction
  • Waistband and cuff construction — Ribbed waistbands, elastic casings, drawstring details, cuff variations
  • Grading methodology — Incremental grading, proportional grading, grading for knits vs wovens, size progression
  • Creating size runs — XS to 5XL grading, creating grade rules, maintaining fit across sizes
  • Pattern nesting and fabric efficiency — Optimal pattern layout, waste reduction, marker planning for production
  • Manual pattern making tools — Pattern paper, rulers, curves, compass, measuring tapes
  • Digital pattern making — Using Gerber Accumark, Lectra Kaledo, and other CAD systems for professional pattern development
  • Quality checks on patterns — Seam accuracy, measurement verification, fit testing on toiles

Module 4: Garment Construction & Quality Control

📚 Topics Covered

  • Knitwear production process — Knitting, dyeing, finishing, cutting, sewing, pressing, packaging workflow
  • Cutting and lay planning — Spreading fabric, cutting efficiency, marker making, waste management, quality checks
  • Sewing techniques for knits — Flatlock seams, cover stitch, serger seaming, rolled hems, twin needle stitching
  • Seam types and their applications — Butt seams, overlapped seams, safety stitch, 4-thread serger, seam strength requirements
  • Needle and thread selection — Needle types for knits, thread weights, polyester vs cotton thread, tension settings
  • Pressing and finishing — Steam pressing, flat pressing, blocking, shrinkage control, final inspection
  • Quality standards in knitwear — Workmanship standards, stitch length, seam strength, defect classification (critical, major, minor)
  • Inspection procedures — In-process inspection, final inspection, AQL (Acceptable Quality Level) standards, sampling plans
  • Common defects and solutions ��� Pilling, snags, loose threads, uneven stitching, colour variations, shrinkage issues
  • Testing and compliance — Strength testing, colour fastness, shrinkage testing, chemical testing, safety compliance
  • Documentation and traceability — Quality records, batch tracking, supplier compliance, customer feedback management

Module 5: Merchandising & Collection Development

📚 Topics Covered

  • Merchandising fundamentals — Understanding customer needs, market segmentation, target audience analysis, price positioning
  • Collection planning — Seasonal collections (Spring/Summer, Fall/Winter), trend forecasting, design direction setting
  • Range building — Creating balanced product mix, core items vs trend items, colour stories, style variations
  • Costing and pricing — Material costs, labour costs, overhead allocation, profit margins, competitive pricing analysis
  • Cost sheet preparation — Detailed cost breakdown, supplier quotes, duty calculations, landed cost for imports
  • Fabric sourcing and supplier management — Finding reliable suppliers, quality negotiations, lead time management, payment terms
  • Production planning — Quantity forecasting, lead time calculation, production scheduling, inventory management
  • Vendor management — Supplier evaluation, quality agreements, delivery commitments, relationship building
  • Trend analysis and forecasting — Fashion forecasting websites, colour prediction services, lifestyle trends, Indian market insights
  • Retail strategy — Wholesale vs retail, distribution channels, retail pricing, markdown planning, inventory turnover
  • Marketing and brand positioning — Creating brand identity, social media strategy, influencer partnerships, seasonal campaigns

Module 6: Professional Practice & Industry Application

📚 Topics Covered

  • Industry standards and certifications — GOTS, OEKO-TEX, Fair Trade, SA8000, compliance requirements for exports
  • Sustainability in knitwear — Circular economy, waste reduction, water conservation, chemical management, ethical sourcing
  • Digital tools and software — Trend forecasting platforms (WGSN, Trendstop), design software, PLM systems, communication tools
  • Portfolio development — Building a professional design portfolio, case studies, presenting work to clients and manufacturers
  • Client communication — Presenting design concepts, managing feedback, revisions, understanding client briefs
  • Production documentation — Technical specifications, measurement charts, care labels, packaging requirements, shipping marks
  • Export procedures and documentation — Invoices, packing lists, certificates of origin, customs documentation, shipping logistics
  • Career pathways and entrepreneurship — Job opportunities in design studios, manufacturing units, retail brands, freelancing options
  • Capstone project — Developing a complete knitwear collection (minimum 8-10 designs) with specifications, costing, and marketing strategy
  • Industry visits and guest lectures — Learning from experienced designers, manufacturers, and merchandisers working in Indian knitwear sector
  • Professional networking — Industry events, trade shows (like Texfair), professional associations, online communities

  • Basic computer knowledge helpful but not mandatory (training provided)

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand knitwear fabric properties, yarn structures, and textile technology relevant to Indian manufacturing
  • Create professional knitwear designs with technical sketches, specifications, and digital tools
  • Develop accurate patterns, size grading, and specification sheets for production
  • Manage quality control processes and ensure compliance with international standards
  • Plan and execute merchandising strategies including costing, pricing, and collection development
  • Build professional portfolios and apply industry knowledge to real-world projects
