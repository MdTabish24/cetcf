Module 1: Fundamentals of Professional House Cleaning

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to house cleaning profession in India — job market overview, demand in cities like Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Pune
  • Types of cleaning services — regular cleaning, deep cleaning, post-construction cleaning, eco-friendly cleaning
  • Cleaning industry standards and best practices — Indian household norms and international standards
  • Health and safety fundamentals — personal protective equipment (PPE), ergonomics, injury prevention
  • Understanding cleaning terminology — surface types, cleaning methods, chemical categories
  • Importance of hygiene and sanitation — preventing disease transmission, maintaining healthy homes
  • Professional attitude and work ethics — punctuality, reliability, confidentiality, respect for client privacy
  • Customer communication basics — greeting clients, understanding requirements, handling complaints professionally

Module 2: Cleaning Chemicals and Equipment Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Types of cleaning chemicals — detergents, disinfectants, degreasers, acid cleaners, alkaline cleaners
  • Reading and understanding chemical labels — safety symbols, dilution ratios, warnings, expiry dates
  • Proper storage and handling of chemicals — ventilation requirements, container labeling, preventing contamination
  • Mixing chemicals safely — correct proportions, avoiding dangerous combinations (bleach + ammonia), temperature considerations
  • Essential cleaning equipment — mops, brushes, brooms, vacuum cleaners, microfiber cloths, squeegees
  • Maintenance of cleaning tools — cleaning, drying, storage, extending tool lifespan
  • Cost-effective cleaning solutions — making homemade cleaners using vinegar, baking soda, lemon for eco-friendly approach
  • Waste management and disposal — segregating waste, disposing chemicals responsibly, environmental consciousness
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE) — gloves, masks, aprons, shoes, eye protection, proper wearing and removal
  • Equipment operation — using vacuum cleaners, steam cleaners, pressure washers safely and effectively

  • Ceiling and wall cleaning — removing cobwebs, cleaning wall marks, maintaining painted surfaces

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (with parental consent if under 18)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior cleaning experience required
  • Already working as a house cleaner? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam for direct certification
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhar, PAN, Voter ID, or Driving License)
  • Physically fit to perform cleaning tasks
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and migrant workers with valid documentation
