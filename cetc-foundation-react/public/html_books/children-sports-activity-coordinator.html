Children Sports Activity Coordinator

📂 Sector: Sports, Fitness & Recreation 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1200
This foundation-level course trains you to become a skilled Children Sports Activity Coordinator who can organize, manage, and conduct safe sports activities for children aged 4-16 years. You will learn child development, sports safety protocols, activity planning, and how to create inclusive, engaging sports environments in schools, clubs, and community centers across India.

Module 1: Child Development, Psychology & Safety Basics

📚 Topics Covered

  • Child development stages (4-6, 7-9, 10-12, 13-16 years) — physical, cognitive, emotional milestones and how they affect sports participation
  • Child psychology and learning behavior — motivation, fear, confidence building, and attention span in sports activities
  • Child safety regulations in India — National Sports Policy guidelines, school safety norms, and duty of care responsibilities
  • Recognizing child abuse and neglect — signs, reporting procedures, and mandatory disclosure requirements under Indian law
  • First aid basics for children — CPR, choking, fractures, sprains, heat exhaustion, and emergency response procedures
  • Infection control and hygiene standards — preventing disease spread, sanitization of equipment, and health screening protocols
  • Special needs awareness — understanding disabilities, inclusive sports practices, and adapting activities for all children
  • Building trust and rapport with children — communication techniques, positive reinforcement, and creating safe spaces
  • Parental communication and consent — documentation, liability waivers, and maintaining confidentiality in Indian schools
  • Legal responsibilities and insurance — understanding liability, accident documentation, and institutional requirements

Module 2: Sports Activity Planning, Organization & Coordination

📚 Topics Covered

  • Activity planning fundamentals — needs assessment, goal setting, age-appropriate selection, and skill progression
  • Creating activity schedules — time management, seasonal planning, school calendars, and event calendars in Indian context
  • Venue and equipment management — assessing spaces, equipment inventory, maintenance logs, safety checks, and storage
  • Organizing sports days and inter-school competitions — registration, scheduling, rules enforcement, and result management
  • Budget planning for sports activities — cost estimation, fund allocation, vendor management, and financial accountability
  • Participant registration and documentation — enrollment forms, medical clearance, emergency contacts, and data management
  • Coordinating with schools and institutions — liaison with principals, teachers, parents, and obtaining approvals
  • Risk assessment and contingency planning — identifying hazards, creating backup plans, and handling weather disruptions
  • Volunteer and staff coordination — recruiting, training, assigning roles, and supervision during activities
  • Post-activity evaluation — feedback collection, performance analysis, and improvement planning for next sessions
  • Digital tools for coordination — using spreadsheets, simple apps, and communication platforms for activity management
  • Compliance documentation — maintaining records, incident reports, attendance sheets, and institutional requirements

Module 3: Sports, Games & Physical Activities for Children

📚 Topics Covered

  • Fundamental movement skills — running, jumping, throwing, catching, kicking, balance, and coordination exercises
  • Traditional Indian games — Kabaddi, Kho-Kho, Lagori, Ludo-physical, Pitthu, and their educational benefits
  • Team sports basics — football, basketball, volleyball, cricket, handball, and basic rules for children
  • Individual sports — athletics (running, jumping, throwing), badminton, table tennis, swimming basics
  • Racquet sports — tennis, badminton fundamentals, grip techniques, and age-appropriate drills
  • Combat sports introduction — basic martial arts, judo, taekwondo fundamentals, and safety protocols
  • Dance and rhythmic activities — folk dances, contemporary dance, aerobics, and movement to music for children
  • Fitness and conditioning activities — stretching, strength building, endurance training, and age-appropriate exercises
  • Water-based activities — swimming basics, water safety, floating techniques, and aquatic games for non-swimmers
  • Adventure and outdoor activities — nature walks, obstacle courses, rope activities (age-appropriate), and outdoor games
  • Adapted games for inclusion — modifications for different abilities, equipment adaptations, and inclusive rule variations
  • Game rules, scoring, and officiating — understanding basic rules, teaching fair play, and simple refereeing for children

Module 4: Coaching Fundamentals & Activity Delivery

📚 Topics Covered

  • Basic coaching principles — understanding learner needs, progressive skill development, and feedback techniques
  • Demonstration and explanation techniques — clear instructions, visual modeling, using simple language, and repetition
  • Warm-up and cool-down practices — injury prevention, physiological preparation, and recovery activities
  • Skill progression and drills — breaking down complex skills, practice drills, and progression from basic to advanced
  • Motivation and engagement strategies — positive reinforcement, goal setting, reward systems, and maintaining interest
  • Handling behavior and discipline — positive discipline, setting boundaries, addressing misbehavior, and conflict resolution
  • Dealing with performance anxiety and fear — building confidence, managing nervousness, and creating supportive environment
  • Providing constructive feedback — specific, actionable feedback, positive sandwich technique, and individual coaching
  • Group management during activities — organizing groups, managing transitions, keeping everyone engaged, and time management
  • Adapting activities on the spot — reading the group, modifying difficulty, addressing boredom, and maintaining safety
  • Creating fun and engaging sessions — using games, variety, music, storytelling, and making learning enjoyable
  • Building team cohesion — ice-breakers, team-building activities, fostering friendship, and creating inclusive environment

Module 5: Health, Nutrition & Wellness for Young Athletes

📚 Topics Covered

  • Child nutrition basics — balanced diet, food groups, hydration, and age-appropriate caloric needs for active children
  • Sports nutrition for young athletes — pre-activity meals, post-activity recovery foods, and energy requirements
  • Common nutritional deficiencies in Indian children — iron, calcium, vitamin D, and addressing through diet
  • Hydration during sports — water intake guidelines, signs of dehydration, and preventing heat-related illnesses
  • Growth and development during sports training — understanding growth spurts, avoiding overtraining, and age-appropriate intensity
  • Preventing sports injuries in children — warm-up importance, proper technique, appropriate progression, and equipment use
  • Common childhood sports injuries — sprains, strains, fractures, and basic management and prevention
  • Overuse injuries and burnout prevention — recognizing signs, managing training load, and rest periods
  • Mental health and sports — stress management, building resilience, handling pressure, and recognizing mental health issues
  • Sleep and recovery for young athletes — sleep requirements, importance of rest days, and recovery practices
  • Screening and health assessment — pre-participation evaluation, medical clearance, and identifying health risks
  • Substance abuse awareness — educating children about harmful substances, peer pressure, and healthy choices

Module 6: Professional Development, Ethics & Career Building

📚 Topics Covered

  • Professional ethics in sports — integrity, fairness, respect for all, and maintaining professional boundaries
  • Child safeguarding and protection — mandatory reporting, background checks, appropriate conduct, and creating safe spaces
  • Code of conduct for sports coordinators — professional behavior, dress code, punctuality, and conduct expectations
  • Continuing education and skill development — staying updated, attending workshops, certifications, and professional growth
  • Building your professional network — connecting with coaches, schools, sports clubs, and creating opportunities
  • Resume building and job applications — highlighting skills, experience, and applying for sports coordinator positions
  • Interview preparation — common questions, demonstrating knowledge, and presenting yourself professionally
  • Starting your own sports activity business — registration, insurance, marketing, and building client base in your area
  • Understanding employment in Indian schools and institutions — contract types, salary structures, and job security
  • Technology in sports coordination — using apps for scheduling, communication, performance tracking, and online coaching
  • Diversity, inclusion, and cultural sensitivity — respecting differences, adapting to local cultures, and inclusive practices
  • Feedback, evaluation, and self-improvement — seeking feedback, personal reflection, and continuous professional development

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (or 12 years with parental consent for school-based programs)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior sports experience required
  • Physical fitness: Ability to participate in sports activities and demonstrate movements
  • Already working as a sports coordinator? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Driving License, or Passport)
  • Medical fitness certificate recommended (especially if you have health conditions)
  • Applicable for all candidates regardless of gender, caste, religion, or background

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand child development stages and psychology to design age-appropriate sports activities
  • Plan, organize, and coordinate safe, engaging sports activities for children in various settings
  • Deliver effective coaching and instruction using proper demonstration and communication techniques
  • Implement child safety protocols, first aid, and safeguarding measures in sports programs
  • Manage sports equipment, venues, and resources effectively with proper documentation
  • Build inclusive sports environments that accommodate children with diverse abilities and backgrounds
  • Evaluate activity effectiveness and continuously improve sports programs based on feedback
  • Demonstrate professional ethics, maintain confidentiality, and uphold duty of care responsibilities
