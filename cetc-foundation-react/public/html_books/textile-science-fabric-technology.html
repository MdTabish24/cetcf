Textile Science & Fabric Technology

📂 Sector: Apparel Design, Pattern Making & Fashion Technology 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the fundamentals of textile science and fabric technology to build a strong foundation in the apparel industry. This course covers fiber properties, fabric construction, quality testing, and dyeing processes essential for anyone entering fashion and textile manufacturing. Gain hands-on skills that directly connect to real factory and design studio work across India's thriving textile sector.

Module 1: Introduction to Textile Fibers & Raw Materials

📚 Topics Covered

  • Fiber classification: Natural fibers (cotton, wool, silk, jute) and synthetic fibers (polyester, nylon, acrylic) — understanding origin and properties
  • Cotton fiber structure and properties — staple length, fineness, strength, and why it's India's most used fiber
  • Wool and animal fibers — characteristics, grading systems, and Indian wool production regions
  • Silk production process — sericulture in India, fiber extraction, and luxury applications
  • Synthetic and man-made fibers — polyester, nylon, acrylic properties and blending advantages
  • Fiber identification tests — burn test, microscopy, chemical tests used in Indian textile labs
  • Fiber properties measurement — strength, elasticity, moisture regain, and thermal properties
  • Fiber blending principles — why cotton-polyester blends dominate Indian apparel manufacturing
  • Raw material sourcing in India — major cotton-producing states, synthetic fiber imports, and cost factors
  • Sustainability in fiber production — organic cotton, recycled fibers, and eco-friendly alternatives gaining market share

Module 2: Yarn Formation & Spinning Technology

📚 Topics Covered

  • Fiber to yarn journey — carding, combing, drawing, twisting processes in spinning mills
  • Count system understanding — Ne (English count), Denier, Tex systems and conversion between them
  • Yarn twist and its effects — S-twist vs Z-twist, twist direction impact on fabric properties and appearance
  • Yarn types in Indian apparel — single yarn, ply yarn, textured yarn, and specialty yarns
  • Ring spinning vs. Open-end spinning — production differences, quality comparison, and cost implications
  • Rotor spinning technology — advantages for mass production in Indian mills, common in t-shirt manufacturing
  • Yarn quality parameters — evenness, strength, elongation, imperfections, and testing methods
  • Yarn hairiness and its control — importance in fabric smoothness and appearance
  • Specialty yarns — slub yarn, nep yarn, and their use in trendy Indian fashion collections
  • Yarn packaging and storage — proper handling to prevent contamination and maintain quality in warehouses
  • Yarn dyeing vs. fabric dyeing ��� process differences and impact on final color fastness and cost
  • Common yarn defects — causes, detection, and quality control measures in spinning mills

Module 3: Fabric Construction & Weaving Technology

📚 Topics Covered

  • Loom basics — understanding warp, weft, reed, healds, and shuttle in traditional and modern looms
  • Plain weave construction — 1/1 interlacing, fabric properties, and use in everyday Indian garments
  • Twill weave — 2/1, 2/2 patterns, diagonal appearance, strength characteristics, and denim production
  • Satin and sateen weaves — float yarns, lustrous appearance, and use in formal wear and home textiles
  • Jacquard weaving — complex pattern creation, use in Indian traditional wear and home furnishings
  • Dobby weaving — small geometric patterns, production efficiency, and applications in Indian cotton fabrics
  • Pile weaves — velvet, corduroy production, and luxury fabric manufacturing
  • Fabric GSM (grams per square meter) — weight measurement, relationship to fabric thickness and durability
  • Fabric count — warp count and weft count per inch, impact on fabric density and strength
  • EPI (Ends Per Inch) and PPI (Picks Per Inch) — density calculations and quality specifications
  • Loom efficiency and production — productivity metrics in Indian textile mills, shift-based manufacturing
  • Woven fabric defects — missing yarns, slubs, holes, and quality inspection procedures

Module 4: Knitting Technology & Fabric Formation

📚 Topics Covered

  • Knitting fundamentals — loop formation, yarn consumption, and elasticity advantages over woven fabrics
  • Weft knitting — circular knit, flat knit machines, and production in Indian knitwear factories
  • Warp knitting — tricot and raschel machines, applications in technical textiles and hosiery
  • Jersey fabric — basic knit structure, commonly used in t-shirts and casual wear across India
  • Rib knit — 1x1, 2x2 structures, elasticity, and use in cuffs, collars, and waistbands
  • Pique knit — textured surface, breathability, and premium t-shirt production
  • Interlock knit — dense structure, comfort, and use in premium baby wear and sportswear
  • Yarn feeding systems — precision feeding to prevent loops and ensure uniform fabric
  • Machine gauge — relationship to yarn fineness and fabric properties (fine gauge = delicate fabric)
  • Knit fabric GSM and course count — measurements specific to knitted structures
  • Common knit defects — dropped stitches, snags, holes, and quality control in knitting mills
  • Finishing requirements for knits — relaxation, stabilization, and shrinkage control

Module 5: Dyeing, Printing & Fabric Finishing Processes

📚 Topics Covered

  • Dye classification — acid dyes, reactive dyes, disperse dyes, and vat dyes used in Indian textile industry
  • Reactive dyes — most common for cotton in India, bright colors, good fastness properties
  • Disperse dyes — use on synthetic fibers, sublimation printing on polyester garments
  • Vat dyes — indigo dyeing for denim production, traditional Indian indigo heritage
  • Dyeing processes — exhaust dyeing, pad dyeing, jet dyeing, and vat dyeing in commercial mills
  • Batch dyeing vs. continuous dyeing — production volumes, cost, and quality consistency
  • Color fastness testing — washing, rubbing, light fastness standards required by Indian exporters
  • Printing technology — screen printing, digital printing, rotary printing methods and applications
  • Screen printing process — design separation, mesh count, ink chemistry, and use in Indian fashion brands
  • Digital textile printing — direct-to-fabric technology, advantages in small batch production and customization
  • Block printing — traditional Indian technique, revival in handloom and designer collections
  • Discharge and resist printing — techniques for creating white patterns on colored fabrics
  • Finishing processes — sizing, softening, water repellent, flame retardant finishes for different applications
  • Shrinkage control and sanforization — importance for garment fit and customer satisfaction
  • Perfuming and fragrance finishing — luxury finishing for premium Indian apparel brands
  • Sustainable dyeing practices — water reduction, natural dyes, and eco-friendly processes gaining adoption

Module 6: Fabric Testing, Quality Control & Industry Standards

📚 Topics Covered

  • Quality control importance — preventing defects, maintaining brand reputation, meeting export standards
  • Fabric inspection methods — visual inspection, automated defect detection systems in modern Indian mills
  • Tensile strength testing — breaking strength, elongation measurement using Universal Testing Machine (UTM)
  • Tear strength testing — importance for garment durability and longevity
  • Pilling resistance — Martindale test, preventing fabric surface degradation during washing
  • Shrinkage testing — dimensional stability after washing, critical for garment fit
  • Color fastness to washing — standard procedures, importance for dark colors and Indian market demands
  • Color fastness to rubbing — dry and wet rubbing, preventing color transfer to other garments
  • Color fastness to light — sunlight exposure resistance, important for outdoor wear in Indian climate
  • GSM (weight) testing — precision measurement using calibrated scales and standard procedures
  • Fabric count verification — checking warp and weft density against specifications
  • Thickness and compressibility — measuring fabric bulk and comfort properties
  • Moisture regain testing — fiber absorption capacity, important for comfort in Indian humid climate
  • Pore size and air permeability — breathability testing for summer wear and sportswear
  • Water absorption and repellency — testing for rain wear and technical textiles
  • ISI (Indian Standards Institute) requirements — understanding Indian fabric standards for domestic market
  • International standards — ASTM, ISO standards for export-quality apparel and textiles
  • Third-party testing labs in India — SITRA (Coimbatore), NITRA (Mumbai), and other accredited facilities
  • Documentation and quality reports — maintaining records for traceability and compliance
  • Cost-quality balance — optimizing fabric specifications within budget constraints for different market segments
