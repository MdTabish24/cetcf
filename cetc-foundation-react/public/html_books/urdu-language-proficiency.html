Urdu Language Proficiency

📂 Sector: Spoken Languages & Communication 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master Urdu language from scratch with this foundation-level course designed for Indian learners. This program teaches you spoken Urdu, basic writing, and practical communication skills needed for daily interactions, customer service, and professional environments across India's diverse regions.

Module 1: Introduction to Urdu Script & Alphabet

📚 Topics Covered

  • Urdu language history and significance in India — Understanding Urdu's role in Indian culture, literature, and commerce
  • Nastaliq and Naskh script styles — Difference between formal and casual Urdu writing styles
  • Urdu alphabet (Abjad) — Learning 28 letters of Urdu alphabet with correct pronunciation
  • Letter shapes: initial, medial, and final forms — How Urdu letters change shape based on position in words
  • Diacritical marks (Aeraab) — Understanding dots, dashes, and vowel marks used in Urdu
  • Numbers in Urdu script — Writing and pronouncing Urdu numerals 0-10 and their usage
  • Basic punctuation in Urdu — Comma, full stop, question mark, and exclamation marks in Urdu writing
  • Hands-on practice: Writing your name in Urdu — Personal script practice with correct letter formation
  • Common Urdu words recognition — Identifying 25-30 frequently used words in daily life
  • Pronunciation drills — Audio-based exercises for correct Urdu phonetics

Module 2: Phonetics, Pronunciation & Listening Skills

📚 Topics Covered

  • Urdu phonetic system — Understanding unique Urdu sounds not present in Hindi or English
  • Consonant sounds (Huroof-e-Sakit) — Correct articulation of Urdu consonants like 'Kh', 'Gh', 'Kaf', 'Qaf'
  • Vowel sounds (Huroof-e-Mut-harrik) — Short vowels (Zabar, Zer, Pesh) and long vowels in Urdu
  • Nasalization and emphasis — Understanding Urdu nasal sounds and emphatic consonants
  • Listening comprehension basics — Training ear to distinguish similar Urdu sounds
  • Audio-visual learning — Watching native speakers and mimicking pronunciation
  • Stress and intonation patterns — How stress changes meaning in Urdu sentences
  • Common pronunciation mistakes — Typical errors made by Hindi/English speakers learning Urdu
  • Speed listening exercises — Slow, medium, and normal-speed Urdu audio clips
  • Dialogue listening practice — Understanding conversations between native speakers
  • Accent training for Indian Urdu speakers — Regional variations in Urdu across India

Module 3: Basic Grammar & Sentence Structure

📚 Topics Covered

  • Parts of speech in Urdu — Nouns (Naam), Verbs (Feal), Adjectives (Sifat), Pronouns (Zamir)
  • Nouns and gender classification — Understanding masculine and feminine nouns in Urdu
  • Singular and plural forms — Rules for making plurals in Urdu (regular and irregular)
  • Personal pronouns — Main, Tu, Woh, Hum, Ap, Yeh, Woh and their usage
  • Basic verb conjugation — Present tense (Hal) verb forms with different subjects
  • Simple sentence structure — Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) pattern in Urdu
  • Positive and negative sentences — Adding 'Nahi' (not) to make negative statements
  • Question formation — Using 'Kya', 'Kaun', 'Kahan', 'Kyun' to ask questions
  • Adjectives and their agreement — Matching adjectives with noun gender and number
  • Prepositions in Urdu — Common prepositions like 'Mein', 'Par', 'Ke Liye', 'Se'
  • Basic sentence practice — Creating 20-30 simple sentences with correct grammar

Module 4: Everyday Conversational Urdu

📚 Topics Covered

  • Greetings and polite expressions — Assalam-u-Alaikum, Adab, Khuda Hafiz, Shukriya
  • Self-introduction phrases — Mera Naam Hai..., Main... Se Hoon, Mera Kam Hai...
  • Asking personal questions — Aapka Naam Kya Hai, Aap Kahan Se Hain, Aapka Kam Kya Hai
  • Family and relationships vocabulary — Father (Baap), Mother (Maan), Brother (Bhai), Sister (Behen)
  • Daily routine conversations — Subah Jaldi Uthna, Kaam Karna, Khana Khana, Sona
  • Food and dining expressions — Khana Pasand Hai, Paani Chahiye, Chai Banao, Namkin/Meetha
  • Shopping and bargaining phrases — Kya Rate Hai, Sasta Karo, Kitne Ka Hai, Paise Wapas Karo
  • Asking for directions — Yeh Jagah Kahan Hai, Rasta Batao, Kaun Si Bus Jaati Hai
  • Time and calendar in Urdu — Ghante, Din, Mahina, Saal, Aaj, Kal, Parson
  • Weather and seasonal vocabulary — Garmi, Sardi, Barsat, Tez Hawa, Dhoop
  • Health and body parts — Sar, Pait, Paon, Hath, Bimari, Dard, Dawai
  • Telephone and communication phrases — Hello Kaho, Naam Batao, Wapas Call Karo, Message Bhejo

Module 5: Professional & Customer Service Communication

📚 Topics Covered

  • Workplace vocabulary — Office (Daftar), Boss (Malik/Saab), Colleague (Saathi), Work (Kaam), Salary (Talab)
  • Job-related conversations — Mujhe Ye Job Chahiye, Mera Experience Hai, Mujhe Ye Skill Ata Hai
  • Customer service basics — Customer (Mushteri), Problem (Mushkil), Solution (Hal), Complaint (Shikayat)
  • Welcoming customers in Urdu — Khush Amdeed, Aiye Baithiye, Kya Chahiye, Kya Madad Chahiye
  • Handling complaints professionally — Mujhe Maafi Chahiye, Hum Theek Kar Denge, Aapki Baat Bilkul Sahi Hai
  • Transaction and payment language — Paise Dena, Paisa Lena, Receipt Dena, Baaki, Thik Hai
  • Hotel and hospitality phrases — Room, Khana, Breakfast, Checkout, Khidmat (Service)
  • Email and written communication basics — Formal greetings in Urdu writing, closing phrases
  • Telephone etiquette in Urdu — Professional tone, taking messages, scheduling appointments
  • Conflict resolution phrases — Samajhiye, Hum Aapki Madad Karenge, Aapka Masla Hum Hal Karenge
  • Negotiation and discussion — Kya Hum Baat Kar Sakte Hain, Aapka Kya Khayal Hai, Mera Sujhao Hai
  • Feedback and appreciation — Shukriya, Bahut Acha, Aapka Kaam Behtareen Hai, Mujhe Pasand Aya

Module 6: Reading, Writing & Cultural Competence

📚 Topics Covered

  • Reading simple Urdu texts — Menus, signs, notices, advertisements in Urdu script
  • Writing basic messages — SMS, WhatsApp messages, simple notes in Urdu
  • Common Urdu phrases and idioms — Samajhna (understand), Yaad Rakhna (remember), Dhyan Dena (pay attention)
  • Poetry and literary Urdu — Understanding famous couplets (Shayari), Mirza Ghalib, Iqbal references
  • Urdu cultural context — Understanding Islamic and Indo-Muslim cultural references in language
  • Regional variations in Urdu — Differences between Delhi Urdu, Lucknow Urdu, Hyderabad Urdu
  • Formal vs informal speech — Respectful language (Aap) vs casual language (Tu), formal letter writing
  • Numbers, dates, and time in written form — Writing dates in Urdu, formal number representation
  • Common abbreviations and acronyms in Urdu — Modern usage in digital communication
  • Practical writing exercises — Composing short paragraphs, filling forms, writing simple letters
