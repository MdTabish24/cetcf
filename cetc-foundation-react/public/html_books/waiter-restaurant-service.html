Waiter & Restaurant Service

📂 Sector: Food, Catering & Hospitality 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Learn professional waiter and restaurant service skills to work in hotels, restaurants, and catering establishments across India. This 1-month foundation course covers table service, customer interaction, food safety, and hospitality standards that are in high demand in India's growing food service industry.

Module 1: Restaurant Service Fundamentals & Industry Overview

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to hospitality industry in India — types of restaurants, hotels, and catering establishments
  • Waiter role and responsibilities — job scope, career progression, and expectations
  • Restaurant hierarchy and communication — understanding chain of command from manager to kitchen staff
  • Types of service styles — French service, American service, Russian service, and English service basics
  • Restaurant layout and sections — understanding dining areas, kitchen flow, and service stations
  • Health and safety regulations — Indian food safety standards and FSSAI compliance basics
  • Professional ethics and code of conduct — punctuality, dress code, and customer confidentiality
  • Introduction to restaurant technology — POS systems, billing software, and basic digital ordering

Module 2: Table Setup, Dining Etiquette & Service Basics

📚 Topics Covered

  • Table setup and laying — correct placement of plates, glasses, cutlery for different meal types
  • Napkin folding and placement — basic and advanced folding techniques for formal dining
  • Glassware and crockery knowledge — types of glasses, plates, bowls, and their proper usage
  • Cutlery handling — understanding forks, knives, spoons, and their correct order of use
  • Table numbering and section management — organizing tables for efficient service
  • Greeting and seating guests — welcoming customers, checking reservations, and table assignment
  • Dining etiquette standards — understanding formal dining rules and guest expectations
  • Service techniques — proper posture, hand positioning, and left-hand service method
  • Table maintenance during service — clearing, resetting, and keeping tables clean
  • Special occasion table setup — birthdays, anniversaries, corporate events, and themed setups

Module 3: Order Taking, Menu Knowledge & Communication Skills

📚 Topics Covered

  • Menu knowledge essentials — understanding dishes, ingredients, and preparation methods
  • Reading and interpreting menus — vegetarian, non-vegetarian, vegan, and special diet options
  • Taking orders accurately — listening skills, clarifying preferences, and noting special requests
  • Order communication to kitchen — using order tickets, kitchen terminology, and timing
  • Upselling and suggestive selling — recommending appetizers, beverages, and desserts professionally
  • Handling dietary restrictions — allergies, vegetarian preferences, and religious dietary needs
  • Managing special requests — customizations, portion sizes, and modification requests
  • Handling customer complaints about orders — problem-solving and escalation procedures
  • Beverage service basics — water service, soft drinks, juices, and traditional Indian beverages
  • Taking payment and billing — understanding bills, GST, service charges, and payment methods

Module 4: Food & Beverage Service Techniques & Safety Standards

📚 Topics Covered

  • Food service standards — temperature control, presentation, and plating basics
  • Serving food correctly — right-hand service, proper plating, and avoiding contamination
  • Beverage service techniques — serving water, soft drinks, tea, coffee, and hot beverages
  • Alcoholic beverage service — understanding beer, wine, and spirits service (if applicable venue)
  • Clearing plates and course management — timing between courses, clearing rules, and waste management
  • Food hygiene and safety — handwashing, cross-contamination prevention, and personal cleanliness
  • FSSAI compliance and food safety standards — understanding Indian food safety regulations
  • Handling food allergies and contamination risks — identifying allergens and preventing incidents
  • Temperature maintenance during service — keeping hot food hot and cold food cold
  • Waste management and sustainability — proper disposal and environmental responsibility
  • Handling food waste and leftover management — Indian restaurant standards and regulations
  • Emergency procedures — handling spills, broken items, and customer safety

Module 5: Customer Service Excellence & Conflict Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Customer service philosophy — understanding guest expectations and delivering excellence
  • Communication skills for hospitality — polite language, tone, and non-verbal communication
  • Handling difficult customers — patience, empathy, and de-escalation techniques
  • Managing complaints professionally — listening, apologizing, and offering solutions
  • Building customer relationships — remembering preferences and creating repeat business
  • Multilingual service basics — essential phrases in Hindi, English, and regional languages
  • Cultural sensitivity in Indian hospitality — respecting diverse customs and preferences
  • Managing rush hours and high-pressure situations — staying calm and organized
  • Feedback handling and continuous improvement — learning from customer suggestions
  • VIP and special guest handling — recognizing important customers and providing premium service

Module 6: Professional Development, Workplace Skills & Career Planning

📚 Topics Covered

  • Personal grooming and appearance standards — uniform maintenance, hygiene, and presentation
  • Time management and punctuality — scheduling, shift management, and reliability
  • Teamwork and collaboration — working with chefs, managers, and other service staff
  • Stress management in hospitality — handling pressure, fatigue, and emotional demands
  • Learning from experienced staff — mentorship, feedback, and skill development
  • Career progression pathways — moving from waiter to captain, supervisor, or management
  • Certification and skill upgrades — advanced service techniques and specialized training
  • Job search strategies in India — finding positions in hotels, restaurants, and catering companies
  • Understanding employment contracts and labor rights — wages, working hours, and benefits
  • Building professional portfolio — references, certifications, and performance records

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (with parental consent for 14-16 years)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior experience needed — beginners welcome
  • Already working as a waiter? Apply for RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) exam
  • Valid ID proof required at registration (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Medical fitness certificate may be required by some employers
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and foreign nationals with valid visa

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Demonstrate professional table setup and service techniques according to international standards
  • Take accurate food and beverage orders while providing menu recommendations and handling special requests
  • Serve food and beverages using correct techniques while maintaining hygiene and safety standards
  • Communicate effectively with customers, colleagues, and kitchen staff in professional hospitality settings
  • Handle customer complaints and difficult situations with professionalism, empathy, and problem-solving skills
  • Understand food safety, FSSAI compliance, and health regulations relevant to Indian restaurants
