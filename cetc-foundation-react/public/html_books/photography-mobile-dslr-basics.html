Photography — Mobile & DSLR Basics

📂 Sector: Media, Photography & Content Creation 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1500
Master the art of photography using both smartphones and DSLR cameras. Learn composition, lighting, editing, and storytelling techniques to launch your career in India's booming content creation industry. This hands-on course covers everything from camera basics to creating professional portfolios.

Module 1: Camera Fundamentals & Equipment Basics

📚 Topics Covered

  • Understanding camera anatomy — parts of DSLR, mirrorless, and smartphone cameras
  • Exposure triangle explained — aperture, shutter speed, and ISO working together
  • Aperture and depth of field — creating blur backgrounds (bokeh) for professional shots
  • Shutter speed mastery — freezing motion and capturing movement creatively
  • ISO sensitivity — managing noise and grain in low-light photography
  • Lens types and focal lengths — wide angle, standard, telephoto for different purposes
  • Camera modes — manual, aperture priority, shutter priority, and auto modes
  • Essential accessories — tripods, filters, memory cards, batteries for Indian climate
  • Smartphone camera technology — computational photography and software advantages
  • Comparing mobile vs DSLR — when to use each for different photography types

Module 2: Composition & Visual Storytelling

📚 Topics Covered

  • Rule of thirds — creating balanced and engaging compositions
  • Leading lines — using roads, rivers, and architecture to guide viewer's eye
  • Framing techniques — using natural and artificial frames within images
  • Symmetry and patterns — creating visually striking photographs
  • Depth and layers — adding dimension to flat images through foreground, mid-ground, background
  • Negative space — using empty areas to create powerful compositions
  • Color theory in photography — complementary colors, color psychology, mood creation
  • Perspective and angles — low angle, high angle, bird's eye view storytelling
  • Composition for different genres — portrait, landscape, street, product photography
  • Visual hierarchy — directing attention to main subject through composition
  • Breaking rules creatively — when and how to bend composition guidelines
  • Indian cultural elements — incorporating local architecture, festivals, traditions in compositions

Module 3: Lighting Techniques & Natural Light Mastery

📚 Topics Covered

  • Understanding light quality — hard vs soft light and their effects
  • Golden hour and blue hour — best times for photography in Indian locations
  • Natural light sources — sun, windows, reflective surfaces
  • Directional lighting — front, side, backlighting and rim lighting techniques
  • Metering modes — evaluative, center-weighted, spot metering for exposure control
  • Exposure compensation — adjusting brightness in tricky lighting situations
  • White balance — color temperature, Kelvin scale, auto vs manual white balance
  • Flash photography basics — when to use flash, avoiding red-eye, fill flash technique
  • Reflectors and diffusers — DIY solutions using Indian materials for light modification
  • Indoor lighting challenges — shooting in temples, offices, homes without equipment
  • Harsh sunlight management — shooting in Indian midday sun without harsh shadows
  • Night photography — street lights, neon signs, moon, stars in Indian cities

Module 4: Portrait & People Photography

📚 Topics Covered

  • Portrait lighting setups — Rembrandt, loop, butterfly, and split lighting
  • Posing techniques — flattering poses for different body types and face shapes
  • Eye contact and expression — capturing authentic emotions and connecting with subjects
  • Headshots for professionals — LinkedIn, resume, and portfolio photography
  • Family portrait sessions — group dynamics, Indian family photography traditions
  • Wedding photography basics — pre-wedding shoots, candid moments, traditional ceremonies
  • Children photography — patience, safety, and capturing genuine moments
  • Senior portrait photography — dignity, flattering angles, Indian cultural respect
  • Self-portrait and smartphone selfies — angles, lighting, composition for social media
  • Diversity and inclusion — photographing different skin tones, cultural attire authentically
  • Consent and ethics — respecting privacy, getting permission for street photography
  • Building rapport with subjects — communication skills for comfortable photoshoots

Module 5: Post-Processing & Photo Editing

📚 Topics Covered

  • Raw vs JPEG — advantages of shooting in raw format for editing flexibility
  • Adobe Lightroom basics — organizing, rating, and basic adjustments
  • Exposure and contrast adjustments — recovering highlights and shadows
  • White balance correction — fixing color casts in post-processing
  • Clarity, vibrance, and saturation — enhancing colors without oversaturation
  • Cropping and straightening — improving composition in post-processing
  • Adobe Photoshop fundamentals — layers, selections, and basic retouching
  • Skin retouching — blemish removal, smoothing, maintaining natural look
  • Sharpening and noise reduction — improving image clarity
  • Black and white conversion — creating monochrome images with mood
  • Filter applications — creative effects, vintage looks, mood creation
  • Mobile editing apps — Snapseed, Lightroom Mobile, VSCO for on-the-go editing

Module 6: Building Your Photography Business & Portfolio

📚 Topics Covered

  • Creating a professional portfolio — selecting best work, organizing by genre
  • Building online presence — Instagram, website, Google My Business for photographers
  • Social media strategy — posting frequency, hashtags, engagement for Indian audience
  • Pricing your services — rates for weddings, events, portraits in Indian market
  • Client management — booking, contracts, communication, delivery timelines
  • Wedding photography business — packages, timelines, Indian ceremony photography
  • Event photography — corporate events, conferences, festivals, celebrations
  • Product photography — e-commerce, food, jewelry for online selling
  • Stock photography — selling images on Shutterstock, iStock, Alamy
  • Freelance platforms — Upwork, Fiverr, 99designs for Indian photographers
  • Legal and taxes — GST registration, invoicing, copyright protection in India
  • Continuous learning — workshops, online courses, staying updated with trends

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior photography experience required
  • Access to smartphone camera or DSLR (can be borrowed/shared)
  • Already working as photographer? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, Passport)
  • Applicable for all Indian citizens and registered foreign nationals
  • No upper age limit — open for career changers and retirees

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Demonstrate mastery of camera settings and exposure triangle for proper image exposure
  • Apply composition principles and visual storytelling to create engaging photographs
  • Utilize natural and artificial lighting techniques to achieve professional-quality images
  • Execute portrait and people photography with proper posing and rapport-building
  • Edit and retouch photographs using industry-standard software like Lightroom and Photoshop
  • Build a professional photography business with portfolio, pricing, and client management skills
