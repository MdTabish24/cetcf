Stock Market & Investment Basics

📂 Sector: Business, Retail & Finance 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the fundamentals of stock market investing and build a strong foundation in financial literacy. This course teaches you how to understand stock exchanges, analyze companies, and make informed investment decisions in the Indian market. Perfect for beginners who want to grow wealth through smart investing.

Module 1: Stock Market Fundamentals & Indian Financial System

📚 Topics Covered

  • What is a stock market and why it matters — Understanding equity ownership, wealth creation, and economic growth in India
  • NSE (National Stock Exchange) vs BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange) — Key differences, trading hours, listing requirements, and market structure
  • Stock market participants — Investors, traders, brokers, regulators (SEBI), and their roles in Indian markets
  • Types of securities — Shares (equity), bonds, debentures, mutual funds, and ETFs available in India
  • How to open a Demat and Trading account — Step-by-step process with major brokers like Zerodha, Angel, HDFC Securities
  • SEBI regulations and investor protection — Rules for retail investors, complaint redressal, and legal safeguards
  • Stock market indices — Understanding SENSEX (BSE 30), NIFTY 50, NIFTY 100, and what they represent
  • Market cycles and economic indicators — Bull markets, bear markets, GDP, inflation, and interest rates affecting Indian stocks
  • Cost of investing — Brokerage charges, taxes (STT, capital gains), and hidden costs to avoid
  • Historical context of Indian stock market — Growth from 1875 to 2024, key events, and lessons for investors

Module 2: Understanding Stocks, Shares & Company Valuation

📚 Topics Covered

  • What is a share and equity ownership — How buying shares makes you a company owner with voting rights
  • Face value, market price, and book value — Understanding different valuations of Indian company stocks
  • Dividends and bonus shares — How companies reward shareholders and impact on investment returns
  • Rights issues and stock splits — Corporate actions that affect your shareholding in Indian companies
  • Market capitalization (Market Cap) — Large-cap, mid-cap, small-cap stocks and their characteristics
  • Earnings Per Share (EPS) — Calculating and interpreting EPS for Indian companies like TCS, Infosys, Reliance
  • Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio — How to use P/E ratio to identify overvalued and undervalued stocks
  • Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio — Understanding asset value and when to buy stocks trading below book value
  • Dividend Yield — Calculating returns from dividends for income-focused investing in Indian stocks
  • Return on Equity (ROE) — Measuring how efficiently companies use shareholder money
  • Understanding Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Cash Flow Statement — Reading and interpreting financial statements of Indian companies
  • Comparing Indian companies — Valuation metrics for sectors like IT, Banking, Pharma, FMCG

Module 4: Technical Analysis & Market Trends

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to technical analysis — Understanding price patterns, trends, and volume for short-term trading
  • Candlestick charts and patterns — Reading Japanese candlesticks, doji, hammer, engulfing patterns in Indian stocks
  • Support and resistance levels — Identifying price levels where stocks bounce or break in Indian markets
  • Trend analysis — Understanding uptrends, downtrends, and sideways movements in stock prices
  • Moving averages — Using 50-day, 100-day, 200-day moving averages to identify trends
  • Relative Strength Index (RSI) — Measuring momentum and overbought/oversold conditions
  • MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) — Using MACD for trend confirmation and reversal signals
  • Volume analysis — Understanding trading volume and what it tells about price movements
  • Bollinger Bands — Using volatility bands to identify trading opportunities in Indian stocks
  • Fibonacci retracement — Using Fibonacci levels to predict support and resistance in trending markets
  • Chart patterns — Head and shoulders, triangles, flags, and double tops/bottoms in Indian stock charts
  • Combining indicators — Using multiple indicators together for better accuracy in trading decisions

Module 5: Investment Strategies & Risk Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Investment vs Trading — Understanding the difference between long-term investing and short-term trading
  • Value investing strategy — Buying undervalued stocks and holding for long-term wealth creation
  • Growth investing strategy — Investing in companies with high growth potential in Indian market
  • Dividend investing strategy — Building passive income through dividend-paying stocks in India
  • Portfolio diversification — Spreading investments across sectors, market caps, and asset classes
  • Asset allocation — Dividing portfolio between stocks, bonds, gold, and cash based on age and risk tolerance
  • Dollar-cost averaging (DCA) — Investing fixed amount regularly to reduce timing risk
  • SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) — Disciplined investing approach popular in India for mutual funds and stocks
  • Risk assessment and tolerance — Understanding your risk capacity based on income, age, and financial goals
  • Stop-loss and profit-taking — Setting exit strategies to protect capital and lock in profits
  • Position sizing — Determining how much money to invest in each stock based on risk
  • Emergency fund and insurance — Building financial safety net before starting stock market investing

📚 Topics Covered

  • Securities Transaction Tax (STT) — Understanding tax on stock purchases and sales in India
  • Capital gains tax — Short-term capital gains (15% STT) vs long-term capital gains (10% without indexation)
  • Dividend tax and tax-saving investments — ELSs, NSC, and tax-efficient investing in India
  • Tax filing for stock market income — Filing ITR forms and reporting capital gains to income tax department
  • TDS on dividends and interest — Understanding tax deducted at source on investment income
  • Using trading platforms — Zerodha, Angel Broking, HDFC Securities, and other major platforms in India
  • Placing buy and sell orders — Market orders, limit orders, and different order types explained
  • Using screeners and research tools — Finding stocks using BSE/NSE screeners and investment research websites
  • Reading live market data — Understanding bid-ask spread, volume, open interest, and market depth
  • Practical case studies — Real examples of successful and failed investments in Indian stocks
  • Building your first portfolio — Step-by-step guide to selecting and buying stocks worth ₹10,000-50,000
  • Monitoring and rebalancing portfolio — Regular review and adjusting portfolio allocation quarterly
