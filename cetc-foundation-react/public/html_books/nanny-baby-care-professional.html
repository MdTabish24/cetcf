Nanny & Baby Care Professional

📂 Sector: Child Care & Domestic Services 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 2 Months 🕐 Hours: 120 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Master the essential skills of professional baby care and nanny services with our comprehensive 2-month certification program. Learn child development, safety protocols, nutrition, and behavioral management to become a trusted childcare professional in Indian households and daycare centers.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Child Development & Baby Care

📚 Topics Covered

  • Child development stages (0-5 years) — Understanding physical, cognitive, emotional and social milestones at each age
  • Newborn care basics — Handling, positioning, swaddling, and creating safe sleep environments
  • Growth monitoring — Height, weight tracking, developmental checkups, and red flags to report
  • Infant reflexes and behavior — Understanding crying patterns, sleep cycles, and normal infant behaviors
  • Bonding and attachment — Creating secure relationships and emotional connection with babies
  • Individual differences — Recognizing unique temperaments, needs, and personality traits in children
  • Play and learning — Age-appropriate activities, toys, and developmental play for different age groups
  • Communication milestones — Speech development, language understanding, and encouraging communication

Module 2: Health, Hygiene & Safety Protocols

📚 Topics Covered

  • Personal hygiene standards — Hand washing, nail care, clothing standards, and infection prevention
  • Baby hygiene routine — Diaper changing, bathing, cord care, and preventing diaper rash
  • Common childhood illnesses — Recognizing fever, cough, diarrhea, rash, and when to seek medical help
  • Vaccination schedule — Understanding Indian immunization schedule and tracking vaccination records
  • First aid basics — CPR for infants, choking relief, wound care, and emergency response
  • Safe environment setup — Baby-proofing rooms, preventing falls, burns, and poisoning hazards
  • Infection control — Handling bodily fluids, disinfecting surfaces, and preventing disease transmission
  • Medication safety — Proper storage, administration, dosage, and documentation of medicines
  • Emergency procedures — Fire safety, earthquake preparedness, evacuation plans, and emergency contacts

Module 3: Nutrition, Feeding & Dietary Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Breastfeeding support — Positioning, latching, expressing milk, and supporting nursing mothers
  • Formula feeding — Bottle preparation, sterilization, temperature control, and safe storage
  • Introduction to solids — Timing, first foods, portion sizes, and identifying food allergies
  • Age-appropriate nutrition — Balanced diet for 6-12 months, 1-3 years, and 3-5 years
  • Meal planning — Creating nutritious menus, Indian food adaptations, and budget-friendly options
  • Feeding challenges — Dealing with picky eating, refusal, choking prevention, and gagging
  • Allergies and intolerances — Identifying symptoms, common allergens, and safe food substitution
  • Hydration and water safety — Water requirements, clean water sources, and preventing dehydration
  • Special dietary needs — Managing constipation, diarrhea, and nutritional deficiencies
  • Kitchen hygiene — Safe food storage, preventing contamination, and maintaining cleanliness

Module 4: Behavioral Management & Early Childhood Education

📚 Topics Covered

  • Understanding behavior — Why babies cry, tantrum triggers, and age-appropriate expectations
  • Positive discipline — Gentle correction, setting boundaries, and avoiding harmful practices
  • Managing tantrums — De-escalation techniques, distraction methods, and staying calm
  • Sleep training basics — Establishing routines, handling sleep resistance, and night-time management
  • Emotional intelligence — Recognizing and naming emotions, validating feelings, and building confidence
  • Language development activities — Talking, singing, reading, and storytelling techniques
  • Cognitive stimulation — Sensory play, problem-solving games, and age-appropriate learning activities
  • Social skills development — Sharing, turn-taking, greeting, and basic manners training
  • Screen time guidelines — Age-appropriate limits, content selection, and healthy digital habits
  • Building independence — Self-feeding, dressing, toileting, and encouraging self-help skills

Module 5: Professional Skills & Communication

📚 Topics Covered

  • Professional ethics — Confidentiality, boundaries, respect for family values, and cultural sensitivity
  • Communication with parents — Daily updates, reporting concerns, handling feedback, and conflict resolution
  • Documentation and record-keeping — Maintaining daily logs, health records, developmental progress notes
  • Time management — Scheduling activities, managing multiple tasks, and maintaining routines
  • Stress management — Self-care, recognizing burnout, coping strategies, and work-life balance
  • Professional appearance — Dress code, grooming standards, and workplace expectations
  • Working with families — Understanding family dynamics, respecting parenting styles, and teamwork
  • Problem-solving — Handling difficult situations, emergency decision-making, and escalation procedures
  • Continuing education — Staying updated, professional development, and skill enhancement
  • Legal awareness — Employment rights, contract understanding, and workplace regulations in India

Module 6: Practical Application & Internship

📚 Topics Covered

  • Hands-on baby care — Practical diaper changing, bathing, dressing, and grooming under supervision
  • Feeding practice — Bottle feeding, spoon feeding, introducing solids, and managing messy eating
  • Sleep routine implementation — Creating bedtime routines, settling techniques, and overnight care
  • Safety demonstration — Room setup, hazard identification, emergency response drills, and first aid practice
  • Activity facilitation — Organizing play sessions, educational activities, and developmental games
  • Behavior observation — Identifying developmental progress, noting concerns, and documenting observations
  • Communication practice — Writing daily reports, speaking with parents, and professional interactions
  • Internship experience — Real-world childcare in homes or daycare centers with mentor supervision
  • Case study analysis — Reviewing real scenarios, problem-solving exercises, and decision-making practice
  • Portfolio development — Creating documentation of skills, achievements, and professional readiness
  • Mock interviews — Preparing for nanny job interviews and professional positioning
  • Certification preparation — Revision, practice tests, and final assessment readiness

✅ Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum age: 14 years (16 years preferred for better job prospects)
  • Minimum education: Class 8th pass or equivalent
  • No prior childcare experience required
  • Already working as a nanny? Apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) exam
  • Valid ID proof required (Aadhar, PAN, or Voter ID) at registration
  • Medical fitness certificate recommended (TB test negative)
  • Applicable for all candidates regardless of gender, caste, or religion
  • Willingness to work with children and commitment to their safety

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand child development stages and provide age-appropriate care for infants and young children
  • Implement comprehensive hygiene, health, and safety protocols to create safe childcare environments
  • Plan and prepare nutritious meals while managing feeding challenges and dietary requirements
  • Apply positive behavioral management techniques and support emotional and cognitive development
  • Communicate professionally with parents and maintain proper documentation and records
  • Respond appropriately to health emergencies and provide basic first aid care
