Nursery & Plant Propagation

📂 Sector: Agriculture & Rural Livelihood 📊 Level: Foundation ⏱️ Duration: 1 Month 🕐 Hours: 60 💰 Exam Fee: ₹1000
Learn the complete art and science of plant nursery management and propagation techniques used across India's horticulture industry. This hands-on course trains you to establish and operate successful plant nurseries, from seed selection to healthy seedling production, with direct job opportunities in government forestry departments, horticultural institutes, and private nurseries.

Module 1: Fundamentals of Plant Nursery & Propagation

📚 Topics Covered

  • Introduction to plant nursery: types, scope, and importance in Indian agriculture — Understanding commercial, forestry, and ornamental nurseries; market demand in urban landscaping and reforestation projects
  • Plant propagation methods overview — Sexual (seed-based) and asexual (vegetative) propagation; advantages, disadvantages, and when to use each method
  • Nursery site selection and planning — Soil testing, water availability, sunlight exposure, accessibility, and climate suitability for different plant types
  • Basic plant anatomy and growth — Understanding roots, stems, leaves, and how plants grow; importance of meristems in propagation
  • Nursery layout and infrastructure design — Planning nursery beds, shade structures, irrigation systems, and storage facilities for Indian climate
  • Regulatory requirements and government schemes — APEDA guidelines, state horticulture department registrations, subsidies for nursery setup under PM-KISAN and similar schemes
  • Safety and hygiene in nursery operations — Personal protective equipment, sanitation practices, and waste management
  • Tools and equipment identification — Spades, forks, pruners, dibbers, sprayers, measuring scales, and their proper maintenance

Module 2: Seed Selection, Storage & Germination

📚 Topics Covered

  • Seed quality assessment and testing — Viability testing, germination percentage, purity testing, and certification standards for Indian seed industry
  • Seed collection and processing — Proper harvesting time, drying techniques, cleaning, and grading of seeds from mother plants
  • Seed storage techniques — Moisture control, temperature management, storage containers, and shelf-life determination for different seed types
  • Dormancy breaking methods — Stratification, scarification, soaking, and chemical treatments for seeds with hard coats (neem, jamun, babul)
  • Pre-sowing treatments — Fungicide treatment, bioagent application, and priming techniques to improve germination rates
  • Germination media preparation — Soil composition, coco-peat, vermiculite, and perlite ratios for optimal seed germination
  • Seed bed preparation and sowing — Bed construction, seed spacing, depth, and watering schedule for different crops
  • Monitoring germination and seedling care — Daily observations, thinning, watering, and protection from pests and diseases during critical germination phase
  • Hardening off seedlings — Gradual exposure to outdoor conditions and sunlight before transplanting

Module 3: Vegetative Propagation Techniques

📚 Topics Covered

  • Cutting propagation — Softwood, semi-hardwood, and hardwood cuttings; selection of mother plant and timing for different species (rose, bougainvillea, guava)
  • Rooting hormones and their application — IBA and NAA concentrations, dust and liquid formulations, proper application techniques for faster rooting
  • Misting and humidity management — Automated and manual misting systems, humidity chambers, and polyhouse setup for successful cutting propagation
  • Layering techniques — Air layering (marcottage) for mango, guava, and ornamentals; ground layering for fruit plants; advantages and success rates
  • Grafting and budding basics — Cleft grafting, whip grafting, and T-budding for fruit plants (mango, citrus, apple); rootstock selection and compatibility
  • Tissue culture introduction — Basic concept of micropropagation, advantages for disease-free plants, and commercial applications in India
  • Division and separation methods — Propagation of bulbs, corms, rhizomes, and suckers for ornamental and vegetable crops
  • Propagation of specific high-value plants — Techniques for roses, carnations, spices (cardamom, pepper), fruit saplings, and forest species native to India

Module 4: Nursery Media, Potting & Transplanting

📚 Topics Covered

  • Potting media formulation — Soil, sand, organic matter ratios; prepared media for different plant types; soil-less media advantages
  • Organic amendments and biofertilizers — Neem cake, vermicompost, FYM, mycorrhizae, and Azospirillum for healthy root development
  • Container selection and sizing — Nursery bags, pots, trays; material selection for Indian climate; drainage hole importance
  • Filling and preparing containers — Proper technique to avoid air pockets and soil compaction; moisture adjustment before use
  • Transplanting seedlings — Timing, handling techniques, depth adjustment, and watering schedule post-transplanting
  • Pricking out and spacing — Moving seedlings from germination beds to individual containers; proper spacing for light and air circulation
  • Hardening off before sale — Gradual exposure protocol, watering reduction, and stress conditioning for field planting success
  • Record keeping and labeling — Plant variety, date of potting, propagation method, and expected sale date documentation

Module 5: Nursery Nutrition, Irrigation & Pest Management

📚 Topics Covered

  • Nutrient requirements for nursery plants — NPK ratios, micronutrients, and deficiency symptoms in seedlings and saplings
  • Fertilizer application methods — Fertigation, foliar spraying, soil application; organic vs. chemical fertilizers; dosage calculation
  • Irrigation scheduling and techniques — Drip irrigation, sprinkler systems, overhead watering; water quality testing and conservation methods
  • Common pests in nurseries — Identification of mites, whiteflies, scale insects, thrips, and cutworms; damage symptoms and life cycles
  • Disease management — Damping off, leaf spots, root rots, and powdery mildew; prevention through sanitation and biocontrol
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM) — Biological control agents (ladybugs, parasitoid wasps), botanical pesticides (neem oil, garlic spray), and chemical pesticides as last resort
  • Pesticide safety and application — Proper mixing, spraying techniques, protective equipment, and residue management
  • Weed management in nurseries — Manual removal, mulching, and selective herbicide use without harming young plants

Module 6: Nursery Operations, Marketing & Entrepreneurship

📚 Topics Covered

  • Daily nursery operations — Watering schedule, weeding, pruning, staking, and maintenance routines for different plant types
  • Quality control and grading — Plant health assessment, height-girth standards, root development evaluation, and certification for sale
  • Record maintenance and inventory — Stock registers, sales records, cost tracking, and profitability analysis for business sustainability
  • Packaging and transportation — Safe handling of plants, packaging materials, transportation methods, and post-delivery care instructions
  • Market linkages and sales channels — Direct sales to farmers, institutional buyers (schools, parks), online platforms, and government tenders
  • Pricing strategies — Cost calculation (media, labor, utilities), profit margins, seasonal pricing, and competitive analysis in local markets
  • Starting a nursery business — Capital requirement, land lease options, registration with horticulture department, and government subsidies (PMKSY-H, state schemes)
  • Digital tools for nursery management — Mobile apps for inventory, customer database, and financial tracking; online marketing through WhatsApp and social media
  • Quality certification and standards — APEDA certification for export-quality plants, organic nursery certification, and ISO standards awareness
  • Scaling operations — Expanding from small home nursery to commercial-scale operation; hiring and training staff; bulk production planning

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and select quality seeds and propagation materials suitable for different plant types and Indian climate zones
  • Execute sexual and asexual propagation techniques (seeds, cuttings, layering, grafting) with success rates above 70%
  • Design and setup a functional nursery layout with appropriate infrastructure, irrigation, and shade management
  • Prepare potting media, pot plants, and manage seedling care following industry standards and hygiene practices
  • Diagnose and manage common nursery pests and diseases using Integrated Pest Management principles
  • Operate and scale a nursery business profitably with proper record-keeping, quality control, and market linkage skills
